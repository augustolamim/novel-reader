"U, UUUuughGG."

Hero Liam was not looking at them.

He had collapsed on the spot clutching his chest. Even at a glance, something was clearly wrong. Then he arched his back sharply and clawed at his throat in search of breath.

His shoulders convulsed, and every time he ground his teeth together, a low groan leaked out. Veins rose all over him, and his body jerked with every pulse. The suffering did not look like illness--it looked as though something inside him was devouring him.

His gaze was directed toward this place. But his eyes were unfocused, not truly catching a single thing in the world.

At least some human awareness seemed to remain, and for now it was still restraining him. That much was fortunate.

Why had this happened?

Was the black dragon simply too strong?

In terms of raw power alone, the black dragon certainly surpassed the three Seven Great Magical Beasts that formed the core of their present force, at least before those were turned into synthetic beasts. Judging by performance alone, it had been the finest material imaginable.

And Hero Liam was no less extraordinary. Gearnote had judged that the gap between them was not so great as to make control impossible.

But reality had proved otherwise. Hero Liam was already showing signs of going berserk.

Why?

Gearnote, being a researcher by nature, began thinking through the cause of the failure.

Depending on the answer, perhaps there was still a way to salvage it.

For the moment, he had at least something like an answer.

Perhaps it was the incompatibility between Liam's ice affinity and the black dragon's fire affinity.

Or perhaps the black dragon's primitive battle instinct was fatally incompatible with the hero's mind as well.

An ordinary human might not have suffered this kind of rejection.

But to a hero, perhaps it had become a deadly toxin.

And yet. And yet.

In return, Hero Liam had gained a terrifying power.

That power was unquestionably a valuable fighting force.

Was there no way to make use of it?

In any case, there was not enough time. They already had to escape from this half-ruined airship.

While Gearnote agonized over that,

Regingule began laughing quietly in his throat.

"To think the day would come when you, of all people, would be the one to pick a fight after refusing every invitation to battle."

Saying that, Regingule stood up with his axe as support.

His body was plainly at its limit. The bleeding showed no sign of stopping. Even so, his great frame did not fall.

"D-don't... coMe... CLOSER..."

Liam, on the other hand, was crouched there as though holding his own head together.

He gritted his teeth in pain and let out groans, but it did not look as though he had completely lost his reason yet.

--Now.

Gearnote slipped a hand inside his lab coat.

If Regingule and Hero Liam fought like this, the airship would go down for good.

He had to suppress Liam before that happened.

What he drew was a small dagger. Its blade had been coated in advance with a potent paralytic.

If Liam was in this condition, there was a chance it could neutralize him.

Gearnote broke into a quiet run, closing the distance.

And at that exact moment--

"White... coat... WHat are yoU doING to MEEEE--!!"

Hero Liam glared at the approaching Gearnote, roared like a beast, and made the air shake.

Gearnote went rigid.

Liam's mind seemed clouded, but he had been trying to hold himself back against Regingule.

But Gearnote...

To Hero Liam, Gearnote was not a "comrade." If anything, he was merely the researcher who had put him into this unrecognizable condition.

"Gah...?!"

Liam swung an arm as though brushing off an insect.

That was all.

He had not struck Gearnote directly. He had not even touched him. But the pressure of the swing alone sent Gearnote's body flying through the air.

He slammed into a wall and rolled across the floor.

Blood burst from his mouth, and his lungs burned.

He could not stand. If another blow came, he was finished. But there was no follow-up.

"...This isn't like you."

Regingule's low voice reached his ears. He was speaking to Hero Liam.

"It is nothing like you. Not in the slightest."

He was staring straight at Liam, utterly unafraid of a hero so monstrously strong.

"Protecting people. I don't understand that myself, but that conviction was supposed to be at your core. You once said it was what made you strong. But what now? You're on the verge of losing it."

Resting the axe on his shoulder, he went on as though spitting the words out.

"Take yourself back. Don't lose to the beast mixed into you."

"Y-you're... harsh..."

Only a weakened voice came back.

"...It can't be helped. Not my style, but I'll indulge you. Go wild once. You might be surprised how much better it feels."

The next instant, Regingule hurled his axe.

Not at Liam. To the side.

With a tremendous roar, a massive hole was torn open in the airship's outer hull.

Air came howling in, and the ship tilted hard.

"What are you doing?!"

Gearnote shouted, but Regingule ignored him as though that were only natural.

"Come. If you're still conscious, fly somewhere there are no people. Use those wings."

As though obeying those words, Liam turned toward the blasted-open hole and vanished.

By the time Gearnote realized it, Regingule was gone as well.

Still lying prone on the floor with his body in pain, Gearnote inched his way over to the nearby hole and looked out.

Two shadows had left the airship and been thrown into the sky.

Hero Liam was there, beating dragon wings through the air.

And Regingule was there as well, moving with him while holding on to Liam's leg.

He had taken the battlefield away from the airship.

Having the wall broken was an error, but this bought them more time before the ship was completely destroyed.

In that time, he had to escape with the soldiers still aboard.

"...No choice, then."

Gearnote gritted his teeth.

There was no way he was standing up.

He could not die halfway through things like this. He had to begin a desperate escape from the airship. Even if he had to crawl, he would move.

Even so, there was one thing Gearnote regretted.

He wanted to recover an important material.

The black dragon's corpse.

That enormous body. Those scales. Its flesh and blood. Even the lingering traces of power left behind like residue--all of it could still be useful.

No, precisely because of what came next, it had to be made useful.

The amount of material used on Hero Liam had been only a tiny fraction.

Using the rest, they could create synthetic beasts even if beastfolk soldiers were impossible, given that even Hero Liam had gone berserk.

No--if they modified the brain as well, then depending on the method, even beastfolk soldiers might still be viable.

Stronger specimens than any they had created before.

He still wanted it recovered.

He could order the winged beastfolk aboard the ship to transport the black dragon's corpse instead of rescuing the crew, making all of them work on that task.

He could give up on everyone except himself and a handful of other top-priority personnel.

There was no emotion in the calculation. It was regrettable, but they would have to die for future victory. The battle was not over yet.

He had already given the researchers they passed on the way here orders to withdraw, but now he needed to change those orders.

And yet the body lying facedown on the floor would not obey his will as cleanly as he wanted.

His lungs were full of the taste of blood, breathing was painful, his vision swayed, and he could feel his strength leaving him.

Far too strong.

What a truly splendid power it was.

Hero Liam had probably held back with the fragments of self still left in him, and the blow had not even connected. Gearnote would likely survive, but he was badly injured.

He stretched out a hand toward the floor.

His fingertips caught the edge of the metal, and he put strength into them.

He dragged his body forward, advancing only the slightest bit.

The distance he could cover in one pull was not even a few centimeters.

But that was fine.

As long as he did not stop, that was enough.

Slowly.

For the sake of surviving.
