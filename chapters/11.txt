Episode 116: Gathering Information







After walking around Area 1 for a while, I began to hear whispers from behind me and from the side.

At first I thought I was imagining it, but I could clearly pick out fragments like my alias Jane and references to a mask.

　

"...That's the one..."

"Jane, the masked guy who's been slaughtering demon generals..."

"I heard rumors he's dangerous..."


It was obvious that my existence had become a topic of rumor, and it felt as if those grating whispers were following me from nowhere.

From the sound of it, they leaned a little toward infamy.

Either way, I didn't like the feeling of my name wandering around on its own, detached from my intentions.

I was being watched, measured, judged without knowing when or by whom.

I really wasn't good with attention.


'Hmm. They're calling you a dangerous person, Brave.'


'Well, I did go around doing things like handing recommendation letters to the people... So it kind of fits, which is what hurts.'


Lumiela frowned at the way people were talking about me, but since I really had caused a fair amount of trouble, I couldn't exactly object.

As we walked farther on, I found a place where people were gathered together in one corner.

When I got closer, I realized it was a market for trading goods.

Improvised stalls made of cloth and wooden planks lined the rock wall, piled high with ore, herbs, preserved food, fragments of weapons and armor, and all sorts of things.

Some of it was even magical tools.

Loud bargaining rose from every direction.

Everyone there was desperate to get what they wanted, glaring at each other one moment, shaking hands and laughing the next.


As soon as I approached, I felt those stares again.

A few people looked over at me from a distance, whispering to one another.

Apparently the rumors had reached this place too.


Thinking this was getting uncomfortable, I told myself to endure it for now and kept walking.


At one stall drawing particular crowds, magical tools of every size were scattered across a cloth laid out by the rock wall.

There were finely crafted pieces and rough, homemade-looking junk all mixed together.

Some of them looked downright counterfeit.

A thin man who seemed to be the owner kept stroking his goatee and repeating, "Fine goods, fine goods," while behind him stood two large men and what looked like a mage.

Maybe they were an adventurer party.

Mage plus two close-range fighters--a pretty balanced setup.


As I looked everything over, I thought through what I wanted to buy and what I had available to trade.

　

There wasn't much that felt truly new, but I did trade for a few things, and that brought my shopping in Area 1 to an end.

For the moment, I picked up a serrated knife that secreted poison from the blade when it cut, though it probably wouldn't mean much against the Demon King.

Still... maybe there was some tiny chance poison could slow him down or something.

　

And also... purely incidentally.

I was hoping I'd be lucky enough to find Tanpa, one of the muscle-party members I had briefly traveled with while returning from the Empire to the Kingdom.

The reason was simple:

to improve my physical fundamentals.

Late as it was, I wanted to do some efficient strength training and raise my base ability even a little.

Before, those muscle siblings had given me something they had written about how to build muscle or something along those lines, but I had stuffed it away in my luggage without reading it.

Then, while fleeing from Rubrum Nox, both my luggage and that paper were lost.

　

When I talked to Eve in the library, I learned that Tanpa had been gathering more fellow muscleheads since then, so I thought maybe he might be here.

If not, I could ask Toshi and the others instead.

Since I was the one who guided them, they were probably in a town near Yuren or something.

But if possible, Tanpa would know the most up-to-date? muscle-training information.

　

So I began casually asking the people around me:

"Do you know an A-rank adventurer musclehead named Tanpa?"




--------------------------------------------------



　

The first person I asked was a female adventurer wearing leather armor.

After taking a swig from her flask, she tilted her head.


"Tanpa? Never heard of him. He's probably not here."


I thanked her, slipped some money into her hand, and moved on.

When I asked a mercenary next, he gave me a wry grin and said, "There are muscles rotting all over the place down here, but that name's new to me."

I gave him some money too.

　

It was true that this underground settlement was overflowing with burly mercenaries and adventurers.

Maybe just being a huge mass of muscles wasn't enough to stand out.


When I next questioned an elderly man who looked like a mage and was seated at a stall, he furrowed his brow and said, "Hmm, Tanpa...? Feels like I might've heard that name recently. But I don't know where he is now."

Apparently Tanpa had at least some name recognition.


But the more I searched, the more I got nothing but unsatisfying answers.

I had hoped maybe by pure chance he'd happen to be in the Koshumar Great Mine, but it didn't look like it.

He might still be in one of the other areas, but at the very least, he wasn't showing up in Area 1.


'What do we do, Brave? Should we look for this Tanpa person and try the other areas?'


'Hmm, yeah, let's do that. I'm done with what I wanted to do here. Though I heard the other areas are packed with dangerous people...'


While asking around for Tanpa, I had also asked about Areas 1 through 3.

Apparently the underworld types the branch office had mentioned started showing up from Area 2 onward.

Area 1 was known as a base where adventurers and mercenaries gathered, full of strong-armed types looking for work and reward.

The taverns and barter stalls were lively, but skirmishes and fights were apparently commonplace in the shadows.

Drunken blade incidents weren't unusual.

So while they couldn't quite call public order "bad," they definitely couldn't proudly call it "good" either.


Area 2, farther in, sounded much darker.

Underground organizations and illegal merchants had taken root there, and people said contraband and shady contracts changed hands openly in ways you would never see in the public square.

Apparently only those with money or strength survived there, and by rumor even human lives were traded easily.

In other words, the place was a complete wreck in terms of public safety, and I really didn't want to go to some dark city like that.

But at least in terms of force, I should be able to manage.

...And there might be incredible magical tools available through the back channels, so I decided I'd go take a look after all.


And as for the deepest part, Area 3...

Apparently it no longer even resembled human society.

Those swallowed by madness wandered there, and laughter and screams stripped of reason echoed through the tunnels.

No one wanted to go near it.

People feared that once you entered Area 3, you never returned.


Yeah, okay, first of all, what exactly is in Area 3?

Well, whatever. Let's go see.

I made up my mind.

　

"Heeey... yo, you doing good?"


Just as I was about to leave Area 1, someone called out from behind me.

When I turned around, I saw an old man swaying in place with a liquor bottle in one hand.

His hair was mixed with white, he had an unshaven beard, and his face was red.

He was completely drunk.

But at a glance, I could tell he wasn't merely some random drunk.

He had a presence that felt strong.

In fact... he seemed about as strong as a demon general.

There were many adventurers and mercenaries in Area 1, but most of them looked weaker than demon generals.

Among them, this old man was clearly one of the upper-tier ones.


"Do you need something?"


"Mmm. I've got a proposal for ya. Wanna be my disciple?"


That was what this completely incomprehensible drunk old man said.


"Huh?"


I couldn't make sense of the sudden words and let out a bewildered sound.

Who even was this old man?

Was he some famous person or something?
