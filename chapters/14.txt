Episode 119: Humans Fighting Humans







I slowly approached the window.

Looking out through the glass, I saw that the entire street had become the center of attention. Eyes were focused on one spot from windows all across the row of houses, and the air itself felt taut. Behind me, Mr. Rowan let out a small breath.


'For people to be on guard just because they're coming... what kind of people are they?'


Lumiela's voice whispered in my head.


'At the very least, I doubt they're decent people.'


Replying through telepathy, I put a hand on the window frame and leaned out to get a better look at the commotion outside.

And then--

I doubted my eyes.


"What the hell... is that?"


The people who had come from Area 3 were beyond anything I had imagined.

On their heads, only the middle strip of hair stood up sharply like a ridge. The sides were shaved clean, their exposed scalps gleaming in the magical light.

That strip of hair had been dyed like flame, colors like red, yellow, and purple dancing through it.

They were...

they were mohawks.


"Hyahhaaaa!!!"


An entire crowd of mohawk-haired lunatics came charging in like a storm while shouting "Hyahha!"

Their mohawks stood on end, their faces were smeared with red paint or something like it, and foam spilled from their mouths as they screamed, "Hyahha hyahha hyahhaaa!!"

They hurled things, rapid-fired magic, swung weapons, ran, and leaped around. There had to be around a hundred of them at a glance. A horde of madness.

Was this the end of the world or something?


'I thought the world hadn't gotten quite that bad yet, but to think people like this would show up...'


'That's a distinctive hairstyle... and they're very energetic.'


'They're definitely hyahha.'


'They are hyahha.'


But the people of Area 2 were not about to sit quietly either.

They lived in the underworld, and they were hardened through and through.

Weapon in hand, they moved to intercept instead. Some of them used magic, and sparks and blood began to fly.

And so the battle began between the mad hyahha horde and the underworld people of Area 2.


Somewhere, screams. Somewhere else, laughter. Somewhere else, explosions.

Chaos and madness mixed together, and a scene from hell unfolded right before my eyes.


"Damn it... persistent bastards. Aren't you afraid to die?!"

"Hyahha!! Who gives a damn about dying?! If I'm happy right now, that's all that matters! We live for the moment!"


Shouting that, one of the mohawks laughed, clashed with a thug, and both of them stopped moving.

In the meantime, the other mohawks were stripping the thugs of everything they had, stealing their belongings and doing whatever they pleased.

If the underworld had rules and order, then this lot lacked even that.

They were hopeless.

I really couldn't understand them.

They laughed everything off with "hyahha," life and death alike, and lived however they pleased.

But on the other hand, if you could go that far, maybe you really were happy.


'...This is the worst. I don't want to get dragged into it. Let's get out of this area.'

'Yeah. I really don't want anything to do with this...'


While we were talking, another enormous explosion went off.

When I looked in that direction, several buildings had already been blown to pieces.

Sparks drifted down, and burning debris rained through the air.


At the center of the blast stood a single woman.

A black lace dress--a gothic lolita outfit. Her eyes were different colors.

Her hair was long, her eyes were swollen, and in one hand she held an empty liquor bottle.

Her shoulders trembled as she drank, and in the very next moment--


"Hic... I lost all my money gambling...! I'm depressed. So depressed. This is too sad. That's right, everyone, the world and humanity, they all bully me. So cruel, so cruel, so cruel!! Then just die already!"


She shouted that while crying, raging, and screaming.

For some reason, she was smiling.

She cried while raging, and raged while smiling.

It was disgusting.

Around her, burning buildings were collapsing and screams were echoing, yet the woman in the goth-loli outfit seemed to take it all in as if it were no more than part of the scenery.


"Die die die die die...!! If everyone dies, that means I'll be the happiest in the world, right?! Ahaha, ahahaha!"


Faced with that deranged figure, all I could do was recoil.

Lumiela was frowning too.


That mentally unstable goth-loli woman looked bad enough in the head, but she also seemed unexpectedly strong. Maybe a little above Edelweiss's Zeluas. Which meant she was strong enough not to think much of ordinary demon generals.


"What a mess," Mr. Rowan muttered after walking up beside me.


"The mohawks are a mad group that lives only for the moment, led by a man who calls himself Captain Hyahha. As for the woman, she's the Kingdom's only S-rank adventurer. Nostalgia of Playing with Fire. When she's drinking, you can't reason with her, and she starts rampaging."


"So that's the S-rank adventurer. I'd heard rumors, but I can't believe they just leave something like that alone."


"Going after an S-rank adventurer carries enormous risk. And besides... she likes gambling, and the damage she causes is usually directed at the underworld. For ordinary people and the Kingdom, she's still one of the better ones, I suppose."


"Is that really how that works...?"


Another explosion sounded, and a pillar of fire stretched high into the air. I didn't know how she was doing it, but she seemed to be using fire magic to cause the blasts.

The goth-loli woman swung her empty bottle around and hummed a tune.


"Aha."


It was like some musical of madness.

The explosions kept catching things up in their blast and bringing one building down after another.

Smoke billowed up, rubble collapsed, and the thugs fled in panic.

Some tried to challenge her, shouting things like, "You pieces of shit, I'll tear you apart and put your corpses on display!" but the screaming mohawks attacked them before they could even get that far.


I had no desire to stick my neck into a fight between humans.

Well, if the people getting hurt had been ordinary civilians, maybe I would have stepped in despite my hesitation.

But the people in Area 2 were scum too, so I decided just to watch.




--------------------------------------------------




While I was thinking that, the people capable of using water magic started desperately trying to put out the flames.


"Hey, leave this side to us! But it's not enough. Get more water mages over here!"

"The mohawks keep getting in the way! Shit, that building over there is catching too!"

"Then put out the buildings where we're keeping the premium women we're planning to sell first! If our profits burn up and die, the bosses will kill us even if we survive this!"

"Right! Ah, what about Playing with Fire? She's good-looking, so if we capture her alive we can make some mon--"

"You idiot! Kill her already! Nobody could ever sell that!"


Water roared as it poured out, colliding with the flames and sending white steam billowing up.

The smell of scorched wood, the stench of liquor, the scent of blood mixed into the smoke.

I could hear what the thugs were shouting too, and the caliber of humanity here was truly bottom-tier.


I didn't want to help either side.

No, if anything, since she was taking out trash, maybe Nostalgia of Playing with Fire was the righteous one...

For a moment I thought that and looked back toward her.


"Ugh... my head hurts from the hangover! I hate this! I'm going to burn people!"


Nostalgia of Playing with Fire, with whatever incomprehensible chain of thought lived in her head, seemed utterly fixated on burning people.

She hugged her own arms, then started laughing as she trembled.

Then she began scratching at her own skin with her nails. Red blood welled up through her pale skin and tangled in the gaps beneath her nails. It was like cutting herself.

The sight sent a chill through me.


"Ihih... ihihihih..."


Playing with Fire's voice sounded like a mix of sobbing and laughter.

Her eyes were wet with tears, yet only her mouth was twisted into a warped smile.


This was no good.

It was nothing but scum crushing scum.

Watching the scene before me, I arrived at a single conclusion.

This place was finished.


The madmen from Area 3 were one thing, but the underworld people in Area 2 were just as dangerous in the way they talked and acted.

Reason had long since burned away and been buried beneath the ashes.


Mr. Rowan let out a deep sigh beside me.


"This place is a poison pit. Full of monsters who kill people without a thought, nursing grudges and laughing all the while as they destroy each other."

"It's the worst..."

"But if one of those monsters survives to the very end, then that monster might become the absolute worst, strongest monster of them all. Come on, disciple. You've seen enough of Area 2, haven't you? If we stay here, we'll get caught in the crossfire."


Saying that, Mr. Rowan quietly slipped out through the back of the shop. Lumiela and I followed after him.


The last thing I saw when I looked back was the eyes of Playing with Fire. Madness and sorrow reflected in turns within them, and her lips shone with dried blood.

More than her strength, it was her state of mind that seemed monstrous.


Reason and justice meant nothing here.

All there was was violence.

Villains and madmen, locked in constant battle.


Until we finally escaped Area 2, the sound of laughter crying out "Hyahha!" kept ringing in my ears.
