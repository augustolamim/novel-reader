[Character Introduction (As of Arc 5)]




At the End of Arc 5

                [Character Introductions]





Brave

The protagonist, whose technique is steadily improving little by little. Since deciding of his own will to defeat the Demon King, he has been putting in the effort to do what needs to be done.

After fighting with scammers in Area 1 and the like, his reputation among the people around him is, naturally enough, not all that good, even if it was technically self-defense.

The Kingdom and Empire are now moving toward a joint effort to face the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings, but because he remains distant from the upper ranks, no information is reaching him. Without knowing any of that, he has gone off to retrieve the Holy Sword Idea.

Sadly, because his relationship with Princess Ceras has not progressed either, they have not coordinated in the slightest.




Lumiela

A spirit who wants Brave to be happy.

Seeing how hard Brave has been training, she still sometimes worries so much that she wonders whether she ought to put him back in a birdcage after all. But when he tells her that he cannot be happy without her, she can no longer act on that thought.

Brave keeps revealing more and more mysterious things about himself, like somehow knowing the location of the Holy Sword, but since it seems she'll understand soon enough, she has decided to trust him for now.




Cain

Cheerful, nausea-inducing evil.

He is multiplying like an invasive species.

He is in absolute bliss, delighted to watch the synthetic beasts utterly destroy the dignity of demonkind.

He thinks he is dominating everything, but in reality the top figures of the Empire are simply exploiting his desires and keeping him nicely fed and domesticated.




Liam

He went underground to investigate the unknown reaches of the Koshumar Great Mine, which is also a dwarven tunnel system, and somehow ended up finding a dwarven nation there and establishing friendly relations. He is still off somewhere doing hero-like things.

Dwarf-made high-performance weapons and equipment have started circulating on the market, so his contribution to humanity is immense.




Princess Ceras

A letter requesting cooperation in battle against the Four Heavenly Kings has arrived from the Empire.

Its contents boil down to:

Good for you on defeating Four Heavenly King Viridis. The remaining three Four Heavenly Kings, plus maybe the entire Demon King's army, may start moving all at once, but we have lovely synthetic beasts over here, so we'll handle it somehow. Still, please lend us the imperial princess, the hero, and so on.

And with that, the curtain has finally risen on the great war.

As expected, Princess Ceras is furious on ethical grounds at the Empire creating synthetic beasts and the like.




Gearnote

He is in a complicated state of mind. Having to create synthetic beasts is the worst, but at the same time it is a good thing that beastmen with the traits of various monsters are being born one after another, so he does not know how to feel.

He is an ordinary human scumbag of an evil scientist, creating synthetic beasts without restraint. Yet despite that, Beelzebub the Balefly, after becoming a synthetic beast, has wiped out most of the demons inside the Empire, so he has ended up being just as useful as he has been disastrous.




Princess Retzel

Since she already defeated Four Heavenly King Viridis, she is now fired up for revenge against Four Heavenly King Plurus and is endlessly searching the north for him, but cannot find him.

Like a maiden in love, or perhaps more like a stalker, she keeps searching so she can kill him in battle.

Since Plurus has in fact already been killed by Brave, the whole search is pointless. However, if her target Plurus were absent, she might well have turned on Hero Liam by now, so in a roundabout way Brave has actually done something good.




Rowan

Brave's master.

He is teaching him technique, but since Brave's technical talent is ordinary to the bitter end, it amounts to little more than being better than having none at all.

He is thoroughly pleased with the liquor and snacks he's being given, and he seems happy to have gained a strange disciple in Brave.

At this point he no longer fears death, and seems content to keep living however he pleases.




Abelia

She has suffered the tragedy of losing Felicite, the mage among the trio.

If Felicite had been killed by demons, that would be one thing, but for her to have been killed by another human in the world as it is now feels like nothing but humans destroying each other, which strikes Abelia as truly hopeless.

Whether it was the Punching Bag Man who killed a noble and died with him in Area 3 or anything else, she now looks upon the fact that people still keep fighting each other even in times like these with a sense of resignation.




Synthetic Beast "Salvation System"■ "Patchwork God-Machine" [Phase 3], Beelzebub the Balefly

The highest-ranking synthetic beast. Its strength lies in both numbers and quality.

On top of the original strength of Beelzebub's own individual body, it is a hybrid synthetic beast built around an S-rank adventurer from the Kingdom who fled there (Baion), along with others it absorbed.

And then there are the children it multiplies through implanting eggs into demons--the child human-faced flies.

After being turned into a synthetic beast, its reproductive ability has declined somewhat, but in exchange the strength of its children has increased dramatically. Though there are individual differences, each hatching tends to produce around three human-faced flies on the level of demon generals, and repeated over thousands of cycles, it has effectively become something like an army made up only of demon-general-class monsters.

However, its life is already a candle in the wind.

It will not be killed instantly, and it can fight to some extent, but being burned away by Rubrum Nox is already guaranteed.

The Empire is currently preparing Shellfiend Dromugant, Hellslime Neclus, and Wolfblight Digitalis--specimens specialized in one-on-one combat ability--but what will come of that remains to be seen.




Four Heavenly King Rubrum Nox

Without question the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings. Even if Brave fought him alone and landed a surprise attack, his chances of victory would be very slim. Compared to the other Four Heavenly Kings, he stands one or two heads above them. On top of that, he has a black dragon that is itself only a couple of steps shy of Four Heavenly King level.

Because of his seeker-like nature, he overlooked the preparations being made by humanity.

His will to savor strength refined to its utmost and then surpass it is arrogant and greedy.

That said, he is not kind enough to simply wait without reason, so once the Empire managed to make him think, "At least until the great war begins, I ought to protect the subordinates the Demon King entrusted to me," then things proceeded exactly as the Empire intended.

From there on, it is simply a contest of which power proves superior.

As for the demons' other forces, there are also remnants left behind by Plurus, and at least a few specimens on the level of the Seven Great Magical Beasts. Most of the latter were either casually killed by Brave when he wandered the north while fighting Plurus, or killed by the imperial princess.




Maria

A resident of Area 3 who helped Abelia look for Felicite.

She absolutely loves turning humans into golems from the inside out and then playing with them like dolls.

In other words, she was the culprit.

Her way of thinking is so warped that it is impossible to tell why she moved together with Abelia in the first place--whether it was merely a prank or something else.




Area 3

A dumping ground for lunatics.

Whether it is the live-for-the-moment hyahha gang, bomb maniacs, people who can say nothing but "punching bag," or a girl who turns humans into golem dolls for play, there is no one there worth much.

Kingdom officials happened to see or hear about some of them killing demon generals, and nobles issued orders to make strong humans, useful as meat shields, live in that area. So invitations were sent out, and some people actually came and settled there, which is how the place ended up like this.

Originally Areas 2 and 3 had been occupied by underworld people, but these madmen drove them out and took over.




The World

Once Rubrum Nox kills Beelzebub, messengers will spread word to the demon forces on the Kingdom side that all-out war has begun, and the surviving demons, mainly those around the Kingdom, will rise all at once, escalating the front into even greater brutality.

It seems likely to become a war of ordinary human knights and beastman troops against demon generals and the lower ranks of demonkind.

Since Brave, the former hero, is not part of that battlefield, it is guaranteed to become a fierce and violent conflict.
