The female swordswoman had apparently launched her surprise attack before fully healing, and blood was still flowing from the cut across her torso. But once the upper and lower halves of her body aligned and fitted back together, blue flames draped over her like a veil.

In less than ten seconds, bone rejoined and flesh rebuilt itself. The female swordswoman clearly remained wary of him, ready to move at any instant, but Rubrum Nox chose to wait.

In this situation, he understood that it was probably the wrong choice. Even so, the female swordswoman had revealed the trick behind an ambush she had not needed to explain. If she had shown him that much, then it was only proper for Rubrum Nox to show respect in return and wait a few seconds for her.

While he waited, he decided to observe her.

Though she had just been bisected, the silver-haired female swordswoman, covered in blood, wore not fear or pain on her face, but delight. Her silver hair, darkened by the blood it had drunk, gleamed dully, and what should have been white skin was mottled with splashback and dried red.

The blue flames rising from her wounds felt less like the glow of healing and more like some dark thing akin to obsession.

She was a ferocious beast that only wanted to rampage.

About two seconds remained before the female swordswoman was fully restored.

Rubrum Nox absently wiped away the liquid dripping from his helmet. When he looked, he saw blood clotted there, blackened by poison.

Keeping the now-healed swordswoman at bay with his eyes as she approached, he also measured the strength of the human elites behind him under Ubel's command. He decided he would fight while devoting his attention roughly seventy-thirty between them.

Suddenly, a chill ran down his spine. His flesh prickled as well.

Since this battle began, nothing like that had happened once.

--This is... from where...!?

The female swordswoman before him? No.

Ubel and the Imperial troops behind him? No.

He denied each possibility at once.

Rubrum Nox was worn down. Given the circumstances, they were certainly troublesome. But only that.

No. This was different.

His body was telling him it was something terrifying.

The sensation resembled what he had felt when facing the Demon King. The first time in his life he had known despair at a being's existence itself.

He reflexively searched the surroundings, but it did not seem to be on this battlefield. He could not catch its presence. And yet his body somehow sensed that something had appeared farther away--still within this continent, but distant.

It felt as though his blood were stirring.

The day he had once lost to the Demon King. Defeated utterly, out of options, reduced to desperate flailing, he had bitten off a piece of the Demon King's flesh with his teeth. The Demon King's blood had mixed into him then.

From that point on, Rubrum Nox's flames had changed color. Flames that should have been red had turned black. They had become black fire.

And now that power inside him seemed to be reacting.

Something opposed to darkness--light--had grown stronger.

--Then is this the hero...!?

It was only instinct, but he looked toward one direction.

Naturally, even at the limit of his sight there was no figure resembling a hero to be seen.

It was on the Kingdom's side. But not so impossibly far as the eastern edge of the continent. It might be somewhat south as well.

If he went southeast from here, the hero would be there.

He pressed a hand to his heart and took shallow breaths, feeling cold sweat break out, until he somehow forced himself calm again.

"Where are you looking? Fufufu... I want you to keep playing with me a good while yet...!"

The enemy was right in front of him.

Her voice pulled him back. Perhaps she had taken his looking away as mockery, because the female swordswoman let a trace of irritation show and closed in.

As he parried her, he forcibly gathered his thoughts.

If that presence truly was the hero, why had he only noticed now? Why had the hero suddenly become so powerful that even Rubrum Nox found him frightening? The questions did not end.

No. If the hero had become stronger, then that was all the better. Before challenging the Demon King, Rubrum Nox need only overcome this fear and defeat the hero.

The problem was timing. It would be bad if, after this battle ended, the hero joined as reinforcements.

Before that happened, he would have to defeat the female swordswoman and the others no matter what. That much he settled as the first thing that had to be done.

But just as he had finally calmed his shaken heart and begun to refocus on the battle, he sensed something coming toward him, as though it had been aiming for this exact moment.

"Tch--what now...?"

There was a presence. Something was racing straight toward him.

But almost at once, he let out a breath of relief.

This one should work to his advantage.

It was a familiar presence. This had to be Linyuvurm's.

The companion he had entrusted with pursuing the first airship was returning safely after annihilating it. It had taken rather a long time, but the first airship too had probably carried skilled fighters.

Linyuvurm was approaching at remarkable speed.

...Had Linyuvurm always been able to move this fast? Thinking that, the presence somehow seemed slightly off.

Was this really Linyuvurm's presence? He kicked the female swordswoman away to buy distance, instantly shot his gaze in that direction--

and the next moment, Rubrum Nox's eyes widened.

The thing flying toward him at tremendous speed was a human.

And yet the wings on its back were certainly similar to Linyuvurm's.

His thoughts froze.

That human carried draconic features--a form one could almost only call dragonkin.

Even so, where had Linyuvurm gone? Why did this dragonkin carry a presence and traits so much like Linyuvurm's--?

The unanswerable questions echoed through his mind, and that became a fatal opening.

The dragonkin thrust out a spear of ice while carrying all of that momentum. He had to stop it--and reflexively, Rubrum Nox grabbed the ice spear.

"Oooooo--!"

The dragonkin roared. The force behind the spear kept climbing.

"What...!?"

The ice spear shoved aside the arm that had caught it and tried to drive straight through his armor. Realizing this was bad, Rubrum Nox sprang one step to the side. The spear grazed past his neck.

He glared at the dragonkin.

Then the ground swayed beneath him, and he dropped to one knee.

The wound at his neck--the one the ice spear had sliced open as they passed each other--split wider, spraying blue blood. The blood spattered against the ground.

Cheers rose from the humans behind him.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

"Ghh...!"

He spat out blood.

That had been a splendid strike. Pure skill, a decisive step without hesitation, and will. Had even one of those been lacking, Rubrum Nox would have fully dodged that spear.

How far had the attack been calculated?

He had made too many mistakes. The dragonkin must have seen Rubrum Nox's attention scatter because of the hero and judged this to be the moment to strike. And when he saw the dragonkin's form, thoughts of his companion's fate had flashed through his mind and frozen him. Had that too been anticipated?

Even now he could still feel the hero's presence, prickling at him like a curse.

And once he had a moment to think, the reason traces of his companion remained within the dragonkin became impossible to ignore.

The beastlike soldiers.

Given their existence, the answer was not hard to imagine.

The technology to mix monsters into humans. Linyuvurm too must have been made into that. And what came out of it was probably that dragonkin.

--So you're gone now. Truly gone.

At the melancholy settling in the bottom of his chest, his gaze dropped of its own accord.

A hundred years.

For a human, a hundred years was a whole lifetime.

Memories of laughing, raging, racing through the skies, and sharing battle together scraped at Rubrum Nox's heart like blades.

But he could not cling to them forever.

The battlefield allowed no sentimentality. It stole the lives of those who stopped moving. Drawing one deep breath, Rubrum Nox stood.

The blood pouring from his neck was halted by force through the contraction of muscle. He identified the wound's position with precision and generated flame there.

The sound and smell of flesh burning.

"He's cauterizing the wound!?"

"Stop him!"

The humans' shouts burst behind him, laced with fear. He had no room to care.

The dragonkin in front swung his spear of ice, and almost at the same time, the female swordswoman behind him cut in with her blade.

He had lost a companion with whom he had shared a century--a span equal to an entire human life.

Perhaps that was why something began burning quietly in the depths of his chest. It was not exactly anger, nor could it be called hatred. But one thing was certain:

he now had one more reason than usual not to lose.

Rubrum Nox was surprised to find such feelings still remained in him, and yet his body did not hesitate.

He stretched out his left hand and caught the incoming spear of ice. Cold flooded his body, threatening to freeze him to the bone, but he did not care.

Then, with his right hand, he seized the female swordswoman's sword. The blade bit into his palm and blood welled up.

But this time--

he had them both.

The dragonkin stared at him with eyes full of powerful resolve. I see, Rubrum Nox thought. Strength of will truly can be a weapon.

But now the conditions were the same.

In power or in will, he would no longer be the one lagging behind.
