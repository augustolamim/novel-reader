Clautia gathered herself and turned her attention back to the magical crystal.

For an instant her thoughts had been stolen by the manifestation of that supernatural power, but she had to see this conclusion through.

Lumiela and Jane had apparently finished checking the capabilities of the united form they had become.

They returned to the starting point, the place where the soldiers had fainted. The noble side should still have reserves left.

And now they likely intended to settle things with them.

What followed, however, was not a battle.

It was trampling. It was an act of destruction against class society itself.

The targets were clear.

The leaders of the enemy--the noble class.

Approaching close enough to make nobles faint, they descended from the sky wrapped in light, picked them up one by one, and collected them as though gathering trash off the roadside.

Then Lumiela forcibly revived them with healing magic.

Without giving the screaming nobles time even to cry properly, they threatened them into giving up their family names, territories, and the locations of their mansions. Then they carefully enclosed them in barriers so they could not escape, kept them in hand, and flew upward.

And the moment they reached each noble's mansion, the destruction began.

Right before the noble's eyes, Lumiela and Jane smashed one symbol after another of the luxury and authority those families had piled up over generations.

"Let's level it all! As of today, you're homeless!"

Jane's voice rang through the crystal.

"Aaaaahhhhhh!!??"

Naturally, the noble's screams were ignored.

Costly statues, historical paintings, magical tools--everything was pulverized without exception.

In this way every noble who had taken part was dealt with one by one, as carefully as if someone were methodically collecting bits of trash fallen by the road.

Not a single one escaped. They moved from mansion to mansion as though on patrol, destroying them all evenly and thoroughly. The level of persistence on display was enough to suggest obsession.

They certainly were not killing anyone.

But socially, those nobles were as good as dead.

"A trial, huh... so it did turn out this way after all."

Clautia had anticipated the worst case for this method, and it had happened exactly as expected.

Perhaps because even this illusion world was being reproduced faithfully, the Kingdom judged Lumiela and Jane's actions to be rebellion. Before long, while the pair were out destroying a noble's villa, soldiers arrived bearing a royal order.

As expected, they had come to capture them.

Formally, the order said "detain them and place them on trial."

But such wording meant nothing.

The nobles they had struck were alive, but they had lost everything. Wealth, name, status--all of it had collapsed.

That was precisely why the other nobles were terrified. The fear that they might be next was what drove the Kingdom into action.

The outcome of such a trial was obvious.

Charges would be fabricated. Testimony would be twisted. The fact that Lumiela and Jane had not killed anyone would be ignored. In the end, the state would force the matter toward execution.

That was what a nation was.

It would never forgive something that broke its order.

As Clautia calmly recorded the events unfolding beyond the crystal, she thought:

Lumiela and Jane had already become beings that surpassed even the Four Heavenly Kings, Demon-King-class existences. Who exactly was supposed to capture them and pass judgment on them?

They offered no forgiveness. They had no intention of compromising or yielding. They no longer meant to trust or respect people or nations.

Perhaps this was the pitiful outcome that resulted, even in an illusion, when an individual's violence surpassed that of an entire state.

It was the opening clash between transcendents and a nation.

But the gap in power was obvious.

It was as vast as the gap between a doll and the girl who owned it.

All the nation could do was be handled.

With a fingertip, an arm could be torn off. The belly could be ripped open and stuffing spilled out. Whether it was broken or left intact depended purely on whim.

Cry or scream as much as you liked, you were granted no choice.

Your life and death were held in another's hands, and once the game ended, you would only be worthless wreckage.

"At this rate, blood is bound to flow."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Clautia's prediction was wrong.

Lumiela and Jane went in a direction even further beyond expectation.

It was far too gentle to call a battle.

And yet it destroyed the nation with perfect accuracy.

The hundreds of thousands of troops sent out to subjugate Lumiela and Jane stood there uselessly, accomplishing nothing at all.

The soldiers merely stared upward at the sky.

Whether they raised bows or chanted spells, there was no means by which they could interfere with the streak of light racing like a meteor.

They could do nothing but watch it tear across the blue heavens.

High above, where humans could not reach, Lumiela and Jane flew gracefully, looking down over everything at their leisure.

The soldiers despaired and could do nothing. Lumiela and Jane alone were free.

And then the nation moved its army.

Naturally, that meant there was someone in command.

The supreme commander. The man who led several hundred thousand troops. Elite guards surrounded him, and his formation had been properly arranged.

Even by Clautia's eyes, it was an impeccable defensive deployment, backed by the accumulated experience and theory of human history.

The supreme commander was a noble in his fifties, dressed with obvious dignity.

His beard was neatly trimmed white, his voice weighty. Before the soldiers, he delivered a speech and drove up morale.

"The hero has fallen, and become a new Demon King."

Before the words had even finished,

a veil of light descended from heaven.

Light had descended, sudden and absolute.

The elites moved instantly, trying to surround and protect the supreme commander. They were too late. He had already been taken.

What enclosed him was a pillar of light stretching straight toward the heavens. At its center the commander stood alone, cut off.

The soldiers outside desperately battered the barrier of light. Swords, arrows, magic--all of it was meaningless. No matter how much force they poured in, the light did not so much as tremble.

And then--the ones who had come to fetch him arrived.

From the sky descended those transcendent beings of light.

The supreme commander drew the sword at his waist, a blade anyone could recognize at a glance as a masterpiece. He never got to swing it.

Before Lumiela and Jane, he could do nothing. He did not faint, but his arm was easily broken, and both his sword and his will to resist were nullified.

And then--before all his soldiers' eyes, the supreme commander was carried away.

The soldiers were left with nothing but despair, unable to do a thing.

Clutched up like prey by something angelic, he rose toward the heavens with the light. Like a sacrifice. Like someone being taken away to die.

Slowly, deliberately, he was lifted higher.

As if to make sure everyone saw it.

Clautia found herself wondering what Lumiela and Jane would do next, but in the end they did not kill the supreme commander either.

They did not kill him, no--but instead they took him to the worst possible place imaginable for a man in his position.

Lumiela and Jane flew with him alive into the neighboring nation--the Empire.

And there, in the imperial capital--more precisely, in the palace where the Emperor lived--they threw the commander down on the ground.

After revealing his identity, they announced loudly enough for it to reach the Emperor's ears, "A gift from the Kingdom."

"I... see... So they're selling out their own country. I see."

Even Clautia recoiled a little.

That act did not stop after one time.

Each new supreme commander appointed by the Kingdom met the same fate in turn. It happened five times.

And next came the king.

Lumiela and Jane had made the royal family their target.

Kidnap them, then abandon them in the Empire.

Whenever the prince of that time tried to inherit the throne, he would be snatched away in the middle of the coronation ceremony. They trampled the Kingdom's dignity into the dirt with absolute thoroughness.

Naturally, the Empire saw this as an opportunity.

The invasion began.

But the Kingdom's soldiers had been broken in spirit, and their commanders had all been taken away. There was no way they could offer any proper resistance.

The Kingdom, which had split the continent in two, was no doubt headed for ruin.

Perhaps because the Empire too was a nation that worshiped power above all, it never tried to lay hands on Lumiela and Jane. Perhaps it feared them, or perhaps it simply understood what they were.

If it did something foolish, then it would be next.

But before that could go any further,

the world reflected in the crystal quietly blurred and vanished.

Yes--the trial was over.

The nobles who had driven the previous hero to death, and the Kingdom to which those nobles had belonged.

In the end, the trial had taken the shape of destroying, together with the nation itself, the despair that had become the cause of death.

For the time being, no threatening existence would appear anymore. That meant a line had been drawn.

It was a severe judgment.

An eye for an eye.

Let the nation perish if it must.

For our sake, let the enemy be destroyed.

That was what it seemed to say.

They had not killed humans. But if their opponent had been demons, just how cold would the methods they chose have become?

"Love really is a frightening thing."

That was all Clautia could say.

Lumiela and Jane should regain consciousness soon enough. She would have to go meet them. Now then, which tea ought she brew?
