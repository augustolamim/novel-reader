With poison as an uncertain factor now in play, there was only one option left.

If the battle dragged on, he would be worn down and killed for certain. Then--
a short, decisive battle.

Linyuvurm spread his wings wide and beat them down deep and hard.

The wind burst. The shockwave tore through the sea of clouds.

His speed spiked. Thinking not at all of evasion, he charged at top speed.

The flying ship rushed toward him from straight ahead.

Gunports opened one after another along the steel hull, weaving lines of interception fire through the air.

But that no longer mattered.

He abandoned evasion. He abandoned feints.

Linyuvurm flew in a straight line. He would crush them head-on with power, with mass, with speed.

He could see the deck. Liam was there, holding up his spear.

The human Liam stood there.

And still there was no fear in those eyes. No surprise, either.

What lay there was only a crystal-clear resolve.

...A spear. A spear again. So this too is fate.

His partner Rubrum Nox was also a spear user. Linyuvurm could not shake the feeling that his life as a dragon had been deeply entangled with spearmen.

Not a sword. Not an axe.

A weapon that gathered everything into a single point and existed only to pierce. It was also the weapon Linyuvurm loved most.

Naturally. It was his partner's weapon. There was no way he could dislike it.

Memory flared back as though burned into him.

He and Rubrum Nox had been together a long time. Linyuvurm, a dragon born to solitude, had never imagined he would spend so much time with another living being.

Once they had met as enemies, traded fists and claws, then before he knew it they had stood side by side--
and then Rubrum Nox had pulled far ahead and left him behind.

At first they had been even. Neither had truly won nor lost, and they had laughed together, covered in wounds, competing to see which of them would grow stronger next.

They had been the sort of companions who fought.

But more than that--
they had been comrades-in-arms.

Rubrum Nox had done nothing but pursue strength. Even now, like a seeker who had cast aside hesitation, he looked only toward the absolute strongest.

Linyuvurm had trusted him. He had never doubted him. All he wanted, as a partner, was to watch from beside him the moment Rubrum Nox reached the strongest height.

They had spent time traveling the Schrekrich Continent, and Rubrum Nox had defeated every living thing upon it--
and then--
then he had lost to the revived legendary Demon King.

Utterly.

The terrifying one was the Demon King. The most dreadful of living beings. Even Linyuvurm himself, a dragon, would tremble like a small animal when facing him.

The Demon King had not killed Rubrum Nox or Linyuvurm. Instead he had looked down on them and said that if they became his subordinates and exterminated humanity, then he might entertain them again.

Nothing more than that. To the Demon King, they had not even been worth killing.

A transcendent being, far beyond the realm of the Four Heavenly Kings.

And yet Linyuvurm believed. Someday, Rubrum Nox would surpass even the Demon King.

That partner--
for a single instant, he overlapped with Liam, the human gripping the spear.

He truly saw it.

Within that human, the same light.

Liam's eyes seemed to be looking at Linyuvurm, yet not at him. They were fixed on something farther beyond.

The future.

Stronger enemies.

Higher walls.

For the sake of humanity's future.

He meant to defeat Linyuvurm, Rubrum Nox, and eventually even the Demon King himself.

Beneath the duty wrapped around him, there burned a pure thirst. Whatever their motives differed on, they were the same in this: both desired strength and victory.

But Linyuvurm had no intention of being defeated.

That only made him all the more determined to end it here.

He would stake everything a dragon had on crushing this human.

I'll kill you!!

With that premonition, time itself seemed to stretch.

The world turned viscous, the flow of the wind slowing enough that he could follow it with his eyes.

He saw Liam take one step forward.

The deck screamed and shattered, steel warping from the shock of that step alone. In the next instant, the spear was thrust.

Linyuvurm did not fear it. He charged straight in.

He would not run. He would not block. He would not deflect. He had no room left to care about damage.

But in exchange, he could throw everything he had straight into the clash.

Collision.

Impact pierced his skull.

Hard. Cold.

His vision burst white, and thought itself was cut apart.

It pierced me.

Only a beat later did that understanding begin to seep in.

Liam's spear had pierced Linyuvurm's proud scales and was tearing through his head.

He still had consciousness. Before he could no longer move--

Linyuvurm swung his claw.

The claw certainly caught the human Liam's body.

"Ah..."

The sensation of human flesh tearing. Bone shattering. Hot blood erupting. Liam's left arm was torn free and spun into the air, and the same blow ripped deep into his abdomen.

Dark red blood scattered through the sky.

I got him.

Certainty filled his chest.

Liam's body flew backward.

Linyuvurm's own vision rapidly darkened.

The sensation of his head being pierced spread belatedly through his entire body. It was not a sharp pain. It was absence. Part of his thinking had been carved away, and the act of thinking itself was slowly breaking down.

Gravity mercilessly dragged Linyuvurm down.

But he did not resist. He could even think, This is fine. Liam would not survive those wounds either.

He had lost his left arm, his belly had been ripped wide open, and his organs had spilled out. Humans were not strong enough to keep living after that.

That was how Linyuvurm understood it. Which was why what spread through his chest was almost relief.

He had no regrets.

He had wielded strength, answered strength with strength, and clashed while staking everything. If this was the end he had gained after that, then as a dragon he could ask for nothing more.

The clouds grew distant. Though he had been flying while tearing through them, now they were receding to some far-off place he could no longer reach.

Seconds.

Judging by the fact that he was still in freefall, it could only have been a handful of seconds. Linyuvurm had blacked out.

His consciousness sank into darkness, then rose again.

...Dying to a spear is not so bad, is it...?

The life force of a dragon still kept Linyuvurm alive.

At the edge of his vision, he saw tremendous power bursting apart.

The moment he turned his awareness toward the heavy explosions reverberating through the air, he understood.

His partner, Rubrum Nox--
was still fighting.

At the center of the battlefield, he was rampaging even now.

Every sweep of his spear detonated black flames, mowing down the ugly monsters rushing him all at once. Mountainsides were gouged open, flame and black smoke whirled together, and the earth split apart.

Something that had sat heavy in the depths of Linyuvurm's chest quietly came undone.

Even if Linyuvurm ended here, the battle would not. His partner was still fighting on.

"...At least..."

At least that flying ship.

He lifted his gaze. The steel anomaly floating in the sky was still there. The hull was riddled with damage, but it remained aloft, even pursuing the falling Linyuvurm in order to finish him off.
