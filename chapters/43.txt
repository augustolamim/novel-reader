Abelia peered ahead through the small carriage window.

The line of carriages stretched on like a serpent. Splashing through the mud of the thaw, they advanced slowly into imperial territory.

Abelia's place was almost dead center in the convoy--around the middle. The safest position, which made sense, since she was serving as a guard in the same carriage as Princess Ceras. Another member of what had once been their trio, Mule the Ironwall Knight, was riding a horse outside and guarding the carriage from there.

Ceras, seated before her, had been silent the whole time. Her hands were clasped, her head bowed as if in prayer.

Ceras ought, by rights, to have remained at Kingdom headquarters. But she had broken away from the place assigned to her and chosen to come to the Empire in order to witness the battle with the Four Heavenly King in imperial territory, Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze.

Nobles were coming too, besides Ceras. One of them had been an earl, if Abelia remembered right. Maybe he wanted the glory of having joined the battle against one of the Four Heavenly Kings and to make a name for himself.

By now, battles all across the continent must already have grown fierce. Whether intended or not, it had become an all-out war between humanity and demonkind.

Two days earlier, a line of black flame had streaked across the sky over the Kingdom.

In the next instant, that burning trail had begun forming what looked like letters.

[Demons, all forces advance]

Those characters burned into the sky as a pattern of flame had been none other than a message to the demon forces on the Kingdom side from Rubrum Nox himself. The Empire had deciphered the fiery writing.

For fire magic to reach all the way from the Empire to the sky above the Kingdom, over such an absurd distance, was abnormal.

Apparently an order from the Four Heavenly King above them had bolstered the demons' morale, because their momentum had increased, and in the north of the Kingdom the war had begun tilting somewhat against humanity. But the Kingdom had responded by sending additional troops, widening the anti-demon front and turning it into the main battlefield.

The reason the human side had started losing ground was that a gigantic golem the size of a small mountain had rampaged across the battlefield, and demons with strength beyond the category of even senior demon generals had begun to run wild.

A giant golem. Everyone in the Kingdom had thought the earth Four Heavenly King had finally appeared, but apparently that wasn't it.

It was a prototype of the earth Four Heavenly King's giant mobile weapon Jotunheim. A downgraded version that Four Heavenly King Plurus of Cragmass had given to his subordinates, or so it seemed.

It was said that inside the fortress there were hundreds of demon generals stationed there, specially raised by the earth Four Heavenly King so they could operate the prototype. Even as a prototype, the frightening thing was that it wasn't just a weapon. It was a living demon "castle."

The earth Four Heavenly King still had not shown himself. Of the Four Heavenly Kings, only Rubrum Nox's location was known. The others were most likely being held back as hidden reserves.

The prototype had probably been sent out just to test the waters.

Abelia herself had been lucky enough to see the real thing from a distance. The sight of it crushing entire forests beneath its feet as it advanced had radiated an overwhelming sense of the gap in military power.

The imperial envoy had provided information the Empire possessed, but the gap in knowledge between the Kingdom and the Empire was enormous. It had been startling enough that they even knew the names of the Four Heavenly Kings' weapons, but the reason, incredibly, was that Cain--the Hero of Foresight, whom everyone had thought dead--was in the Empire, they said.

That report had made Ceras's eyes go wide.

Apparently the Hero of Foresight, Cain, had been rebuilding the Empire's fighting strength from their side. Abelia had never met Cain, but at a time like this, learning that a hero everyone had thought dead was actually alive felt like an enormous opportunity.

With his power to see the future, that was probably how he had obtained information on the Four Heavenly Kings for this operation too.

If he had that power, it felt like World Breaker shouldn't have fallen. Maybe some sort of accident had happened.

The Four Heavenly Kings had crushed the old Soleil with ease.

They truly were monsters with extraordinary power.

And now humanity had to fight an opponent like that.

In practice, Abelia would probably only be watching as Ceras's guard, but she might still get caught up in it and die. Whether it was instinct or not, the trembling in her body would not stop.

According to the plan relayed by the envoy from the Empire, the Kingdom-side fighters would be Hero Liam and some of his companions. The other was Imperial Princess Retzel, who had originally belonged to the Empire's side and for some reason had ended up in the Kingdom.

Ahead of the carriage Abelia and the others rode in was another carriage carrying Hero Liam and the Kingdom's proud champions.

No one knew where the princess was. Ceras had sent a carrier pigeon, and soldiers had confirmed from a distance that it had been received, but she had not shown herself since. Since there had been no sightings of her in the Kingdom, maybe she had entered the Empire on her own.

Hero Liam and his companions had been asked to split into two groups this time. One would remain in the Kingdom to deal with things like Plurus of Cragmass's prototype, while the other would head to the Empire for the decisive battle against the Four Heavenly Kings.

The plan was for the Edelweiss faction, who commanded ancient golems, to take the lead in bringing down the prototype.

Some nobles had complained that, with neither Hero Liam nor the princess remaining, the Kingdom side would be too thinly defended. But the Empire's reasoning seemed to be that the other Four Heavenly Kings were likely to hold a grudge against the princess who had killed the wind Four Heavenly King. On top of that, they apparently believed that Rubrum Nox alone would not be able to stop a force of this size, and that the other Four Heavenly Kings would come as reinforcements.

In other words, the decisive battle against the Four Heavenly Kings really would take place in the Empire.

They had entered imperial territory, and Abelia was leaving herself to the carriage's swaying while sinking into thoughts like that when it happened. The carriage ahead came to an abrupt stop in a large square.

Why here?

Before she could even fully form the question, the princess had already stepped down from the carriage. Abelia hurried after her, and the instant her boots touched the ground--

she caught her breath.

"What... is... this...?"

A gigantic "vehicle" sat in the middle of the square.

Its shape was closest to that of a ship floating at sea. But it was a ship and yet not a ship. Its hull was pieced together from dark blue-black metal and white stone, all of it covered in patterns like ancient script. Instead of sails, what jutted out were winglike structures curved like bones, their surfaces covered with countless grooves for circulating magical power.

There were two of them.

They stood in symmetrical formation, as though they had been peeled away from a ruin and set down in the modern age exactly as they were.

On top of them, men who looked to be imperial soldiers hurried back and forth, inspecting magical devices of some kind. A few robed figures who seemed to be mages also stood on deck, forming a circle as they prepared something.

This was outside any category of "ship" Abelia knew.

And the people aboard them were even stranger.

One woman in robes was looking down at them from the ship. Of all the robed figures, she was the one who frightened Abelia the most.

Her cool pale-blue hair swayed in the wind, and her expression was calm--but the magical power swirling inside her pressed against Abelia's skin like intimidation.

That emblem on the robe... maybe she was a mage from the Empire's Frefel Magic Institute.

And then there were several beastmen.

They wore specially adjusted equipment that looked uniform at a glance yet differed slightly from one to the next, and they made no effort to hide features like horns, fangs, and beast fur. Humans fused with monsters--beastmen.

Abelia could tell just by looking. They were clearly on a different level from Abelia and the demon generals she knew.

They were close--frighteningly close--to Hero Liam.

"Heh heh, fine stuff, isn't it?"

A sticky voice came from behind her.

When she turned, a man who looked like a researcher was standing there. His body was thin, yet his eyes alone shone intensely out of proportion to the rest of him, and his twisted lips held a grin.

"Ah, yes. I've never seen anything like it. What is this... exactly?"

"This, you see, is an airship."

The man proudly stroked the hull.

"We found it in some ruins. Only two exist in the whole world. It's a tool for letting humans fly through the sky, and it can carry a great many people at once."

A pale light pulsed as if in response to his fingertips.

"This time, we'll board one of them and bring down the black dragon."

"The black dragon...?"

"That's right."

The man grinned and looked at her.

"We'll have Hero Liam join us aboard, you see. Once we set three synthetic beasts on Four Heavenly King Rubrum Nox, we'll split him from the black dragon. Liam will help us finish the dragon off."

A ship that flew through the sky.

Hero-class fighting strength.

It seemed Hero Liam's battlefield this time would be the skies.

Against the terrible might of the black dragon, humanity would challenge it with a ship built on the technology of a lost civilization--a floating aerial warship.

"The hero goes to the sky..."

Abelia murmured softly.

By the time she noticed, the man with the oily way of speaking had vanished from her side.

Abelia was no engineer, so she had no idea how something like this ship could fly. It was incomprehensible, but precisely because it was unknown, she felt a flicker of hope that it might be enough to break the Four Heavenly Kings.
