"This won't do..."

"The Empire's ultimate weapons are being held back...!"

"Shut up! The Imperial Princess is there too, there's no way they can lose!"

The soldiers' voices reached him like shrieks. Some of them were shouting, but it was only bluster.

They had also arranged it so the battle situation could be watched from the airship through a paired mirror, and it counted as a stroke of luck that the black dragon had not destroyed it.

Thanks to that, even though they had withdrawn from the battlefield itself, they could still observe the fighting while retreating toward the nearest base aboard a crippled airship.

Gearnote had been busy treating Liam, but after being summoned in haste by the soldiers, he saw the scene and broke into a heavy sweat.

--This is bad... far too strong. Much too strong...!

They should have made preparations.

They had paid sacrifices, spent time, and gathered every scrap of information they could.

And even so--had the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings still exceeded all expectations?

Digitalis the Wolfblight.

That poison was no longer something that could be dismissed with the word poison. It was a crystallization of pure killing intent that denied the very concept of living things. The kind of thing that ended you the instant it touched you.

And Princess Retzel had plunged into it without hesitation.

There was no calling it anything but reckless, but that was simply the kind of person she was.

Even so--she was still alive.

He did not know why. Her arms had been burned off again and again, yet each time she regenerated, stood back up, and kept going.

She had long since stopped being human.

Meanwhile, Hellslime Neclus had been blown away.

A thrown spear wrapped in black flames of the highest order. At the instant it struck, Neclus's existence itself had scattered like mist, leaving no trace behind.

But that did not mean it was over.

Had they really finished it off? Against a Four Heavenly King, one was not permitted the certainty of believing so.

As long as Digitalis's poison remained, close combat was impossible.

The very act of standing there killed living creatures.

It was not reason that said so, but instinct.

As a second wave, General Ubel's Imperial force--the beastfolk troops and the mages--stood by.

But how far would they get? Honestly, it was hard to feel confident.

The first wave had been the decisive one.

It was supposed to be settled there.

If more Four Heavenly Kings appeared on top of that, the entire situation would collapse at once.

That was why Gearnote had already made his decision.

Hero Liam had to be brought back alive.

And not merely brought back. He had to be enhanced.

"Heh heh... beastfolk really are the best. Once I combine him with the black dragon, I'll turn him into a splendid dragonkin."

The voice, dripping with delight, echoed from deeper inside the facility.

Of course, if he rushed too much, a rejection reaction would occur. If that happened, not only would everything be wasted, it could lead to an irretrievable disaster.

So he had driven everyone out, saying he needed space to treat Liam. If what they were doing here was discovered by Regingule, Liam's warrior companion, there was a real chance he would get angry.

That was why they had no choice but to take time.

If they got impatient, everything would collapse.

He urged the airship onward. It had to land at the nearest stronghold as quickly as possible. The hull was already near its limit. The armor was warped, and even the propulsion mechanism sounded as though it were screaming. Losing it here would be far too costly. As research equipment and as transportation, it was not something that could simply be replaced.

Fortunately, the destination had a well-equipped facility. They could continue treatment there while keeping a close eye on the battle outside. If the front collapsed, all of this would amount to nothing. But if they could endure, then they could make the next move.

So that the instant the balance of power tipped, they could send out a third wave.

Things were about to become busy.

Even so--if they wanted to win, they had no choice but to do what needed doing.

That was Gearnote's job as a researcher.

Still, dragons really were wonderful. They had wings, so they could fly.

He envied that.

If the circumstances were different, Gearnote would have wanted to go first himself.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

A voice that made him feel sick just from hearing it echoed from deeper in the corridor.

Shrill, shallow, tactless. Ahh, that voice.

"Heeey, Gearnote! Heard Liam nearly died, is that for real? Pathetic, hah!"

There was no need to turn around.

Gearnote knew the owner of that voice all too well.

The man who appeared was the one once called the hero of foresight--Cain.

That said, being called a hero belonged to the past. What remained in him now was only warped knowledge and hopeless frivolity.

As an experimental subject, he was excellent.

Memories, knowledge, remnants of foresight. There was value there.

But his personality and intelligence, frankly speaking, were pathetic.

--Why is he here?

For an instant Gearnote thought that, then immediately remembered.

Cain had not been put on the airship. He had been "left" at the nearest base they were now heading toward.

As a source of information to use if anything happened. That had been the reasoning, more or less.

But the airship was still in the sky.

"See, the airship got the crap beaten out of it by that black dragon Linyuvurm, and it was wobbling this way, so I came to lecture you! Had one of the flying beastfolk soldiers carry me!"

I see.

Gearnote understood the reason.

If General Ubel had ordered them to treat him carefully, the soldiers probably could not refuse.

Or perhaps the people on site had judged, on their own, that Cain's future knowledge might prove useful.

Either way--he was an unnecessary visitor.

Cain kept jabbering on and on.

There was nothing in it worth listening to. Gearnote gave vague responses while thinking about something else.

Hero Liam.

He had left him to the other researchers for now, but he would have to check the progress.

...That said.

Cain was a nuisance, but in this situation it would be inaccurate to say he had absolutely no use at all.

The more knowledge they had, the better.

Because they were cornered.

As he thought that, Cain wandered over on his own, stood beside Gearnote, and slapped him on the shoulder.

"So, what kinda synthetic beast are you gonna turn that pretty-boy bastard into? How about a caterpillar?"

He sounded as though he truly did not care either way.

"Give me a break, Cain. Liam's a replacement fighting force. I'm planning to use the black dragon to turn him into a dragonkin."

"Huh? That sounds normal-cool, doesn't it? Unforgivable."

"If we lose here, it's all over, so we don't have a choice. Let's have him become cool."

Cain clicked his tongue and began muttering complaints.

Was there no way to make him quiet down?

There was no gourmet food or beautiful women on this airship.

The lack of materials with which to humor him was a serious problem.

Gearnote shook his head inwardly in exasperation.

Then it happened.

The air went cold.

"...I heard something strange."

A low, icy voice.

"What exactly do you mean by that? Let me hear it too."

When Gearnote turned, the one standing there was the warrior Regingule.

He held an axe and glared at them, and there was not a trace of a joke in his eyes.
