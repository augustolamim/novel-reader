Liam did what emergency treatment he could for Regingule. Tearing strips from clothing to stanch the bleeding, he worked his way through the bare minimum.

It was nowhere near proper treatment, but at the same time, leaving him in the Empire's facilities felt unsafe.

Now that Liam himself had ended up like this, he could not trust the Empire enough to leave Regingule there.

Even though Regingule had to be in pain, he fixed Liam with a hard stare and told him to listen, then slowly filled in the missing pieces of the current situation.

Through that conversation, Liam learned that the Empire had apparently saved his life when he had been on the verge of death. Certainly, the wounds the black dragon had inflicted were gone.

But being altered into something mixed with a monster's body was... hard to feel any one way about.

No, he was grateful. Liam still had things he had to do.

For the moment, he drew a line in his heart.

He himself no longer mattered.

But he could not let an ally be put through the same thing. This was the Empire. Surely they had healers who could use recovery magic, but if he rashly handed badly wounded Regingule over to Imperial people, they might turn him into something altered just like Liam.

If anyone had to suffer this, let it be Liam alone.

In ordinary times, Liam could simply stand guard, but the battle against the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings was not over yet.

In the worst case, there was still the risk of Liam rampaging again, and he would likely have to fight a Four Heavenly King as well. It was not good to go too far from here.

Regingule was certainly gravely injured, but paired with his natural toughness, he would probably manage to hold on through natural recovery. From Liam's own experience in battle, that was how it looked.

If so, then all the more reason not to let him undergo some last-resort alteration.

A place that was safe, and that could treat him.

Thinking it over, only one person came to mind.

This was Imperial territory, but Princess Ceras too had come with a small number of troops.

She had said she had a duty to witness this battle, one so important to all humanity, so she should not have returned home yet.

First, Liam thought he should deliver Regingule to Princess Ceras. Among the soldiers she had brought, there was a high chance there would be at least some users of healing magic for emergencies.

The problem was that Liam had not been told where she was, but he had to find her quickly. She was probably not all that far from the place where they had split up in the Empire.

As soon as he finished Regingule's emergency treatment, he would have to go.

He took a deep breath in and let it out slowly. He pared away thought and sank his consciousness inward. He felt the black impulse oozing out from deep in his chest, writhing with heat.

He neither feared nor rejected it, only grasped its shape. He read its flow and made space to let the power escape. If he rushed, it would swallow him. By maintaining stillness, the raging urge gradually lost its outline and was forced down until it sat within his grasp.

Regingule's emergency treatment was done as well.

It was time to move.

But first, because he could feel a mismatch in his physical body, he moved it around and checked.

His physical abilities had obviously improved to a degree incomparable to before. His sight reached far into the distance, and he could even read the tremor in the air.

But at the same time, the mismatch in sensation was indeed enormous.

His body had not caught up to the power. Even for the smallest movements, it was painfully clear that full adjustment would take time.

Even so, he made the minimum adjustments he could without wasting time, then finished checking the state of the battlefield.

He pushed his eyesight to the limit and looked out.

He could see something like the wreckage of the airship he had been riding falling to the ground.

But the soldiers with wings had already scattered and escaped into the sky. They had gotten well clear of the ship.

From this far away, he could not tell clearly, but while the damage to the airship was severe, perhaps human casualties had been avoided.

He felt relieved.

His memory was blurry, but when he had regained consciousness, he had a sense that he had failed to restrain the black impulse and destroyed part of the inside of the airship in the process.

The biggest problem was still that side.

Looking that way, Liam instinctively gripped his dragon arm with his human hand.

The battle between the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings and the three monsters was still going.

It was evenly matched... no, there had been a change. The three monsters had become two, and there was now a small figure that looked like a person added to the fight.

Maybe one of them had been taken down.

But more fundamentally, those horrifying monsters inspired nothing but bad premonitions at a glance. If Liam's suspicion was right, then the Empire would have to be stopped.

As for that human-looking figure, what was it?

He could also see blue flames, so perhaps it was the dangerous person Princess Ceras had mentioned--the Imperial Princess.

Huge explosions and pillars of fire kept surging up. Earth and rock were flying, and the terrain itself was being carved away.

He had never seen such violent combat. Compared to this hell, even the brutal battles against the demon army in the north of the Kingdom seemed mild.

Even from this distance, the shocks reached him by making the air itself tremble.

He would have to go closer to judge the situation more accurately.

And yet he understood something instinctively.

That Four Heavenly King was a foe who far surpassed even the Earth Four Heavenly King, the one he had once been able to do nothing but witness as despair.

Power so overwhelmingly threatening.

He had that thought, and at that exact moment,

a single sound, as though the air itself had been beaten flat, thundered out from the battlefield, and earth and stone blasted several kilometers into the sky.

The next instant, a shadow fell from overhead.

Liam reflexively looked up. Something passed over the heads of both him and Regingule, then crashed into the ground with a heavy impact that shook the air.

It was the ruined head of a gigantic body.

One of the monsters that had been fighting the Four Heavenly King. The being that had looked like a mighty ogre walking on two legs.

That powerful existence was now beyond ruin.

It had been severed below the neck, and only the head had come flying this way.

Its face was half crushed and no longer retained its original shape. The two horns that should have jutted out had been snapped off at the base.

From the force with which it had been smashed down, the blood and chunks of flesh stored within it scattered everywhere.

Bluish blood, faintly tinged toward purple, poured from the cut neck and dirtied the ground.

The rolling head spoke all too eloquently of death.

There was no doubt it had been the remains of something on the level of a Four Heavenly King, or close to it.

"How cruel..."

At the very instant he thought he should at least bury it, the head lying on the ground began to tremble irregularly. He was just about to dismiss it as the sort of convulsion common to corpses, when--

"■■■■■■■■■■■■■------!!!!"

Something like a disgusting scream leaked from the head's mouth.

And then--

the cut surface of the neck split open. The flesh turned inside out, and from there human hands and feet burst forth in impossible numbers.

Fingers, palms, shins, heels. Limbs in what felt like their thousands writhed and tangled together, moving as though they existed for the sole purpose of scraping at the ground.

In an instant they formed a shape, propped up the head, dragged it, dragged themselves--

and then began heading back toward the battlefield.

Leaving only a trail of blood, it vanished at a grotesque speed.

Words left him.

He had seen something he should not have seen.

That instinct froze his spine. Judging that he was on the verge of losing his sanity, Liam slapped himself hard across the cheek. The dull pain barely dragged him back to reality.

Fortunately, Regingule was on his back and seemed not to have seen any of it.

At this moment, that alone could be called luck.
