"You should calm yourself. From what I've heard, it's a reenactment of the past that feels just like reality, but it is still only an illusion. Whatever you do there will not change the present. Even so, you will likely be forced to choose."

"..."

Lumiela fell silent. Her fists were clenched tight.
In other words, it was like a much more realistic kind of VR.
And one that was guaranteed to get gruesome.
The former hero's cause of death--being attacked by nobles consumed by desire and soldiers who moved for money. A nightmare like that.

Only I, as a user of light magic, and Lumiela, as the spirit of light, could enter it. Even if we had companions, we couldn't bring them along, but I had no one besides Lumiela anyway, so there was no problem there.

The original Brave had suffered pretty badly in this trial too, if I remembered right...
Right from the start, the innkeeper who had been bought off poisoned the food with some hideous basilisk venom. Basilisk venom... would that even work on me?
Then again, the former hero had already been physically wrecked from fighting the Demon King, and on top of that he couldn't bring himself to go all out against humans, so that was probably why.
Since this was before the tragedy, Lumiela still believed in humanity, so they tricked her with smooth words into going out to buy a surprise present "for the hero who's done so much for us," separating her from him while the poison took effect.
They had even arranged things so the poison would circulate properly and be hard to detoxify... it was all truly awful.
Part of me did think the former hero should have just beaten the hell out of the humans attacking him, but I remembered him hesitating, saying something like, "In the world we finally saved after losing comrades, I don't want to hurt people."

So maybe the nobles' malice had simply outdone him. Though it also felt like the former hero had just been too kind.
No, I guess it had taken both for him to lose.
Even the original Brave had looked to be in serious trouble in the trial... Villagers tempted by money had come at him with kitchen knives, and while there was no physical damage from that, the psychological damage...
There had also been strong knights disguised as villagers suddenly slashing at him...

In the original story, Lumiela had been about to kill them quite normally, and he had been the one stopping her. All in all, it looked like the sort of trial that piled on mental strain from every direction.
If the enemy had been demons, it would have been easy. Instead, what waited there was only the ordinary ugliness of humans.

In the original story, companions who grew worried because so much time had passed cheered him on from outside, and supported by their voices, he recovered while suffering through it. After long dialogue and conflict, he got through it by negotiating with some reasonably decent nobles and some more martial nobles who could at least be talked to.

It was the sort of trial meant to torment a hero.
But if the premise was different, it was simple.
I just had to destroy everything. The nobles and all the rest.
If necessary, I would wipe out the entire country. That would erase the cause of death and let me break through.

It would be an unpleasant experience, but it was only an illusion...
The way I'd lived up to now made it impossible for me, unlike the original Brave, to steer this into something like a discussion.
I'd been too inconsistent. Lumiela would probably be angry too.

Honestly, even if it was only an illusion, I didn't want Lumiela to have to kill people... but if talking failed, then I'd dirty my hands right along with her.
We'd go in with the intention of blowing away every last one of them...
so that the number Lumiela had to kill would be as small as possible.

That part was fine. The real problem came before that.
Very soon now, I'd have to show my own past too...
No, maybe it would be better not to tell her about my past...

"Brave."




------------------------------------------------------------------------




Lumiela's voice pulled me back up out of the sea of my thoughts. Her gaze pierced me.



"Ah, sorry. I was thinking."



"I have to ask... Did you know about this trial too? If you did, then why didn't you tell me--"



Her voice trembled as she asked. I answered by telepathy. Clautia was still here.



"Once the trial begins, I'll show you all my memories too. Just like this is an unpleasant memory you wouldn't want dug up, for you. That's why I'll reveal mine as well."



"...Understood. I'll decide after I've seen everything."



Ah...
There was no escaping now.
I didn't want to talk about my previous life.
But if I was going to step into Lumiela's past like this, then I had to open up my own insides too.
That was only fair.



What I didn't want to say was... what Lumiela would think once she knew about the original Brave.
I was an intruder. Originally, Lumiela should have been able to stay beside the original Brave, someone almost like a living copy of the former hero.
And then Lumiela and the original Brave would save the world together. Wouldn't that have been the more hopeful ending?



Even with Cain there, I hadn't acted. I couldn't even fulfill the former hero's lingering wish of defeating the Demon King through the power of bonds with comrades.
What would Lumiela think of me once she knew, from my previous-life memories, all that original-story knowledge?
If she said something like, "I wish it had been the real hero, the original Brave, not you," I wouldn't be able to endure it.
It terrified me.
At the very least, every other human being would say exactly that. They'd all say it would have been better if there had been a radiant hero who was kind enough to save everyone.
And then they'd all deny my existence. That itself was fine. But if Lumiela said it, it would hurt.



Ah, my head hurt. I had heartburn too. I felt sick.
Just imagining it left me in this state.



That was why I hated this.
I trusted Lumiela, and I knew she thought of me too as an important companion.
But I couldn't believe I surpassed the original Brave in any way. I was ordinary, while the original Brave had been someone special.



I didn't know what Lumiela had thought at the time, in the original story.
But afterward, she had still cooperated in the battle against the Demon King.
Surely she had seen something in the kind of person Brave was. Surely she had found some way to reconcile herself with that ideal of saving people.



Or rather... because I had done so many things far removed from what a hero should be, maybe I'd dragged this Lumiela's feelings along with mine too. Maybe what Lumiela really wanted was to pursue that ideal...



No, this was impossible. Or maybe not. I thought about too many things until my head went strange.
If I didn't sort out my own feelings, I was going to be in trouble.
I'd believed I was prepared for the fact that someday I'd have to face this.
But when the moment actually came, all that came first was agitation.
I knew I had to calm down, but maybe I still wanted a little more time.



Not even deep-breathing anymore, but nearly hyperventilating, I tried to steady myself--



"Are you all right, Brave...? You don't have to force yourself. If you don't want to say it yet, then you don't have to. I'll wait."



"...N-no. I can do this. I'm fine."



Maybe Clautia knew we were speaking by telepathy, or maybe she didn't, but she approached me and drew out a small box-shaped magical tool from inside her cloak.
Made of metal and gemstones, it was engraved with intricate patterns whose purpose I couldn't guess.



"After continuing to study the Holy Sword, I created this magical tool. If you carry it, we on this side will be able to observe what happens. I won't force you... but as someone involved in those events, I want to know how you two will choose within that past."

Clautia had taken the Holy Sword back from the nobles and had guarded it like a watchman ever since. If she hadn't, then given how nobles were, the Holy Sword might well have been lost by now.
Thinking of it like that made it hard for me to refuse.



"I think the outcome is going to be ugly..."



"I don't mind. I'm not asking for any particular choice."



"Understood. What about you, Lumiela?"



"I... um... all right."



Though hesitant, Lumiela seemed to understand Clautia's feelings.
I accepted the magical tool from Clautia.



Lumiela and I met each other's eyes. Right now, even that felt hard. I found myself looking away.
The thought of those beautiful, trusting eyes turning to contempt...
Lumiela squeezed my hand tightly.
I understood what she meant. Are you all right? Let's go.
After taking a deep breath, Lumiela and I both reached out and touched the Holy Sword Idea at the same time. Though the hilt should have been cold, it held a strange warmth. Light pulsed once.



The scenery around us twisted.
The grave receded, and it felt like the world itself was peeling away from us.



And then--
we stepped into the illusion.
