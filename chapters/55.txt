Lumiela's Side

The moment she thought she had touched the Holy Sword beside Brave, the ground beneath her came undone. Rather than sinking, it was more like diving. Though there was no water, only Lumiela's consciousness seemed to descend, the outline of her body growing vague as something soft wrapped around her. There was no weight, no direction. She simply drifted.

The sensation was very much like the time she had once been sealed away. That quiet prison where time lost meaning and only thought rose and fell.

Softly, gently. A fall she could do nothing to resist but surrender herself to.

Even so, her heart was calm. She could feel Brave holding her hand. Not a strong grip, but a warmth that told her he would not let go. The sensation at her fingertips. That alone melted away the anxiety nesting in the depths of her chest as though it had been a lie.

No matter where they went, she would not be alone.

If that was the case, then even being sealed again might have been all right--

"Lumiela."

"Mn?"

The voice calling her name dragged her consciousness back. Apparently she had been dozing. She had gone and thought something confused.

At the same time, a natural scent tickled her nose. Damp soil, young leaves, the trace of running water somewhere far off.

It felt different from the artificial natural dwelling where Clautia had just been. This was outside. And yet they should have been on a snowy mountain, but there was no cold. Her body hesitated at the sudden shift in temperature.

"This place..."

"Feels like some kind of illusionary world created by the Holy Sword."

"--It feels peaceful. The sunlight is nice and warm."

When she looked up, light was streaming down through the forest leaves. Shadows of the trees swayed, and the wind brushed the branches. It truly was a gentle, peaceful world. If you cut out only this one place, it was as though conflict had never existed there at all.

"Yeah... I see. So the starting point is the same after all."

Brave looked around and quietly let out a breath. The expression at his profile was a strange mixture of familiarity and vigilance.

Brave really was hard to understand.

"Brave."

When she called his name, he swept his eyes around the area as though on guard, then turned only his head slightly toward her.

"Hold off on the memories for now. If we stay in this place, being found sooner or later is unavoidable, so before that I'll cut off the seeds of defeat."

"...I wonder how I should punish them."

Something dark welled up from the bottom of her heart.

"--Sorry, but for now, leave this part to me."

Without thinking, she frowned. It was Brave's request, so she wanted to listen, but this of all things--
No, Brave had his reasons too. Then for now, it would be better not to go wild against the enemy just yet.

Still--

"All right... but if I can't hold it in anymore, I might explode and rampage, you know?"

"Yeah. I'll keep that firmly in mind."

Brave understood, then. He understood that if Lumiela fought, a massacre would happen.

"...Also, should I turn invisible?"

At the question, Brave nodded immediately.

"Please do."

Brave let out a small breath and, for a moment, looked almost relieved.

Remaining invisible, Lumiela walked beside him. As they walked, Brave began speaking as though to himself.

This world was a fantasy that had simply been assigned roles. The people and events within it were no more than traces of the past being replayed. And apparently, to the residents of this world, Brave was recognized as the former owner of the Holy Sword--the previous hero who had defeated the Demon King himself.

Brave said he was curious whether the world would still recognize him that way even with the mask on.

The path through the forest sloped gently downward, and before long the view opened up.

An old-fashioned pastoral town. Stone houses. It even felt nostalgic.

Before she was sealed, civilization had been around this level.

The land and buildings were different, but there could be no doubt that this was near the Shrine of Selyad, the place where she had first met Brave.

Around ten villagers were walking toward them from farther up the road.

"Ah..."

Something unpleasant rang in the depths of her chest.

She understood. She understood where this was, and when.

The villagers smiled brightly.

But their eyes were not smiling. They glittered like beasts', and in their hands were hunting weapons.

Yes, this was where the previous hero had made one mistake.

Lumiela felt as though this had been the beginning of the end.

The farmers, enticed by great sums of money from nobles, had attacked the former hero here using poison given to them by those nobles--a deadly toxin scattered by Digitalis the Wolfblight, the beast of calamity that had once rampaged through the Empire before it was sealed away.

Slashed by blades coated in such a poison, the previous hero's body had been eaten away by it together with the wounds from the battle against the Demon King that had never fully healed.

Even so, if he had chosen to use force against the villagers, he should at least have been able to escape. But he had not wanted to hurt ordinary people, and those misfortunes had piled up until things turned out that way--

She had to warn Brave to get back.

Lumiela was about to tell him to fall back.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

"I'm not kind."

With those words, the wind split.

Screams went up, and poison-coated weapons rolled across the ground. The villagers' arms had been broken with precise, merciless force.

Brave moved without the slightest hesitation, as though he had known all along.

"You... knew about this too?"

"Yeah."

With cold eyes, Brave looked down at the villagers sprawled on the ground.

"Damn... just because you're some winner-at-life hero... how dare you... to poor ordinary folks like us...!"

Before the man could finish, Brave stepped on his back, turning the words into a groan. Blood spread out wetly.

No different from reality.

After clicking his tongue, Brave said nothing more and simply started walking as though it were the most ordinary thing in the world.

Beneath the warm sunlight, the pastoral townscape remained unchanged.

Only the ugliness of people certainly remained there.

For the moment, Lumiela felt relieved.

Even against ordinary people, Brave crushed them without mercy.

The previous hero had not been able to do that. He had hesitated to cut down villagers who had merely been enticed and manipulated.

And yet, Brave had not killed a single one of them.

Lumiela hesitated over whether to finish them off, but barely restrained herself.

That was right. The nobles who had incited them, and the knights and soldiers who served them, were the ones who truly should not exist.

"All right, the warm-up should be enough. Where's the relic that sealed you away? Let's destroy that first."

"...Over there."

During the previous hero's journey to defeat the Demon King, they had discovered two ancient relics along the way.

Both were excavation finds specialized in sealing, and none of them had had the faintest idea what sort of technology had been used to create them.

Of the two, the more powerful one had been used to seal the Demon King.

The other had been handed over to the Kingdom as a national treasure. And yet somehow, despite supposedly being under the Kingdom's control, it had ended up in the hands of the nobles who attacked the previous hero and Lumiela. And then it had been used to seal Lumiela herself.

Chains of visible magic. They wrapped themselves around the target as though binding the very being itself, allowing no resistance as they dragged it in and sealed it away.

"The Shrine of Selyad used to be somewhere along the Glanze River, so if we search the riverbank, we should find it. First, let's wipe out the enemies who'd try to seal you."

When she told him that, Brave listened to the sound of the river and ran off with, "That way."

Lumiela found it strange.

She had thought she might burst out from the very beginning and run wild, driven by the darkness inside her.

And yet, while there certainly was anger welling up from her depths, she was still able to act calmly.
