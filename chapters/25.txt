Abelia's Mission (1)



Abelia's Side






"Ahh... what a pain."


Abelia accidentally muttered her inner thoughts aloud.

A sigh came with it. She had been handed another troublesome assignment. She continued down the dim mine passage. Area 3 was close now.


Infiltrate Area 3 and investigate it, then carry out a rescue.

That was the order Princess Ceras had given Abelia.


The rescue in question concerned her friend, the mage Felicite, along with two close-range knights.

A week earlier, the three of them had secretly infiltrated Area 3.

Their objective had been to investigate the eccentrics and lunatics nesting there. Could they truly be counted as military strength or not?

But the result had not been good.

Not a single one of the three had returned.


There had been no contact at all.

That was abnormal.

It could only mean something had happened.


Area 3--

these days, it was apparently a kind of bottommost pit where freaks, madmen, and criminals drifted together.

Reason no longer worked there, and order and rules had collapsed entirely. Area 1 was supposedly the most decent of the zones, but even there, it wasn't only the straightforward strong who had been invited in; merchant groups wanting to do business with such people had also found ways to worm themselves inside.

Area 2 was said to be even worse, full of underworld types, but even there rules still existed, and evil or not, people could at least probably be spoken to.

Area 3 went beyond even that. It was dangerous on an entirely different level.


Even so, Abelia did want to save Felicite, her friend.

Felicite had helped her a great deal in various ways, and if possible, Abelia wanted her to still be alive. But with a whole week having passed, the outlook was grim.


And as if that wasn't enough, Area 3 itself was already on the verge of becoming even more chaotic.

According to Princess Ceras, some of the nobles were trying to lay their hands on Area 3.

It seemed they intended to dispatch knights, subdue the area from above, and absorb it into the Kingdom's fighting strength by force.

So beyond rescuing Felicite and the others, Ceras had told Abelia she also wanted her to assess the condition of Area 3 itself.

Could these madmen actually be brought to heel?

It certainly was a question worth asking.


Well, if anything happens, I need to run for it immediately.


Abelia decided to infiltrate the place alone.

There were no others close enough to her own strength who could be mobilized right away, and if they would only become dead weight, it was better not to bring them.

If escape was the plan in an emergency, then she judged it would be easier alone.


At last, she reached the very Area 3 in question.


She pulled a tattered hood low over her head and covered her mouth with cloth, making herself look every bit like someone with a shady past. No one would think she had money on her. That should help ward off anyone after profit.

The ragged hood made her suspicious as anything--

and yet even dressed like that, she did not stand out much here at all.


The instant Abelia stepped into Area 3, all she saw were eccentrics and lunatics.

People polishing bones at the roadside, old men muttering incomprehensible words endlessly, people who did nothing but scream, others laughing as they slammed their heads into walls.

Amid a crowd like that, no one would pay any mind to what she was wearing.


But the real problem was elsewhere.

In order to get information on Felicite and the others, the usual approach would be to gather information from somebody.

But Area 3 did not look like a place where any normal conversation could be expected to work.


In that case--

she would have to use the chaos as cover and search with her own eyes and feet.

Abelia could feel on her skin that the "situation" Princess Ceras feared was already almost upon them.

She had heard the footsteps of many people approaching.


Hah... here they come.


Keeping her distance, Abelia quietly listened in.


"Get down there!"


A raised voice rang out.

At the same time came the clatter of armor.

It was a line of men who looked exactly like the chosen sort, their noble house crests glinting in gold thread.

Helmets dulled with reflected light--

and from within one of them, a knight slowly removed his helm. A hard jawline, a faint smile lingering at one corner of his mouth. He swept a glare over the area.


"Filth! We are going to carve order into the lot of you!"


The command was low, but distinct.

With ruthless precision, they fell upon one of the nearby residents of Area 3.

At the first slash, the resident staggered and spat blood.

After knocking him down, they casually beat him with a whip.

Like a demon instructor.


"This is education!"


The lead knight spat that out.

The resident had been thrown off his feet and now groaned as armored heels kicked into him.

Yet the residents of Area 3 did not seem to care at all. Most of them did not even bother looking toward the knights. That was what made the scene so frightening.


The sharp clanging of metal echoed through the mine, and the smell of blood mixed with the smell of steel.

This was violence meant to force obedience through pain. Make an example of one, and the rest would listen.

In the military too, rule through fear could produce results--keeping deserters in line through corporal punishment, maintaining discipline that way.


But that only worked on ordinary people.

Whether it would have any effect on lunatics was doubtful.

Still, because even lunatics were living creatures, the possibility was not zero.

Abelia continued watching.




--------------------------------------------------




Many nobles had atrocious tastes.

Quite a few of them looked down on everyone beneath their station and liked watching others fight as gladiators, or had a taste for blood in general.


And because their social rank and the violence of knights shielded them, they believed they themselves would be safe.

That was probably why they had appeared in dangerous Area 3.

An elderly nobleman stood protected in the center of the knights.

Clapping his hands, he watched the knights torment the residents as though enjoying a stage show.


"Excellent, knights! These trash-like creatures must be re-educated thoroughly and turned into loyal pieces on the board!"


The nobleman nodded, basking in the moment.

He stood in safety behind a wall of knights, taking pleasure in trampling on the dignity of others. His expression shone with something ghastly.

At a glance, the old noble radiated vile character.

His face was covered in wrinkles, like the dried skin of a snake.

His eyes glimmered with a muddy light.

His nose was sharp, and his mouth was twisted at all times in a crooked smile--not a smile of mercy or superiority, but one drunk on the pleasure of looking down on others.


Every finger he clapped together bore a golden ring.

He wasn't just unpleasant.

He had the worst kind of personality.


But under normal circumstances, defying a noble was unthinkable.

Do that, and your entire family line would be wiped out.

That was the reality of this country, an unwritten law everyone knew.


Yes--

normally, no one would do it.


But this was Area 3.

A place where common sense and reason had long since fallen to pieces.


Abelia suddenly felt a chill crawl up her back and lifted her face.

The air had gone strangely still.

The armored knights were glaring about, the old noble steeped in pleasure, and for a brief instant the atmosphere froze solid.


Then it happened.


"Punching bag!"


The completely out-of-place shout rang through the area.

Everyone turned toward it at once, just as a single oddball leaped.

Rags for clothes, barefoot, eyes so unfocused that no one would have judged him sane at a glance.

That man launched himself high enough to land directly above the old nobleman.


"Huh?"


The nobleman's muttered word became his last.

In the next instant came a heavy impact.

The sound of flesh being struck.

Then again, and again.


"Punching bag! Punching bag! Punching baaag!"


Shouting like a madman, the man who kept repeating the words punching bag rained blows down upon the old noble.

The knights froze at the suddenness of it.

And in those few seconds, the old nobleman took blow after blow to the face, stomach, and chest like an actual practice sack, helplessly battered.


Blood flew, bones shattered, and the old noble's body lost its shape.

Just like that.

Far too easily.

Far too one-sidedly.

He was reduced to mincemeat.

Dead.

A nobleman had been beaten to death by a single lunatic.


The sight left the surrounding knights stunned.

Their minds could not keep up. The order that should have protected the noble--and thus themselves--the common sense of the world itself, had collapsed in an instant.

Then a furious roar exploded out.


"You cur!"


At the bellow of the demon instructor, the knights snapped back to themselves, drew their swords in rage, and burned with murderous intent.

In that moment, the place became hell again.

It was now a battle of revenge.


But that madman--the Punching Bag Man--possessed unbelievable strength.

He caught blades, punched, kicked, crushed bones, and laughed as he put men down.

He was madness given flesh.

And yet numbers were overwhelming in the end. Faced with so many knights, he was gradually cornered.


Spitting blood, dropping to one knee, he still kept swinging his fists.

At the very last moment, he was smiling.


"Punching... baaaag..."


That was all he left behind before collapsing.

Another life was gone.

The price had been immense.

The Punching Bag Man was strong enough that even Abelia would have struggled with him. He had probably set foot in demon-general territory.


Half the knights had fallen, and the survivors stood there soaked in blood.

There were no winners.

All that remained were hollow eyes staring down at the red-black ground.

There had been no meaning in it.

It was nothing but violence crushing violence.


Not justice.

Not salvation.


What even is this...?


Standing there motionless, Abelia muttered that to herself.

Order and madness had collided, both sides bled, and in the end only meaningless death remained.


And now the time of reckoning came even for the surviving noble knights--including the demon-instructor type who had overseen the beatings.

From nowhere appeared a giant frame--a man roughly two and a half meters tall. Chuckling, "Ihihi... uhehe," he seized the demon instructor by both arms and lifted him up like a child's toy.


"Let go! Let go of me!"


The screams rang out, but the giant said nothing. Ignoring the instructor's flailing legs, he dragged him away deeper inside.

Then a scream echoed from farther in.


The knights who saw that panicked and tried to flee, but it was already too late.

The residents of Area 3 had surrounded them and cut off every escape.


"Hyahha!!"


Among them, a group made up entirely of mohawk-haired men shouting "Hyahha!" displayed astonishing coordination as they hunted the knights and stripped away their armor and belongings.


"Hyahha Strategist! Awesome! There's a sparkly gold ring here!"


"Ohh. You lot... do you know? Gems and gold... sell for a lot!"


"That's amazing! Hyahha!!"


"By the way, was killing nobles supposed to be bad? Eh, whatever, if today's fun then that's enough! Hyahha!!"


Listening to them, Abelia realized that even within the constantly shouting hyahha crowd, there were several central figures.

The so-called Captain Hyahha was the boss. The so-called Strategist Hyahha handled the brains. There were apparently others who directed things by role too.


There were several individuals on the level of demon generals.

Captain Hyahha in particular was clearly stronger than Abelia.


One knight after another was captured. Some were dragged away somewhere, while others had their throats slit on the spot and fell dead.

The socially elevated knights had been turned into toys.

The hyahha crowd lived only for the moment. One could feel in them a determination to live however they pleased.


What would happen if Abelia returned immediately now and testified to what she'd seen?

The nobles would surely move at once to suppress the place under the name of rebellion.


But there were powerful madmen here.

They would absolutely rampage to the very end.

And the noble side too would suffer massive losses.

At a time when humanity still had to fight the demons.


She was certain they would never simply accept death gracefully.

They were the kind of people who did not care at all what happened to the world.


These people are never going to become allies...


She had to find her missing comrades quickly and get out of this hell.

And then she would tell Princess Ceras:

madmen could never be made into allies after all.
