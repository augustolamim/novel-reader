"Brave. I'm going to tell you this properly."

Out of the corner of her eye, something like a twitching, blood-dripping rag lay on the ground. Ignoring it, Lumiela looked straight at Brave and spoke.

"--? What is it?"

"You seemed worried that I might think the original Brave would be better for me, didn't you? But that's completely off the mark."

After confirming that Brave had gone rigid in silence, she pressed on with the momentum she had built.

"I want you. No, it has to be you! You're the one who has to be my contractor!"

The moment she said it, she grew embarrassed.
Heat rushed to her face all at once. She no longer had the courage to meet his eyes, and she could feel her cheeks turning so red they almost hurt. Wrapped in feelings with nowhere to go, Lumiela could only let out a vague breath.

For a moment, Brave's eyes wavered.
To hell with it. She pressed the attack even harder.

"I'm happy, you know? Much happier than the me in the original ever was."

Once she had declared it outright, she reached out and sandwiched Brave's cheeks between both hands.

Brave looked as though he had lost the ability to speak. He neither denied it nor accepted it. He simply looked at Lumiela.
For the time being, she ought to explain why the current Lumiela was happier too.

As she came to understand the original story, one thing had become clear to her.
The "me" inside that manga had stood on the battlefield for a reason different from the current Lumiela's.

"The original Brave... he really did resemble the previous hero. Too much, really. The way he carried himself, his personality, all of it. He was a complete look-alike. That's exactly why I understood it. The me in the original stayed trapped in the past the whole time. She wasn't moving forward at all."

When she watched the battles and how they ended, she had become almost certain.
The me in the original did not keep fighting for original Brave himself. Because he looked so much like the previous hero, she had overlapped him with that image. And because of that, she had only cooperated in finishing the previous hero's unfinished business--slaying the Demon King.

Brave lowered his gaze, thinking a little.

"...Still trapped in the past, huh."

"That's right. To the me in the original, original Brave was probably nothing more than a shadow that reminded her of someone else. That's a cruel way to put it, though."

"If Lumiela herself thinks that, then it probably is."

"I'm probably not far off."

Brave seemed convinced.
Now was her chance. Lumiela took her feelings, organized them, and sent them straight into Brave.

"Whoaa?!"

Brave let out a startled cry.

"Heheh, that's your return gift."

She could not help but laugh.

"And one more thing. Watching those memories made me think this too. You're worrying too much about the original. Wasn't this world basically headed for destruction unless miracle after miracle happened? If that's the case, then you don't need to feel responsible."

Brave pressed a hand to his head and muttered that it was too much information at once.
Even so, the severity had left his face, and he looked relieved somehow, refreshed.

"Thanks.... Your feelings definitely got through to me."

"Yes. Now we understand each other completely."

And because of that, they had grown stronger once again.
Outside the barrier, the soldiers were shouting at the tops of their lungs.
"That barrier has to vanish eventually, kill them!!"

An honest thought rose in her chest.
How noisy.
It was not emotional, and it was not anger. They were simply loud.
She and Brave had only just grown even closer, and it felt terribly wasteful to have that sullied by such meaningless shouting.
And from illusions, no less.
A little reading of the room would have been nice. They were nothing but a nuisance.

"...Lumiela."

"What?"

"Your eyes are dead."

She immediately covered both eyes with her hands and kneaded them a little. If Brave said so, then maybe they really had looked awful.

"More importantly!"

She desperately changed the subject.
Lumiela held out a fist toward Brave. Brave immediately understood what she meant and made a fist as well, and the two of them gently knocked their knuckles together.
The sense of their contract grew stronger. It was not merely a connection of magic power. It felt like will and will overlapping.

"Let's show them, Brave."

Lumiela declared it plainly.

"Yeah, Lumiela.
...Though it's awfully ironic that our opponents are human soldiers instead of demons."

"That's because the soldiers are the ones in the wrong. We're closer than anything in the original. This isn't on the level of standing shoulder to shoulder or pushing each other forward. We're one body. If the two of us combine our strength, there's nothing to fear."

In Brave's terms, maybe that meant fusion, or transformation.
Either was fine. Even the Demon King transformed.
Heroes could transform too.

That three-legged-race practice had paid off as well.
Two people becoming one.

The ultimate power that could not exist with either side missing.
If it was now, surely they could do it.

Pure white light quietly rose from beneath their feet and wrapped around them.
Their forms overlapped, their boundaries grew vague.
The distinction between "me" and "him" as separate bodies melted away.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

"............ Isn't this taking a while? We still haven't transformed."

"No kidding."

There was definitely a sense of combining, but it was unstable somehow, as though they had been sealed inside a cocoon.
Cut off from the outside world, power swirled only on the inside, wavering without quite settling into shape. It might be possible to think of it as a charge-up period.
Lumiela frantically kept adjusting things as well.
Just as the moment of emergence took time, perhaps a first fusion could not be completed in a single leap.
Brave's memories also held the idea that transformation originally required some kind of motion, so perhaps this was just how it was.
They would have to improve it until they could combine instantly.
Since they had nothing else to do, Lumiela shared information with Brave.

About what they would do in order to part with this illusion that was unpleasant merely to look at.
The breakthrough condition was almost certainly "remove the factors that led to the previous hero's death."
There might be some small difference, but if they wiped out the noble faction that had caused the attacks and made up this illusion, it should be enough. That much had to be right.
However, if they laid hands on the nobles, the Kingdom might move to save face. A larger power could intervene, proclaiming justice and order.
Even if this was only an illusion, its structure should be no different from reality. Violence bred the next violence in turn.

They would crush legs and arms, make the enemies unable to wield weapons, dump them on the ground, and neutralize them so they could never fight again.
They calmly built those calculations one by one.

"What about this?"

Brave's thought flowed directly into her, without words.

"...I see."

That certainly would be faster. Efficient, and also--
kind.

"Well, if that doesn't work, we'll just keep trying things."

"We've got plenty of ways to do it. Let's call the strategy... yes. The 'Dog of Flanders Plan.' How does that sound?"

"It is exactly what it sounds like... but I don't object."

They would use their overwhelming power to surpass the violence of a whole nation.

It was almost time.

"What is this...?!"
"What is that light?!"

The soldiers cried out.
A power from an entirely different dimension than before was overflowing.

Their viewpoint felt higher than usual.
The base was Brave, with his mask and black cloak. Lumiela was the support.
That said, three pairs of gigantic pure white wings were growing from their back, and they were clearly no longer human.

"Hey, where did the wing part come from?"
"No idea. Let's check later."

If they wanted to return, they could separate back into two forms at once. They had that sense.
It felt as though Brave's and Lumiela's wills now dwelled in a single vessel.
Their boundary was vague, but it had not vanished.

"As the first enemy after transforming, let's have them keep us entertained a little."

The moment they directed fighting spirit outward with those words, the change had already begun.

"Huh?"

Every soldier dropped to the ground one after another.
The knees that had been trembling lost all support and buckled. They could not even crawl.
Their screams cut off halfway through, and only faces twisted in despair remained turned toward them as they collapsed.

Honestly, Lumiela could not keep up with what had happened.

The battlefield had gone silent.
Neither Lumiela nor Brave had done anything.
And yet the soldiers had all fainted, foam at their mouths, their eyes rolled back, frozen in expressions of despair.

"What do we do?"
"...Let's go check. It could still be a trap."

They stepped outside the barrier and confirmed it. Everyone lying there had merely lost consciousness. They were not dead. Yet they bore no external injuries either.

Because they had been prepared for a fight, it almost felt anticlimactic.

"There's one who hasn't passed out."
"Ah, you're right."

Still on all fours, there was indeed one soldier there whose raw power looked as though it exceeded even demon-general class.
But the moment they stood before him, he was done for.

The soldier began trembling like a small animal. His face rapidly aged, drained of blood and going pale.
He looked up at them with unfocused eyes and chattered his teeth, letting out meaningless sounds like "Uh! Ah!"

"Want me to punch him?"

Brave's thought reached her.

Lumiela was angry at every soldier in this place. But seeing one so broken left him looking less like an enemy than something pitiful, and she could find no reason to raise a fist anymore. Leaving him alone was fine.

They passed by the devastated soldier. The instant they stepped past him, a noise came from behind.

When they turned around, the soldier had thrown both hands up.
Lumiela wondered if his earlier state had all been an act, and perhaps Brave had thought the same, because their shared body started to turn back toward him.
But the soldier's shoulders were shaking, and he was crying while trying to suppress the sound. "Ihih, I'm alive! Ahaha!" he cried in delight.
"Aryahya!" he laughed.
He had broken.

Time to refocus. Next came the nobles. Since they had gone to the trouble, they might as well fly there.

Lumiela took charge of controlling the wings. She checked each motion one by one, found no problems, and sent the signal to Brave.
Brave planted his feet and jumped. At that instant Lumiela moved the wings, layering on the effortless levitation natural to a spirit.

"We overdid it."
"Yes, a little."

Apparently they had misjudged the force. Their view tore straight up into blue, punched through a sea of clouds, and they could feel the air thinning.

Far beyond the sky, the two of them began fine-tuning together, saying this and that.
With this power the two of them shared, it felt like they could do anything.

"Valuen Stein."

The name of this form was decided at once.
They were keenly aware of themselves as outsiders, as heretics to this world.
It was the point Lumiela and Brave had reached together, after meeting one another.
