Gearnote understood that, by the standards of the world at large, he was evil.

He believed he was doing something good, or at least something necessary.

But it was a lonely path. Gearnote had already accepted being called things like mad.



Would other people laugh and say this was what he got for the way he had lived?

From somewhere far off, a single voice rang out, urging people on.

It was only because Gearnote was slowly crawling back toward where he had been before that he had come close enough to hear it.



"Gearnote says he wants us to leave him behind with that warrior guy I don't really get! He whispered it to me himself! He's probably got some kind of plan, right!? So come on, everybody, get out of here now!!"

There was no doubt. That strangely glib voice that riled everyone up, that infuriating voice that was aggravating just to hear, was not one you could mistake.

It belonged to that man.

Cain.



His voice was deliberately loud.

The kind of categorical, inescapable phrasing meant to make every soldier around him hear it.

--Incitement.



The instant Gearnote understood that, he clicked his tongue inwardly.

Ridiculous. He had never said any such thing.



He was sprawled across the floor, coughing blood from deep in his throat, his lungs creaking with every breath, unable to stand after all.

Regingule had gone off somewhere with Hero Liam.

In this state, Gearnote needed help if he was going to escape from here. He had no plan.

Even as a joke, this was far too malicious.



But once he realized the man saying it was Cain, it made sense at once.



That man was first-rate at one thing only: dragging others down. Whether by instinct or not, he always did it at the worst possible time.

And he was excellent at taking his frustrations out on people.

Most likely, he meant to kill Regingule, who had crushed his balls, and Gearnote, who had not helped him.



Gearnote had handed Cain over to the soldiers to be treated, but they had healed him too well.

Cain had recovered.

Now he was making things up off the top of his head and spewing convenient lies.



But this was bad.

Gearnote trusted almost no one but himself. Whenever something important had to be done, he had always handled it alone.

From time to time he had even given orders like the one Cain was claiming now: I have preparations to make, so I'll handle this myself.



"I saw the future! If we stay here, we'll just get in their way! Leave everything to those two and let's survive this!!"



Cain was, at least officially, a guest the Empire had been instructed to treat with care as an important source of information.

The soldiers also knew his epithet, hero of foresight, and that supposedly he could see the future.



On top of that, the soldiers had seen Regingule's strength.

The Empire also believed that strength was supreme. That pure violence was the sort of thing soldiers trusted.



He was General Ubel's guest, he supposedly could see the future, he seemed to be relaying a message that retreat was permitted, and Regingule had also been near Gearnote.



This was the worst.

It might become a very bad gamble. If the soldiers obeyed Cain's words, Gearnote would be left behind on this airship that was already on the verge of collapse.



The moment he understood that fact, his spine turned cold.

The position he was in right now--



"W-wait...!"



Gearnote somehow managed to force out a shout, but only a ragged voice came.

Even as he did, he could tell from the sounds of the soldiers moving that they were gradually getting farther away.

The wingbeats of the beastfolk soldiers overlapped, cutting the air and echoing through the ship, and before long even that faded.



All that remained was the sound of Gearnote crawling forward.



He had been abandoned.



His thoughts wandered vaguely.

Had it been a mistake from the very beginning to enter into a cooperative relationship with Cain?

And after that, when the Wind Four Heavenly King annihilated World Breaker in the Kingdom, had it also been a mistake to decide to bring Cain to the Empire? At the time, Gearnote had privately been delighted to have gained an experimental subject who had lost his fighting strength.



Apparently those choices had come back to bite him.

He had underestimated Cain, dismissing him as a fool in every way except knowledge. Yet it had seemed too wasteful to dispose of him. That brain still held marvelous things.



The irony of it.

In the end, he had tightened the noose around his own neck.



If he had been free to move, it would have been different. He would never have tolerated treatment like this.

But the reality was that he could not even properly steady his breathing.



Strange noises came from the airship.

Black smoke was rising from all over it.

It had already been near its limit in the battle with the black dragon. It would not have been surprising if Hero Liam's impulsive rampage had finished it off.

The smell of scorched metal stabbed at his nose and clung to the back of his throat. The hull groaned intermittently, as though screaming.



It would not hold much longer.

Gearnote, who possessed a fair degree of knowledge about airships, understood that.



A wall creaked, and part of it broke away.

Through the hole, far out in the sky, he saw the beastfolk soldiers flapping their wings and withdrawing.

Not one of them looked back.



If it was going to be like this, Gearnote should have become a beastfolk already.



Gearnote still had not become one.

The reason was simple. The possibilities were simply too attractive, and he had wavered. There were so many monsters whose traits he wanted. Choosing just one had been difficult.



And after all the trial and error, after advancing the technology, he had wanted to use some truly special beast and gain some truly special result for himself.



This was the outcome.



If he had undergone beastfolk conversion, there was a good chance he would still have had enough mobility left even after taking Liam's blow.

He might have flown away on his own.

He had been too greedy.

To say he had no regrets at all--that made his chest ache, if only a little.



"How vexing..."



Then part of it--no, the airship itself--exploded.



------------------------------------------------------------------------



An impact ran through it as though something had smashed outward from within.

Next came a dull but decisive sound of destruction. Unable to endure it any longer, the airship burst apart from the inside like an egg with its shell broken from within.



Gearnote reflexively scraped together the magic power inside his body.

But there was no way Gearnote's unfamiliar body reinforcement could possibly be enough to withstand this.

Even so, it was far better than nothing. All he could do was cling to the possibility that it might help.



He curled up reflexively and wrapped his arms around himself to shield his neck and abdomen.

If nothing else, he had to protect the vital spots.



The shock of the explosion tore through him.

He somehow survived, but his whole body was burned--

And in the next instant,

out of the corner of his eye he saw armor plating from the airship, pieces of the frame, ripped metal shards and the like, all blasted outward with terrifying force.



They came raining down.

Dull, wet impacts stabbed into his body, tore flesh, and shattered bone. Belly, side, back--there was nowhere to run.



Even the vital areas he had protected were struck by searing pain a moment later.

His body had already suffered fatal wounds.



His footing disappeared.

His body was thrown into the air and began to fall together with the shattered pieces of the ship.



Wind battered his entire body.

A roar like tinnitus spread through his ears, his vision blurred white, and gravity dragged him downward whether he liked it or not.



At the edge of his vision, he saw one shadow.

A being flying through the distance at high speed.



A pair of dragon wings.

A shadow spreading magnificent wings wide and cutting through the sky toward somewhere. And clinging to it, one human.



It seemed Hero Liam and Regingule had survived.



The instant he caught sight of them, Gearnote found himself thinking of something entirely unrelated to escaping this situation.



--Wings really are wonderful. To fly freely through the sky.



Come to think of it, in the beginning he had simply wanted to try flying through the sky on his own wings.

Purely and simply, he had thought it looked like it would feel good.



To take the wind, be freed from gravity, and choose your direction with a single act of will. That kind of simple freedom.



To die by falling--what a tragedy that was. The irony of it was almost perfect.

Those without wings could not return to the sky. Those who could not fly fell.

That was all.



And yet, perhaps because it was the very end, seeing Hero Liam's figure gave him a small sense of satisfaction.



Was it because, at the end, he had managed to create not mere garbage that was strong and nothing more, like synthetic beasts, but the strongest beastfolk of all?

If so, then even if he had taken too many detours, he had still managed to close his life with a certain beauty.

Hero Liam was rampaging at present, but...

Regingule was with him. And Liam too, with the pride of a human being, would recover.

Surely. Let him pray that would be so.



Do your best, humanity. If only you can--the world--



Right before he could finish that wish before dying,

he heard the wet sound of flesh being crushed.
