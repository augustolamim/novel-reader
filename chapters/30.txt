Episode 127: Humans Fighting Humans (3)








The shopkeeper's hands trembled, his lips twitched, and then all at once his face changed into something that looked almost resigned, almost enlightened.


"If words don't work, then immediately violence. More and more roughnecks these days try to solve every last thing with violence... Why has the world become like this..."


"Who knows. But in your case, you brought it on yourself. You hired mercenaries too, didn't you?"


"Don't compare me to them... For me, violence was a threat. A last resort. I was... meant for more..."


He was trying to project some kind of tragic aura, but I still didn't feel even a trace of sympathy. That air of "I'm different from the rest" got on my nerves.


"By the way, you were talking earlier about being a person of substance. Do you think I'm small-minded?"


"Hah...? A man who goes on a wild rampage with that blessed violence of his just to get back one or two objects, and you would claim that isn't a narrow heart?"


...So he really did look down on me from the bottom of his heart.

Pointlessly proud, that was the type.

...Maybe I should try and see if I can break that pride while I'm at it.


"To begin with, what kind of person do you think you are, that someone like you can talk about another man's worth?"


The shopkeeper's eyes flew open.


"What did you say...?"


"You called me nothing but a violent brute. Fine. I'll admit that much. I have no grounds to deny it. That's right, I'm not some great man."


"But--"

I paused there deliberately.


"If the celebrated Hero Liam came and lectured me, then I'd have no choice but to accept it. He has the right to do that, and I could even understand it. But you..."


I took one step forward, onto a floor streaked with wine and mercenary blood.


"Can someone like you really say that to me? A plain con artist? A man who, just like me, thinks only about himself?"


I made sure my tone carried an intentional sneer as I drove the words into him.


"I'm no great man. But neither are you. You're no great man either. The kind of person who talks about someone else's worth is usually exactly the kind whose own worth amounts to nothing. Later, scammer. Don't ever get full of yourself again."


All right.

Let's begin.

...The demolition work on this building.

It had to be flashy enough that even the people of Area 1 would see it clearly.


And while I was at it, I might as well use it as a scarecrow to test out the techniques I'd learned.

Using trees growing nearby as targets didn't make it easy to feel improvement, but if I cut through walls and pillars, maybe I'd get a better sense of the impact.

If anything, it was an ideal practice dummy.




--------------------------------------------------




Standing inside the shop, I settled into the stance for yet another practice swing.

But before that, something floated gently into view.

Lumiela furrowed her brows and spoke in a whisper.

Wondering what it was, I looked over.


Could it be that, from the perspective of a spirit of light, blasting a scammer's shop to pieces was maybe a little too much after all?


'Don't talk yourself down like that.'


'Hm? ...I'm not talking myself down.'


'You haven't done anything bad of your own accord, Brave.'


It was true that I generally tried not to start things with humans first.

My motto was retaliation, not aggression.

Of course, if even retaliating counted as evil, then the whole conversation was over.

And in the end, even my having killed three of the Four Heavenly Kings was only an extension of guilt.

The reason I was going to kill the Demon King from here on was also for Lumiela's sake and my own.


'...Is that really true...?'


'Of course it is! Compared to a scammer like this, you're way more decent! Have some confidence! When you had room to spare, you helped people. You're not a bad person!'


Helping people...

...It was true. When I had the leeway, I had saved people being attacked by demons, pretending I was merely hunting demons for the reward.

But even then, how much of that was because "if I ignore this, my chest might hurt later"? It was questionable how much I actually cared about those people's lives themselves.


I had never wanted great good, nor flashy evil.

Unless something truly serious happened--something threatening what I held dear--I was the kind of person who preferred stability and didn't move unless I had to.


I had done nothing.


And yet...

Lumiela's voice had a rare heat in it, and that sound softly struck some tender place deep inside my chest.


I gave a wry smile and scratched my head.


'Thanks. I appreciate the encouragement.'


She gave a little laugh.

When I started to sink into myself, she pulled me back like this.

Really, this life wasn't something to throw away.

After all, I had met a wonderful companion.


"...All right. Let's clear my head and get started."


Lately, all I'd done was repeat practice swings over and over.

Morning, noon, night--it didn't matter.

Somehow, the feel of the movement had started sinking into my body.

If I closed my eyes, the trajectories I had swung through on all those days floated through my mind like faint afterimages.


Following that memory, I stepped in and swung without hesitation.

The slash split the air with certainty and sliced a straight line through the shop wall.

The shockwave blew the rest apart too, improving the ventilation considerably.


"My shopppp..."


I could hear the shopkeeper's voice somewhere behind me.

Yes. That was a good slash, I decided.

I could feel the repeated practice paying off in that instant.


According to Old Man Rowan, when you got tense, your body stiffened on its own, you put too much unnecessary strength into it, and your trajectory wavered.

The more the muscles hardened, the more sluggish the movement became, robbing you of both sharpness and speed.

That was why overexertion was no good.

Instead, if you relaxed and let the motion flow naturally, your original speed and accuracy came out better.


Completely ridiculous.


Even that flying-slash knockoff from just now already used to produce a sort of sonic-boom wind pressure, so the broad outline itself hadn't changed.

But it felt as if the power for cutting had been compressed, and the quality itself had stepped up a level.


Wait...

This isn't my imagination, right?

If I was just feeling that way because I'd been taught swordsmanship, that would be depressing.


"Here, then, like this..."


One more shot.

This time at another wall of the shop.

Cut!

I loosened the tension in my shoulders just a bit, flexed my wrist, and brought the blade down.

One wall of the shop vanished outward in an explosion.


'You're better than you were before practice!'


'Thanks. Well, before this I was basically starting from nothing...'


Lumiela was praising me, so I was probably improving.


All right.

Riding the momentum, I followed up with a horizontal sweep, a return stroke, a reverse diagonal cut, and a standard diagonal cut in quick succession.

Sharp whistling sounds sliced the air one after another, and mixed in with them came ominous cracking and explosive crashing from the shop itself.


Walls blew away board by board, shelves tore loose from the base, and the merchandise stored on them went flying into the air.


"My shop... my shop... my... my shooop..."


The shopkeeper's spirit was being shaved down nicely too.


At the end, I put a little more force than before into the final strike.

The shop's main beam split in two, the ceiling groaned, and then started collapsing.

And then, for the true finale, one encore slash up at the ceiling.

The scammer's store was completely destroyed.


Amid the scattering wood chips and dust, I quietly lowered my sword.

I felt a little more skilled than before.

The result of my growth had been proven at the expense of the shop.


The wind passed through, moving through the hollow spaces where walls had once stood, brushing emptily along my back.


"Ugh... waaah... my... shopppp!!"


Passersby from Area 1 had gathered around to see what was happening.

A crying scammer.

Mercenaries collapsed in pools of blood.

And me.


I had to make sure people didn't get the wrong idea.

I needed to play up being the victim too.

I did this because they did it first.

This had been a scammer's shop. Therefore the scammers were not to be sympathized with.

That was the story that needed to spread.
