The grave was carefully kept.

I could guess whose it was. In fact, it felt as though it could not possibly belong to anyone else.

If so, it had probably been made more than three hundred years ago. And yet there wasn't even any moss on it. The only explanation for how clean it remained was that Clautia had continued tending it without fail.

I could confirm it just to be sure, but that wasn't the sort of thing you casually asked about, and I doubted she would appreciate it either.



Standing beside the grave, Clautia looked down for a moment, then turned back toward us.



"Before you reach out to the Holy Sword... would you pray at the grave?"



She asked it with a sorrowful face, as though recalling a painful past.

There was no reason to refuse.



"Lumiela, let's pray. I will too."



Lumiela didn't answer. She only gave a small nod.
She probably wasn't in the mood to talk.

Her expression had clouded over. Her lips were pressed tight, and her hands were clasped before her chest.



"...Yes. It's late, but still."



With that, she walked slowly toward the grave.



"...Thank you. If Lumiela pays her respects, he would be glad too."



Clautia said that with a faint smile.



I took my place beside Lumiela, confirmed by feel that the sage was behind us, and removed my mask as a matter of respect.

Standing before the gravestone, words naturally left me. I had no idea what to say. So I simply closed my eyes, bowed my head, and brought my hands together in prayer.



Beside me, I heard a small sniffle. Lumiela was crying.

There were people left behind to grieve, for Lumiela and Clautia too. Thinking that, there was something I couldn't help dwelling on.

But the fact that this world still existed even now instead of having fallen to the Demon King's hand--all of that was thanks to the former hero, whose name had never even been carved into history.

It was the result of the battles he had fought. The result of actions heroic in a way I could never imitate.

If so--



Thank you for your work. ...I won't inherit your will, but I will at least accomplish the one goal of defeating the Demon King.



When I finished praying, I put my mask back on. Lumiela was still praying. She probably had a great many things to say. I would wait.

Then I heard footsteps behind me.

When I turned around, Clautia was still watching Lumiela, who remained in prayer. There was no reason for me to keep standing there after I'd finished, and all it would do was make things awkward.

I quietly stepped back from the grave and went to stand near Clautia.



"...It's nostalgic. Maybe because I've seen Lumiela again, old memories are coming back to me."



Clautia began speaking in a quiet voice, slowly, without taking her eyes off Lumiela.



"It reminds me that he thought of Lumiela almost like a daughter."



Lumiela's shoulders trembled slightly.



"What was Lumiela like back then?"



"When we first met, she was younger and more innocent. She's grown. How nostalgic. When we first met, she didn't even know the value of money and dined and dashed--"



"Stop! We don't need that story!"



Lumiela cut off the tale from the past. She'd probably judged that if this continued, Clautia was going to start bringing up inconvenient old stories.

Though even so, dining and dashing...? Well, she was a spirit, so maybe that sort of thing happened.

I was looking at her from behind, but Lumiela probably wiped at her tears. Still facing the grave, she began to speak in a halting voice.



"I... am grateful, you know."



Her voice was hoarse, but clear.



"I thought of him like a teacher. The Demon King was only an enemy to me, and I didn't care much about humans either, but during that journey a lot of feelings began to grow in me. If that hadn't happened, I might never have formed the relationship I have now with Jane. But..."



Her words broke off.



"There's one thing I still can't accept. Why is it that the result of acting for the sake of others had to become... something so irrational? Clautia, what do you think? ...Just because they're human, does that mean we have to save all of them unconditionally?"



Lumiela threw her question at Clautia.
After a while of silence, Clautia answered, "...I'm frustrated too. But even knowing that, we chose to fight the Demon King. All of us except, probably, you."



"I don't understand."



"...I see."



"If they're enemies, then you have to defeat them... because that's obvious, isn't it? Sniff..."

With tears in her eyes, Lumiela glared sharply at Clautia.

The grave said nothing back. It only quietly remained there, proof that the past had once existed.



I bowed my head to the grave one more time.

Still looking at the grave, Lumiela murmured softly.



"...You know, I chose not to save a great many people, but to follow just one person instead. ...Would you be angry with me?"



There was no attempt to hide the anxiety in her voice. It was probably meant as a soliloquy to the grave. I couldn't say anything.

But there was someone here who could answer--Clautia, who knew the former hero too.

"He might make a slightly troubled face. But he wouldn't force the right choice on anyone. He was that kind of person."



In the original story he only appeared a little in backstory, so I barely knew him, but even so, it was clear that he really had been a heroic sort of man.

He had been a good person. There was no doubt.

And that was exactly why--
he died.



Humanity as a species was not something to trust out of goodwill. Trust had to be narrowed down to only a part of it.

Otherwise, you would simply be used.

Especially the privileged class, the nobles. Those people were professionals at probing for weakness in each other and dragging others down.

They pulled at each other's legs and seized profit. Using one another in negotiation was still one thing, but human goodwill was probably just bait to them.

Righteousness would be eaten clean away, leaving nothing but bones.

If so, then what you needed after all was malice. The resolve to sink your fangs into a throat before you were devoured yourself.



So that was what I would do now. With my malice. With force.



...It seemed Lumiela and Clautia had finished their conversation, and the grave visit as well.

So it was about time. Miserable. No, more than that, I was gradually starting to get scared.



"So, the thing next to the grave really is the Holy Sword, right?"



I asked it even though I already knew perfectly well.

With where Clautia had been standing and with the matter of me knowing things I shouldn't, it was obvious she'd realized something. But since she hadn't pressed me on it, I would at least pretend.

"Yes, that's right.

...I should tell you what I know about the Holy Sword. There is no other sword in the world that can compare to the power of the Holy Sword Idea."



"I see," I said, nodding.

Clautia paused there for a beat, then closed her eyes as though searching back through memory.

"When you touch the sword, a trial begins... or so I have heard. Your consciousness is drawn in, and you are invited into an illusory world. I have never seen it myself; I've only been told. And apparently... the owner's 'desire'... or rather their personality... becomes reflected directly in the form of the ability it grants."



"Brave... I was already contracted before the trial finished, so I never experienced it... I'm sorry."

"No, it's fine. I'll manage."

"...And the trial itself?"



I asked for form's sake.

Clautia frowned and answered with some difficulty.



"You must overcome the cause of death in the life of the former owner, or else the most painful past they experienced... which means--"



"...!"



In that instant, Lumiela's entire atmosphere turned dark and sharp.
