The Empire's Operation (2)







"At last, we have reached the stage where we will directly face the Four Heavenly Kings. From here onward, a single mistaken move could prove fatal."


General Ubel said that while staring sharply at the city of Aibelk in the mirror.

His concern was probably fixed on only one thing:

the struggle against the Four Heavenly Kings.


Hmm...

Gearnote let out a quiet hum.

There was one thing he wanted to ask.

His curiosity was itching.

It was true that the synthetic beasts had gone too far, far enough that he could never bring himself to like them.

But their strength was real.

After all, they had been created for the express purpose of killing the Four Heavenly Kings.

Synthetic Beast "Salvation System"■"Patchwork God-Machine" -- [Phase 3].

Reconstructed using one of the Seven Great Magical Beasts as the base, it had gone beyond anything so simple as merely "strong."

And its flesh, bones, everything within it was all tangled together.

Perhaps because of the traces of human material mixed into it, it let out cries that faintly resembled human speech, but there was no meaning at all in the words.

That was no beast.

It had surpassed even the Seven Great Magical Beasts--

a true monster.


Then which was stronger, the Four Heavenly Kings or Phase 3, Beelzebub the Balefly?

What interested Gearnote was not his own opinion, as someone who did not specialize in combat, but how General Ubel saw it.


"Fufu, I can assure you of this: no one could deny the sheer rank of Phase 3's existence."


With exaggerated delight, Gearnote wagged a finger and smiled.


"After all, that thing was based on Beelzebub the Balefly himself, then fused with the Kingdom-born S-rank adventurer who kept causing trouble, along with specially selected Imperial criminals of the very worst sort pulled from prison. It has enough power to bring down an entire nation...! Even if the Four Heavenly Kings are beings who destroyed the imperial capital itself, wouldn't it be fair to say that, in terms of power alone, they stand on equal footing?"


In the silent room, only his words echoed strangely loud.

After a moment of silence, General Ubel folded his arms and gave a slow nod.


"It is certainly true that a single Phase 3 specimen is capable of bringing down a nation. Those things are indeed the Empire's trump cards."


At that assessment, Gearnote involuntarily shrugged.

General Ubel clearly acknowledged the overwhelming power of Phase 3, yet there was something reserved in the wording.


"However, the Four Heavenly Kings are also unquestionably mighty beings."


General Ubel's voice cut in low.

It was the kind of tone that drove in a nail and left no room for interruption.

His eyes pinned Gearnote in place.


"Then the advantage still lies with the Four Heavenly Kings--"


"No, not that either. The Four Heavenly Kings possess the power to destroy nations. They stand beyond the limits of human reason. But even the Four Heavenly Kings are still living creatures in the end. They have bodies, and they have limits. That does not make them invincible."


"I see."


General Ubel's mouth twisted faintly.

The smile resembled the joy of a man standing before war.

After a pause, Gearnote opened his mouth again.


"Then what exactly does that mean?"


For an instant, an awkward silence fell over the room.

After a moment, General Ubel looked straight at him and answered briefly.


"It means both are strong."


"...I see. So we won't know until we make them fight."




--------------------------------------------------




For the final conclusion to be merely "both are strong" was rather unsatisfying.

General Ubel added, "And besides--"

There was no fear in his voice. To Gearnote, it sounded like the resolve of a soldier who had fixed his gaze on the enemy he must defeat.


"We will find out soon enough. Though of course, even that result would not settle everything in simple terms. To begin with, Phase 3 based on Beelzebub the Balefly was created first and foremost for the purpose of exterminating the demons within the Empire. Comparing it directly to the strongest Four Heavenly King purely in terms of combat strength misses the point."


"Is that so? Well, the other Seven Great Magical Beasts are more combat-specialized than Beelzebub, so perhaps that's true. In any case, I heard that Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze has already returned to the Empire, so it seems a clash is finally about to happen...!"


It had been about a week ago that reports began spreading that a giant shadow had flown from the Kingdom toward the Empire.


A black dragon spreading jet-black wings and tearing through the sky.

Gearnote had understood by instinct what it was, but after describing the features to Cain just in case, he learned that it was the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings, Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze, and his beloved dragon, Linyuvurm.

After hearing Cain's information, he had prudently double-checked it through a captured demon general who had been modified so as to be incapable of resistance.

A black knight astride a black dragon--

there was almost no doubt that such a figure was one of the Four Heavenly Kings.

No one knew whether Cain's knowledge came from the future or from somewhere else, but it was undeniable that he really could somehow see things.

They had tried forcing the matter out of him before, but he rambled only about nonsense: reincarnation, past lives, how this world was inside a book. The ravings of a complete lunatic--

though admittedly that was usual for him.

It sounded like the talk of a man high on drugs, yet his information kept proving correct, so they had postponed trying to understand it. For now, he was simply a useful source of intelligence.

Someday, Gearnote looked forward to dissecting him.


His thoughts had strayed.

That Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze had already returned to the Empire. It had happened only the other day.

And then everything collapsed with an audible crash.

It had taken only about an hour.

The synthetic beast corps deployed on the border between the Empire and the Kingdom had been annihilated in the blink of an eye, and ninety percent of the ten thousand troops stationed nearby had been wiped out.

All that remained was a blackened wasteland and mountains of corpses.

It was said that the faces of the few soldiers who returned were etched with a frozen terror beyond words.


At Gearnote's words, General Ubel frowned and answered with a sigh.


"No, not yet. So long as we have only a single completed Phase 3 specimen, battle must be avoided. However, if we leave that strongest Four Heavenly King alone, the front itself will collapse. That is why we need to buy time."


"I see. So Beelzebub the Balefly will be used to hold him back. But come to think of it, shouldn't there be another Four Heavenly King in the Empire as well? Yes--Kaeruleum the Luminous."


"No, that one appears to be missing. We interrogated the captured demons thoroughly. Even after torture, even after burning through their nerves one by one, all we got was the same answer. Who knows where he went. Perhaps he returned to the Schrecklich Continent...?"


"That is rather fortunate, then. But how exactly do you intend to use Phase 3 to hold back Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze?"


Without a word, General Ubel handed Gearnote a single document.

Fine diagrams and a few lines of annotation were arranged over faintly yellowed parchment. Gearnote took it and quickly scanned it.

It was the framework of the operation.


The report was written in an unadorned tone.

First: the synthetic beast based on Beelzebub the Balefly had been completed, and the demons had been almost entirely exterminated. There were now scarcely any demons left capable of operating within Imperial territory.

Apparently the demons within the Empire had almost all been wiped out already. The slaughter at the city of Aibelk just now had likely been the hunting down of the few remaining demons. General Ubel's invitation--that it was a chance Gearnote would never see again--had been entirely true.

However, this operation...


"...Hmm. So you mean to use the vast numbers of child flies, an unforeseen enhancement produced as a by-product of Phase 3 the Balefly, to take demon generals and below hostage?"


When Gearnote confirmed it, General Ubel gave a short nod.


"Exactly. We let a few survive on purpose and drive those small numbers of demons onward. Then we guide them with the child flies to the fortress city of Retle, surround them there, and seal them in."


Turning the page, Gearnote found an attached report.

At first they had considered repurposing Beelzebub's power for an assault on the demons' homeland--the Schrecklich Continent itself--but had concluded that they had no room to spare for that, leading to the present plan instead.


What was written there was a strategic theory built around the psychology of Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze.

Specialists had examined and cross-checked the fragments extracted from Cain, profiling the target's tastes and behavioral tendencies, and the operation was designed on the basis of that result.


The conclusion was simple.

First, lure Rubrum Nox to the fortress city of Retle.

Meanwhile, the main body of Phase 3 the Balefly would be kept far away, up in the clouds if necessary, while the child swarm would disperse and shift into a distant surveillance formation.

If Rubrum Nox tried to force his way toward the main body, they would pressure him with the threat that Retle would be seized in that opening.

The demons could be used as hostages.

In essence, by combining controlled movement with localized assault, they aimed to take strategic advantage while minimizing damage.

Because Rubrum Nox could not fly on his own, he could not split up with the black dragon if he wished to strike down the Balefly high above. That meant separate movement by the two could be sealed off as an option.


According to the assessment of the specialists and Cain, Rubrum Nox had a comparatively serious temperament, and he also viewed the demon generals as "subordinates" entrusted to him by the Demon King.

Therefore, the odds were low that he would discard them immediately. Taking that into account, the operation had been designed to focus his attention step by step and constrain his actions.


As a concrete measure, they would allow the rough location and movement patterns of the Balefly to be estimated in stages, thereby guiding the lower demons to think that, given enough time, they could kill the flies and save themselves. In other words, they would preserve morale and make the Four Heavenly King stay by letting them cling to hope--not by driving them into despair until they stopped moving.


The greatest uncertainty was the whereabouts of Kaeruleum the Luminous.

If he moved, the operation would collapse in an instant.

But up to now, sightings of him had vanished completely, and although attacks had been made on major demon outposts, there had been no sign of a crushing counterattack from a Four Heavenly King-class being.

If things went well, this really might buy them time.


"Excuse me!"


At that moment, a breathless soldier came rushing in.

When the soldier whispered into General Ubel's ear, the general stood up before he could stop himself.


"So that calamity of a princess, Retzel... is alive?"


At the soldier's words, Gearnote was confused.

Cain had said she had been killed after a Four Heavenly King turned the tables on her--

but in truth the princess was alive, and according to spies, she had even slain Viridis of the Gale, one of the Four Heavenly Kings on the Kingdom side.

Furthermore, another of the Four Heavenly Kings, Plurus of Cragmass, whose mountain-like giant body had made sightings frequent simply by virtue of its size, had apparently vanished as well.

Perhaps Princess Retzel had killed that one too without saying a word to the Kingdom. She was that kind of woman. She paid no heed to her country at all.

Meanwhile, it seemed that with a new ace called Hero Liam on the Kingdom side, the war against the Demon King's army was turning in humanity's favor.


At that report, the atmosphere in the room changed in an instant, turning buoyant.

The survival of an unexpected military asset, and the confirmed defeat of one of the Four Heavenly Kings, were pieces of information that shook the assumptions underlying their entire plan.

And incidentally, Gearnote rather hoped Plurus of Cragmass had been killed as well.


General Ubel returned his gaze to the mirror showing the scene and burst into laughter.


"Hah hah hah! Good. Then we revise the operation. When the time comes for the decisive battle, the Empire will propose cooperation with the Kingdom. We will request Princess Retzel--and Hero Liam as well, while we're at it--as reinforcements, and strike simultaneously with a united Imperial-Kingdom force. Since the strongest Four Heavenly King is involved, the Kingdom should accept if they value certainty."


The legendary magical beasts--

the Seven Great Magical Beasts.

Three of them.

Dromugant the Shellfiend, whose hardest body offers absolute defense.

Neclus the Hellslime, made entirely of mucus that melts all things.

And Digitalis the Wolfblight, whose ultimate poison kills with a single touch.


The arrangements to release the seals on those three had already been completed.

All that remained was to kill them and remodel them into Synthetic Beast "Salvation System"■"Patchwork God-Machine" [Phase 3].


One of the Four Heavenly Kings was gone, leaving three.

Two were missing and might still return to the battlefield, but if the princess and the Kingdom's forces handled one of them, then the Empire's strongest beastman fighters led by General Ubel--fighters capable of hunting the Seven Great Magical Beasts before synthetic conversion--together with three Phase 3 synthetic beasts would dispose of the remaining two Four Heavenly Kings.

That count assumed the worst-case possibility that the Balefly used for stalling would be defeated.


By that calculation, the difference in military strength was starting to look unexpectedly manageable.

Gearnote felt as though the road to humanity's victory had suddenly opened wide.
