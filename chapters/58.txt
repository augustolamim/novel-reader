Lumiela was a little tired.

Being able to understand memories and the feelings attached to them was nice, but she had learned there were problems with it too.

She had been confident in her processing ability.

Even so, this time she had thought she might overload. She had sorted things, trimmed away what was unnecessary, and even then the flood of memories and feelings had kept pouring in as though testing her limits.

One thing was certain: if she sent things back to Brave in this state, it might cause serious trouble.

If Lumiela was going to send something from her side, she would have to be much more careful about that. She would have to keep the volume down.

Quietly warning herself of that, she steadied her breath deep inside.



Lumiela opened her eyes and gently took Brave's left hand, where he stood beside her.

She laced their fingers together and squeezed tightly. When she put a little force into it as if confirming he was really there, Brave looked at her in slight surprise. Meeting that gaze, Lumiela murmured, "I'm back."



"Welcome back, Lumiela. So... what are we going to do? I've made up my mind too."



She looked straight back into the eyes where Brave's resolve lay hidden.

With a single choice from Lumiela, this place would likely be dyed in blood.



Brave's hand was warm. He was alive. He was here now.

This world was full of nothing but Brave's enemies.

In the truest sense, Lumiela might be the only ally Brave had who existed for his sake alone. Even Mr. Greg had those two lackey-like followers besides Brave.



Lumiela wanted to become an ally for Brave alone.

That came first.

Precisely because of that--



Her gaze swept the area.

What she saw were the soldiers outside the barrier. Perhaps they had found that nothing they did could break it, because they stood there with weapons raised, staring this way with fear and hostility mixed together.

Taking care of them would not be wrong. But there was no need to slaughter them.

There was almost no losing line here.

There were any number of ways to do this.

The previous hero would not have wanted revenge either, and there was no reason to carry out a massacre here. And if it happened, it would be by Lumiela's own will.



A single decision.

This was not even revenge.

The real enemies were already in their coffins while Lumiela had been sealed away. Only feelings that had lost the place they should have been aimed at still smoldered, searching for an outlet. She had only been about to vent them on lookalike sacks of meat.



Once more, she drew in a deep breath.



"I won't kill them."



The original story offered a model, but following it would mean aiming for a heroic happy ending.

Reconciliation, understanding, salvation--none of that was needed here.

What she would choose was Brave's way.



"That said--"



"That said?"



"I'm going to beat them to a pulp!"



Brave blinked.

She would hurt them.

But she would not kill them.



That did not mean she had forgiven them.

Even if they were only illusions, the beings here were far too real. If she killed them, the sensation and weight of it would certainly remain inside Brave. It would carve yet another wound into his heart, one that could never be wiped away. That alone, she absolutely did not want.



After all, they still had a long way ahead of them.

The road would be difficult too. If a trap could be avoided, then avoiding it was simply good risk management.



So she would not do it.

She would make a coolheaded, practical judgment.



--Goodbye, XXXX. My longing.



In her heart, she offered the previous hero a single apology.

To Lumiela, he had been... yes, something close to a schoolteacher.

The sort of person who knew the answer when she was troubled, who would scold her when she got something wrong because her senses as a spirit were different.

If she put it in terms from Brave's memories, he had been like absolute justice Anp*nman.

That was why Lumiela had looked up to him like a child. Even though she knew the world was cruel, as long as he was there, some part of her must have believed things would be all right.



But that had not happened.

What had awaited them was a bad ending in which righteousness went unrewarded, kindness was exploited for convenience, and the hero quietly vanished from both name and history.



That bad ending had been unbearable for Lumiela.

Quietly, she bid it farewell within her heart.

Ideals are beautiful. Precisely because they are beautiful, someday one has to draw a line under them. To live in the present. To let go of the past.

She had to plant her feet on the ground and move forward.



She took one step forward.

Physically and mentally, it was a resolve to move ahead.

She tugged Brave's hand and drew him with her, forward.



------------------------------------------------------------------------



Brave blinked just once, as if asking, Are you really sure?

When Lumiela gave him a firm nod,

he smiled as if he understood at once. His hand gently squeezed back in answer.



"...In exchange, though! We're going to hurt them just short of killing them! You'd better do your part properly too, Brave!"



She shouted from her belly.



"Yeah! I'll break every bone in their bodies! I'll swell their faces up till they aren't recognizable anymore!"



"Heh, good. Then I'll handle their spines with elegance."



"............ ...Wouldn't that kill them?"



Manipulating the barrier, Lumiela snatched just one soldier inside in an instant--a high-ranking-looking man in gaudy armor who had been slamming his weapon against it again and again.



The gaudy soldier came charging at Brave, yelling, "I've taken your head!"



"You're loud."



Naturally, there was no way Brave would lose to someone like that.



"--My aaaaarms...?!"



He snapped both the man's arms, then immediately swept his legs. Judging by how the soldier skidded across the ground, both of those must have broken too.

Then Brave grabbed the soldier by the neck with one hand, turned him toward Lumiela, and said, "Let's use this one to warm up. We'll do some sparring."



Feeling like a boxer, she recreated one from Brave's memories, raised her fists, and bounced forward in little steps toward the restrained soldier.

The soldier went pale.



"Phew... hey! Hey!"



It had been since that thief woman.

Thump, thump, thud, thud.



"Bweh!!"



"Ohhh. Lumiela, you could become a heavyweight boxer. You might take the world title!"

Brave sounded like he was having fun too.

Then the soldiers on the outside started shouting even more loudly, but Lumiela was too busy punching to care.

Punch, punch, punch, punch, punch, kick.



Kick?

--She had kicked him without meaning to.

Let it count as some kickboxing having gotten mixed in along with the regular boxing.

"Eeeeek. Th-the cracking and crunching sounds are awful...!"

"Captain!!"

"No way... the places she's hitting are caving in... please stop already!"



Some moderate exercise had left her nicely refreshed.

She felt wonderfully clear and bright.
