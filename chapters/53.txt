Rubrum Nox's Side

He ought to have broken bones and inflicted damage on the woman, and yet there was no agony on her face. It was probably not forced endurance. Her cheerful footsteps alone told the story.

"If you lacked resistance to flame, what would have become of you? Ahh, this is excellent."

Because she was speaking in a space choked with poison, black blood was flowing from her nose and eyes.

Even so, her steps were light, as though she were strolling through the battlefield.

The blue-black blade slowly rose. Seeing her smile within the flames, Rubrum Nox even thought she might possess a sensibility somewhat close to his own.

A creature that loved life-and-death combat beyond help.

The woman smiled. Their eyes met, and in a single glance he knew she was insane. A clear madness, as though she had been built from the beginning to love the hell called battle.

Since she had gone to the trouble of speaking to him, it would be crude not to answer.

"Human--are you alone?"

From inhaling the poison, blood was now flowing from Rubrum Nox's eyes as well.

"Of course. I came here to fight alone. But rest easy. I'm not reinforcements."

"...What do you mean by that? To begin with, I do not care how many come. If they are going to come, I would only ask them not to hold back and to come all at once."

The more they spoke, the more both of them were polluted by the poison, discolored blood spilling from each of them. The human woman had said she was not reinforcements, but what was she getting at?

As he watched her, she suddenly stretched out a hand to the side and attacked the poison wolf with blue flames.

The woman should have been an ally of the monsters, part of the human side's forces. Yet that silver-haired woman was targeting everyone.

He did not understand at all what she was doing.

"Why did you attack it? Is it not your ally?"

The silver-haired woman gave an evil grin.

"Not just one. I want them all. The synthetic beasts too, the Four Heavenly Kings too--I'll attack them all. Heh, friend and foe don't matter."

That was a declaration of war. Words that deliberately stepped over the boundary between ally and enemy.

She had no sense whatsoever of where to stop.

He had thought she might be similar to him, but he took that thought back. Even Rubrum Nox had more restraint than this.

"A greedy one, are you. I can't say I don't understand the feeling. But know this: conceit invites death."

Even so, he thought, that thing she had called them--synthetic beasts. She had probably meant the ogres and the like, and it was an apt name. Creatures mixed with humans and who knew what else. A fitting term.

Shrugging her shoulders, the silver-haired woman laughed lightly.

"Death? Why should that matter? A trivial... heh, a trivial problem."

Blue flames burst from inside the enemy woman's body, then spread to cover her whole frame like an outer shell. A distorted grinding of bone and flesh rasped out as parts that should have been broken were forcibly dragged back into place.

Even within the domain of flames, the woman's body was being burned by the black fire. Her skin was charred, her flesh scorched.

And yet every time the flames pulsed, her wounds sealed at once and her shape was restored. Regeneration that denied death itself in exchange for pain.

Wrapped in blue fire, the swordswoman still kept her blade raised and stared straight at him.

Two synthetic beasts, and one regenerating human.

Things were getting interesting. Even so, this had been supposed to be a fight against humans, and instead every opponent before him was some bizarre thing or other. He was no longer sure what he was even fighting.

Still cloaked in blue flames, the woman reached toward the more ominous of the two swords at her waist.

The instant her hand touched the hilt, the air creaked.

"--Demonic Sword Tyrfing."

The moment the woman spoke its name, the demonic sword answered. The blade itself seemed to pulse like a living creature.

A golden hilt shining with a sinister light.

The gemstone embedded in the blade throbbed like a heart, giving off an eerie glow.

The enemy woman--
without a moment's hesitation, drove that demonic sword into her own chest.

The moment the blade sank in, veins stood out across her body, and the sound of a heavy throb echoed out.

The swordswoman's breathing changed.

Shallow, quick--
and in the next moment, unnaturally steady.

The woman lifted the corner of her mouth. The demonic sword might have been pushing outward from within, widening the limits of the vessel called a human being.

Her presence swelled.

What until moments ago had still barely been recognizable as human now transformed into a force capable of shaking the balance of the battlefield.

That was a clear opening. Rubrum Nox had merely watched because it looked interesting--
but then--

"■■■■■■------!!"

A roar.

And after it, the ogre that should have gone down under the flames was suddenly standing where the woman had been, as though they had switched places.

Only then did the shockwave arrive. The woman's body had already been struck and sent flying into the air.

She took the impact properly, then stood right back up with a soft, amused laugh.

Whether from the impact or from being crushed from within, her left eyeball had burst.

In the next instant, blue flames flared up.

In less than a second, blue fire spilling from the depths of the eye socket wrapped itself around the destroyed tissue as if tracing it, and regenerated it.

It felt as though her regeneration had become faster than before she used the demonic sword.

A line ran across the ogre's hard skin.

The woman had cut it as they passed one another, in the instant she was being blown away. Movements optimized purely for cutting.

No waste. Astonishing skill.

She was exactly the sort of existence one would call a near-immortal technical magic swordswoman.

The synthetic beasts roared. The human laughed. Black flames split apart, and poison, blue fire, and black fire all mingled together.

It was sheer chaos.

A melee so lawless it was as though the distinction between ally and enemy had never existed to begin with.

The woman swordsman casually picked fights with the synthetic beasts, slashed at Rubrum Nox as well, and rampaged without restraint.

Rubrum Nox's body felt somewhat heavy. Wounds and poison had piled up and were unquestionably taking effect. There had been moments that even sent a chill through him.

There were strong enemies here.

It was rare, even in his life as a demon, for him to be driven this far into a corner.

And to his own surprise, his heart was dancing.

Magnificent.

A certainty burst in the depths of his chest.

This was what battle ought to be.

He wanted no safety, no leeway, no calculated victory. Skin scorching, nerves screaming, death guaranteed if he made even the smallest mistake in judgment--that taut sensation filled his entire body with a burning thrill.

Anything less was meaningless.

This was not for survival. It was for becoming stronger.

Trading blows, grinding one another down, stepping forward even after crossing the limit. There were things that could be gained only at the far end of that.

Time to test himself, shatter himself, rebuild himself. A realm impossible to reach without someone to crash against.

He wanted to transcend.

Not simply win. He wanted to step over fear, limits, even absurdity itself, and surpass them as though crushing existence whole.

Everything--his enemies, fate, even the self he had been yesterday.

He wanted to become the strongest.

The reason was simple. Any man dreamed of it at least once: a state where he bowed to no one, was bound by nothing, and stood on strength alone. He did not think the pain and sacrifice required to get there were too high a price.

That was why a smile rose to his face.

He could feel it with certainty. This very moment, with murderous intent leaving no avenue of escape, pressure that made his bones creak, and death beside him with every breath, was the greatest furnace in which to temper himself.

He would live through today and fight again tomorrow.

Those who fell vanished without even leaving their names. Only those who survived earned the right to stand upon the next battlefield. Turning defeat into fuel, turning wounds into strength, he would move forward again and again.

And someday--

At the far end of all those accumulated death-lines, he would stand in a place from which even the Demon King could be looked down upon. He would go on surpassing all things until the instant he himself became the strongest.

Isn't that right, Linyuvurm?

He cast that thought toward the partner who must even now still be fighting.

His body needed no reason beyond that.
