Linyuvurm the Black Dragon's Side

The sky is a dragon's domain.

That was an undeniable fact, an unshakable law. Against creatures without wings, simply flying imposed an overwhelming disadvantage.

Altitude became a weapon, and long-range magical attacks the only effective means of striking back.

Wings were the privilege of freedom.

And with that freedom, Linyuvurm inflicted constraint upon others. Exceptions like Viridis, the Wind Four Heavenly King, existed, but against any enemy, it was safe to assume he would first seize control of the skies.

Linyuvurm flew on, scattering the clouds.

Whenever he thrust his wings into the thick-hanging sea of cloud, the white masses split apart without resistance, tore away, and spiraled behind him. Damp chill stroked his scales, and updrafts writhed beneath his belly.

He could read every last one of them like words. Hold your altitude here. Accelerate here. Turn here. His instincts felt as though the sky itself were whispering to him.

The thickness of the clouds, the flow of the wind, the habits of the rising currents. All of it was the language of those with wings, and Linyuvurm had been reading it for roughly a hundred years.

He did not avoid clouds. He bent them to his will, used them as footholds, and advanced by tearing them apart.

"Where did it go?!"

For Linyuvurm, battle normally meant looking down on enemies on the ground from high above.

But this time, the enemy was not on the ground. It was in the sky. Even now it was flying while hiding among the clouds.

A human. A human was flying. Riding some incomprehensible ship-like thing.

The crawling race of the earth acting as though it understood the heavens was, in Linyuvurm's eyes, nothing but a laughable dream. Do not grow so conceited, he thought in irritation.

And yet.

Even so, on this day the sky carried a strange sound.

A thunderous roar like a regular heartbeat. An unnatural vibration, slicing through the wind.

What burst out through the clouds was a colossal body of steel: a flying ship.

"...Tch."

He was already tired of human-made toys.

Those human weapons they called tanks, with iron-reinforced bodies, wheels, and drivers at the front making two horses pull them along, had been nothing but ponderous and fragile.

So how had they suddenly come to fly? He had never seen such a thing before.

Rubrum Nox had begun battling the three disgusting monsters, and that battlefield was too much for Linyuvurm alone. Judging by ability alone, all three monsters had reached Four Heavenly King class.

While he was considering supporting from a distance with breath attacks, the flying ship had appeared.

He could not allow them to interfere with Rubrum Nox's battle. Linyuvurm had decided to separate from his partner in order to destroy this vessel.

He beat his wings to their limit and raised his speed.

This was a dragon's full power. He closed in on the flying ship all at once, but before he could reach it, panels on the side of the hull opened and a mistlike haze spread over it. It was using the fog to obstruct his vision and blur its position while trying to shake him off.

He drew in breath. He would finish it before it could hide again.

His chest filled with searing heat, and he loosed his breath--
only for it to be canceled out.

A human holding a spear swung it, and the breath was split cleanly in two, then crushed beneath cold.

What remained of the flames was caught by some tribal warrior carrying a giant axe, who smothered them with a fist burning hot.

Linyuvurm's sure-kill breath was "dealt with" before it ever reached the ship.

He had already tried several times to shoot it down with breath attacks, but none had worked. It seemed the ship was carrying quite a formidable force.

Then close combat it was. Linyuvurm closed the gap in an instant, spun his huge body, and swept out with his tail.

But a shock ran through him, and he was thrown back.

The tail had been stopped head-on by the spear of the ice-wielding lancer.

A contest of strength--or so he thought, when suddenly the direction of force was thrown awry, and his posture in midair was broken.

A figure in strange clothing--one of those beings called researchers, if he remembered right--grinned at him from the rear while barking orders, and disgusting soldiers with beastlike traits pressed the assault with magic and weapons.

A command rang out.

"Fire!"

Successive blasts tore through the sky. What erupted from the muzzles was not only explosives, but giant harpoons of grotesque shape as well.

He could not evade all of them, and several harpoons drove into Linyuvurm's scales. Even those scales, proud of their overwhelming hardness, cracked slightly with an ugly sound.

It was a flesh wound. Nothing more.

"It didn't work... no, what is this...?!"

His muscles went numb, and his wings stopped obeying him. He could no longer put proper force into them.

"Good! The poison from Digitalis the Wolfblight that we coated them with is working!"

"That should keep him from moving!"

The triumphant voices of soldiers certain of victory rang through the sky.

So it was poison. Even so, Linyuvurm had no intention of ending here over something this minor.

Feeling the abnormalities in his body, he ground his teeth.

Even the soldiers moved too well to call mere grunts. The ship was packed with fighters stronger than demon generals. It seemed to be loaded with special equipment as well.

And above all--

There was the figure standing on the deck. The ice lancer held his spear and stood without the slightest fidget, watching him.

There was no fear in those eyes.

Those were the eyes of someone who truly intended to kill him.

"Spearman... name yourself."

The lancer looked slightly surprised that Linyuvurm had spoken, then gave his name.

"Liam."

"I'll remember it. Until I've killed you!!"

Linyuvurm roared and rallied himself.

He changed altitude and banked. First he would circle around to the opposite side of the ship, destroy it, and then cook them at his leisure.

"--Ngh!"

That had been the plan, but the human Liam reacted at once.

The air froze.

Linyuvurm folded his wings and dove. Ice grazed his back, and scales shattered apart.

His blood froze in midair.

This was neither chance nor luck.

That man, Liam, was reading Linyuvurm's speed and moving accordingly.

Before anger even came, a chill ran through him.

The airship moved.

Human soldiers deployed along the deck's edge, and magical sights locked onto him all at once.

Even when he tried to bank, frigid air spread out to block his escape route.

He lashed out in anger with flame, but the blazing heat he spat out with a roar clashed head-on with the cold and burst apart in the air. Deadlock--almost even. The shock of the cancellation made even the depths of his scales tremble.

"Regingule!!"

Liam shouted someone's name.

In the next instant, a wild-looking warrior leapt into the sky.

His whole body boiled with flame, glowing red, converting fire itself into propulsion as he rushed in.

The axe came in fast and heavy. Even so--it was still within the bounds of what Linyuvurm could accept as a threat.

He traded blows with the warrior called Regingule. He knocked the axe aside with a claw and swept with his tail. Linyuvurm took some wounds of his own, but he sent the man flying. It was an even exchange.

Even while being blown away, Regingule twisted his body in midair, killed the impact, steadied himself, and returned to the airship as though nothing had happened.

The sky felt narrow. Never had Linyuvurm imagined that a dragon could be pressured by humans in the air.

After that aerial battle, damage steadily piled up on his body. He had worn the humans down as well, but the opening came from the fatigue mounting even more heavily on Linyuvurm himself, and Liam made his move.

The instant Linyuvurm caught the sign of it at the edge of his vision, it was already too late. Realizing he could not evade it, he used his tail as a shield.

"Gh... guah..."

Pain flooded in.

Along with ice, his tail began to break apart. This was no dull impact. It was destruction as though the inside had been denied and its very form undone. The cold sank its teeth into his nerves, severing sensation.

His tail shattered in the sky and scattered like powder.

Even when he tried to move it, no response came. Only then did Linyuvurm fully understand that his tail was gone.

"...You bastard...! How dare you... my tail!!"

"Not just your tail. I have things I need to do too. Next time, I won't stop at your tail...!"

Linyuvurm roared and loosed a full-power blaze. The same flames that had burned cities and wiped them out were unleashed without hesitation.

But the flames froze.

No--they were frozen.

Using that opening, he kicked off the air and retreated for the moment.

Now it was his turn to hide in the clouds.

Protecting what remained of his shattered tail, he sank into the white sea of clouds while using distance to evade the pursuing ice.

Linyuvurm understood.

The sheer potential of the human Liam might already surpass his own.

If that human, alive for barely twenty years or so, was already trying to overtake a dragon who had lived for more than a century, then that was what it meant.

He was still only a bud. And yet he was unquestionably nearing a realm beyond Linyuvurm's own--the realm of the Four Heavenly Kings.

Someday, that blade might even reach a realm beyond the Four Heavenly Kings.

Linyuvurm could feel that growth happening even in the course of this battle.

He had to win. His pride would never permit him to lose to a mere human who had not even lived twenty years.
