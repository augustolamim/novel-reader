Human-Faced Fly Parade (3)







Safe place--

never before had those words sunk so deeply into Valmar's chest.

Looking up at the walls he had at last reached after that long flight, relief welled up from the depths of him.

His breath was ragged, and his body had long since gone beyond its limit, but his heart alone felt lighter.


Then suddenly a chill ran through his spine.

Standing there was one of the Four Heavenly Kings.

The symbol of power.

Terrifying, yes--

but dependable.

The instant that presence entered his field of vision, it felt as though all the tension in his body might finally come loose.

That monster would kill the human-faced flies if they came close--

and that thought alone seemed to clear the fog from the world.


At length, the gate opened, and demons emerged from within.

They waved, beckoning the refugees toward them.


"Just a little farther!"


That voice held together hearts that seemed ready to snap.

But behind them came a vast buzzing sound.

When Valmar turned, he saw a mass of human-faced flies surging toward them. More than two hundred. Packed together like a black cloud, they skated along the ground in pursuit.

It was terror itself.

Only moments ago, the sight of that shadow alone would have driven him to his knees.

But now it was different.


He was not afraid.

Not at all.

One of the Four Heavenly Kings was here.

That alone lit a fire in his chest.

Facing forward, he ran for the gate.


Bzzzzzzzzzzzzz...


Valmar saw Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze casually raise one arm.


And then--


Bzz... n... ... ... ...


Before he realized it, the buzzing was gone.


There was no longer any reason to run.

When he turned around, ashes were drifting through the air.

The swarm of human-faced flies had never even touched him; they had simply scattered into ash and vanished into the sky.

As if they had never existed at all.

He could do nothing but gasp at the sight.


The only thing left behind was the fact that he had been saved.

But--

for some reason, his chest hurt.

His head hurt too.

Was it only that the relief had let all the exhaustion come crashing back?

The pain felt like a strange heat spreading through him from the inside.

His breathing went ragged.

Something deep in his chest was restless, and his pulse ran far too fast.

All that remained was a growing sense that something inside him was wrong.




--------------------------------------------------




"Ahh... so you made it back safely!"

"You got lucky!"

"Once you've made it this far, you're all right now. Damn those human-faced flies, making such a mess of everything!"


As soon as he passed through the gate, voices called out one after another.

Every one of them rang with heartfelt relief; some were close to sobbing.

Inside the gate, demons wrapped in bandages were sitting here and there, leaning against the walls, looking as though even just breathing was all they could manage.

That scene alone made Valmar realize, yes, he truly had clawed his life back from hell.


But immediately inside the gate, he was stopped.


"Please wait here."


"...Huh? Let me rest already. I'm a demon general."


When he glared at the ordinary demon soldier who had addressed him, the man trembled before Valmar's obvious strength--

yet still shook his head.


"I-it's an order from the Four Heavenly King, Lord Rubrum Nox... please...!"


"...An order from one of the Four Heavenly Kings?"


In theory, that made it unavoidable.

But it did nothing to calm the irritation boiling in him.

He wanted to sleep.

He wanted to eat.

He wanted to collapse.

As it was, his body was already throbbing from within, and his thoughts were beginning to break apart.


Yes.

He was close to his limit.

Just as he was about to raise his voice, it happened.

The air grew heavy.

An overwhelming presence of death shook the space itself, and Valmar--

everyone--

prostrated themselves.


Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze was looking their way.

Beside him stood the massive black dragon. Its mere presence made throats tighten.


"Tch... more worthless straggler trash again, huh, Nox."


The black dragon's words were all edges and barbs, but in this place there was no one who could possibly object.


"Those flies are too numerous. It was inevitable that things would turn out like this."


Rubrum Nox answered in a calm voice.

Or rather, he did not deny it.

Valmar immediately understood that whether demon general or common soldier, none of them were regarded as all that much in terms of combat value.


"Then just abandon them already. That would let us move freely."


"...That I cannot do. So long as I serve the Demon King, if all the soldiers entrusted to me end up as feed for flies, then I have failed in my duty."


They could do nothing but prostrate themselves as that conversation unfolded right in front of them.

Angering him meant death.

Voices begging for mercy leaked out from all around.


The black dragon snorted from its nostrils as though declaring demonkind itself beneath notice.

The breath was like mockery, like ridicule given form.

It carried damp heat, and in the next instant it became a gale.

Valmar barely managed to remain standing. Soil was carved out from beneath his feet, and he had the sensation his body lifted off the ground. He clenched his teeth to avoid being blown away.


Merely by existing there, the dragon made every movement feel overwhelming.

It was disaster itself.


"Still, I have a bad feeling. While we're tied down here, who knows what the humans might do... These human-faced flies are troublesome too."


"...I know. But if I am to surpass the Demon King, I require trials. I would even call this a good opportunity.

If I cannot let the humans prepare and then slaughter them anyway, I have no right to speak of surpassing him."


"So you understand that much. Fine. ...Though I do worry whether pathetic humans will prove sufficient fodder for your growth."


"And yet... it is true that waiting forever does not suit me either."


Hearing that exchange, anxiety seized Valmar's chest.

Even if he pretended not to have heard the part about surpassing the Demon King, the idea that giving humans time might let them create even more monsters like these human-faced flies was the worst possibility imaginable.

That doubt flickered through him.

Even so, if the Four Heavenly King did not remain here, Valmar and the rest would die out in no time at all.


Had demon generals truly ceased to function as fighting strength?

The humiliation alone was enough to make him feel he might die of fury.

And above all, neither the black dragon nor the Four Heavenly King intended to protect them forever. It was obvious they wanted to go on the offensive.


As Valmar stood there burning with humiliation, two figures approached Rubrum Nox and the black dragon.


They were demon generals with thin legs bending backward at the knee, wings growing from the shoulders like half-formed armor, and beaks gleaming like metal.

Their long tail feathers swayed.

Two bipedal demons like monstrous birds--

Shif and Zaf.

Valmar recognized the names.


"P-please, just a little longer! If we can only kill that giant queen-fly the soldiers described, their coordination will break!"


"We are continuing the search in the surrounding area...! If you can spare us a little more time, we'll be able to pinpoint the queen-fly's exact position, and prepare enough to hold out while Lord Rubrum Nox strikes her down...!!"


It was a desperate report from Shif and Zaf, but Rubrum Nox gave them only a single glance from behind his black helmet.

Valmar watched, heart hammering, waiting to see where things would go.


"Very well. ...Then I too shall fulfill my role. Maintaining this place as a refuge."


With those words, Rubrum Nox slowly turned his hand toward them.


Toward them.


"...Eh?"


"W-what...? Why...!"


A wave of unrest spread at once.


"There are thirteen beyond saving. Those serving as breeding beds for the flies will be disposed of."


It took several seconds for the meaning of those words to sink in.

...Thirteen?

...Breeding beds?


Pain stabbed through his chest again.

His head rang as if splitting apart.

His stomach felt heavy.

Hot.


--Ah.

Of course.

The reason they had been stopped here.


It was to dispose of those who had had human-faced fly eggs laid inside them.


Some among those who had fled here must have become breeding beds as well.

There were also those who, during the escape, had apparently been implanted without ever realizing it.

"Beyond saving" referred to those cases where hatching had already progressed too far--because if it was immediately after implantation, there were still ways to help.

And indeed there had been those who were saved even after being implanted. If one found an egg lucklessly close to the surface and tried to peel it away, it clung so fiercely that flesh came away with it, but by gouging it out together with the flesh and having demons capable of healing magic continuously restore the wound, they had somehow managed.


According to the demons who could use healing magic, it was still manageable if done soon after implantation.


Beyond saving.

In other words, those whose eggs had developed too far to do anything about.

Disposal was perfectly rational.

There was no value in trying to save those beyond saving.


Ahh.

My chest, my stomach, my brain--

they hurt.


It was horrible.

Nausea welled up.

Pain like something inside him was writhing, rampaging.


...No.

Surely not.

I'm fine.

I'm a demon general...!


After all that desperate running, after getting this far through sheer luck, there was no way.

That could not be happening.


And yet--

what was this, then?

Black flame filled his vision.

It would burn him away.

Just as it had turned the human-faced flies to ash, Valmar too would become ash.


"Ah... eh...?"


The words would not come properly.

It felt as if burning heat brushed his cheek--

and in the next moment, the world went quiet.

Everything sounded far away.


Why...

why why why why--

Why me?


I've been implanted.

Since when...?

Human-faced fly eggs...


"Good. Then we inform Viridis as well and attack with the whole army. Let the curtain rise on the next battle, the last and greatest decisive clash that will determine the fate of this continent."


He thought he heard Rubrum Nox say something like that.

As though killing Valmar meant nothing at all.

The world kept turning.

How--

how utterly cruel this world was.
