Human-Faced Fly Parade (2)







His abdomen had swollen unnaturally.

It also moved in grotesque, uneven bulges.

Perhaps he no longer had any precise sense of his own body.

But the signs of a living thing writhing beneath the skin were unmistakably there. It swelled like a wave, sank, then pushed outward again.

Just looking at it was enough to make one sick.


His companions were frozen by fear.

This was not the kind of sight anyone could ever get used to.

Only the victim himself still failed to grasp what was happening, struggling on the edge of terror, even his breathing in disarray.


"No... what is this... what... is...!"


The scream tore deep into the forest, and after it came an eerie silence.


And then--

everything ended in an instant.


Even the sound of skin tearing was vague.

Shadows scattered.

Wings shook the air.

As if newborn life were reaching out to touch the world.


Human-faced flies.

They burst from inside his flesh and dropped in sticky fluid, a writhing swarm of mixed sizes. Ten of the smaller ones.

And then--

three large ones appeared.

Enormous, powerful wings.

Clouded eyes.

And mocking "human" faces that seemed modeled after demonkind.

The fewer, larger ones were the main specimens, each possessing combat strength on the level of a demon general.

The smaller ones were incomplete byproducts, merely extras.

And yet even the instant they were born, they already moved like predators that knew how to take life.

Even the little ones possessed enough strength to overwhelm ordinary demon soldiers.


This was how they were born.

This was how they increased.

Even those captured by the human-faced flies who somehow returned alive were, in the end, only being used as breeding beds.

There was no escaping the end.


Valmar had seen this scene many times by now.

And once it reached this point, the pattern was always the same.

The newly born human-faced flies attacked immediately.

There was nothing to do but fight.


Battle.

Companions screamed.

Somewhere, bones broke.

Some had the smaller flies latch onto their skin, clinging like leeches as if trying to burrow into their bodies.


Valmar desperately took down one of the larger human-faced flies.

But in the brief opening, another latched onto his back.

It did something.

Pain shot through him, and he flailed wildly to throw it off.

Bodies tangled together.

His breath caught.

All he could do was swing his weapon in blind panic.

Unable even to form words out of his screams, feeding himself only on terror so sharp it felt like madness, he swung and swung--

and only then managed to smash the second large fly at last.


Blood and bodily fluid splattered everywhere.

His breathing tore harshly at his throat.

His knees shook.


He could no longer hear the wingbeats.

While Valmar was dealing with two of the larger flies, some other demon general among the survivors must have killed the last one.

The battle was over.


Their numbers had dropped further still.

Even those still breathing were not unharmed.

Everyone was on the verge of collapse.

Everyone was wounded.

And yet the only thing the survivors could do was flee again.


Perhaps from the aftereffects of battle fatigue, one of their number dropped to his knees while they were running.

His mind had gone beyond its limit.

He could no longer scream or reject what was happening. He only trembled, his shoulders shaking.


"...I can't do this anymore..."


Muttering that, he dug his claws into the ground and kept clutching at his own head.

And so he fell away from them too.

All that waited for him now was death, but even giving up had become, in a way, a kind of salvation.


There were also those who had their minds eroded without anyone noticing during their battles with the human-faced flies.

Unable to move, invaded by the writhing sense of something inside their bellies, they lost their sanity.

Broken by pain and fear, they eventually began laughing in trembling voices.


"This is... proof that I was chosen...! I can become... a breeding bed...! I'm the lucky one...!"


Clutching at their stomachs, dripping tears and saliva, they shouted like zealots.


"The human-faced flies are... the true Demon King! Ahh, ahh... eat me...!"


Some cried out with voices mingling worship and despair.


Others, unable to bear the endless terror, stood dazed for a while and then quietly drove their own blades into their chests.


They had wanted to toy with weaker creatures--

humans--

and kill them slowly for sport.

Cruelly enough, that wish was granted in the opposite form:

demonkind was the one being toyed with and killed slowly.


It was a brutal reality.

Someday they would be tortured to death too--

and even knowing that, they still could not stop walking.

Northward only.

Toward the continent they ought to return to, forcing one step after another as though out of pure stubbornness.


And at the end of that despair, salvation truly did appear.




--------------------------------------------------




Through a break in the trees, they saw a stretch of connected walls.

A city of medium size.

As the swarms of human-faced flies approached its vicinity, they changed course and pulled away, as though instinctively rejecting it.


On top of the wall stood fellow demons, waving toward them.

Valmar exchanged glances with the others and dragged his trembling legs toward the gate.


Perhaps the place was already crowded with demons who had likewise escaped there.

No matter how many demon generals might be within, it was no longer enough power to reverse the situation.

The attacks of the human-faced flies would not stop.

Their numbers would only continue increasing without end.

All this would achieve was a slow wearing down.

There was no point in trying to hold out behind walls either.

Instead of clinging to defenses they could never truly hold, if one wanted to survive--

then one had to run.


Still, if there were enough others around, perhaps the flies would target someone other than Valmar.

The weaker would die first.

It might at least offer a brief rest.


Thinking such grim thoughts as he neared the gate, Valmar saw it.


Suddenly, black flames stirred.

They swirled with enough heat to scorch the sky.

And in a single instant, the loathsome human-faced flies were erased.


He immediately understood why the place was safe.

The master of those black flames repelled even the hideous flies.

Tears spilled out all at once.

Relief and bitterness filled his chest.


This, then, was why the demons could still remain holed up here in good health even though they neither fled nor hid.

Why only this place had survived without being trampled beneath the human-faced flies.

The answer was simple--

and despairing.

An absolute strong one.

A monster who burned away even the threat of the human-faced flies as though it were beneath notice, no matter if thousands of them gathered.

Because they had that protection, the demons laying siege here could still cling to this place.


Feeling that with painful clarity, Valmar gave himself over to relief.

It was all right now.

One of the Four Heavenly Kings was here.

The strongest of them all, Rubrum Nox of Black Blaze, was here.


Relief flooded him.

At the same time, hatred toward humans surged up.

So what if they are eaten and killed?

Did humans, no different from livestock in the end, truly think their lives balanced against those of demonkind?

Laughable.

If anything, they ought to offer themselves gladly.

After all, it was demonkind that would consume them.


It was only natural for livestock to offer themselves up to their masters.

To become nourishment, to become part of us--

that was the only role humanity had ever been permitted.


There was no longer any reason to fear the human-faced flies.

No--

there was still one source of unease.

That especially gigantic boss among them, dozens of times larger than the others.

That one alone possessed not only quantity through its swarm, but quality as well.


But even that unease faded.

In the end, they were only insects.


There was a black dragon there, emanating pressure fierce enough to belong to legend itself.

And beside it stood a Four Heavenly King whose pressure was even more terrible, a being commanding even that black dragon.


This was it.

This place had to be the final safe haven for demonkind on this continent.

Valmar believed that with all his heart.


Even so, he was feeling oddly unwell.

His head hurt, and his body felt heavy.

He staggered.

But just a little farther.

A little farther, and he would be saved.

It felt as though tonight he might finally dream good dreams again.
