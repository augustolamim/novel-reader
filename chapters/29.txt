Episode 126: Humans Fighting Humans (2)






The clerks stirred and shouted, "Stop!"

But my feet did not stop.

There was no way I was going to halt just because someone told me to.

If they wanted to stop me, then...


"Do it!!"


That was the shopkeeper's voice.


At once, mercenaries who looked every inch like hardened veterans appeared and pointed their weapons at me one by one, speaking in low voices like spitting.


"Stop. Otherwise we'll kill you.

You can tell just by looking, right? We hold the right to decide whether you live or die here. Sorry, but those are the boss's orders. If you want someone to resent, resent yourself for misunderstanding the limits of your own strength."


Yes. Violence.

If they wanted to stop me, then violence was their answer too.

The shop had apparently hired mercenaries precisely in expectation of rough situations like this.

Quite the establishment.

Of course, I meant that in the worst possible way.


"...I see. And even if I said I only want my stolen gem back and won't touch anything else?"


"There is no such fact. You are merely a thief who forced his way into this shop."


"Ha."


I laughed coldly.

So in the end, violence really was all there was.

Even if I said I just wanted back what had been stolen from me, of course they weren't going to hand it over that easily.


'Lumiela, we'll settle this with violence.'


'Yes. Let's beat them black and blue, just like they want!'


'Yeah, but... wait a second. I want to watch how they move first.'


When I said that, Lumiela turned her mouth down into a pout and nodded reluctantly.


The mercenaries had formed a semicircle around me.

Some held swords, some gripped axes. Every one of their faces wore the same cheap smile of men lured by the smell of money.

Little by little they closed the distance.

Then one mercenary's sword stopped right beside my throat.


The shopkeeper swelled with triumph.


"...Haha, checkmate!!"


The mercenaries were grinning too, as if pleased with themselves.

But Lumiela was still invisible and completely free, so what exactly were they talking about?

I rolled the word around in my head.


"Checkmate? Which part of this is checkmate?"


I looked straight at the shopkeeper without moving my eyes from him.


"A blade at your neck. If that isn't checkmate, what is? No matter how strong a human is, once the head comes off, he dies. That is what we call truth."


Mocking me as he laughed, the shopkeeper slowly walked closer.


"Don't move now. Not unless you want your head cut off."


The mercenary at my neck toyed with the blade, bringing it close and pulling it back again.

That only reaffirmed my conclusion that he was someone worth punching.

More importantly, I had to stop Lumiela.


'Lumiela--'


"Ei."

Still invisible, Lumiela had somehow grabbed a sword lying nearby and was just about to swing it at the arm of the mercenary holding the blade to my throat.


'Muu. ...Why? I was about to make sure that arm never does bad things again...'


Lumiela had been about to cut off the mercenary's arm, so I stopped her for the moment.

Since I was the one who had decided to rampage, it felt like if anyone was going to do something bloody like severing limbs, it ought to be by my own hand.


The shopkeeper, unaware that we were exchanging such ominous talk through telepathy, still seemed convinced he held the advantage. Laughing, he came all the way up to me--


and then drove the toe of his shoe into my shin.


"Oraa! You got a little too full of yourself!"


Then another kick.

And another.

They did not hurt even slightly.


"H-hard!"


The shopkeeper had hurt himself instead.

Still, nothing broke, and that made me think back to that hulking guy called something like Dragonslayer whatever during the treasure-hunt business. He really had swung hard when he punched.


Then I noticed Lumiela watching the shopkeeper with a gaze that looked ready to kill him, and decided it was about time to hit back.

This was my weakness.

Not kindness.

I wasn't standing there in the hope of the shopkeeper showing goodwill and changing his ways.

No.

This was only a ritual to let me take revenge cleanly afterward.


The shopkeeper, apparently having hurt his foot, rubbed it with one hand while glaring at me.


"...Well? How does it feel to be unable to do anything?! You understand your place now, don't you? You'll be taking responsibility for the commotion you've caused!"


The shopkeeper barked that at me, his voice trembling.

His mouth shook, his eyes were bloodshot, and all that remained in him was the filthy feeling of borrowed power.

The mercenaries laughed mockingly.


"Heh. First, from now on, most of whatever money you earn killing demons will be paid up to us. And then you'll also do our work--"


He was babbling on about all sorts of dreams that were never going to happen.

One mercenary snorted and spat out, "Rough life, huh? Errand boy until the day you die."


It was a grotesque little joint performance.

Only their laughter rang high through the room.


'Brave! I'm at my limit now...!'


Lumiela's angry voice echoed in my head.

There was no longer any reason to hesitate.


I slowly started walking toward the shopkeeper.

"Tch!" clicked one mercenary, and he swung his blade at my neck.

But the blade shattered into pieces, and his eyes shook with brief astonishment.


The other mercenaries lunged at me all at once.

Their blows were all shallow.

They did nothing.

Humans were weak.

Laughably frail compared to demons.

The people in front of me looked ridiculous.


So I answered them on the same scale.

I closed the gap with one mercenary in an instant and lightly twisted his arm up. Bone and flesh shattered into pieces, and after spilling giant tears and a scream, he rolled back his eyes and fainted.

I also tried lightly flicking one away with a finger.

Slamming into the ground, that mercenary spat blood, let out a groan of agony, and writhed across the floor.


The remaining mercenaries tried to leap back and flee--


'You're not getting away.'


Invisible attacks from Lumiela sliced the tendons in their legs, and all of them collapsed onto the floor.


"My legs... my legsss!!"

"I can't run...! No, I don't want to die!!"

"Mooommyyy!!"

"Please forgive me... please, forgive me...!!"


With that, the fight was decided, and we made our example in Area 1.

We were not marks.

If you tried to deceive us or harm us, this was what would happen to you.




--------------------------------------------------




With the mercenaries neutralized, all that remained was the shopkeeper.


"D-don't come any closer...! Stop! I have connections with the underworld people in Area 2! If you lay a hand on me, they won't stay quiet! Don't come! Ugh, you mercenaries, do something...! This is exactly why I pay you so much...!"


As the shopkeeper backed away, he bumped into a shelf, sending a bottle of wine falling to the floor where it shattered and spread a red stain. He shouted in a trembling voice, already crying.


"If those underworld bastards come after me too, then I'll just retaliate against them as well."


"Damn you, all you've got is brute-force violence, you lowlife...! You should have just stayed fooled...! I'm not the kind of man who's supposed to end in a place like this...!"


"You are ending here."


"Why... why is this... how did it come to this... I was supposed to be the one doing the exploiting..."


"It's the chain of hatred. You deceived me, and I hated you for it. Humans glare at each other, crush each other. That's just the ordinary flow that's existed since ancient times, isn't it?"


"Uwaaaaahhh--! Why, why, why, whyyyyy!!"


The shopkeeper clawed at his own head over and over.

At last he stopped, and perhaps because he had finally lost the will to resist, he took on an unpleasantly fawning expression.


"P-please... please, I beg you, let at least me go... I'll give you all my money... You can have the lives of all those mercenaries who pointed weapons at you too...!"


"...No. I could spare the mercenaries. You're the one who gave the orders. You're the one I hate most. So you're the only one I won't let escape."


"Hii... B-but there are much bigger evils than me, aren't there?! Area 2 and Area 3 are crawling with them...! Or demons, even! Use that overwhelming violence of yours on them instead... why me...?"


"Because you're the one who tried to swindle me in the first place."
