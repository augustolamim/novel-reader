'All right, we can fuse in reality too.'

'Now we just have to be careful not to forget this feeling.'

We released Valuen Stein.

The sense of near-omnipotence faded, and the two of us returned to our original separate selves.

As soon as the trial of the Holy Sword ended and our awareness returned to our real bodies, we tried fusing in reality while the sensation was still fresh. If we had suddenly become unable to do it here, that would not have been funny.

But the result was a success.

It wasn't some one-time power-up. It was real.

Honestly, that was a huge relief.

That trial was rough.

Just as expected, once we crushed the nobles, the Kingdom got mad.

They put out notices that were basically wanted posters, saying we had disrupted order, and even sent the army after us. The paper talked about trials and justice, but that was only for show. They had been fully prepared to dispose of us.

So we resisted and carried out the Dog of Flanders Plan--modeled after the way angels carry someone up to heaven at the end of the story.

It wasn't exactly a public execution, but publicly abducting important people and dumping them elsewhere did a good job of giving the Kingdom despair and paying them back.

Neither of us had to directly experience killing anyone, so I think it was a fairly peaceful solution.

...Well, the Kingdom did get destroyed.

Honestly, it doesn't feel great, but it was an illusion, so let's try not to dwell on it.

In any case, the trial put a serious strain on us mentally, but what we got out of it was enormous.

There are no more clouds hanging over things between me and Lumiela either.

My memories of a previous life. The fact that I had read about this world as a story. If I ever tried to explain that, it would sound like insane nonsense.

If I had still been in my previous life and a friend suddenly told me something like that, I wouldn't have believed them either. If our positions were reversed, I know I would have thought it was ridiculous.

Even so, Lumiela understood.

Maybe the key was the accumulation of trust between us, and also... if I had to name something else, it was that I showed her the memories themselves instead of only explaining in words. Maybe what mattered was sharing them as lived experience instead of fragments.

That alone would have been more than enough, but the biggest gain was still that we got a decisive new power.

The original Brave's final form--that power-up achieved through cooperation with Lumiela. That was something we absolutely needed in order to fight the Demon King.

Though ours turned out a little differently from the original.

In the original story, it wasn't quite a complete fusion into one being.

It had been more like Lumiela lending her power to strengthen the original Brave.

Then the smaller Lumiela floated near the original Brave's shoulder and supported him from there. At least, I'm pretty sure that was how it looked.

And unlike ours, he didn't grow those ridiculously radiant wings either.

Why did that difference happen?

I can think of a few possibilities, but... well, we got stronger, so whatever.

In terms of output and stability, we're clearly above the original version.

Even thinking back on the scenes from the source material, I can say our current form is on a completely different level.

Still, even so--

I pictured the state of Valuen Stein and started thinking about how much we could really do with our current specs.

'If we started with a straight-up fight at the signal, I'd say there's still a higher chance we'd lose to the Demon King.'

Lumiela said it casually through telepathy.

'You really don't sugarcoat things.'

I couldn't exactly argue, since she was right.

Judging from what I remembered of the original, whether we could win was still extremely questionable.

Maybe surpassing the Demon King as a living being in raw terms is just plain difficult.

For now, let's strengthen our side even further.

Namely, with the Holy Sword Idea that had just finished its trial.

"Heave-ho."

I gripped the hilt of the Holy Sword.

This time I was not dragged into the illusion world of the trial.

I quietly drew the Holy Sword from its pedestal--

and in that instant, it began to shine, and a dazzling pillar of light shot up toward the heavens.

A column of radiance rose that looked as though it could pierce space itself, bleaching everything white.

There probably isn't a sight more deserving of the word divine.

Beside me, Lumiela looked up and murmured, "...Beautiful."

...That said.

If someone like Liam had been the one holding this radiant sword, it would have looked impressive.

But the one causing it right now was me, dressed in my suspicious mask-and-cape ensemble.

That cut the solemnity by about half.

"Hm. It doesn't suit you."

I felt like I heard somebody mutter that under their breath, but I pretended not to.

The Holy Sword Idea in my hand felt as though it were quietly pulsing.

This sword reads the wish of its wielder and manifests power.

The thought process part was probably handled during the trial, when it read my mind. It was basically an ability gacha.

Now then, would I get the same ability as the original Brave?

The reason the original Brave's combat abilities were always so vague was partly because of the Holy Sword Idea.

He had possessed a sort of power that was strengthened by people's feelings, so its output fluctuated depending on the state of the story.

The story leaned on the power of bonds all the way.

If I got that same ability, though, I probably wouldn't be very good at using it.

But--

that worry turned out to be meaningless.

"...This is different."

The sensation flowing into me was a completely different ability.

Not just the ability itself, but how to use it too, became clear to me not through logic but through intuition. As naturally as breathing.

Strange, but I was certain.

I turned to tell Lumiela, but before I could even open my mouth--

'This ability... how do I put it? It feels like it could turn into a full-on hellscape. But it'll be an important trump card. We hit the jackpot!'

Since she was contracted to me, Lumiela seemed to understand it naturally too.

I thought about the ability calmly.

It was definitely powerful.

'Though the setup is heavy... and it'd be bad if we overdid it.'

It required careful groundwork before activation.

And once it went off, the damage would spread on a huge scale.

No, really... in terms of fighting the Demon King, it was an absurdly welcome blade to have... There was no doubt that, used right, it would become a terrifying weapon... but still.

I took a deep breath.

I'll think about it later.

'...A lot of our assumptions just changed.'

With this power included, we'll need to rebuild our strategy from scratch.

Now that I understood the Holy Sword's ability, maybe I should practice transforming while I've still got the hang of it.

If I don't drill it over and over, it won't stick.

"...Seeing it this close is bad for the heart. My survival instinct keeps telling me to get away."

With her arms folded, Clautia watched us from a slight distance.

Her voice was matter-of-fact, but her gaze was serious. I could tell there was a sage's analysis mixed into it.

Lumiela and I repeated the transformation and release several times, adjusting things so we could transform faster. We also spread the wings of light and tried some short flights to check output and stability and everything else.

Clautia watched, and from time to time she offered observations worthy of a sage.

Honestly, after all the havoc we caused in the Holy Sword's trial, I had expected at least one sour look. I was ready for her to tell us to get out.

But Clautia wasn't angry.

With the flower field behind her, she merely stood there with her arms folded, her back straight as an old tree, not a wasted motion in even her fingertips.

And with those eyes full of deep wisdom, she only observed.

More than that, whenever she looked at Lumiela, her expression was faintly gentle. There was even something relieved and proud in it.

Yes, with her arms folded.

How do I put it.

She really had that air of a mentor standing in the background.

I didn't say it out loud, but I couldn't help letting out a small laugh.

Maybe Clautia noticed, because she looked at me suspiciously.

Uh-oh.

For a second I got cold sweat, wondering whether she had somehow read my unserious thoughts.

I turned away with a straight face, pretending nothing had happened. Like I was just naturally glancing up at the sky.

A few seconds of silence.

She did not press the issue.

"...Ahem."

Clautia deliberately cleared her throat once.

Apparently this was something serious.

Lumiela and I turned back toward her almost at the same time, and without unfolding her arms, she lifted her chin slightly and said,

"I have certainly seen it through. The trial of the Holy Sword, and the choice you made there. ...As thanks, if that is the right word, choose whatever you like from among the valuables I possess."

"...Valuables?"

I asked before I could stop myself.

At that moment, something in my memory caught.

My head had been so full of the trial and the Holy Sword that I had forgotten, but I was pretty sure there had been a similar scene in the original too.

The original Brave had also been shown a selection by Clautia and received one item from among them. The act of choosing was skipped, and the next scene simply showed him with the item, so it was never treated as a big moment.

What the original Brave got was... if I remember right, some sort of cloak-like equipment made by processing dragon scales into thin layers.

To be honest, it was a pretty faint event in my memory.

Maybe because it came right after the Holy Sword trial scene, it left almost no impression. I only remembered it vaguely.

Even so, if a sage called them valuable,

then they couldn't be bad items.

Lumiela too looked deeply interested, eyes sparkling as she stared at Clautia.

"That's an incredibly generous offer."

Clautia's mouth softened very slightly.

"Then come with me."

Saying that, she turned her back on us, and we followed after her.

I wonder if there's something really amazing in there.

My expectations were getting pretty high.
