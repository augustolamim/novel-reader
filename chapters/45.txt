Beyond the surrounding mountains, the sacred peak Mont Celerune had been even harsher.

The moment we entered its range, thick fog rolled in, our visibility dropped sharply, and the temperature around us fell even further all at once.

Icicle-like masses of ice kept falling intermittently from overhead and from the slopes, and getting tapped by them over and over made for a thoroughly unpleasant time.



As our altitude rose, the air seemed to grow remarkably thin, making it harder to breathe. The ground was repeatedly freezing and collapsing, leaving little stable footing, and the dense fog distorted any sense of distance, making it difficult even to keep track of which direction we were moving in.



Obstructions caused by the environment were annoyingly effective because they weren't some culprit you could just defeat and make disappear. They wouldn't bother me much for a short time, but over a long period even I started feeling it little by little.



They were far more troublesome than demon generals.
The original Brave, back when he had gone to retrieve the Holy Sword in the story, had already been stronger than demon generals too, but since he'd come here seeking the sword because he still couldn't beat a Four Heavenly King, maybe this had been a perfectly balanced razor-edge level of difficulty for him.



But for the current me, it wasn't life-threatening. Lumiela was supporting me too.
It did take time, but we crossed all of it.
Though that was partly because we made too many detours, like attacking frost dragons.
And though we did reach the summit, we found neither the sage nor her dwelling. I was starting to feel at a loss when Lumiela sensed that a powerful barrier had been set up at a certain spot near the peak, and while we investigated that place, we found a hidden space.

Covered by the barrier and made hard to notice from outside, it was exactly the sort of place that befitted a secret hideaway.

It really helped that Lumiela knew so much about barriers. If she hadn't, we probably would never have found the entrance.



"It seems like there's someone inside the barrier, so I'll turn invisible again and stay ready in case of an emergency."



Lumiela suggested that, but I answered, "No, you don't need to turn invisible. ...You don't have to go that far. I'll use a false name as usual, though. And when you talk to me, do it by telepathy," and put on my mask.

Lumiela seemed puzzled by that, but she let the idea of turning invisible go.
The climate inside the barrier was mild. The temperature and wind were steady, and the effects of the outside world were shut out. In the original story, it had been explained as the work of a magical tool.



The ground was covered in carefully tended soil and paving stones. Flowers bloomed in abundance as though ignoring the seasons entirely, white, red, and pale-violet petals swaying gently despite the lack of wind. Vegetables were growing in the garden beds, fresh fruit hung from low trees, and the branches bent under their weight. A small waterway wound through the grounds, clear water flowing through it without a sound.
It was the scenery of a secluded paradise, completely severed from the savage world outside on the sacred peak.
There was a single house standing in the middle of that flower garden. But the person we had come for was not inside it.



"To think the day would come when guests visited me like this... fate is amusing."



A woman's voice reached us.
I found an elf watering the flowers.

She was tall, and her slender frame was trim and without waste. Her long ears were not fully hidden by her hair, and her hair itself, faintly glowing green--no, shining like emerald--flowed down her back.

Her clothing was simple but clean, and harmonized with the garden scenery to an almost unnatural degree. Like an elf, it was difficult to judge her age. She looked young, yet carried an air of maturity beyond that appearance.



Seeing the elf standing in the garden, Lumiela drew a small breath and fell silent.
That elf mage. The sage of the former hero's party. Clautia looked straight at us, and her gaze felt like it was measuring all the way down to the bottom of my heart.
It wasn't quite hostility or goodwill. It simply felt like she was trying to determine what kind of person I was.



"Hello. We came here to obtain the Holy Sword. I am... yes, Jane, the current wielder of light magic."
Meeting Sage Clautia's gaze head-on, I stepped forward and bowed honestly.
I felt resistance to calling myself a hero. If someone from the former hero's party heard me use that title, she might well find it unpleasant.
And as for my real name, I didn't think she'd go around spreading it, but just in case.
Other than that, I judged it best to tell the truth--that I was simply what I was, a user of light magic.




------------------------------------------------------------------------




There was no point in trying to hide the fact that I could use light magic from this sage. More than that, even if I tried, it would be useless.

From her perspective as a former member of the previous hero's party, the moment I arrived here together with Lumiela, she probably already had a good idea what I was.



She began in a calm voice, "Ah, I am--" as though to introduce herself.
But then--



"Clau! So it really is you! It's been so long!"



Lumiela got there first, calling out happily.
She smiled at Clautia. This was an emotional reunion. My introduction could wait.
It wouldn't have mattered much either way whether I told Lumiela about Clautia beforehand, but if I'd explained it in advance and she'd known already, some of the emotion of this reunion would have faded, so I had chosen not to tell her.



It had been several hundred years since they'd last seen one another. They were bound to have much to talk about.
Since the two of them were chatting, I decided to simply watch.



"I'm glad to see your seal has finally been lifted as well, Lumiela."



Clautia let out a breath tinged with relief.



"...I've had enough of being sealed away. Still, you're right. If I think about it properly, you're an elf, so with that lifespan it wouldn't have been strange at all for you to still be alive."



"I'm sorry. There was no way around that seal except waiting for time to pass. I see... I'd thought the time was close, but for you to come to me before I went to see you saved me the trouble. ...Since you've come all the way here, it seems the code I left behind reached you properly after all."



"The code? ...What are you talking about?"



Lumiela tilted her head slightly.



"...?"



At Lumiela's words, Clautia tilted her head too and seemed to lose her train of thought.
This sage had gone into seclusion like some hermit after leaving behind all sorts of information that Lumiela would understand.
Encrypted phrases, bits of information left only in fragments so Lumiela could piece them together.



In the original story, it was explained that she had done that so that when Lumiela was eventually freed from her seal, she could learn where Clautia was. And also in case the Demon King's seal happened to weaken around the same time, she had considered the possibility that whatever human Lumiela contracted with might want the Holy Sword that Clautia had taken back from the nobles.



...We hadn't solved that code this time. It would have taken an absurd amount of time, and a fair bit of luck besides.
I already knew where the sage was, so I figured why bother and took a shortcut. We came straight here without going the long way around.



"I've never seen any code at all."



"...What do you mean? Then how did you get here?"



Seeing the two of them standing there with troubled expressions, I looked away and pretended to admire the flower garden.
And then the conversation stopped.



"Brave..."



Lumiela's voice reached me by telepathy.
With no choice, I looked back toward them, only to find Lumiela giving me a somewhat flat stare. Clautia, influenced by that, looked suspicious too.



After a brief silence, Clautia gave a small sigh.



"Standing around won't do. Let's talk inside."



Turning on her heel, Clautia continued.



"Right. Since we've got guests, I'll brew some of my homemade tea."



"Thank you very much."



"Tea... Clau, do you have any sweets?"



"No."



"I figured... if you're living on a mountain like this, of course you wouldn't..."



Lumiela sounded genuinely sad at the fact that there would be no tea sweets.



"...When we get down from the mountain, let's stop in town and eat some."



"But you're limiting your food, Jane... so I should too..."



"No, I'll eat too. Snowy mountains make you want sugar. You have to adapt to circumstances."



"Snowy mountains really are wonderful."



As soon as Lumiela praised snowy mountains, she started thinking about what she wanted to eat.
"Lumiela and her contractor... heh, you two really do get along well."



Listening to our exchange, Clautia's expression, which had been stern until now, softened into a smile. Then she began walking toward the house standing deeper in the garden.
The two of us followed after her.
