Abelia's Mission (3)







They had found one of the search targets.

And yet Abelia felt not a shred of happiness.

Perhaps he was still technically alive, but there was no way she could call his condition normal.


There were nearly ten silent humans there.

Not one of them had any light in their eyes.

Their movements were orderly, but there was no life in them.

They did not speak. Abelia could not even sense the sound of breathing.


The thought came to her that it was like watching corpses move.

And if corpses moved, could they still be called human?

An icy chill ran down her spine.


The moment their eyes met, it happened.

The thing that had once been a knight tilted its head stiffly--

and then all the other things around it moved at once.


Like puppets suspended on strings, responding to some unseen signal.

Without a word, they ran toward Abelia.


Her body moved before thought could catch up.

She leveled her spear and swept it diagonally.

There was a heavy impact.

A dull sensation.

An arm spun into the air.


But--

there was no blood.

What showed within the severed surface was not flesh or bone, but layers of dry earth.


"...What is this...?"


The arm crumbled like a clod of dirt.

And still, the main body did not stop.

Even with an arm missing, it charged on.

In the next instant, she struck again.

Its head flew free.


One after another, she laid the attacking things out on the ground.

They were imitations, made with horrifying precision.

If only she could have convinced herself they were not humans at all, merely golems--

how much easier that would have been.


Constructed beings without blood, tears, or life of their own.

Not lives, only mechanisms.

Even understanding it that way in theory did not quiet the pit of her chest.


They are not human.

She told herself that.

And yet, in her gut, she understood.

Whatever the circumstances, they were surely too horrible to want to know.


The things still kept moving.

Headless bodies reached for her as they staggered forward.

Only after she smashed all four limbs completely did they finally stop.


This was in unimaginably bad taste.

And just as darkness settled over her heart, someone clapped both hands together and lit up with delight.

Maria.


"Waaah, amazing! So cool! Maria couldn't take her eyes off you just now!"


Her voice was absurdly cheerful for the scene.

There was something wrong with this girl.

Or perhaps she lived with some entirely different kind of senses.


Steadying her breathing, Abelia knelt beside the fallen remains of what had once been a knight.

The appearance was unmistakably that of her missing companion. The hair color, the wear on the armor--everything matched.


When she touched the hair, it felt exactly like human hair. Soft and pleasant to the touch.

But the interior visible at the neck was only earth--dry, lifeless dirt.

Even the skin was soft only on the surface. Pressing into it with a fingertip revealed a hard layer beneath.


It was as though only the outside of a human had been peeled away and restuffed with earth.

Her spine went cold.

If the hair and skin were real, then--

had this thing once actually been human?

The moment the thought occurred, nausea surged up inside her.


"...Haha. This is in terrible taste."


A dry laugh escaped her.

Someone was using human beings as material to create golems.

That had to be what this was.


Only the inside had been remade.

If so, then it would hardly be an exaggeration to say the original person was already dead.


And just then--


"Ah!!"


Maria-chan suddenly lifted her face and waved happily.


"Maria found them! The person you were looking for!"


Suspicious, Abelia followed her gaze.


And there--

she stood.


A woman with hollow eyes.

Dressed in a pale purple robe, carrying a staff.

There was no mistaking her.

It was Felicite, the mage.


But the way she walked was wrong.

Each movement was stiff, like a body being pulled by strings.

Her lips remained shut. Her eyes did not even blink.


Slowly, she came toward them.

There was no life in her gaze.

She had been turned into one of those things--

into a golem.




--------------------------------------------------




The blood drained from Abelia's face.

It felt as though all color was being drawn out from inside her body.

Her fingertips went cold, and even the sense of her own clenched fists seemed distant.

This was a reality she did not want to believe.


"It's like an emotional reunion! Maria's so happy she could help!"


Her hand almost reached out on its own, but reason restrained it.

She desperately held back tears.


She had to get away from here.

She picked up the severed arm of the thing that had once been a knight.

The cross-section, crumbling away like dirt, somehow still seemed to retain warmth.

Even so, it was too dangerous to remain here.


At the very least, this arm had to be examined.

If she could bring it back to some scholar somewhere, perhaps they could learn something from it.

Perhaps there might even be a chance of reversing it.


No.

There probably wasn't.

Inside, there was nothing but dirt.

Looking closer, it was red dirt, like clay kneaded together with blood.


"...Maria-chan."


When Abelia turned toward her, Maria was still standing there.


"What is it?"


She smiled innocently, her cherry-pink hair swaying.

Like a child at play.


The instant Abelia saw that smile, it felt as though a cold blade traced down her spine.

No.

That wasn't right.

She had been wrong from the beginning.

This girl was strange.


She was smiling in a place that was hell.

Somewhere inside her, the part that should have recognized this abnormality as abnormal was missing.

And that was the most terrifying thing of all:

that she could smile at this.


The objective had been fulfilled.

Felicite and the others had been killed and turned into something else.

To begin with, perhaps there were actually very few living humans left in Area 3. Maybe most of the residents had already become these things.


She did not want to remain in a place like this for even one more second.

Leaving Maria behind as she poked at one of the things and laughed, Abelia fled.


On the way back, she saw the corpse of the demon instructor tossed into what looked like a garbage dump.

It was a miserable sight.

Area 3--

the worst place imaginable, where only madmen who had cast aside reason roamed free.


There was nothing here but malice.
