The Princess Cannot Honestly Rejoice, Part 2






Abelia rolled the words over in her chest again and again, hesitating over whether she ought to say them aloud.

Silence would be easier, but then her doubts would only swell further.

In the end, she steadied her breath, raised her eyes, and spoke to Princess Ceras while trying to hide her unease.

　

"...I've never met her, but that 'Imperial Princess' who supposedly killed the Wind Four Heavenly King is at the top of the list of dangerous people too, right? Will that person move for money?"

　

The person who had slain one of the Four Heavenly Kings being, of all things, a deranged lunatic...

Was that really something to celebrate?

Abelia truly could not decide.


What the princess had done after killing the Wind Four Heavenly King had been the height of madness.

She had announced she would build a grave for the Four Heavenly King, then slaughtered half the soldiers present there as sacrifices.

And apparently she had spared the other half only to use them as "tools" to carry the message not to destroy the grave.

It was beyond all reason.

No one could predict how a person with that kind of insane way of doing things would act from now on.

　

She was far too dangerous to welcome as an ally.

After all, she was a person mad enough to offer soldiers as sacrifices in order to build a grave for one of the Four Heavenly Kings.

And yet they could not simply ignore that strength either.

It would be stranger not to place hope in a human being who possessed the power to defeat a Four Heavenly King.

Abelia wanted her, like the other dangerous figures, to focus solely on the war against the demons.

But if money would not move her, then they would need to create some system that directed the princess's spearpoint toward the demons without provoking her carelessly.

Abelia wanted to know what Ceras thought.

　

Ceras slowly closed her eyes and fell silent for a short, prayer-like moment.

Then she spoke quietly.


"I wouldn't count on it. She is not an existence that can be moved by money or status. In arrogant defiance of all things, she merely wields her strength as she pleases, driven only by her own desires."


"Then... does that mean that from now on, that princess is going to attack humans as well as demons...?"

　

"...I can't help pitying the soldiers who were killed. But... if we interfere carelessly, it will only bring about further tragedy. There is no law or code in this world capable of binding that princess, other than force."


Few things were more troublesome than a broken soul with power.

Whether you tried to bring such a person down or walk beside them, they could not be measured by ordinary standards.


"...Honestly, I kind of wish she'd just take the demons down with her."


Lowering her voice, Abelia let slip the thought she had hidden deep in her chest.


"That is going too far."


Ceras rebuked her.

But Abelia truly thought that if the princess disappeared, the Kingdom would certainly lose fighting strength, yet more than that, the very fact that such a princess continued to exist was itself a threat to humanity.

The best outcome would be if an uncontrollable monster and the demons fought each other and conveniently destroyed one another.


At that, Ceras pressed her left hand to her forehead and sighed.


"Speaking of the princess... there is something I fear. If the princess were ever to meet hero Liam, battle would be unavoidable."


Ceras's strained voice made the air grow heavy.

Just imagining such a situation made Abelia's stomach turn cold.


"Ah... the princess would never overlook someone strong like hero Liam, and hero Liam doesn't seem like the sort who'd let the princess go either..."


Abelia continued with a bitter expression.

If the two ever crossed paths, a bloody catastrophe would surely follow.


"The princess and the hero absolutely must not be allowed to meet. We have to stop it somehow... ah."


"If they meet, hero Liam's the one in danger..."


Just imagining it sent cold sweat down her back.

If hero Liam were killed, it would become an irreversible nightmare for the Kingdom.

And for the hero to be felled by a human--by a madwoman, at that--would be nothing but a nightmare.

If the hero were to fall after a mortal struggle with the demons, that would be one thing. But such a ridiculous ending could not be allowed.




--------------------------------------------------

　


No one could predict what a madwoman might do.

Reason and logic meant nothing to her, and it was foolish even to think about controlling her.

And yet the truly frightening thing was that there were still people who believed they could approach such a person and make use of her. That reckless delusion was what invited tragedy.

　

Among the nobles, another movement had begun to surface during the meetings.

The faction that had once proposed shutting themselves away with a limited few inside the Koshumar Great Mine was now trying to lease upper-floor living quarters to those who had registered through the branch offices and had experience hunting demons, bringing them inside.


Publicly, the measure was explained as a way to strengthen the forces at their main base.

But behind the scenes, it looked to Abelia like little more than a scheme to disappear through some hidden escape route while the gathered fighters did the actual fighting.

If a group like that ever conspired together, matters could spiral into something uncontrollable.

Granting them the upper halls as living quarters and even permitting them free movement seemed dangerous.


Hero Liam, not only toward demons but also toward nobles who committed wrongdoing, showed no mercy at all.

That purity was praised as a virtue, yet among the nobles it had become a spark ready to catch.

Though they cooperated outwardly, the antagonism beneath the surface was only deepening.


--Hmmm, we're definitely not united.

　

Yes. It felt as though if anything happened, everything might collapse all too easily.

That unpleasant foreboding would not leave the depths of her chest.

Order rested on thin ice:

the people's dissatisfaction,

the nobles' plotting,

the collection of dangerous but powerful figures,

the expendable prisoner soldiers,

the uncontrollable princess,

and the hero's own rigid purity.

　

She could not shake the feeling that any one of them might become the trigger that sent the whole thing crumbling in a chain reaction.

Outwardly, the mood was that they had gained the advantage by defeating one of the Four Heavenly Kings.

But beneath that surface, everything was full of cracks, and anyone might end up dragging everyone else down.

If even one gear of reason slipped out of place, the whole thing would tumble into irreparable chaos.

There was something terrifying about that, as if it might one day become the trigger for ruin itself.


Abelia let out a sigh.

The more she thought about it, the less certain she became that the situation was truly improving.

　

--Ah, come to think of it...

　　

Come to think of it, the place where the Wind Four Heavenly King's death had been confirmed and the place where a tornado had been sighted roughly an hour earlier were astonishingly far apart when viewed on a map.

No ordinary soldier or adventurer could possibly have traveled that distance.

But the Wind Four Heavenly King could freely control colossal tornadoes and apparently move herself at high speed through the air, so perhaps it made sense.

The distance covered and the scale of the battle went beyond common sense. Most likely they had moved over a vast area while fighting.


Because it had still been dark before sunrise, witness testimony was inconsistent.

Among those reports, there were apparently even a few who claimed that, though seen from afar, the area around the tornadoes had at times glowed with an intense light.

For an instant Abelia thought, Could it have been the Hero using light magic and intervening?

But she immediately shook her head.

It was too late to be hoping for something that convenient now.

In darkness, the wavering of flame could easily look like light. And flame magic was the Imperial Princess's specialty. If so, that glow was easily explained.


Apparently the princess herself had also suffered serious wounds in the battle with the Four Heavenly King, but the fact that she had killed one and gotten off with only that much was horrifying in itself.

The princess surpassed even a Four Heavenly King.

That was what it meant.

If they could hear a more detailed account of that battle, Abelia thought it would be of tremendous use later.

But no one could approach the princess.

If you got close, you would be cut down, burned, or sacrificed.

Either way, you would be killed.


In the end, all Abelia and the others could do was gather rumors and reports from afar, then stack guesswork upon guesswork.

Only the princess knew the truth.

But no one could touch that truth.

Trying to touch it was itself an act of danger.

Even with one corner of the Four Heavenly Kings fallen, the war was still only beginning.
