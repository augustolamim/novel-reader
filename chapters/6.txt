The Princess Cannot Honestly Rejoice, Part 1




Abelia's Side






When Princess Ceras's speech ended, the crowd gathered in the square burst into applause and cheers all at once.

Standing behind Ceras as one of her guards, Abelia could feel the heat of the people.

The speech had been a success.

　

In front of the people, Ceras had carried herself with dignity, but her back showed the slightest droop in the shoulders. She was exhausted.

Recently, Ceras had been traveling from the central region to the southern region, visiting towns all across the Kingdom and giving speeches to the people.


She had succeeded in gathering the once-famous Team Old Men--no, the Legends--and having them now take charge of training a new generation of hero candidates.

After that, Ceras had placed the greatest emphasis on speaking to the people.

She showed her face at the meetings held in the Koshumar Great Mine, but in practice it was the seasoned nobles who handled the agenda, while Ceras did little more than add her opinion.

At most, she had arranged weapon allocations and supplies for strong hero candidates such as the hero Liam.

　

Abelia had asked her why, and Ceras had said it was because her own battlefield experience and tactical eye were nowhere near those of the experts.

Even if she would not be swinging a sword on the battlefield herself, she apparently judged that what she ought to do, as royalty, was support the people's morale at least somewhat by giving speeches.


Abelia did not understand the details of politics or war.

But normally royalty were distant beings, and yet here one had come to their towns in person and spoken directly to them. That alone made the people smile in delight at what was surely a rare chance, waving back eagerly.


Even while wrapped in cheers, Ceras kept her cool smile in place as she returned to the carriage under the protection of Abelia and the other guards.

The moment the door shut, that gentle smile quietly faded, and a stern expression returned to her face.

　

"...It seems one of the Four Heavenly Kings has been defeated."

　

She spoke with a voice heavy with a deep sigh.


"Seems that way. It still doesn't quite feel real, though..."

　

One of the Four Heavenly Kings had been slain.

Normally that should have been wonderful news for the Kingdom, a report of victory.

But the face of Princess Ceras sitting opposite her in the carriage was not bright at all. Her finely shaped brows were drawn slightly together, and with her lowered eyelids, a heavy shadow fell across her expression.


The air around them, too, had sunk into quiet gloom.

Abelia herself wanted to rejoice in the victory, but given what that victory had entailed, she could not bring herself to do it openly, and no words came.



--------------------------------------------------



　

"The situation certainly has improved... One corner of the Four Heavenly Kings has fallen, and apparently hero Liam has even taken on a new comrade, a mighty warrior from a village called Kagutsuchi."

　

"I heard hero Liam even survived against that Wind Four Heavenly King, so it's definitely a good thing for that kind of hero to gain a powerful ally, right?"


According to the rumors she had heard, that warrior had apparently met him by punching him in the face.

In fact, they had traded blows for an entire day, and it had ended in a draw.

Abelia had no idea why they had fought in the first place, but if the result was that they had recognized one another and become allies, then clearly that warrior was no ordinary person.

If one thought of it simply as the hero gaining new strength, then it was something to welcome.


"Since we're at it, perhaps continuing to use a name that already collapsed once would be bad luck. Shall we add 'Luvant' to 'Soleil' and rename it Soleil Luvant--Morning Sun?"


The princess abruptly suggested it in a half-joking tone.

Just from the sound of it, Abelia felt that the word morning sun carried within it a sense of force still rising and a pure radiance that drove away darkness and lit the world.

The light that came with dawn symbolized not an ending, but a beginning.

To Abelia, Morning Sun seemed like a fitting name for an organization that fought demons.

　

"That's a good name!"


"Then let us propose it at the next meeting."


Ceras gave a small, soft laugh.

For just an instant, the atmosphere relaxed.

Perhaps being alive in itself was already proof that things were improving.

No matter how cornered someone became, no matter how close they came to losing hope, as long as they were still breathing there remained room to change the situation.

Once you died, that was the end of everything. Nothing went on.

Thinking that, Abelia remembered that moment of despair they had once faced.

That time when most of their comrades were struck down, and Abelia herself lost consciousness after nearly being killed by those ork-like demon brothers.

　

"Come to think of it, Lady Ceras, have you found that masked person who saved us when we escaped the royal capital by defeating that ork-like demon? I'd like to thank them too."


Abelia spoke up as the memory returned to her.

The princess knit her brows slightly and nodded.

　

"That suspicious masked person, you mean... We actually have reports now. It seems they are staying in the trade city of Eryudia and have defeated multiple demon generals. However... apparently there are also a number of bad rumors about them."


"That... sounds like we probably shouldn't expect much from their personality, like with hero Liam..."

　

For a while, Abelia listened as Ceras told her those bad rumors.

Certainly, there were cases where that person had saved lives, and it was difficult to simply label them a dangerous figure.

At the same time, however, their behavior often seemed far outside the bounds of sanity.


"In the end, it would be best to keep the relationship purely financial. We pass demon general information through the branch offices and pay rewards. We should not become any more deeply involved than that, and must maintain a certain distance."


Ceras concluded that way.

Establishing branch offices had been a good move.

The hero candidates were studying under Team Old Men, gaining companions and growing stronger.

At the same time, they would keep a certain distance from dangerous but capable fighters, using money to have them reduce the demons' strength while each side made use of the other.


Apparently, the branch offices were already registering not only powerful figures who had operated in the underworld of the cities, but even those who made their living as unseen assassins.

It prioritized practical gain over morality and appearances, but as a result, handing money to dangerous people who walked the line between the underworld and the world above, and letting them grow stronger, was surely a double-edged sword.

Bearing people who could not even clearly be called allies or enemies seemed to carry the peril of walking on thin ice: in exchange for immediate fighting strength against the demons, betrayal or violent disaster could erupt at any moment.


And recently, yet another new idea had begun surfacing in the meetings.

Could they perhaps make use of the criminals rotting in prison as well?

The manpower required to run the prisons was itself wasteful. Rather than cling to rehabilitation in name only, better to wear them out in the war against the demons instead--that was the cold logic behind it.

In fact, a mad serial killer who delighted in stuffing human corpses had apparently volunteered by saying, "I like turning humans into taxidermy, but demons look like humans too, so I'd like to try killing them."

Likewise, a torture maniac who took pleasure in gathering screams had reportedly declared, "As long as I get to make someone bleed, I don't care if it's the enemy," and was deemed cooperative enough to release.

　

They were now being folded into the battle lines under the name of parole.

When Ceras received that report, she had grown furious and bitterly snapped, "Murderers are no better than demons!" Yet she had been unable to overturn the decision.

Whether the logic was right or not, reality had become this: a strategy of hurling monsters crawled up from the bottom of prisons onto the front lines was already advancing.


Make dangerous people cooperate for money.

And support hero Liam in growing stronger and gaining companions.

Whether or not they could manage both would decide the fate of the Kingdom as a whole.

A plan to handle dangerous figures and a plan to guide the hero.

Only when both wheels turned together would a chance of victory be born.

And yes--how to deal with the princess who had defeated one of the Four Heavenly Kings would prove just as critical.
