"Enem--gyaaah?!"

The scream was cut off faster than it could melt into the forest.

The battle was decided in an instant. Even the word ambush felt too mild. The gap in strength between Brave and the enemy soldiers was simply that large. He could have won easily even in a head-on fight. With a surprise attack, it ended even faster.

Brave closed the distance the moment he stepped in and went straight for the captain of the mage unit managing the sealing relic.

Lumiela prepared to support him just in case, but there was no need.

That sealing relic really was a thing beyond understanding.

Though it was a small, well-shaped box, the moment one looked at it a strange impatience seemed to linger in the depths of the chest. A cursed object.

From the gaps in the box seeped something that could not be distinguished as either light or darkness, as though hope and ruin had been sealed inside with equal weight.

The captain of the mages seemed to have tried opening the box to seal Brave away, but--

"Stay locked up for a few hundred years."

At the same time Brave said that flatly, he kicked the mage captain into the magical chains that had emerged from the box. The box seized him. It coiled around his arms, torso, and throat, sealing in his very existence.

"Stop, pleeeeease--?!"

There was nowhere to run. Resistance meant nothing.

The captain's scream warped, stretched out, and was finally swallowed into the box. As though he had never existed in the first place, he vanished.

"Capacity's one, so all's well that ends well."

Without the sealing box, there was nothing left to fear.

Brave proceeded to carefully incapacitate the others who remained.

They would probably be left with lasting damage, but no matter what, he still did not kill them.

He simply shaved away their strength and will one piece at a time so that they would never bare their fangs again. After what had just happened, battle itself would probably become a trauma for them.

Eliminate every possibility of defeat while it is still only a bud.

Brave was thorough.

Poison, sealing--every enemy method that could have led to defeat, he crushed in advance. He left no room for counterattack whatsoever.

It was all too easy.

While looking down at the defeated people, Lumiela could not deny the feeling that rose in her chest.

If you did not try to protect humanity as a species, if you threw away mercy and hesitation, then even that tragedy could be handled this easily.

Nearby, the sealing box lay on the ground.

Lumiela looked down at it and quietly let out a breath. The hateful magical tool that had once bound her and dragged her inside had now seized the mage captain in her place.

It was satisfying.

Even though this was only the beginning.

From here on, nobles and their soldiers would likely attack in waves. In terms of fighting power they were no threat, but Lumiela fully intended to kill them. It was going to turn into a bloody battle.

The nobles had tried to kill the previous hero--
to kill Brave.

Driven by fear and greed.

That was unforgivable.

Fine, then.

Lumiela let a smile rise to her lips.

She would paint this hateful world of memory white with light. The dark grudges and resentment festering here, she would pour it all out in this place.

Yes--

I won't let them hurt Brave...
...Huh?

No, revenge?

At that point, she suddenly noticed.

She had been thinking about revenge, and yet at the same time, without even realizing it, she had naturally been thinking that she would not let them hurt Brave.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

While Lumiela was troubled by that realization, the forest grew noisy.

A second wave, perhaps. Extermination by force.

From deeper in the woods came the sound of armor scraping together, and then a disorderly marching group emerged.

They were dressed like knights, but mixed among them were people who plainly did not belong. Their eyes were clouded by greed, but they were amateurs.

They had merely been made to wear the same equipment as knights. They had once been nothing more than villagers. Their grips on their swords were weak, and there was no resolve in them at all.

A nasty trick.

If they had all been soldiers prepared to fight, then the previous hero would probably still have found it easier to fight back. But by mixing in ordinary people as well, it had been arranged so he could not simply fight on momentum.

"The moment they picked up weapons for a payout, they stopped being civilians. They're enemies."

Lumiela heard Brave mutter that in exasperation.

She reflexively gathered magic power. A distance and number that could be erased all at once.

"Lumiela, put up a barrier for me. I'll tell you what I've been hiding now."

"At a time like this...? I'll kill them for you instead. It can wait until afterward, can't it? Just looking at them gets in the way."

Confusion brushed across her chest.

Wouldn't later be fine?

She almost said it aloud, but swallowed the words. Lumiela thought it would be better to erase them all and leave just the two of them before talking at leisure. Did it really have to be now?

It should not have been a problem, since the one doing the killing would be Lumiela, not Brave.

This was not a matter of taking on the dirty work for him.

This was not Brave's battle. It was Lumiela's own battle to settle her past.

It was not a sin he should bear, and there was no reason to drag him in any further.

Even so, Lumiela silently raised the barrier.

Cut off from the outside world, light wrapped around them like a thin-domed membrane. The soldiers were trying to break it with magic and weapons, but it meant nothing.

"I... don't really want to kill humans if I can help it."

"I know."

"Yeah, and maybe it's selfish of me, but... I don't want someone important to me doing that either. I don't want you to kill them either, Lumiela."

"----------. ...That's impossible. Absolutely not. Give up."

She wanted to protect Brave. That was true. But so was the hatred she bore toward these soldiers.

Brave lowered his gaze slowly. "I see," he said.

Then, choosing his words carefully as though handling something fragile, he continued.

"Then I'll kill with you. We... ought to share the same fate. I can't let only you do the killing."

"I'm doing it because I want to. There's no need for you to do it too."

"No. I'll do it."

"Why...? You don't have to. Why would you...?"

"If I can't stop you, then I thought what I ought to do is become your accomplice."

That was unfair, she thought.

If he said something like that, then Lumiela would no longer be allowed to fall alone of her own accord. Mass slaughter was too extreme, after all.

She did not want to drag Brave into something like that as well.

"An accomplice..."

"Before that, look at the memories I've been hiding. Going into a massacre while carrying this knot inside would be a little rough."

"...All right... I'll look at your memories first, Brave. Then I'll decide whether to kill the soldiers and the nobles."

"--! Yeah. Understood."

For the moment, she wanted time to think.

She had to look at Brave's memories too and choose a path she would not regret. Was it really all right to rush straight into revenge like this?

The Lumiela from before meeting Brave would never even have considered such a thing.

To see his memories more deeply, she slowly closed the distance between them, pressed her forehead to his, and shut her eyes.

The order of time collapsed, and memories and emotions pulsed into her.

Following the bond that tied them together as contractor and spirit, it went deeper and stronger than telepathy.

She touched Brave's memories.

And then--
Lumiela understood this world.
Brave's previous life.
She understood everything.
