I should have killed Liam.

But to be certain, all he had to do was sink the ship as well. If it fell from the sky, every human aboard would die.

Not yet.

That thought burned through his mind hard enough to splinter it.

Linyuvurm was a dragon. The dragon partner of the man who would someday reach the strongest height.

He would not end over something this small. Until the instant he died, he would seize the sky, bare his fangs, and rage on.

Poison means nothing!!

Even if poison ravaged him, even if his lungs burned and his vision warped--

Even if part of my head is gone!

He would die soon enough. Even so, he could still move.

This would surely hasten his death. If he did nothing, perhaps he might live a few minutes longer.

That did not matter. He spread his wings and, ugly though it was, desperately held his posture.

"Gwooooooooooooooo--!!!"

Linyuvurm chose one final charge at the flying ship.

It was not even a charge. It was merely a heavy body slam, forcing what remained of his life straight onto it.

Even so, it was not reckless.

The hull approached. He could see the deck.

And there--
the human Liam was gone.

Only a vast pool of blood remained where he had fallen, along with traces that showed someone had dragged him away. There was no sign of the man in the white coat either.

Linyuvurm did not choose the side where the warrior stood. There were still a few seconds before that one could rush over.

Then came the sensation of flesh and bone smashing into the ship in a full-body collision. The impact of contact with the hull crashed over him.

For an instant, consciousness almost slipped away.

Even so, Linyuvurm did not let go. He seized it. He stretched out a foreleg and raked at the armor. Claws bit in and sparks flew. He wrapped an arm around the hull and clung to it.

"...I won't let gooooo!!"

The finest of human elites were aboard this ship.

Their counterattack came immediately. Magic burst. Cannon fire howled. Shockwaves hammered his whole body, scales shattered, flesh tore, and one sensation after another went dead.

Even so, he did not loosen his arms.

A dragon's claws, which could tear through most things in existence if that was not too bold a claim, slowly ripped through the armor plating.

He exposed mysterious components within. The revealed mechanisms spat sparks and white smoke, and the ship tilted sharply.

"We're going down!!"

"Hurry up and kill that half-dead thing!!"

Screams spread across the deck. Confusion chained into confusion. Some fell, some clung to the railings, some tried to grab their comrades only to slide together.

He had no future left, but if he dragged this ship down with him, it would lead into his partner's future.

He gathered what strength remained for one last effort.

Into his arms, into his fangs, into the full weight of his body wrapped around the ship, and pressed it all toward the vessel's center.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

"Good grief... what in the world is this?!"

A voice unpleasantly sticky in the ears came from inside the warped ship.

The outer hull was dented and crushed in places from the collision, beams groaned, and wiring spat sparks.

From deeper inside, through a gap in a half-crushed partition, emerged a man in a white lab coat stained red with blood.

Sweat beaded on his brow, and while pushing up his glasses he darted his eyes busily around the area.

His voice was loud with bluster, yet his breathing was shallow, and he could not hide his panic.

So the white-coated man was still alive too.

He was putting up resistance, but the ship was almost destroyed.

The deck tilted violently, and metal screamed. Soldiers who lost their footing rolled, some grabbed at the rails, others tried to catch their comrades and slipped together.

"Uwaaaah!"

"We're falling! We're really falling!"

And yet Linyuvurm knew, more than anyone, that it would not end there.

The warrior had already arrived.

The axe he swung bit deep into Linyuvurm's body.

The destruction did not stop. His body continued being broken. His flesh convulsed violently; his whole frame jerked.

He had no strength left to destroy the ship.

"We did it! Regingule of the Kingdom finished him off!"

"He stopped wrecking the ship, but if this keeps up--!"

"Mr. Gearnote, repair it!"

The human soldiers were rejoicing. There was no pain anymore.

Half drifting toward sleep, Linyuvurm remained stuck to the ship with hazy awareness.

He watched the little scene before his eyes through a dimming gaze.

After thinking something over, the man in the white coat spoke in a voice gone chillingly cold.

"The ship has been hit rather badly, hasn't it. All hands, change course for the nearest eighteenth hideout. That place has the proper facilities. Until then, we'll make do with this ship's equipment. We'll have to improve... treat... him, after all, heh."

As though the ship had turned in a different direction, it swayed.

"Now then, hurry up. ...First we need to recover the necessary materials. You returning to the ship was lucky, perhaps... no, you did wreck it, so perhaps not."

The expression of the white-coated man--the researcher--twisted into a smile that was impossible to tell apart from delight or obsession, and rotten either way.

"What magnificent material and specimen stock. He'll make a fine beastman."

Footsteps drew near. The voice grew clearer bit by bit.

"...Good thing I left the equipment aboard. Though I wonder whether we'll make it before we crash."

Muttering as if to himself, he walked closer.

Toward Linyuvurm.

His gaze held no emotion. It was a hideous look, measuring Linyuvurm as raw material.

Linyuvurm watched in silence. He had no voice left to shout, no fangs left to threaten with. All he could do was watch the bizarre exchange among these humans.

Step by step, the researcher approached and extended a hand toward him without the slightest hesitation.

In the hand protruding from the sleeve of his lab coat was a slender shining blade like a scalpel. With fascinated delight, he pointed it toward Linyuvurm. That cold gaze crawled across the gaps between his scales as it searched for weak points.

He was not going to be devoured. He was going to be examined, gouged open, and broken. Linyuvurm understood that.

Even so, he had no regrets.

He had fought to the end.
Only...
it was a shame he would not get to witness Rubrum Nox's glory.
