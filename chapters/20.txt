Episode 120: Training







The morning air was crystal clear.

Five days had passed since I finished looking around the areas of the Koshumar Great Mine. Lodging inside the mine in those cave-hotel? places and the inns outside in the town of Rifat had been made free, so I had been happily wandering from one place to another, staying at all sorts of inns for fun.


I still hadn't run into Liam or Edelweiss. It felt like the people of Area 1 and I were outside hires, while Liam and the others were part of the Kingdom army proper, directly under the Kingdom, so they were probably busy going to the front lines and the like.


Well, it made sense that those justice-minded types would belong to the Kingdom army.

Deciding that moving my body was always better than not, I had recently resumed the jogging I had been slacking off on.

At this point, I didn't even know how far I had run, but I had ended up a long way from the coal-mining town of Rifat.


I ran through grasslands stretching all the way to the horizon.

Every time my soles struck the damp grass, tiny drops of morning dew flew up.

Seriously...

where even was this place?

I had gotten lost, I thought, but my meeting time with Old Man Rowan wasn't until the afternoon, so I still had plenty of time. Besides, that old man was always getting drunk and falling asleep, then showing up late anyway, so taking that into account, things would probably work out somehow.


As I ran through the cold wind, heat built in my cheeks, and a warmth spread all the way into my chest.

It felt like my body core was waking up. My shoulders felt light, and my breathing settled into a comfortable rhythm. I was properly warmed up now.

I had probably run several dozen kilometers already, but I still wasn't tired at all, so there was no way this could count as training.


'Running really is the foundation of building your body... I should run too... ah, but maybe just a little more rest...'


Lumiela, floating beside me, had already run as much as she could and given up.

Her voice rode the wind, clear as if cutting through the morning haze. I laughed over my shoulder.


At first, Lumiela had been enthusiastically running beside me.

But after a fair amount of time had passed, just as expected, her pace had started to drop...

and in the end, well, this happened.

Still, Lumiela was basically a backliner, so it couldn't be helped.

She usually traveled by floating anyway.


'Just don't overdo it.'


'Mm... okay.'


Still, in the battle with Viridis of the Gale, I couldn't fly, so Lumiela had ended up handling the aerial close-quarters combat. Thinking that it might be safer if she steadily built up more stamina, I encouraged her.


'Anyway, I have to keep learning technique from Old Man Rowan today too. I feel like I've made a little progress.'


We crossed a hill and entered an animal trail.

The footing was bad, but I kept going without caring. Branches brushed my cheeks, I leaped over rocks, and I charged up the slope.


'Oh? You're climbing a mountain.'


'Yeah. That's today's goal. It'll also help me get my bearings. If you get lost, you can usually manage by climbing somewhere high.'


Before long, the trees thinned and my view opened up. The moment I reached the summit, the wind passed through my whole body. A town in the distance reflected the morning sun with a faint gleam. Pulling out the map from inside my clothes, I frowned over it and tried to judge from how long I had been running. Maybe it was that town?


On the way back, I reached a small hill and stopped. Dew glinted in the grass at my feet, and somewhere far away, little birds were singing.


"Let's rest a bit. We'll have breakfast here."


After checking that there was no one else around, I spoke aloud instead of using telepathy.


"Breakfast! I'm starving too!"


The sun rose over the mountain ridge.

The wind passed by, warm in a gentle way, quiet and peaceful.

Perfect picnic weather.

Just standing here together like this somehow made my heart feel full.


When I sat down on a rock atop the hill, the core of my body gradually calmed too. My heartbeat settled.

The wind stirred my hair, carrying with it the faintly sweet scent of grass.

I opened the bag on my back and pulled out the wrapped bundle inside. It contained sandwiches I'd bought from a bakery in the mining town early in the dark of dawn.

They were simple sandwiches: ham, lettuce, and just a little cheese between hard bread.

Only...

after all that running, they had gotten squashed, and the corners had fallen apart a bit.


"...Ahh, yep, the shape really did get ruined."


"But they smell great."


I handed one piece of sandwich to Lumiela.

She happily started munching away at it.


"Mm, it's a little squashed... but it tastes amazing!"


"Glad to hear it."


I took a bite too.

The salty ham went well with the crisp, fresh lettuce. The simple flavor sank pleasantly into the hunger that came after running.

On that quiet morning hill, the two of us sat side by side with softened cheeks.

This world was what it was...

but while we were like this, it felt gentle, if only a little.




--------------------------------------------------




By the time we finished breakfast, the hill had slowly begun to warm under the sunlight.

Thinking I'd do one last jog to settle my stomach, I stood up and lightly loosened my body.


"All right, maybe I'll try some new training today."


"Training? Ah, that strange thing you mentioned yesterday."


Lumiela tilted her head, and I gave her a grin.


"We need to train our coordination properly too. We're already decent as we are, but I want us to be even more in sync in actual combat."


"You're right, I agree. But are we really doing that?"


I nodded and pulled a cord from my bag.


"Yeah. The three-legged race."


"Hmm. Does this three-legged thing really count as coordination training?"


Lumiela looked puzzled, but she came over to stand right beside me.


"We'll run on my count."


"All right. This feels very strange."


I tied my left leg to Lumiela's right leg, and we both faced the same direction.


"Okay, here we go. Ready, and!"


The first step.

Slip.


"Kya!"


Lumiela lost her balance, and I hurriedly caught her with my hand. We both toppled together onto the grass, but since I ended up cushioning the fall underneath, Lumiela seemed fine.


I let out a breath and stood again. I could smell the grass.


"This time we'll go slowly. Let's match each other."


"Mm... okay!"


This time we started slow, calling out, "One, two. One, two," as we moved.

Even though it was awkward at first, I could feel our rhythm gradually starting to align. Little by little, I was getting a sense for the movements and timing I needed to match Lumiela's stride.


"Hey, I think this is... actually starting to be fun!"


Apparently Lumiela had grown fond of the three-legged race too, because she sounded delighted.

At the end, we slowly collapsed together onto the grass in a rolling heap, and under the blue sky the whole thing somehow struck us as so funny that we both burst out laughing.


Bit by bit, I could really feel us learning how to breathe together.


If we kept syncing up like this and perfected our coordination, then according to my knowledge of the original story, we should be able to reach a new stage.

The two of us together had to become even stronger than the original Brave's duo...

because otherwise, defeating the Demon King without the power of bonds would remain nothing but a dream beyond dreams.
