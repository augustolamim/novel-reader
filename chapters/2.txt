Joining Forces

Brave's Side






"This is... kind of embarrassing."


"You think so?"


Held in Lumiela's arms, Brave was flying through the sky.

To be honest, it was embarrassing. If he were flying proudly with his back straight, it might still look dignified, but the reality was different.

His sides were being supported, his posture was awkward, and every time the wind buffeted him and shook him around, his arms and legs flailed in an undignified way.


He had thought this would let him take part in an aerial battle, but apparently he had been too optimistic.


Still held in her arms, Brave's field of vision was thrown wildly in every direction. Up, down, right, left. Had they broken through the sound barrier? There was an incredible roar as well.

Lumiela was chasing after Viridis, who was desperately trying to escape.


"Here we go, Brave!"


Lumiela gave a short warning and made a sharp turn with her entire body.

There was a sense of his bones creaking and his brain being pressed inward.


An enemy figure swept past before his eyes.

As they crossed, Viridis sent a tornado spiraling at them. But Lumiela, as though she had foreseen everything, avoided it smoothly and accelerated.

Now that they had closed the distance, Brave tried firing Radiant Funeral, but even while coughing, Viridis reflexively twisted her body and dodged the beam along an unbelievable path, spraying bloody phlegm as she did. She looked like death itself fighting to survive.


The current situation was embarrassing, yes, but more importantly, he could not swing his sword properly. Even if he tried to move his arm, he could not put any strength into it while supported like this by Lumiela, and before he could bring the sword down, his whole body would be pulled away.

If he tried to swing with only one arm, his posture would grow even more unstable, and he might hinder Lumiela instead.

If they had been on the ground, planting his feet would have solved that. But floating in the air like this, he could not even keep track of his own center of gravity.


No matter his posture, if he could not use the sword, then he would fight mainly with magic.

They had already succeeded in tearing off all of Viridis's wings. By his estimate, her speed had dropped by about forty percent.


Lumiela's presence had not only made aerial combat possible in the first place, it had also increased the accuracy of his attacks.

The beams he fired used to fly only in straight lines.

If the target moved after the instant he aimed, that was the end of it.

Now, with Lumiela's control added, even after being fired, the beams could freely alter their course and pursue the target. They bent as though they had wills of their own, steadily cutting away every path of escape.

The same was true of the light bullets. They were no longer simple projectiles, but had transformed into something like funnels, attacking the target from every direction.

They chased a fleeing foe and steadily wore them down.


Lumiela's existence created a decisive difference from when Brave had fought alone.

She also possessed healing power that Brave himself lacked, and even in the midst of battle, she could weave light and close his wounds.

Their offense had improved, their defense had improved, and now recovery support had been added on top of that.

As far as he could tell, the optimal fighting style was finally taking shape. Brave's power, by fighting together with Lumiela, had become something close to complete.




--------------------------------------------------



Brave watched Viridis claw at the air with her one remaining arm, desperately forcing along a body that looked like a blood-soaked ruin, and was reminded just how tenacious demonkind could be.

Her figure was like the very embodiment of mad vitality.

Even bearing wounds that would have killed any human long ago, she still wrapped herself in wind, tried to flee, and struggled with obsessive determination to shake off their pursuit.


But even that could not last much longer.

The light bullet Brave fired, which Lumiela guided, punched cleanly through Viridis's abdomen.

Her insides spilled out. Blue blood gushed forth. A hole like a wind tunnel opened through her stomach.


Viridis's groan echoed, and her body staggered. She could not have much strength left to resist.

With one more move, this battle would end. Brave was sure of it.


"At last, we can finish her."


"Yeah... no. Looks like she's about to try something at the very end."


As if wringing out the last of her strength, Viridis raised her magic power to a peak.

Tornadoes that seemed to pierce the heavens spiraled up in layers. Before long, they formed a single enormous structure like a cocoon.

Like a cocoon protecting herself, her final fortress. A wall of violent wind that allowed not even a touch concealed Viridis within it.


Each time the cocoon of wind swelled, thin threads of air stretched from its outer shell. Those threads tangled with the giant tornadoes around it and, in an instant, were drawn into them.

Like ivy taking root and winding around a great tree, the winds of the cocoon bit into the flow of the tornadoes, forming knot after knot.

At first it had been no more than a faint wavering, but soon the boundary between cocoon and tornado blurred, and the two began to pulse in unison, amplifying each other like a gigantic heart beating.


That was not something ordinary attacks would break.

Judging so, Brave raised both hands toward the cocoon of tornadoes.

He would simply force out magical power. He would not think about control. There was no need for delicate handling or refined technique in this moment.

What he needed was one thing alone: output. He would pour out his vast magical power without hesitation and drive it all into a single point.


Feeling magical power racing through his entire body, Brave kept his hands raised.

And Lumiela, as though weaving light, applied control to it. She wrapped the torrent that stood on the verge of rampaging and gave that power form. If Lumiela had not been there, it would already have exploded out of control.


"Can you still go on?"

"Of course!"

"...All right. I'm counting on you for the control!"

"Leave it to me. All you need to think about is raising the output--!"


Those words pushed him on, and Brave forced out even more magic power.

He already knew the sensation. It was the reproduction of that blow he had once fired in anger by himself, the one that had blown away Viridis's leg. But now it was slightly different.

Lumiela was with him.


The air trembled.

The scenery around them distorted.

Light compressed, converged, and gave off a shriek at the tips of his hands.


"Brave... now!"


At Lumiela's cry, Brave poured all the strength in his body into a roar.


"Take thiiiiiis--!!"


And then he unleashed a gigantic beam of light from both hands.

The impact was like a white-hot warhorse charging through the sky in a single line, tearing the atmosphere apart and erasing sound itself.

It struck the cocoon of tornadoes head-on.

The cocoon was swallowed by light and wiped away.


Before he realized it, the tornadoes had stopped, and even the sound of the surrounding shell of wind had vanished.

The moment the tornadoes dispersed, the sky that had raged so wildly became crystal clear as though it had all been a lie.

The atmosphere that had been filled with rumbling and thunderous noise until moments ago now recovered a pure stillness, and even the wind brushed his cheeks gently.


Golden morning sunlight was peeking through breaks in the clouds.

The battle had surely begun at dawn, when the sky was still dim with lingering darkness, and yet before they knew it, the sun was already climbing above the eastern horizon.

That wind, cooling the body heated by battle, was so refreshing it felt almost like a reward, carrying fresh air all the way into his chest.


"...Did we win?"

　

"Yes. A splendid victory for us."


The words had slipped out like a mutter, but Lumiela caught them and answered.

Her voice was calm, but her eyes shone with pride.


Viridis, one of the Four Heavenly Kings, the Wind King, was nowhere to be seen.


"...Let's confirm it, just in case."


"You're cautious. All right. Let's go to where that cocoon was."


In the air, carrying the faint smell of something singed, he saw a black shape fluttering down at the edge of his vision.

At first he thought it might be Viridis herself.

But it was far too small. When they drew closer, he saw that it was a hand.


It was charred black, and its skin color and even its shape were hard to make out. Even the outlines of its fingers had gone vague. Beyond the shoulder there was nothing, as if any connection to an arm had been severed, and only the hand floated there, falling in obedience to gravity.


Why had only the hand remained?

Had it been the last part to peel away as the rest of her body vanished?

Or had the side she had turned away in an attempt to flee been swallowed first, leaving only the hand, farthest from the blast?


Whatever the reason, that miserable fragment quietly told the story of the end. There was no sign of Viridis herself. It was as if that hand were the final proof she had left behind in this world.

As he tracked its falling arc with his eyes, Brave said, "Let's follow it," and Lumiela nodded.


Quietly, Lumiela began to descend.

That blackened, shriveled hand fell ahead of them toward the misty ground below, like a guide.


Before long, the outline of the earth sharpened, and below them spread cratered land scarred by the battle, bathed in the morning sun.

Still holding the lingering aftertaste of battle in their hearts, the two of them descended toward the ground as though chasing after that falling remnant.


A wet, sickening sound, as though flesh and bone had been crushed, rang out faintly.

Viridis's hand had fallen and struck the earth.


Brave stared at that fragment for a while.

Why didn't it feel like he had won?

Maybe he had imagined a more dramatic finish.

But the reality was simply this: one leftover piece announcing the end. He did not feel any triumph--

but perhaps this was enough.


He straightened himself, then carried out the action he had wanted to take the moment they reunited.


"Eh?... W-what suddenly brought this on!?"


"Thank you, Lumiela."


He hugged Lumiela tightly.

She let out a troubled sound, but then placed a hand on his back.


He was happy that Lumiela had come back to him. And he was relieved and overjoyed that they had fought together, defeated one of the Four Heavenly Kings, and both come through safely, so he held her close.
