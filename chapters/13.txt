Episode 118: To Area 2







Mr. Rowan staggered a few steps unsteadily, then somehow managed to regain his footing.

I found myself thinking that maybe he really was just a drunk after all.


But then, the very next instant, Mr. Rowan tossed his liquor bottle straight up into the air.

Then he smoothly swept the sword through the air, slashing the bottle dozens of times in an instant as though merely brushing against it.

A sharp sound rang out, but the bottle was not cut clean in half...

it had only been shaved down ever so slightly. It was delicate swordsmanship.

Across the surface of the bottle that remained, fine, orderly cuts spread in swirling patterns, layered like overlapping flower petals.


'Oooh...!'


'A flower pattern... how skillful!'


Lumiela and I both cried out in surprise through telepathy.

Though there was a wildness in the blade's movement, there was also a kind of stillness to it, like the work of a sculptor carving a pattern into stone.


To throw something into the air and draw a design on it with a sword before it hit the ground...

it was an artistic level of skill I couldn't possibly imitate.

Before the bottle could fall and shatter on the floor, Mr. Rowan caught it.


"...That's incredible swordsmanship."


When he heard me mutter that without thinking, Mr. Rowan grinned with unconcealed pride.


"Right? Wahahaha!"


His booming laugh echoed through Area 1, and I could even hear applause. Everyone was looking at him with unmistakable respect.


"This, y'see, is the crowning achievement of my technique. And when you pull this off, young girls love it."


Mr. Rowan declared that proudly.

It was such a stupid reason that it almost made me want to take back my praise.


"That is such a waste of technique..."


"Heh heh. Don't say that. It makes for a fine goal, doesn't it?

And once you can do it, you won't just be popular, you'll be a master swordsman too. The way you handle the blade, the way you set your center of gravity, the way you close distance, all of it will have changed."


Mr. Rowan sounded amused, yet somehow serious as he said it.


"...Is that how it works? Well, one thing is clear enough. Your skill is incredible, Mr. Rowan. I'd really like you to teach me swordsmanship."


"Good. Then it's settled!"


I shook hands with Mr. Rowan.

After introducing myself as "Jane" while we shook hands, Mr. Rowan said, "Here," and held out the sword I had lent him.

I took it back and looked down at it. Up until now, all I'd ever done was swing a sword around recklessly.

But now the time had come for me to truly learn the sword and make those techniques my own, and that thought left me feeling strangely moved.


"Now then, let's talk about your training. Where should we do it..." Mr. Rowan said, stroking his chin as he thought it over.


When it came to starting my training, I said that it would be best to do it somewhere as far from prying eyes as possible.

I told him that if I wanted to focus on honing myself, it would be better without people watching or making noise around us.

In the end, we decided to leave this underground base and train outside the coal-mining town of Rifat. It was a fair distance away, but running there would make good endurance training too.


"So what do you say? Want to start training now? I mostly spend my time drinking, so I'm free. Any time I'm not hung over is fine. Even right now."


I gave that some thought.

But for the moment, another curiosity won out.


"I'd like to start training right away if I could... but this is my first time here today. I want to have a look at Area 2 and Area 3 first."


"...Huh?"


Mr. Rowan's eyes went wide and he froze at my words.

Had I said something that strange?


"Are you serious? Area 2 is one thing, but Area 3... Even I find that place disturbing."


"Is it really that bad?"


"Bad doesn't even begin to cover it. It's a cauldron of madness. If you go in there, there's no guarantee you'll ever come back out."


"That bad...?"


"Well, whatever. With two of us, we should manage somehow. I suppose I'll show you around."


With that, Mr. Rowan turned his back and started walking.

His steps wavered in a drunken zigzag. So this man was going to be the first master of my life.

I followed after him.


'Now your swordsmanship is going to get better too, Brave!'


'I hope so. I don't exactly think I have any talent for the sword, so who knows how it'll turn out... Still, I'll do my best.'





--------------------------------------------------




Area 2.

The air was different there. It should have been about as bright as Area 1, yet somehow it felt dimmer. Probably because the atmosphere itself was darker.

The air felt sticky.

And on top of that, I could smell blood.


Everywhere I looked as I walked down the street, there were people with nasty faces, the sort who looked like criminals.

One man had a prosthetic arm. Another had half his face wrapped in bandages. A bald man had something like a tattoo across his back.

All of them had sharp, dangerous eyes.


"So this is... Area 2."


I quietly muttered that while keeping my breathing steady, careful not to speak too loudly.


'This place feels dangerous in every possible way. It doesn't seem like somewhere we should stay long.'


'But if we're looking for good magical tools, a place like this might actually have some.'


I walked on while discussing it with Lumiela.

Mr. Rowan, walking ahead of me, surveyed the area around us with complete confidence.


"Let's see... last time I came around here, there was a diner with some good liquor in stock. Wonder if it's still here. Oh, there it is. Well, Jane? Want a drink too?"


Saying that, Mr. Rowan staggered right into one of the shops.

From inside I could hear drunks shouting at one another, along with the sound of something breaking. I had an absolutely terrible feeling about this.


When I stepped inside too, I doubted my own eyes.

It was such an obviously shady place that it looked like I was about to get ripped off the moment I sat down. The owner had tattoos on his arms and dark circles under his eyes. A complete outlaw. Every last customer was a thug. On top of that, they were looking our way and grinning.


Mr. Rowan sat down without a care in the world, but he had nerves of steel.

The customers around us began standing up from their chairs and cracking their knuckles. It felt like a fight could break out at any moment.

Thinking there was no helping it, I braced myself mentally.


That was when it happened.


"--Residents of Area 3 are coming! Stay on guard!"


A sharp shout rang through the room.

The diner fell silent in an instant. The thug customers all went pale.

Someone dropped the bottle they were holding, and it shattered with a crash.


"That damn mohawk gang and an S-rank adventurer are here!!"


Outside the shop, I could see through the window a thug with bloodshot eyes yelling that out between ragged breaths.


Mr. Rowan began speaking slowly.

He said that the ones in Area 3 were all madmen who had long since thrown away their reason.

People who sharpened blades while laughing, people who kept shouting incomprehensible words, people who wore the smell of blood like perfume.

It was a place like the end of the world, ruled by nothing but madness and strength, with neither order nor rules.


And apparently, the residents of Area 3 would sometimes show up in the other areas.

No one knew why. They appeared on a whim, walked down the streets laughing, things were broken, people were struck down. It was apparently common for several people to vanish from that day onward.

Even while humanity was on the verge of being wiped out by the Demon King, people were still fighting among themselves as naturally as ever. The thought left me exasperated.
