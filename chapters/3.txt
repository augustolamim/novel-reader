Toward a Happy Ending for the Two of Us






　


First, stay close and keep her anxiety from overflowing. That was the first step of persuasion available to me.


"Why... would you do something so dangerous... challenge one of the Four Heavenly Kings?"


"To prove it. ...I want you to live, Lumiela."


I said exactly what I felt.

Lumiela's shoulders twitched.


"To show you that I'd even fight one of the Four Heavenly Kings for that. Maybe I had nightmares and only showed you pathetic sides of myself, but I wanted to tell you that it's really going to be okay."


Lumiela listened in silence.


"I want to stay with you forever, Lumiela. Greg and the others are my friends, but they can't replace you."


"But..."

　

Lumiela started to say something, as if struggling to force the words out, but she fell silent again.

I grew a little impatient. I had to get these feelings that had sprouted inside me across to her, no matter what.

I couldn't find the right words.

She mattered to me. Lumiela, who had helped me again and again, simply mattered too much now.

And when I thought of all the burdens she had carried, my chest hurt enough to feel as if it were tearing apart.


"If there's something hard for you to say, it's okay. Just tell me slowly."


"..."

　

As I said that, I patted Lumiela on the back with the hand I had placed there.


"It's okay, Lumiela. If it's the two of us, we'll manage somehow."


If it was the two of us, we ought to be able to do most things.

But--


"...We can't."

　

Lumiela murmured weakly. I could hear the trembling breath that escaped her.


"It's impossible, Brave... The Demon King creates eternal darkness, endless night, over the lands he rules. And if that happens, I won't be able to bask in sunlight anymore. I'll gradually weaken, and in the end, I'll disappear."


I froze in shock.

There had been nothing about that in my knowledge of the original story.

Though maybe the original Brave never had the option of not fighting, so the route where he chose not to fight had simply never been explored in depth.


Even so... that had to be what it meant.

If things stayed as they were, Lumiela would eventually disappear even if we kept running.

Unless the Demon King was defeated.


I trembled at that truth.


"Isn't there... any other way? Some way for you not to disappear?"

　

My voice shook as I asked, praying there would be one.


"...No. That's why... even if I run away with you, the time when we have to part will still come. And I'll leave you alone in a world ruled by the demons..."


Every time her voice broke, my chest tightened painfully. I held Lumiela even more tightly.

　

Silently, I thought.

I had to defeat the Demon King.

It took me a little time to properly swallow that fact.


Even while I thought, Lumiela kept speaking.

She told me that even if we kept running, we could never stay together forever. She wanted me to imagine the world after she vanished someday. If so, then maybe I could still be happy, living on with my friends inside the birdcage called Yuren.

The price would be Lumiela's life, but she was fated to disappear eventually anyway, sooner or later, so I didn't need to worry about it--that was how she spoke to the weak part of my heart.


Inside me, my desire to save Lumiela fought against my cowardice.

But the outcome was obvious.

The answer had already been decided.

I just needed time to cut away hesitation.

　

What rose in my mind were Lumiela's gestures, her warmth, the fragments of all the times she had saved me.

All of it took shape as something I had to protect. My thoughts were still messy, but they converged into a single line.


"Then I..."


Yes--there are times when a person has to do what must be done.


"I'll defeat the Demon King."


Now was that time.

Even if my opponent was the Demon King, even if he was far stronger than I was, that was all the more reason I couldn't give up.




--------------------------------------------------

　

　


"You can't... y-you can't do that!!"

　

Lumiela's voice shook, almost becoming a scream. She beat at my chest and twisted desperately, trying to push me away.

She was afraid, for my life in that battle, as if it were her own.

The body I held struggled. Every time her slender arms fought to break free, the pain in her heart came through to me.

But I didn't let go.

I put strength into my arms and held Lumiela tightly.

　

"I'll beat the Demon King. I swear I will."

　

I swore it.

No matter how much Lumiela rejected it, I couldn't turn away from this path. Not if I wanted to protect her.

My resolve came quietly.

Not as a shout, but as certainty spreading outward from within. Before, I had only thought about defeating the Demon King up until the point I decided to leave that role to Cain.

After that, I had never truly thought I could do it.


But now, for the first time, I seriously thought:

I'll kill the Demon King.

I'll sever him at the root.

Not out of duty, or because it was supposedly the mission of a hero, but by my own will.

　

My fear hadn't vanished.

But even while carrying that fear, I could still move forward.

Lumiela gave no answer to that vow for a while.

She only trembled.


"Brave... you're shaking."


At those words, I caught my breath for just a moment.

I had thought only Lumiela was trembling, but apparently I was too, without realizing it.

Only then did I become aware that my own body was indeed shaking in fine tremors.

Was it some primal fear welling up from the depths of my chest?

Even so, I couldn't admit it.

I answered shortly.


"...Battle fever."


My voice was stiff, forced out almost by sheer pressure.

I said it as though trying to rally myself, turning the trembling into strength by denying what it was. Fine. Let this be battle fever at the thought of defeating the Demon King.

　

"It might hurt terribly."

　

Whether she saw through the lie or not, Lumiela warned me.


"I'm prepared."

　

I pressed my lips together tightly. As if shoving down the unease smoldering in my throat, I solidified that single resolve.


"...You might die."

　

Lumiela's voice as she spoke of that possibility was small and hoarse. Of course I was afraid of dying. But I had no intention of throwing my life away.


"I'll do everything I can to avoid dying.

I have no intention of dying. Not before I defeat the Demon King--and not after, either. If the country or the nobles learn who I really am and try to execute me, then I'll crush them too, even if they're human."

　

I had no intention of taking the straightforward route.

My knowledge of the original story, the possibility of coordinated fighting with Lumiela, obtaining the Holy Sword--I still had room to grow stronger.

Using all of that, I'd gain enough real power to defeat the Demon King, and I'd prepare thoroughly.

　

The moment Lumiela raised her face and our eyes met, time seemed to slow as if its flow had been warped. It should have been only a few seconds, yet it felt as long as being trapped in eternity.

My chest tightened, making it hard to breathe, and a squeezing pain twisted deep in my stomach.


At last, Lumiela's small voice slipped out.

　

"...You wouldn't be happy with my plan, would you, Brave?"


"No... I wouldn't. I can't be happy unless I'm with you, Lumiela."


At that question, I answered with a deep nod.


"Tell me, are you really... sure?"

　

She asked again. I gave the same answer to the repeated words, but Lumiela still seemed uncertain.


"Yeah. Lumiela... you look scared."


"Of course I am. Your life is on the line, Brave..."


At this rate, it felt like we were still one final step short.

I found the words that could persuade her, though I knew I was forcing my will on her too much.


"I think I can somehow create a chance to win... but if, right before we fight the Demon King, you decide that victory is absolutely impossible, then I'll accept an ending like being spirited away. How about that?"

　

"I... might lie and say we can't win."

　

At her words, I let out a breath that almost sounded like a laugh.


"Then I'll notice again."


"...All right. On that condition, I'll accept it too. But! Don't you dare start thinking you'll win even if you die! Just like you can't be happy all alone, I can't be happy all alone either!"


"Of course. Then--"

　

Let's begin.

Let's begin our attempt to seize a happy future for Brave and Lumiela.

　

"Let's aim for a happy ending for the two of us!"


Sacrifices would be unavoidable.

We wouldn't be able to save everything, and there would surely be things we'd have to throw away.

The two of us would live, and we would win.

The question was what we would abandon, and what we would protect.

Our own lives came first.

Not the world.

So if nobles or nations weren't necessary for our happy ending, then we'd abandon them too.


Either way, the fight ahead would be harsh. We'd be busy.

First we'd leave this place, make arrangements to obtain the Holy Sword, and gain whatever kind of secret technique? of coordination existed with Lumiela--■■■■.

And if there were any magical tools out there that could serve as a joker in our hand, we'd need to seek them out and gather those too.


"All right, Lumiela. Let's go."

"Wait... let me stay like this a little longer."


Lumiela still wouldn't let me go while I held her. Her arms were wrapped around my back.

I wanted to grant Lumiela's wishes as much as I could, so I continued hugging her just like that.


"It's about time."

"A little longer."


I kept holding her.

Bit by bit, embarrassment and heat started to fill my chest over the words I had blurted out earlier. I felt like my face was going to catch fire. Maybe I had tried to act too cool.


From now on, we'd do our best together.
