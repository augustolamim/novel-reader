At some point Lumiela had come to stand beside me. She really was fast.

Though this wasn't exactly a situation where I appreciated that speed...

Holding the wooden ladle, Lumiela looked over at me as I gently stirred the pot.

I had taken my mask off too, on the logic that there wasn't going to be anyone else climbing snowy mountains out here. Maybe she'd seen from my expression that the hot pot had come out with the salt at just the right level.

"Hey. What should we call today's hot pot?"

"...Let's see. We'll go with 'Loaded Hot Pot with Perfect Salt Balance.'"

As I traced a circle along the bottom, the flavor of the meat rose with a soft puff of scent, reaching even my nose dulled by the cold.

"...All right. Let it simmer a little more and we can eat."

Inside the pot, the bubbles danced and gurgled. Scum had started to rise too, drifting like white smoke as it mixed with the cold air over the snow.

With nothing else to do while I stirred, my mind wandered to something pointless.

--If only we had frozen food.

Retort curry. Convenience stores. Supermarkets.

Meals you could finish just by heating them in a microwave.

Of course, none of that existed in this world. And there was no microwave here to heat anything with.

Still, even knowing that, I couldn't help longing for it.

My repertoire was dead. Literally.

No frozen meals, no curry roux, no little packets of dashi stock. In this world, all my cooking experience had been shoved entirely into salt.

While I was indulging in a little self-mockery, the pot reached a good point.

It had simmered nicely, and the ingredients had softened.

When I ladled portions into our wooden bowls, thick steam rose up, and in the middle of that white world, the smell of something alive spread clearly through the air.

I set two wooden bowls side by side.

I dipped the wooden ladle into the steaming pot and first poured the clear broth into the bowls.

The surface quivered, and white steam billowed up. Then I carefully scooped up the meat and vegetables that had settled on the bottom.

The softened venison fell apart at a touch, and the vegetables had taken on pale color, enough in this cold to look like a feast all by themselves.

When I added them to the bowls, our simple but warm meal finally took shape.

The instant the steam touched my face, the frozen core of my body gradually began to loosen.

With my resistance--or maybe just my physical specs--I wasn't going to freeze to death or anything, but cold was still cold.

I handed a wooden bowl to Lumiela, and the preparations for eating were complete.

"All right, then. Let's eat."

"Yes. It's your finest masterpiece yet, Brave. We have to eat it while it's hot!"

Unable to wait, Lumiela took the bowl and immediately put it to her lips.

"...Delicious...! Just like the name says, the salt balance is perfect."

With her eyes wide, Lumiela said it with genuine delight.

It took a fair amount of work to make, but moments like this made me glad I'd done it.

"There's more if you want seconds."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

While glancing over at Lumiela as she ate, I picked up my own wooden bowl and carefully scooped up a bite.

Inside it, the meat and vegetables rocked gently. A thin film of venison fat had melted into the clear salt broth, scattering tiny flecks of light across the surface. The smell rising with the steam did, without question, stir the appetite.

--It looks good, at least.

But the moment the faux vegetables hit my mouth, they turned into something else entirely. The texture was oddly hard, and when I bit down there was a faint creak. Maybe the butterbur-sprout lookalike really was something meant for tempura or the like, and just didn't suit this at all.

"...Well, at least it's edible."

I muttered it like I was trying to convince myself. The only saving grace was that the meat's flavor had seeped into the soup; the vegetables themselves made me feel less like I was eating a meal and more like I was some animal taking in fuel.

Still, warmth didn't betray you. When I drank a second mouthful of broth, blood flowed properly all the way to my fingertips, and the trembling in my back stopped completely.

Even bland vegetables were probably enough to keep the nutrition somewhat balanced, and that was good enough.

The broth had the best salt balance we'd managed so far.

The venison still had a bit of that gamey taste left in it, but it was better than the vegetables.

Still, it had been salt for quite a while now. Salt yesterday. Salt today. Probably salt tomorrow too. Occasionally a different seasoning.

If I were even hungrier, it would probably feel like a feast, but right now it was starting to get a little rough.

When I glanced sideways, Lumiela had steam on her face and was happily munching away at the vegetables.

That smile... maybe she was forcing it for my sake. While I was watching her like that, Lumiela swallowed a bite of vegetables and turned her eyes to me.

"It really is good."

The words came out so smoothly that I blinked.

When I stared in surprise, Lumiela went on, her cheeks faintly flushed by the steam.

"I realized something. Taste matters, but the process matters too."

"The process, huh..."

Only Lumiela's voice sounded soft in the cold mountain air.

"I mean... I get that, but this isn't exactly..."

When I said that with a wry smile, Lumiela wrapped both hands around her wooden bowl and lowered her voice as if she were about to say something important.

"Brave. Do you remember? Back when we went treasure hunting with Edelweiss and the others... the food from that village you made the thieving woman eat on the way?"

"Ghk!"

Of all things, she had to bring that up now.

The worst possible memory came rushing back into my head through the steam from the soup.

Sweets swarming with flies, cheese gnawed on by rats, juice a middle-aged man had stepped in, soup cooked with an old woman's elbow submerged in it, bread a village woman had warmed in her armpit--that village's food stalls.

Not the sort of thing you wanted to remember in the middle of eating. My stomach tightened.

"That village's food... the thieving woman thought it was delicious, because she didn't know anything. But we knew the process, so there was no way we could bring ourselves to eat it. Even if she said it was tasty."

"She did end up with a stomachache, too... I'd hesitate before eating that stuff..."

"Exactly. That was an extreme example, but that's why I came to think the process matters."

Lumiela leaned closer and looked straight into my eyes.

"Your 'loaded salt hot pot' has slightly strange vegetables and all... but the process behind it was a very good one. That's why it's delicious."

After saying that, Lumiela peered down into the bottom of her bowl, then silently looked at me.

"I'm getting seconds!"

Happily, she filled another bowl and started eating again after blowing on it to cool it.

"You really had to use that village as your example... That gives it some weird persuasive power."

A wry laugh slipped out of me.

The process behind that village's food had been an absolute disaster. The taste, if the thieving woman was to be believed--a woman who seemed exactly like the sort to complain and be picky about everything--had apparently been great.

Compared to that, my cooking was... probably a good process and a mediocre taste.

Maybe it was like the food you make in home economics class, chatting and laughing with everyone while you cook, and even if the result isn't amazing, it somehow feels special all the same.

Still... because she'd compared it to that village, I couldn't quite tell whether I'd been praised or not.

That said, Lumiela's words were probably sincere.

We searched for ingredients together in the snowy mountains, shivered through the cold while getting everything ready, simmered the pot, warmed our fingers in the steam... she was probably counting all of that as part of why it tasted good.

I picked up my bowl and gently scooped up some of the salt hot pot.

As I brought the carved wooden spoon to my mouth, I thought again about what Lumiela had said.

--Taste matters, but the process matters too.

Maybe that was true.

Thinking that, I took another bite.

"Then maybe it is delicious."

Muttering it to myself, I tasted it again, slowly.

The savor of the meat, the mild bitterness of the vegetables, and the gentle taste of salt spread across my tongue... and somehow it really did taste better than before.

Just thinking about the process could change the flavor a little. I found that strangely moving. Separate from the warmth of the pot, it felt like my heart had warmed too.

At the foot of the sacred peak, with no human settlement in sight and monsters all around, it felt strange to be sitting here sharing one pot with someone.

Beyond the snow, the unseen summit of the sacred peak must have been rising there. Beyond that waited the sage and the Holy Sword Idea.

I knew what we had to do from here too. I couldn't think about it in any especially positive way, but...

At least for now, in this white world, there was a warm pot and a gentle spirit beside me.

That alone was more than enough reason to keep walking.
