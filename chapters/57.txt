Brave's memories were full of surprises.

There was so much of them that seeing everything would have taken time, so she skipped through and looked only at what seemed important.

Lumiela knew already.

That Brave thought of Lumiela as a precious partner. Watching it made her feel so happy and embarrassed that her face could have turned bright red.

From feelings like that--
down to little things like that Famichiki joke.

As she traced the memories back, the Brave there knew neither swords nor blood. Unfamiliar buildings. Hard paved roads. A city bright even at night. A mechanical world more colorful than any paper book.

There was no magic there, and no spirits either. It was a world of humans alone.

Laughing, hesitating, being surprised. Living utterly ordinary emotions in an utterly ordinary way.

Another world.

Even if she wanted to deny it, the world of that previous life was far too concrete, too precise, too real. Its culture, its values, even the way emotions flowed through daily life--it was too vivid to dismiss as something fabricated.

In that world, ordinary people almost never had any chance to kill their own kind. Nearly everyone obeyed law and ethics.

A person's values do not change easily. Those things were common sense inside Brave, and crossing that line made him hesitate.

That was why Brave had not wanted to kill people.

--I really can't let Brave stain his hands after all...

The tale of another world was endlessly fascinating, but she set that aside for the moment.

The real issue was, after all... reincarnation from another world.

The fact that this world had been told in the form of a "manga" had been another shock. The idea that the world itself had been nothing more than a work of fiction was certainly jarring.

Even so, the current Brave had not existed in that manga, and neither had that hateful piece of oversized trash called Cain.

If so, perhaps it was best to treat them as separate things and not think too hard about it. Thinking about it would change nothing.

Honestly, part of her thought he could have told her much sooner.

According to the original story knowledge, the Demon King had been even stronger than the one Lumiela knew, and there had even been a plan for how they could power up on this side if Brave and Lumiela truly cooperated.

It made her feel sad, as though she had been left behind.

But the feelings and thoughts that followed in Brave's memories swept that feeling away.

Not just the memories themselves, but what he had thought at the time mattered too.

That so-called original Brave from the manga. That original Brave really did resemble the previous hero.

Brave had been afraid.

Afraid of being compared to that "original" Brave.

Afraid that if Lumiela knew both the manga's idealized true hero and his own ordinary self, she might think it would have been better if he had never reincarnated at all.

--............ I need to tell him properly later.

The feeling came through with painful clarity.

That was why he had been unable to say it.

Why he had not wanted to say it.

She understood the logic. Perhaps it really could not be helped.

But Lumiela still felt a little irritated. She wanted to tell him not to be ridiculous. Even if they could not know the deepest parts of each other's hearts, Lumiela was not such a heartless spirit.

Lumiela tightly gripped the amber necklace at her chest, Brave's gift.

After she finished seeing these memories and feelings of his, she would send her own feelings back to Brave in return. She would make him see just how precious he was to her.

Also, she would never forget that when he had first met her, he had seen her eating manju and thought her stomach was a black hole.

She would absolutely have to press him on that from now on as well.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

She had already understood the other memories. Brave's mysteries, the business about being reincarnated--she understood the circumstances.

She also knew what she should say to Brave from here on, and what she should do.

All that remained was to look a little more closely at the memories of the original story.

Watching them made her feel terribly sorry for Brave.

A peaceful world in his previous life.

There were conflicts there too, but at the very least it was not a world overflowing with the despair of humanity standing on the brink of extinction.

He had no power to decide the fate of the world. He could have drifted through life in peace.

Compared to that, this world was irredeemably awful.

Brave was unfortunate. Because of that filth named Cain, he had been thrown into mortal danger almost as soon as he reincarnated.

As if it were not enough that he had already been forced to fight a mad Empire that wanted war and the most vicious Demon King of all. The fact that the human side was not wholly on his side either, rotten nobles included, made her furious.

And that panda princess Ceras in the original, always ordering people around and lecturing them with nothing but pretty ideals--from Lumiela's point of view, who did she think she was, looking down on everyone like that?

Of course nobody would want a world that burdened him with a load he had never asked for and forced him to cross the line of death again and again.

If only that thing called Cain had at least been able to defeat the Demon King, then he could have gone off and played at his little harem or whatever he liked. Instead he had merely dragged down a valuable fighting force.

And on top of that, in Yuren he had taken that condescending, utterly patronizing attitude toward Brave. Guilty.

How had the filth of the world even dared to show his face like that? Going by Brave's memories, it would have been better to sink him in Tokyo Bay.

What remained was only the suffering of Brave nearly starving to death, and a world even more hopeless than the original.

It made her even angrier that Cain, together with the Kingdom's organization Soleil, had died and escaped into the next world.

To begin with, Brave may have been reluctant to fight, but this world felt all but destined to perish anyway.

The world depicted in the manga had been the best possible world, blessed with outrageous luck.

When she looked through the original story's knowledge, there were several places where people who seemed dead had actually not suffered fatal wounds and conveniently turned out to be alive. Original Brave had been saved by pure convenience as well.

A world rescued only because miracles piled one atop another, because someone always arrived in time, because only then, at last, it could be saved.

As a story, it was beautiful. But as reality, it felt far too convenient.

And yet in that original story not a single member of the previous hero's party had died, even though in reality most of them had been killed by the Demon King. That was simply strange.

If they had won through some elaborate scheme, maybe it would have made sense. But they had challenged him head-on, which made even less sense.

Perhaps the way this world had turned out was more natural instead.

A world where the odds of salvation were vanishingly small.

Which was exactly why, even if this world were to perish because Brave had reincarnated into it, would that really be so different from anything else?

Perhaps she only thought that because she was a spirit, because her feelings leaned too far toward Brave.

But if she said it, it might at least ease his heart a little, so she would tell him later.

Lastly--she also peeked at Brave's plan for defeating the Demon King, and it was seriously dangerous. He seemed to be thinking through several approaches in parallel, but every one of them was reckless.

...She had thought his magic power had simply gone down without her noticing because he used a lot, but this was what he had been doing.

She was starting to think maybe it really would be better to turn Yuren into a little boxed garden. To save him from a world this unhappy--

"This world isn't unhappy."

Suddenly, that feeling flowed in from Brave.

He had met Lumiela.

That had meaning.

That was what he was telling her.

Something tightened painfully in the depths of her chest.

Almost like speaking to herself, Lumiela answered too.

"I feel the same.... So I'll do everything I can to reach the best possible outcome."

The best possible outcome.

Would it be the best thing to slaughter these soldiers and nobles, nothing but illusions that had killed the previous hero?

If they were real, that would be one thing. But illusions.

It would mean nothing. It would only be taking her anger out on them. All it would do is make Lumiela feel better.

--Let's put an end to this!
