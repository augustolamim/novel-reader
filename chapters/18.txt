The Empire's Operation (1)







The metal apparatus hummed low, and green liquid pulsed through the tubes crawling across the floor.


Gearnote had piled countless monster encyclopedias across his desk. Their pages were filled with illustrations of distorted limbs and organs.

The shape of fangs, the location of magic cores, the paths of blood circulation--

he compared them all, considering what combinations would produce the most efficient fusion.

Everything was for the sake of creating stronger synthetic beasts.


On the parchment spread across his desk, Gearnote scribbled down each idea the instant it came to him. The handwriting was disordered, even the grammar had collapsed.

His hand moved before thought could stop. Sheets he had ruined were scattered over the floor like cursed doodles.


"Working hard as ever, I see, Gearnote."


A low voice broke the silence of the laboratory.


Gearnote stopped his hand and turned.

In the pale glow of the magical lamps stood a man in military uniform.

His sharply lifted eyes were foxlike in their impression. His thin lips were always pressed into a cold line.

His black military coat was fastened neatly all the way up to the collar, and his back was straight as a blade.

And then--

above his head, a pair of gray wolf ears twitched.

Though human, he was clearly something inhuman. A middle form between synthetic beast and man, known as a beastman.


"Of course I am. If we don't do something about the demons, humanity has no future, General Ubel..."


When Gearnote answered in a tired voice, the fox-eyed man--General Ubel--let a faint smile touch the edge of his lips.


"Hah hah hah. Quite right."


General Ubel laughed.

Gearnote glanced at the general's clothing. Some dark purple upper garment from a certain monster, what was likely a lion's mane on the shoulders, decorations at the waist seemingly carved from dragon bone fragments.

It was clothing so distinctive one could confidently say there was not another like it in all the world.


"As always, your clothes are made of excellent materials."


He knew how to choose his words.

Gearnote knew very well what kind of man General Ubel was.


"Indeed they are. Today's outfit is--"


As though he had been waiting for that very cue, General Ubel thrust out his chest and began speaking with delight.

It seemed to be his pride that he never made clothes from anything except the materials of monsters he had killed with his own power.

Though he sighed inwardly, Gearnote smiled and nodded along.


"Truly magnificent, General. Yes, attire perfectly worthy of a general of the Empire."


"Hah hah hah! When you put it like that, I can't say I mind!"


His laughter rang through the room.

Gearnote was just wondering whether he ought to add two more compliments, when General Ubel abruptly stopped laughing. His face had turned serious.


"Enough flattery. More importantly, would you care to come watch the slaughter show courtesy of Phase 3, which you created? It is likely a chance you will never have again. I came to invite you."


General Ubel delivered that short statement and turned his back.

Even so, Gearnote understood that it was an order to follow.


As he trailed after the general, Gearnote unconsciously straightened the collar of his lab coat.


Before long, they arrived outside a room with a heavy door.

On that door was engraved:

Observation Room.

When they stepped inside, mirror-like devices lined across the walls lit up one after another. They showed scenes from across the Empire. Distant cities, battlefields, the outer walls of research facilities.


There existed an expensive magical tool called a paired mirror.

Through a matching mirror, one could exchange voices and appearances with someone far away.

The other person's face would appear upon the surface, along with their expressions and even the details of their surroundings. The technology originated in an ancient civilization, but in recent years it had been deciphered and could now be reproduced.

Still, because of the immense cost, it had once seemed impossible.

And yet now, with the Empire under emergency conditions, the whole nation had united--and because it was an emergency, it had managed to make the impossible real. A bitter irony.

But through that, they had created the very eyes of the Empire--

a system through which the movements of the whole nation could be surveyed at a glance.


If Gearnote and the others were the unit responsible for creating synthetic beasts, then the magical-tool unit was working to win the information war that would come after their military power had been strengthened.


"Kuheheh, this technology. It's fair to say we've pulled far ahead of the Kingdom. Wonderful, isn't it?"


"Hmph. I no longer even spare the Kingdom a glance."


There was pride in his voice.

Without hesitation, General Ubel walked to the center of the room--to a luxurious leather chair placed at the best vantage point.

Then he dropped himself into it as though it were his throne. A heavy sound rang out, and the leather creaked faintly. Next he crossed one leg over the other and draped an arm over the armrest.

The pose was overbearing, full of the ease of a strong man who would not yield a single step before anyone.


Gearnote stopped in place and stared at one particular scene reflected in the mirrors.

There, vast swarms of human-faced flies were streaking freely across the city of Aibelk, implanting eggs into demons and quite literally trampling the place flat.

It really was, in a certain sense, a magnificent sight.

General Ubel looked deeply pleased.


Apparently they had attached one half of paired mirrors to human-faced flies flying high overhead, ones not participating in battle.

Strong though they were, this could never count as human evolution.

They retained only the human element of the face. They were nothing more than insects that had lost the wonderful human capacity for thought and all else besides.


Then Gearnote noticed ten demons escaping from the city of Aibelk.


"There are surviving demons. Will they not be disposed of?"


"No. For the sake of a certain operation."




--------------------------------------------------




"Now then, where should I begin?"


General Ubel leaned deep into the backrest and murmured in his heavy voice. Lightly tapping the armrest with his fingertips, he slowly raised his chin. His gaze rested on a faintly glowing paired mirror.


"This is the result of making effective use of the hero of foresight."


Gearnote followed General Ubel's gaze to the paired mirror and frowned.

He had no desire whatsoever to watch the breeding activities of Cain, the hero of foresight. In the mirror, Cain sat in a lavish room, surrounded by many women and voraciously devouring equally lavish food.


"Thanks to him, we were able to study the mentality of the enemy. You really did pick up something valuable, Gearnote."


That satisfied voice rang low and made the air in the room tremble slightly.


"...Come to think of it, I hadn't seen Cain lately, but to think he was simply shut up in his room."


Cain's personality was abysmal.

He had a habit of taking his frustrations out on others, toying with researchers and spreading ill temper wherever he went. Gearnote had considered killing him as a nuisance, but Cain occasionally let slip fragments of genuinely useful information, which made him troublesome to handle.

Since it had become annoying, Gearnote had left him to General Ubel. Cain's fate rested entirely in the general's hands.


"All it took was giving him a room, women, and food to quiet him down. He must have become satisfied with his present life. Since we need nothing from him except the memories in his brain, there is no issue."


As he said that, General Ubel pulled a heavy golden pipe from inside his coat and slowly lit it.

Its tip glowed red, and white smoke drifted silently upward. The scent was sweet, yet somehow tinged with a metallic coldness.

Watching the smoke he exhaled, General Ubel smiled thinly. The drifting haze sharpened that cold smile further as it spiraled upward and dissolved into the darkness near the ceiling, almost as if sketching the shape of his thoughts.


"That's surprising. I assumed you would kill him."


"If we had a magical tool capable of extracting memories, I would kill him immediately."


Gearnote nodded. "I see."

Memory extraction--

if such a thing could be realized, they could completely pull out all the fragments Cain had stockpiled. But that device remained a dream, still unable to overcome the wall of design.

Of course, if the research advanced far enough, perhaps even such a magical tool could be made--


"That would be difficult, especially at a time like this."


Gearnote shrugged.

Research resources were being prioritized toward the war situation and the synthetic beast project. There was no surplus to be had.


"Exactly. Right now, we have no time left to spend on such unnecessary things, Gearnote."


General Ubel answered in a tone of certainty. His eyes had already shifted away from Cain's image and back to the mirror showing the battle in Aibelk.


"Cain might end up living quite a while after all."


Gearnote voiced the prediction with a mischievous note.


"Who knows. Either way is fine. So long as his information remains useful, let him indulge himself. Give him a room, women, food at random, and he leaks information about the Four Heavenly Kings and the occasional useful bit of knowledge."


A room, women, and food--

vulgar, simple things, but more than enough to keep a creature enslaved by desire docile until it died of old age.

Cain had likely been snared with laughable ease.

General Ubel had judged that preserving him as an information source was the better choice, rather than paying the risk and effort of disposing of him immediately.


Gearnote inwardly appreciated that cold ruthlessness.

Any amount of human damage caused by Cain could be offered up as sacrifice if it served the coming war against the demons.

That was what it meant.
