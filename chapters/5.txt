Neither Demon nor Human Matters


Typhon's Side






The sounds of battle ceased, replaced by laughter.

Lady Re was laughing happily.

Azure flames scorched the sky as though painting over the pale light of dawn. Neither red nor orange, that bluish flicker of fire was a blaze born from a single human being.


Lady Re was laughing.

The fact that she could laugh, that she could still stand, was proof that she lived.

Retzel held both hands high above her head, and in that gesture there was less a display of victory than a kind of ecstatic joy.


"Ahh, that really was... fun."


Her voice was sweet, melting, almost like the whisper of a maiden drunk on love.

How strange.

What floated across her beautiful face was not bloodstained madness, but simply the lovely smile of a maiden.


What those raised hands held was the head of the Wind Four Heavenly King.

Everything below the neck had already been mercilessly sliced apart by peerless swordsmanship and swallowed by azure flames, flesh and bone reduced to ash.

Only that symbolic head remained, cradled in Retzel's hands as proof of the battle.

Retzel held it like a treasure, gazing at it as though caressing a lover she hated to part from.


Typhon thought she wanted to stare at that sight forever.

　

The severed head of the Wind Four Heavenly King was still lit by azure flame in the heat-laden air. The skin had changed to a scorched gray, yet parts of it still seeped vivid blue blood, which dripped down in drops.

The wound running from forehead to cheek had not fully hardened. The flesh pulled tight around it, speaking of remembered pain.

The eyes remained wide open, already beginning to dry, and the reflected light in the bloodshot whites gave them a disturbingly hollow gleam.

The pupils had scattered. Focus had long since vanished.

The terror and fury that had run through that final moment were mingled there together, reaching the level of art.


--B-beautiful.

　

I want to display it.

I want to display it in my room.

The end of the Wind Four Heavenly King had been magnificent beyond words.

For some reason, both arms and both legs had already been gone, and yet even while scattering blood, that body had continued to stand upon the battlefield.

With only the torso and head remaining, it had whipped up tornadoes in a frenzy, split the sky, and gouged the earth.

Retzel had not even used the demonic sword Tyrfing, which granted temporary power in exchange for cutting down her lifespan, and yet the battle had still become fiercely heated.

The Wind Four Heavenly King had rampaged with lionlike ferocity, as if growing stronger each time her body was shattered, burning away the last flame of life.


--That final rampage... kuh, I think I'm in love with the Wind Four Heavenly King too!

　　

Even that cool personality of hers, not returning Typhon's wave even though Typhon waved at her, had been wonderful.

So many lovely things came to mind.

It was a good memory.


Yes--the battle was over.

Emotion rose in Typhon's chest so strongly it made her tremble. The battle amid blood, flame, and death had surpassed both expectation and imagination by an enormous margin.


--Yes, yes, yes, battles between monsters ought to be like this. Still... that's exactly why it's such a shame I couldn't watch it from closer up.


Typhon had only been able to watch from a distance.

Of course, by instinct she had been seized by the urge to witness that spectacle from nearer at hand.

But the combat of those monsters had permitted no audience nearby. The weak were not invited.

Simply standing there as a spectator would have cost her life.

Even without taking a direct hit, the aftershocks alone would easily have scorched flesh and crushed bone. Her skin stung, the back of her throat burned every time she breathed, and as waves of heat crashed over her, she watched rocks at the edge of her vision melt red and collapse.

　

Typhon had understood instinctively.

Only a limited few possessed the right to stand there.

She had to keep her distance.

The Typhon of old would have ignored danger, prioritizing instead engraving the force of that single instant into her eyes even if her body burned. She had once lived for the pleasures of the moment, pretending that tomorrow did not matter.

　

But now Typhon was different.

She could not allow herself to burn out.

Throwing her own life into the flames would be easy.

But if she did that, she would be abandoning the role she still had left to fulfill midway through.

She had to carve Retzel's progress, her carnage, her words, all of it firmly into memory.

That was what compelled Typhon to go on living.

So if she was to die as a fleeting spectator, that would come later.


Typhon roughly wiped away the sweat running down her forehead and, through the searing air, slowly approached Retzel. The earth still held heat, as though she had been thrown into the tropics.

　　

"She was wonderful, wasn't she?"


"She was... It's such a pity. I almost wish she'd come back to life and try to kill me again."


"...Let's display that head. Then the two of you can stay together forever."

　　

As Typhon looked at the head of the Four Heavenly King in Retzel's arms, she found herself unconsciously pondering how best to preserve it against decay.

And then--


"Hold."


While Retzel had been fighting, the human army had diligently retreated even farther away than Typhon had.

Yet the moment they realized Retzel had won, they came close again.

At their front stood a middle-aged man in expensive-looking clothes, with a beard and a pompous air.



--------------------------------------------------




The Kingdom army appeared through the dust, ranks straightening as they advanced. Their shields and spears were aligned, but the heat of the azure flames lingering over the battlefield made their faces tighten.


"...I see. So a Four Heavenly King truly can die."

　

The man snorted. He glanced at Typhon for an instant, but immediately turned his gaze toward Retzel.


"But you... why is an Imperial Princess who was supposed to be dead here, in the Kingdom of all places?"


Retzel, absorbed in the head she was hugging, did not even turn around. So Typhon answered in her place.

　

"And what's that got to do with you? If you don't have any business, go home. Lady Re is busy right now!"


"No business, you say? I have plenty! I am the commander entrusted with ten thousand Kingdom soldiers. For a princess of another nation--and a criminal besides--to set foot in the Kingdom as she pleases cannot be overlooked. This is a grave matter!"


Typhon frowned.

He was using strong language, but did he seriously think he could threaten Lady Re?

Was his head all right? The thought made her worry more than scoff.


"You're awfully full of yourself."


"You too are, however nominally, an Imperial Princess. Surely you do not wish to cause trouble for your homeland, the Empire. If you turn your blade against me, it will worsen relations between the Kingdom and the Empire."


Typhon did not understand political maneuvering at all, so she could only remain silent.

　

The middle-aged commander steadied his breath and raised his voice again.


"It is true that you slew a Four Heavenly King. I acknowledge that you struck down our mortal enemy. However, that ought to have been a victory won by the Kingdom's own hand, and no one will rejoice if it is known to be the result of a foreign criminal's unilateral interference. Therefore, this fact must be concealed, and it must be reported as an accomplishment achieved together with our army."


Typhon tilted her head.

In other words, wasn't he just demanding the credit?


Taking advantage of Retzel's silence, the commander cheerfully went on.


"Naturally, there will be compensation. Even if a guilty princess enters this land without permission, that does not change the fact that she is a criminal. However, in light of your aid in defeating a Four Heavenly King, I can intercede with those above me."


"I see."

　

Retzel replied with utter disinterest.

The commander lifted the corners of his mouth and added:


"And one more thing. Hand over that Four Heavenly King's head. We require it as proof of the kill."


When Typhon heard that, she saw Retzel's eyes narrow.


"That's impossible."


"W-what did you say?"


"I've decided. I'm going to build this Four Heavenly King a proper grave."


Retzel gently stroked the Wind Four Heavenly King's head as she said it in a matter-of-fact tone.


"Don't be absurd!"


The commander shouted in fury. The soldiers joined in.


"Demons don't need graves!"

"Turn her to ash and be done with it! Hand over the head!"


Perhaps, to soldiers who bore hatred for demonkind, it was truly unforgivable.

But as if ignoring the soldiers' roars entirely, Retzel only looked bored and would not even meet their eyes.

The commander, unable to hide his irritation, drew breath to raise his voice even further--

and in that instant, his head tilted slightly and then rolled from his shoulders.

Blood splashed. Screams ran through the ranks of soldiers.

The commander collapsed from the knees, convulsed, and fell.


The one who broke the silence was Retzel, in a clear voice.


"...Perfect timing."


Retzel grinned.

The smile was beautiful, and at the same time so cruel it made the air freeze.


"In the Empire, when royalty die, there is a custom of burying living humans in the grave as sacrifices. I've heard that, to demons, humans are a delicacy... don't you think the Wind Four Heavenly King would be pleased?"


The color drained from the soldiers' faces.


"W-what are you--!"

"You're no human! You devil!"

"We are the Kingdom army!"

"Back off! Back off!"


The soldiers' lines instantly dissolved into confusion.

Someone drew a sword. Someone screamed. Having lost command, the force was swallowed by fear.

Then, from above their heads, blue flames came down roaring.

They became a torrent, engulfing the soldiers, melting their armor, and sending their screams skyward together with the smell of burning flesh.

Iron shrieked.

Bloody mist swirled.


Typhon stared steadily at the hell unfolding before her eyes.

Rain of blood, tongues of flame, the voices of collapsing people--

she engraved every bit of it into her memory without letting a single part go.
