Liam somehow managed to find Princess Ceras. He vaguely remembered her presence, so he relied on that, flying with Regingule away from the clamor of the battlefield until he arrived halfway up a mountain. Imperial soldiers had formed up at the foot of it, so he had hoped someone important would be there. When he focused his dragon-tainted eyes, he spotted the Kingdom's flag at one of the encampments. Princess Ceras was there too. The position was surrounded by rocks and trees and looked down over the battlefield. If she had been observing from some underground facility through magical tools, Liam never would have found her. But fortunately, because Princess Ceras had found the courage to place herself here in person, he was able to discover her. He approached the Imperial soldiers below, gave his name, and briefly explained that he wanted to see Princess Ceras. Liam still had dragon features, so he had braced himself for the possibility that they might mistake him for a demon and attack him. But perhaps because the Empire was accustomed to beastfolk, things proceeded smoothly. When he was brought to the Kingdom's position, however, Princess Ceras's attendants reacted just as he had expected. Weapons were raised. Sharp, wary eyes turned toward him. Even though he had been prepared for it, it still hurt. The moment Princess Ceras saw him, she covered her mouth with both hands and stood there speechless. "What have they done to you...!" In that taut air, Liam kept his distance and explained. Then he entrusted Regingule to her and made her promise he would be treated. Watching from a distance as Regingule received care, he let out a breath of relief.

Princess Ceras's attendants kept glancing his way, swallowing nervously, then whispering among themselves. "He's disgusting." "Well, if he's stronger now, that's reassuring." "Maybe this can turn the tide." "The Kingdom can't afford to lose to the Empire either." Almost everything in their eyes was negative, and even the positive remarks looked at Liam as nothing more than military strength. It was easy for them to say, because it was someone else's body. Even so, he could not bring himself to blame them. They were anxious too. Liam himself feared whether his body or mind might mutate further, but if he showed that fear openly, he would only unsettle them more. He endured it and tried to act as though nothing was wrong. Was he human, or was he a monster? The question kept repeating in the back of his mind. Did he look like some abomination in other people's eyes now? A few even came up and spoke to him. "If becoming beastfolk means you've grown stronger, then congratulations! But if that's the case, shouldn't you stop standing around here and hurry up and use that power for the Kingdom's sake?" Liam felt as though something nameless had lodged in his throat. He could neither deny it nor affirm it, and could only nod vaguely. He knew that he ought to return to battle at once. But the black impulse he was holding down was tied precisely to fighting. What worried him was whether he might be swallowed by it. He was torn. If he rampaged, he might not become strength at all. He might only become a hindrance and throw the battlefield into greater disorder.

The battle did not grant him time to wrestle with that. A change was taking place at the center of the field. The revived ogre monster seemed to be weakening and becoming a burden, but the others were still hanging on. The air warped as massive currents of magic collided. Blue flames surged up fiercely and spread low across the earth. Then a poisonous purple gale rose to meet them. But black flame overwhelmed both. It devoured the purple, smothered the blue, and spread as though it would swallow everything. The flames were overwhelming. Right after that, Liam saw a giant body go hurtling away. It was wolf-like, and yet not something he wanted to call a wolf. That monster rolled across the ground, gouging trenches through the earth before finally coming to a stop. The strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings followed it with another attack, and by the time a mushroom cloud had risen, the wolf monster had vanished from this world. The user of the blue flames--Liam had learned from Princess Ceras that she was the Imperial Princess--and the wounded ogre monster were the only ones left. When the smoke cleared, the Imperial Princess was missing one arm. But flesh welled up wetly from the severed surface, bone stretched outward, and skin formed over it. In only a few seconds, a new arm was there. The ogre monster was bad enough, but the Imperial Princess too must have already ceased to be human. As though nothing had happened, she readied her sword and charged at the Four Heavenly King again. Liam had to support them.

"The airship...!" "It's moving in toward the battlefield!" When he heard those voices and turned his eyes, he saw an airship indeed flying toward the battle. It was not the one Liam had been on. The Imperial researcher Gearnote had said that once those three monsters were defeated--more precisely, once the poisonous wolf was killed and the poison stopped spreading--they would join the battle. The second wave. The final reserve force. The Empire was being driven into a corner as well. General Ubel at the front, along with a handpicked group of elites. Even from Liam's perspective, they were wielders of tremendous power. They were probably meant to finish off the Four Heavenly King, but... there was no doubt they were strong. And yet the enemy was the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings. To be fair, yes, Liam could plainly see that the Four Heavenly King was worn down. His movements were slower, and the intervals between attacks had grown slightly longer. Even so, Liam could predict it would be a brutal fight.

"Liam." Liam turned before he thought. Regingule, still being treated, had called his name. Their eyes met. Then Liam's gaze dropped to the hideous wounds. These were injuries he himself had inflicted while rampaging. Regingule had told him not to worry about it, but of course he worried, and now that things had come to this he no longer knew what face to show him. Liam approached in silence, and Regingule let out a short laugh. "You still look bothered, but at least where I'm concerned, you really don't need to be. If anything, it was fun. You know what I'm like. Worrying about things doesn't suit me." "Fun...?" Liam's mouth twitched. Regingule truly did not seem bothered in the slightest. There was nothing like resentment in the eyes he turned on him. "So what will you do? At this rate it's doubtful they'll win. Look. Even this so-called second Imperial wave is already being pushed back." "...Yeah." He still could not decide. If he rampaged again and hurt allies, the future waiting for him was one where he would only disrupt the field at the worst possible time instead of serving as reinforcements. "I see. A serious wound, then. Well, all I can tell you is this: follow your own heart. Do that, and even if the result isn't what you want, you'll have fewer regrets." Having said that, Regingule closed his eyes as though he had nothing more to add.

What should he do? The current state of the battle was obvious at a glance. The airship's barrage from the sky did not cease. It was barely holding things at a stalemate. But there was no decisive blow. At this rate it would turn into a war of attrition. Given enough time, they would be crushed.

"...I have to go."

The words spilled out before he could think them through. They were his true feelings, with no place left to run.

Liam had to be the one to act.

And since he possessed the strength to do so, there was no reason not to.

Don't lose, he told himself.

The moment he lost in spirit, everything would be over.

If he was going to fear rampaging, abandon them, and stand still, then he should never have held power in the first place.

Liam kicked off the ground. His body floated upward, and dragon wings beat reflexively from his back. He caught the air and kept his altitude. The headache did not fade. He could feel instinct baring its fangs in the depths of his awareness. In his right hand he shaped a spear of ice. Condensed cold gave off a faint grinding sound. Even this power, as the power of a dragon that was a monster, felt horrifying to him. But a sword did not kill people by itself. The heart of the one who wielded it decided everything. Then all he had to do was use it correctly. He might cease to be himself. That fear gripped his chest with certainty. Even so--what he had to protect was before his eyes, and the responsibility he had to bear was already on his back. They had called him a hero. That name meant the responsibility of having shown people hope. If he failed to step forward now, he would betray that hope. He was the one who had shown it. Then he had to answer it to the very end, without turning his back.

Carrying a spear of ice, Liam flew toward the strongest of the Four Heavenly Kings.
