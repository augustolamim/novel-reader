They left the square where the airships stood.

They had been told that the synthetic beasts would be deployed first, and once the time was right, one of the airships would sortie after them.

This was where Abelia and the others parted ways with Hero Liam's group. It seemed a different place had been prepared for Abelia and the others, who were apparently only there to witness the battle.

A safe zone, away from the battlefield. That was where they were being told to watch from.



The imperial soldiers guiding the carriages were oddly cheerful. They traded banter and even laughed.

What they talked about, proudly and at length, was how strong the synthetic beasts were.

Abelia thought they might be doing it to show off before the Kingdom: We possess this much power. Ceras, meanwhile, had to listen to it all without interruption and looked displeased.



The place they were led to was an unnatural clearing cut into the mountainside.

The trees had been thinned out in a wide circle, leaving the view open in that one area alone. The fortress city of Retle, where the Four Heavenly King waited, was visible only faintly in the distance. That much space separated them. For the Empire to call it a safe zone, it was certainly far enough away.



Everything there had already been prepared.

One luxurious tent that seemed meant for the princess. Its white fabric was embroidered with golden thread, and thick carpets had been laid inside.

Around it, a considerable number of simple wooden chairs had been set up as well.



They had already parted ways with the noble who had accompanied them from the Kingdom.

He had deployed his own elite troops at the base of the mountain and had remained there himself. Remembering the glitter of ambition in his eyes, Abelia frowned. They had been eyes that saw nothing but merit and fame.

No doubt he was waiting like a hyena, hoping some opening would appear somewhere.



It was then.

A dull, heavy sound rolled up from the earth below.



"...What is that, an earthquake?"

"Shouldn't we get away from here...?"



Some nearby knights muttered that, but it became clear right away that they were wrong.

These were footsteps.

Even as they stood there, the shaking and the noise grew stronger.

They were halfway up a mountain, yet the tremors still reached them. Stones skipped beneath their feet, chairs creaked, the tent poles swayed, and the horses let out frightened cries.

And then--when they looked in the direction the sound had come from, three enormous shadows appeared.

Abelia regretted seeing them at first glance. She never wanted to look at them again.

Grotesque wasn't enough of a word.

The moment they entered her vision, something deep in her stomach twisted, and something seemed to rise up in her throat. Her mind began rejecting them the instant it tried to understand what it was seeing. It wasn't just their appearance that was terrifying. It was their strength.

She had never seen monsters at this distance before, but the pressure pouring off them, the pressure that made her feel death itself, was unmistakably real.



The first one.

It was a giant ogre. Or rather, it might have been better to say that a hideous mass of muscle had taken the shape of an ogre.

Its entire body was made of countless bundles of muscle fibers tangled together, crushed together, and swollen into knots. Bone and skin remained only in form; the structures that should have supported it had lost all meaning. Every time it pulsed, dark red ropes of muscle writhed and bit into one another to keep the body together.

Several eyeballs were embedded in the gaps between those bulging masses of flesh, shifting places as though dragged by the fibers while they looked around.

The second was a giant red lump of slime--something close to a liquid slime. It was vivid red, as if composed of blood.

A mountain of clotlike matter writhed while somehow keeping its shape from completely collapsing. Dark, muddy red-black fluid swirled inside it, flowing thickly, and every so often it would push out a protrusion that might have been an arm or a tentacle, only to drag it back into itself again.

Its slick surface pulsed, and lines like the remnants of blood vessels stood out upon it.

The colors were the red of rotten blood and the black of sediment. Just looking at it made the inside of Abelia's head feel squeezed tight. Wherever it touched the ground, the earth foamed and melted.



The third could only be described as the rotting carcass of a gigantic wolf.

Its frame alone still retained the form of a wolf, but the flesh on it looked as though it had already died.

Purple-discolored mock-flesh hung from it, while beneath the skin, blood vessels poisoned through and through stood out in a glowing violet throb.

Its split-open jaws exposed sharp fangs, and from them a sticky purple liquid that was probably venom poured without cease.



All of those monsters had countless human faces embedded in their surfaces.

The ogre-like synthetic beast was covered in weeping, screaming faces. Inside the slime one, human faces surfaced and sank like bubbles, appearing and vanishing. The wolf-like synthetic beast might have been female, because a row of breast-like swellings hung beneath its belly, and from them dangled figures shaped like fused-together humans, like grotesque fruit.

They had been made by mixing in human beings. It could be nothing else. It was a vision of hell.

Terrifying.

Yes, they were supposedly powerful allies. But Abelia's reason rejected their very existence no matter how she tried.

Everyone around her from the Kingdom side was trembling too. Abelia decided to look down, at the ground.

The ground was good. Unlike those nauseating life-forms, it wasn't sickening at all. She almost wanted to stare at the ground forever.

"Aaaahhhhhh! Aaaahhhhhh!"

While Abelia was staring very intently at the ground, countless screams rose from the foot of the mountain. Only then did she stop staring downward. Thinking back on it now, she couldn't understand why she'd been so fixated on the ground.



Maybe it was because she'd seen those synthetic beasts.



--No, more importantly, what are those screams?



The source of the voices was below them, at the foot of the mountain.

The Kingdom noble aiming to swoop in like a hyena and all his soldiers were fleeing on horseback in one great rush.

Maybe terror at the synthetic beasts' sudden appearance had driven them half-mad. The three gigantic synthetic beasts were charging down from behind the noble's position at terrifying speed. The noble's people were fleeing with the beasts behind them in view, but the direction they were fleeing in led toward the fortress city of Retle, where the Four Heavenly King was waiting. Driven on by the synthetic beasts, they charged ahead like the opening spearhead of an assault.

The noble's group surged forward toward the city where the Four Heavenly King waited. They had lost all formation; screams and the shrill cries of horses mixed together in chaos. No one among them seemed to have their reason left.

At the sight of that, Princess Ceras caught her breath. "We have to stop them...!" she cried in a hoarse voice. But no one moved. There was no way to stop them. They were too far away, and there was already too little time.



The noble's group had clearly misunderstood. They must have believed the synthetic beasts were bearing down on them from behind in order to attack them.

So they ran. They fled.

Maybe it wasn't a matter of logic anymore. They had abandoned thought entirely and were being driven by fear alone. If Abelia had been down on the same ground as those synthetic beasts, her own ability to think normally might well have been worn away too.



All Abelia and the others could do now was pray. Even while thinking how pitiable it was, a cold sense of relief crept into the back of her chest. She wasn't down there. The moment she realized that, self-loathing and relief struck her at the same time.



She had understood it now.

In the face of that power, all anyone could do was cower.



Then, far off from the city where the Four Heavenly King waited, something black rose into the sky. It was Four Heavenly King Rubrum Nox and the black dragon. In the next instant, black flames poured down from the heavens and swallowed the fleeing noble's group.

She had not looked away. Even so, she could not properly see the moment they vanished. All she could do was infer the end from the ruin left behind.



As a matter of course, those black flames were then loosed upon the three synthetic beasts as well. A descending torrent of destruction. Yet even that was not a decisive blow. When the ogre monster swung its arm, the air itself split open and blasted the black flames aside in a gale. The slime mass heaved its body and released a great quantity of red-black fluid like blood from within, taking the impact and thinning out the burning. The poison wolf threw back its throat and unleashed a purple-black torrent that was neither quite breath nor quite roar, canceling the black flames head-on.



Roaring impacts. Explosions. Howls. Endless noise, overflowing just from standing there and watching.

All of it mixed together until none of it could be distinguished anymore.



The distance between the Four Heavenly King riding the black dragon and the synthetic beasts closed, and the Four Heavenly King's black fire blazed all the fiercer.

On the other side, the ogre roared, the slime pulsed, and the poison wolf sprinted.



They blasted one another away, crushed one another, melted one another, burned one another to ash. The ground was gouged out, the earth collapsed, and shockwaves like consecutive explosions erupted again and again.

At the edge of her vision, huge slabs of rock flew into the air, melted away, charred black, and vanished.



Then, in the middle of it all, the ogre jumped.

It was a leap beyond common sense. The giant body tore through the sky, and its shadow blotted out the sun.

In the next instant, its fist slammed into the head of the black dragon--that being who could almost be called the Four Heavenly King's own avatar.

The Four Heavenly King had been fending off the other two beasts' attacks, and this strike slipped into that opening.

With an impact like thunder, the black dragon's huge body was sent flying sideways and smashed down into the ground.



But at once the black dragon loosed a furious roar, then flew again and reclaimed the skies.



All Abelia and the others could do was stand there halfway up the mountain and watch. They held weapons in their hands, but there was no meaning at all in swinging them at something like that. Before true monsters, swords and spears were worth less than sticks.



At that moment, deep in her heart, Abelia understood it clearly.



--This wasn't war.



It was nothing like any battle Abelia knew. Did human beings truly even have a role in a fight like this?

This was a clash between monsters.

Human beings were nothing more than creatures getting trampled for no reason in the aftermath, and Abelia could only resign herself to that.
