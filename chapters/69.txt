Rubrum Nox side

Rubrum Nox was perplexed by the female swordswoman's stubbornness. She used relaxation to keep avoiding direct hits with a masterful technique. He had slipped around that several times and punched holes through her body, only for healing magic to mend them.

And the sky was a nuisance too.

It was raining magic from the airship floating overhead.

High-density, high-output volleys calculated to leave him no avenue of escape. The earth was gouged, rocks vaporized or frozen, and every possible phenomenon smothered the battlefield.

Not only Rubrum Nox, but the female swordswoman too was caught in it all. Yet even when she was wounded, she closed it up at once with healing magic and kept stepping in as though nothing had happened.

Rubrum Nox was different.

The poison was steadily eating away at his body.

With every breath, his lungs burned, and the edges of his vision occasionally blurred dark. He had entered dangerous territory.

If possible, he would have liked to trade more techniques. There was much to learn from her. But if he let this drag out, he would die.

It was about time to use it.

The female swordswoman too, after such a long battle, seemed to have grasped his habits and was showing the kind of reading that looked predictive.

She only thought she had seen through him.

That was exactly why using an attack he had not shown even once until now would yield the best possible result.

Rubrum Nox deliberately exposed an opening.

Not too obviously. Naturally, as though he were simply wearing down.

The female swordswoman did not miss it. Like a beast, she shot straight through the gap and, taking it for her chance, came charging in with killing intent.

That was the moment.

He caught the female swordswoman with his eyes.

"--Oculus Atrum."

A shrill peal split the air as twin heat rays fired from both his eyes.

At the instant they launched, the air scorched white, the view along their line warped, and sound disappeared.

Then, a moment later, a thunderclap-like roar tore through the battlefield.

The female swordswoman managed to evade on reflex alone.

She had lost her balance and exposed an opening, but that decision was unquestionably the best one she could have made.

But if she thought the beams could only travel straight forward, then she was mistaken.

All he had to do was shift his gaze sideways toward her.

The heat ray swept across.

The female swordswoman could not avoid it with her balance broken.

A heartbeat later, her upper and lower halves slid out of alignment.

Cut cleanly in two at the torso.

The smell of scorched blood was rather pleasant. The female swordswoman's bisected body slowly toppled to the ground.

She was only human. If he went this far, she ought to die.

Though in this battlefield, there seemed to be creatures who would not die even after that.

"■■■■■■■■■■■------!!!"

"How disgusting."

The ogre monster whose neck he had severed from its body still was not finished.

From the cut surface of its neck came a wet noise, and human arms and legs multiplied without restraint, crawling about like a swarm of spiders.

Its jaws clacked, snapping and lunging as it tried to bite and tear him apart.

Meanwhile, the body that had lost its head did not stop either.

It should have had no sight and no breath, yet its fists struck with perfect accuracy. Their blows were still powerful, still heavy. There were many human faces surfacing over its body; perhaps they served as its eyes.

That form was far too distorted to be called life, as though it mocked the very concept of death. Even after having its head cut off, it kept moving. The sheer monstrosity there made even the phrase tremendous vitality feel lukewarm.

The visceral revulsion and abnormality of it were steadily wearing away at the mind.

This lump of flesh, which did not seem to have even thought about what it was fighting for, had become tiresome to look at.

Even the disgust it inspired only sharpened its outline as something that ought to be erased.

He raised his beloved spear high and brought it down with all his strength. The blade howled and split the headless body vertically in half. The giant frame collapsed sideways in two separate pieces.

But it still was not over. The crawling, severed head came at him like something insane, trying to bite.

Without hesitation, Rubrum Nox planted his spear in the ground and seized its jaws with both hands. He forced apart the mouth that was trying to close.

Then he slammed heat rays from his eyes into its mouth.

Over and over, on a path that pierced the brain and angled up through it. The smell of melting, burning flesh burst outward.

The ogre's head convulsed, and at last went limp.

It still twitched from time to time, but it no longer tried to bite.

"Next."

He moved to kill the last enemy: the human reinforcement unit riding the airship.

The airship had positioned itself on the outer edge of the scorching domain, Atra Ignis.

It had chosen the precise distance needed to avoid his heat and shockwaves, then stayed there while wearing him down with bombardment and magic from afar. It was a rational, cool-headed decision.

At the same time, however, it also meant they feared entering the range of Atra Ignis.

He could have bothered fighting at range with long-distance magic from here, but he had already spent a great deal of magic power. He could continue, but as long as the human side still might be hiding something, there was no value in indulging them.

The distance to the airship overhead was one kilometer.

He kicked off the earth. The landscape turned into lines streaming behind him, and the airship swelled rapidly in his sight.

--Eight hundred meters remaining.

The next instant, the airship unleashed a simultaneous barrage. Shellfire and magic bullets filled the sky. He made tiny adjustments to his course, shooting down only the projectiles that would hit him, and never slowed.

--Four hundred meters remaining.

The blast waves struck his back. Fragments grazed his cheek, but he ignored them. There was no fear. Only calculation.

--One hundred meters remaining.

The moment he felt their aim gathering on him, he pressed farther into the sky.

--Fifty meters remaining.

He forced his way through a gap in the curtain of fire and slipped directly beneath the airship. He clung to the underside of the hull. Its firing angle was now a perfect blind spot.

That meant the magic and bombardment from above could no longer reach him.

"So they had prepared interception measures for beneath the airship as well."

He was impressed as much as exasperated.

Something was jutting down from the ship's belly.

Structures like turrets protruded like upside-down smokestacks.

From one of those stack-like tubes, a mass of high-energy power was discharged.

Some kind of magical-tool weapon. Fired from point-blank range. It was almost like a jack-in-the-box.

He released the heat rays from his eyes.

Scorching light crossed the incoming blast, shockwaves whirled--

and once his heat ray overpowered it, the beam continued on to punch a straight hole clear through the airship's hull. That was enough. He let go and dropped away.

The next moment, the airship let out a sound like its frame was screaming from the inside, then exploded. Scattering fire and fragments, it became a colossal wreck plummeting from the sky.

Flashy to the very end, Rubrum Nox thought as he watched it while falling.

He saw multiple shadows leap from the ruined airship.

One after another, human figures jumped out and landed on the falling wreckage as footholds. Not a single one hesitated. Their recovery was perfect, and the moment they landed, their eyes were already on Rubrum Nox.

Ahead of him, the human elite immediately formed up.

The front line became shields, the rear line kept their distance. Movements shaped by cooperation, no doubt refined in wars among humans.

From among them, one man in military uniform stepped forward. He was a man mixed with beastlike traits, and he glared with a face like someone chewing bitter roots.

"You've certainly made a mess."

"It was a fine struggle. It's been rare for me to fight this much. Your name?"

He had some instinctive sense that this man stood as the enemy leader. If so, then Rubrum Nox would at least hear his name.

"Ubel."

"...Remembered. Humanity will perish with this battle. But--your resistance was real."

He began walking slowly toward the elite formation.

This battle was about to end.

And yet--

"Now, will it? I'd rather you not underestimate us."

Ubel grinned and very deliberately stepped forward. It was not the sort of thing one could dismiss as mere bravado. A final upheaval here would not have been surprising.

Rubrum Nox's caution spiked upward. What would they do, these people who were the last remaining fighting force?

He focused with everything he had so that he would not miss a single movement, prepared to answer whatever they tried.

That was probably why.

At that exact moment, something slashed across his back.

He had not been guarding behind him.

There had been no movement from the elite that would have allowed them to circle around to his rear. He had not made that kind of mistake.

His back burned hot.

His armor had been cut through. His flesh as well.

He let out a sigh.

He had simply been looking in the wrong direction.

He had assumed there were no enemies left behind him.

"...So you were alive. I've never seen a human survive being cut clean in half."

"Neither had I. It was quite a surprise.

But forgive me. The sight of an undefended back gives me an irresistible urge to slash it."

She truly was stubborn. He had perhaps underestimated humans too much.

When he turned around, a woman stood there.

Wrapped in blue flame, the female swordswoman remained standing, a line still visible across her torso where she had been cut in two.

With a small laugh, she spoke.

Having his back taken was a first for him. There had to be more to it than merely watching the wrong direction. How had she gotten that close? It was interesting.

"I don't mind. If I was undefended, then cutting me was the right choice. But how did you get close enough to me to reach my back at all?"

"Nothing complicated. I've lived the sort of life where assassins have been after me for years, so I've had more than enough chances to steal a trick or two."

Assassins. I see, he thought. So she had killed her presence and approached.

The wound on his back was no light matter either.

Still, as the price of a lesson, it had been instructive enough.
