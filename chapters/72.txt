--It's all good stuff!

Everything in the storage room Clautia led us to was incredible.

After hearing what each item did, all I could do was stare.



The first thing that caught my eye was a bag. Apparently it was called a Void Vault. The kind of bag you get in isekai stories, the kind that can hold far more than its appearance suggests. To put it simply, it was basically a four-dimensional pocket. ...Nice.

I'd never seen anything like it in all my life, so it had to be extremely rare. I wanted it on a very normal level.



Several magic swords were propped up against one wall.

Draw one, and it would call lightning and destroy your enemies--that was the kind of dangerous description Clautia recited in her usual flat tone.

Lightning-like patterns ran across the blades, and faint blue-white sparks snapped from them. They looked cool as hell. Exactly the kind of thing that hits a man's heart directly.

And it wasn't just swords. There were similar spears, axes, bows, even chain sickles, in every kind of weapon style you could imagine, and on top of that they came in all kinds of attributes like fire and ice.

It was a lineup that practically told me to go become a magic swordsman.

The thing the original Brave had received was there too. Apparently it was called the Dragonscale Vestment. Some kind of armor made by processing dragon scales into thin layers, and it supposedly reduced both physical and magical attacks to a high degree.

I mean... it feels kind of plain.

I'm sure the performance is excellent, but still.



I couldn't make up my mind right away. Lumiela was floating around too, apparently thinking over what she should take.

Clautia was so generous she said that if we needed them, we didn't have to stop at one and could take as many as we liked.

Still, I'm not brazen enough to strip the place bare.

I figured I'd choose only what we truly needed.

...Though deciding that was probably going to take a while.



--But still, if I'm getting something, it has to be this.

A small bottle.

Inside it was a glowing liquid.

The Elixir of Life.

An elixir.

This was what interested me most.



Apparently it had been made by Clautia herself, using rare materials that only turned up once every few hundred years or so according to legends from the lost elven kingdom. She had even tested the effect herself by drinking it.

I mean, she's an elf, so from appearances alone it's impossible to tell much.



"...So this is the elixir of agelessness, said to turn back the years."



I stared fixedly at the faintly swaying liquid in the little bottle.

What mattered was not immortality, but agelessness.



Immortality...

honestly, no thanks.

It's common enough in fiction. Stories where being unable to die becomes suffering. A hell that can never end. I'd rather pass on that too.

But agelessness?

That's different.

If I simply don't age, that doesn't mean I can never die. When it ends, it ends.

In that case, it doesn't sound bad at all.



As I studied it, I felt a gaze on me. When I looked up, my eyes met Clautia's. She was giving me a wry smile, like she had expected exactly this.



"...So that's the one that caught your eye?"



"Yeah... well, I'm only human."



The original Brave probably would never have chosen it, but I couldn't help being interested.

Apparently I really am worldly to the core.



I lifted the elixir.



"For the first one, this."



"Yes. I thought you might choose it, but I didn't expect it to be your first pick."



I'd already heard the explanation.

So I pulled off the lid and drank it down without hesitation.



...The taste was hard to place. Sweet, maybe. Bitter too, maybe. Some flavor I couldn't quite describe. The moment it went down my throat, the core of my body gave a tiny electric shiver. Then that was it.



No flashy transformation happened.



"So I'm ageless now?"



"Time will prove the effect."



I hadn't really meant that as more than a mutter to myself, but Clautia answered anyway.



"Fair enough."



After a short pause, Clautia looked as though she was considering something.



"There is one thing I want to ask."



"What is it?"



"Why do you want to be ageless? A long life is not always a good thing."



Coming from an elf who had lived a long life, that probably carried weight.

There had to be partings and all kinds of grief mixed in with the years.



"You're going to ask me that...?

Probably for the same reason as anyone else, honestly."



I broke off there.

Then I added:

"...Though if I had to name another reason, there are two, I guess. One is that spirits apparently live a long time, so I didn't like the thought of leaving Lumiela behind on her own."



The instant I said that, a dry clack rang out across the stone floor.



My eyes turned there reflexively. A small magical tool had rolled from Lumiela's hand. It had a faintly glowing jewel attached to it, and looked expensive no matter how you sliced it.

But Lumiela had gone still without looking away from me.



No, wait.

You just dropped a valuable magical tool...?



The thought sent a cold sweat through me.

And then, in the very next instant,

her expression burst open.

The surprise melted away, replaced by a smile spreading across her face like spring sunlight. Her cheeks softened, and her golden eyes shone.



The next thing I knew, she was right in front of me.



"Hm--"



Before I could finish, both my hands were squeezed tight. Warmth seeped into them.



"Whoa."



She tugged me closer and spun once in place with me.

Her hair flared softly.

As if the magical tool no longer mattered at all, Lumiela looked up at me with a radiant smile.

I'll apologize to Clautia later.



My heart was beating absurdly fast against the hands clasping mine.

It felt like Lumiela's joy was flowing straight into my chest.

"Brave!"



With graceful poise and wholehearted happiness, she smiled like the sun.



"Yeah."



I'm going to live in this world.

I'm going to live with everything I have until I'm exhausted enough to say the sort of line ageless humans in stories always say--that they're tired of living.



Even if I were reincarnated again someday, I want to live this life until my spirit burns out enough that I could honestly say there would be nothing left of meaning afterward.



"Ahem."

I heard a cough.

Clautia was looking at us with a troubled expression.



"...Shouldn't you be thinking about fighting first? Whether this becomes a long relationship or not depends entirely on whether you can defeat the Demon King."



"It does. If we can't defeat that monster, then dreams of living long are just dreams. I understand that. But... drinking this elixir was also a declaration of intent toward Lumiela. No, toward both of us. That's the second reason."



"A declaration of intent?"



Clautia tilted her head. I gave a small nod.

Then I said it plainly.



"I don't want some tragic heroic tale where we die taking the Demon King down and the world is saved. I don't want that at all. There's only one thing we're aiming for: total victory. Anything else is defeat."



Out of the corner of my eye, I could see Lumiela nodding over and over as I looked straight into Clautia's eyes.



------------------------------------------------------------------------



Right. Mutual destruction is not an option.

Dying to kill him might be the correct answer if all you want is to save the world.

But that's not it.



We're not trying to defeat the Demon King in order to save the world.

Defeating him is only a step we have to pass through.



"It's a declaration of intent... that we'll kill the Demon King and keep on living afterward. I have to make sure Lumiela understands that too. This stubborn obsession of mine to survive. Otherwise I wouldn't touch a longevity elixir in the first place."



What I want is a happy ending.

Ours.



After a few seconds of silence, Clautia let out a small laugh.



"...Fufu. How frightening. To state it so completely. It sounds as though the battle ahead will be dreadful indeed. Not a heroic tale, but a war... the sort where victory itself is called justice."



"Yeah. I'm sure it'll be a horrible battle."



I shrugged.

It's vague, but if I carry out the strategy taking shape in my mind, I already know it will turn into a hellscape of human sin thrown at the Demon King, something that could never be called a heroic tale.



But pretty ideals won't be enough.

Defeat the Demon King and survive afterward--

that means winning thoroughly.

Against that Demon King.

I have no intention of underestimating him.

But if I'm going to accomplish that, then I understand I can't run to sweet ideals or pretty stories of self-sacrifice.



Even so.



I don't want to be robbed.

I don't want anything to be trampled.



Everyone feels that way.

But I understand something.

Humans trample each other for the sake of their own interests.



In times like these, trouble is everywhere.

Just like when I got tangled up with that group of swindlers back in Area 1.

The structure of the world itself is built that way.

Before the final battle with the Demon King, we have to face humans.

A mutual cursing among humans.



If they make the first move, then we curse them back.

We crush each other.

What a bitter irony.



If everyone really could come together as one.

If people could cooperate beyond their conflicting interests, then maybe there might have been some other way to resist.

But that's a fantasy.

I know that facing an imminent threat, unity is the optimal answer.

But the larger the group gets, the more ideologies and desires multiply with it. The more people there are, the more different the things they want to protect become.



The world really is cruel.



Even so, as long as I hold on to that guiding star, I think I can still walk into this gruesome battle.
