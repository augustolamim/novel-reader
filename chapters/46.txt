The house was built of wood and had an elfin feel to it. Something about a wooden building simply felt warm. I had no idea whether it was hinoki or not, but the wood smelled good. It was filled with a warmth that made me forget, if only for a moment, how brutal the sacred peak had been.

Inside, there were no unnecessary decorations at all, only the things needed for life, arranged in neat order. And everything looked quite old. It was simple, but meticulously maintained, and that made it easy to imagine that she had lived here for many long years.

If you moved close to the window, you could see the whole garden at a glance. The flower beds spread outside like a painting set inside a frame, holding a silence that belonged to another world entirely, separate from the harshness of the sacred peak.

In the room she led us to, an elaborate carved wooden table sat in the center, with sofas placed around it on all four sides. Every piece was sturdily built, far too well made to seem like an amateur's handiwork. It was obviously too much furniture for one person to use alone, which made me wonder whether this place had been prepared on the assumption that someone would come one day.

"Sit wherever you like."

At that short invitation, I sat down as told. Clautia took out tea leaves with practiced hands and quietly began boiling water with the help of magical tools and the like. A little while later, tea was placed in front of us.

The rising steam carried a gentle fragrance that tickled my nose. It wasn't only the tea leaves. There was also the scent of flowers, perhaps, or fruit peel. Something sweet mixed with something fresh and cool. Clautia and I exchanged names and brief explanations of our positions, and after that she asked nothing further. I understood that her attention was directed not at me now, but at her old friend Lumiela, whom she was seeing again after so long.

Maybe thirty minutes passed. Lumiela and Clautia talked while I stayed as quiet as possible so as not to interrupt, barely breathing, occasionally bringing the tea to my lips and letting its warmth ease my throat.

The view beyond the window was so lovely that I wasn't bored. While I looked out over the garden and sat wrapped in the scent of wood, peaceful time flowed by. I enjoyed another sip of the tea's lingering aftertaste. Lumiela and Clautia's conversation swelled, and then--

Clautia cleared her throat. Their talk must have ended. I turned my eyes away from the garden and back toward Clautia. In the meantime, she'd handed Lumiela a pile of fruit in place of sweets.

"Now then, sorry to have kept you waiting... Your reason for coming was the Holy Sword Idea, wasn't it?"

"Yes."

I met her eyes and gave a short nod.

"...I do sometimes leave this mountain. I know the state the world is in as well... and it seems things are already in a rather critical state. Why move now, at this late point?"

Her tone was even, without accusation in it. She was simply stating the facts.

"Well... it's not that I've done nothing until now. In secret, I've already disposed of three of the Four Heavenly Kings."

"Oh?"

"That said, I only very recently settled on a clear reason to fight. Until then... I only had the objective judgment that maybe I ought to fight. I didn't have a reason of my own."

Clautia listened in silence. Through the steam of the tea, there was no escaping her gaze.

Then, without pause, she pressed farther in. She didn't hurry me, but she unfailingly approached the core of it.

"I see. Then--for what do you fight the Demon King?"

Through the rising steam, her eyes pinned me in place. She wasn't asking why I wanted the Holy Sword. Not about duty, and not about orders. She was trying to discern something far more fundamental.

Lumiela made a small sound in her throat. She seemed to want to say something as she looked at me, but she kept chewing the fruit Clautia had given her in a hurry. She had been stuffing herself with the little mountain of fruit from earlier, juice spilling out from it. Apparently she didn't want to open her mouth while food was still in it.

"Because the enemy exists, because the world is in danger... is that not enough?"

"Not enough."

Clautia's voice was low and full of certainty.

"That alone makes the role of hero far too harsh. Faith, ideals, or perhaps a reason tied to someone. A person needs a hard reason to keep swinging the sword."

From the way she said it, I understood clearly that depending on my answer, she might refuse to hand over the Holy Sword.

Memories from the original story crossed my mind. The former Brave--the hero of that tale--had answered here that it was for the sake of saving people. A proper, admirable reason. The sort of answer anyone would accept. But this elven sage had declared that such an answer would not let him overcome the Holy Sword's trial. That the ideal was too pure. That with a reason like that, he would break in the trial before ever managing to save the world by defeating the Demon King.

So I didn't dress up my answer at all.

"For Lumiela's and my happy ending. For that sake, the Demon King, who stands in our way, has to leave the stage. And after I defeat him, if humans bare their fangs at us, then I'll crush every single one who gets in the way."

"For yourself and Lumiela, is it."

The surface of the tea trembled very slightly.

Clautia did not respond at once. She closed one eye and kept the other on me, silent as though weighing something in the balance.

"...So you would defeat the Demon King for your own ego. In other words, nothing else matters?"

"I wouldn't say nothing else matters... If I have room to spare, I'll help other people. If I don't, I won't. What I can't do, I can't do. If you can't even handle your own situation, then there's no room to worry about strangers, is there?"

I knew it sounded cold. But it wasn't a lie. I had no intention of holding up lofty ideals I couldn't protect and swinging a sword while being crushed under their weight. I had no wish to deny words like for the sake of the world or for the sake of people, but I wasn't noble enough to make them my axis.

"That's true enough."

Clautia seemed persuaded. Still, I got the sense that it wasn't the answer she had expected.

...Lumiela still wasn't saying anything.

Ever since hearing my declaration, she had been silently chewing with puffed cheeks while apparently thinking something over. Clautia let out a small breath. It was the sort of gesture that revealed nothing of her feelings.

"I see. It isn't a hero-like answer, but... that is fine in its own way. It differs from what I had imagined, but if that is your answer, then I have no reason to refuse you the sword."

With that, she quietly stood up. Without a single creak from the floor, she walked toward a shelf against the wall and put a hand on it.

"I'll guide you to where the Holy Sword is."

At those words, a mechanism activated behind the shelf, and a hidden underground passage quietly revealed itself. After walking through it for quite a while, we came out onto a narrow dirt path packed hard underfoot.

At the end of that dirt path, beyond a sheer cliff, the peaks stretching through the sea of clouds spread out in the sacred peak's magnificent light.
And when I looked down, there was a precipitous drop plunging into the distance below.

An overwhelming view--
and there, resting quietly in a pedestal, stood the sword.

It did not feel holy.
On the pedestal, there was only a well-kept blade and hilt, simply existing there.

The Holy Sword Idea carried not even the faintest glow. The blade showed only the natural color of metal. It was neither rejection nor acceptance. It was simply silent, as though waiting for something.

--The trial should be fine...

I'd solve it with force. The power required... should be more than enough. In the original story, the former Brave had reached this place before he had defeated even a single Four Heavenly King.

And--
right beside the Holy Sword stood a small grave. Like the sword, it remained there in silence, saying nothing of the past. Whose grave it was... that much was obvious.
