The Wounded Four Heavenly King

Viridis's Side






Viridis flew low, crawling her way forward through the air.

She had lost all four of her limbs. Aside from levitating, she had no way to move.

Bloody froth kept spilling from her mouth. Each time it slipped through her teeth, blue-black droplets scattered into the wind and trailed behind her in streaks.

The trees of the forest rushed up at her.

Lifting her body with wind, she slipped through at such low altitude that the branches nearly brushed her.

Her blood-soaked body trembled and her muscles convulsed, but even so, she forced them tight as though strangling herself, squeezing shut the flow of blood through her arteries to stanch the bleeding.


Bone and sinew screamed.

But if she did not stop the bleeding, then in this very instant all that awaited her was to run dry and fall.


She had opened up some distance, but even so, the Hero and the spirit were still far behind her.

If they caught her, this time it truly would be over.


Viridis had wrung out a plan and succeeded in escaping.

She had used a cocoon of wind to wrap everything up so that the inside could not be seen, so that it would not matter whether Viridis herself was inside that cocoon or not.

Then, to isolate the area again with the Wild Hunt that covered the surroundings, she bent the top of the towering wall-like wind cage and entwined it with the cocoon, fusing them together.

The cage and the cocoon had been stitched together into one gigantic cocoon. To any outside observer, it would have looked as though she were simply trying to create an even larger tornado.


In truth, Viridis had connected the cocoon and the wind cage so that she could pass through them, then slipped into the passage within.

After that, she interfered with one portion of the wind cage and forcibly pried open a single hollow tunnel.

Betting everything on that escape route going unnoticed, she created her one and only path to survival.

And as a result, she escaped.

　

As she desperately put more distance between herself and her pursuers, the sensation she had maintained through the cocoon of wind behind her vanished completely. The Hero's attack must have destroyed it.


Her internal organs were full of blood, and every time she vomited, pain stabbed up through her skull. Even if she wanted to scream, her vocal cords had stiffened as though burned. All that emerged was a rasping breath.


"Bleegh..."

　

A cry of pain that could not even become a scream leaked out, only to be snatched away and erased by the wind.


She longed for the last arm she had lost.

The fact that she had cut it off herself made her miss it all the more.


When she escaped, she had lost both her hair and her arm.

More precisely, she had severed them and left them behind inside the cocoon of wind.

She had been able to decide on cutting her hair away immediately, but the arm had been another matter. That had taken a few seconds.

She could not afford to sacrifice organs that directly supported life, such as her entrails. But an arm would cause comparatively little trouble, and it would also make an obvious corpse.


Leaving behind part of a corpse would create a far stronger impression on anyone who saw it than if everything simply crumbled into dust and vanished.

Traces of the life that had definitely existed there, remains as tangible "evidence," would become proof that made the enemy feel death had truly occurred. Blood, scraps of flesh, a lock of hair--they were fragments of the proof that she had lived, and would plant certainty in the viewer that it was over.

If those remains could act as a trap and make it seem as though Viridis had died, then that illusion would lead to her survival.

Even if the chance was only a few percent, it was worth increasing the odds that the Hero and the spirit would mistake it for proof they had killed her.

This deception, she told herself, was what would keep her alive.


Better to cast away a single arm and survive than to cling to it and die.

That resolve had moved Viridis forward.

Her long, lustrous green hair had scattered soundlessly into the air.

She had converged her wind blades and hacked through her arm down to the bone.

　

And so Viridis had escaped.

But if she was not treated, she would die.

First of all, she lacked blood.

She was keeping the bleeding down by compression, but that too was only a matter of time.

　

Thirst echoed through her.

The stench of the blood she had vomited was drifting through the forest.

Branches rustled, and a beast drawn by the scent emerged.

It was a giant boar.

Its fat-swollen body bristled with coarse hair like needles, and its jutting tusks gleamed in the moonlight.

Viridis narrowed her eyes and sent a blade of wind racing forth.


The boar's head flew through the air.

A torrent of blue blood burst from the thick vessels in its neck and stained the earth.


Staggering, Viridis crash-landed on the ground and put her mouth to the corpse.

She latched onto the monster's wound.

Its blood filled her throat with warmth and flowed into her starving guts.

Its iron taste pricked her tongue.

The pit of her stomach burned with heat, and still she devoured it.

Licking away every stream of blood with her tongue, she drank even the droplets that ran from the beast's body without wasting a single one.


But it wasn't enough.

It was drying out faster than she could replenish herself.

She needed more.

So she sank her teeth into the flesh next.

She tore through the stringy meat and chewed.

Too hard.

The fibers clung to her teeth and weighed heavily on her tongue. No matter how much she chewed, it only swelled and refused to go down.

When it hit her stomach, it sat there like a stone and brought nausea rising with it.

Viridis twisted her face in disgust and spat out the meat, coated in saliva and blood.


"Disgusting..."

　

She muttered in a hoarse voice.

The monster meat in her mouth was as flavorless as if someone had sprinkled salt over mud, nothing but feed that fouled her survival instinct.

There were monsters that tasted good.

But the most delicious creature of all was human.

Soft, sweet, warm.

Break the skin and the flesh tore easily, and the blood moistened the tongue like nectar.

There was no prey more delicious than that.

Was there any nearby?


Staggering, Viridis looked to the northeast.

If she remembered correctly, the main battlefield between humans and demons was in that direction.

And by necessity, there should also be a demon defensive outpost there.

If she went there, she would find human blood and meat.

And she would find servants capable of healing magic.

If they treated her, Viridis could survive.


The arm and legs she had lost were not a problem. There were magical prosthetic arms and legs. Since her body was not built for hand-to-hand combat in the first place, losing flesh would hardly reduce her power as long as her magic remained.


She twisted her bloodstained mouth into a grin.

She would survive.

She would crawl, devour, and take.

Even half dead, she had no intention whatsoever of dying. Her obsession drove her onward.


--That's right... once I'm fully healed, next time I'll kill the Hero for sure. I'll call Rubrum Nox too... and after that... ah yes, I'll eat the Hero. That would be best. I don't know if spirits can be eaten, though...


Just imagining that future made her tongue twitch.

When she thought of something enjoyable, strength welled up inside her in a strangely reliable way.

Even amid despair, hope to keep moving forward could still be born.




--------------------------------------------------

　

　

"Hah... hah... what... is this...?"


Before she even reached the main battlefield, she unexpectedly found a demon defensive outpost almost at once. The dark smoke rising into the sky served as a marker even from far away.

But what she saw was a mountain of ash and charcoal.

The wooden framework of the fort had collapsed like bleached bones, and the gate had become a lump of melted iron. Here and there, blue flames still remained.


"Tch..."


Viridis let out a short, convulsive snort, and then clicked her tongue.

While she had been fighting the Hero, human foot soldiers had done all this.

"Useless when it matters," she spat bitterly.


Forcing her unsteady body onward, Viridis shook her head and rose into the air in small, wavering movements.

If she did not get treatment soon, it would truly be bad.


About twenty minutes later, the battlefield finally came into view.


Humans and demons were intermixed there, with moans, screams, and the clash of steel overlapping.

From amid the human ranks, fingers pointed at Viridis and angry shouts rose.

Being pointed at was rude, but Viridis ignored it for the moment.

Food could wait until after treatment.


Batting down the arrows flying at her more or less at random, she headed toward what appeared to be the demon camp--

and it was decided by less than a second.

The intermittent sound of something burning fiercely.

And then the defensive outpost collapsed, engulfed in blue flames.

The blue fire devoured all shape and reduced it to black charcoal.

Her head grew hot, as though boiling.

Anger made her flesh tremble.


"Heheh. Found you, Four Heavenly King."


A woman's voice.

Light, cheerful, delighted.

There was no fear in it at all.

Viridis turned around.

Standing there was a silver-haired woman with a sword at the ready.

Another woman following her came into view as well. For some reason, she waved at Viridis.

Viridis had no idea what that was supposed to mean.

　

She wanted to avoid trouble.

The silver-haired woman was not mere trash.

She was strong enough that dealing with her would be troublesome.

But humans had already put Viridis through far too much today.

The Hero and the spirit were hateful, yes, but they had undeniably been overwhelmingly strong.

What about this silver-haired woman?

Not quite that strong.

But definitely troublesome.

If Viridis had been in top condition, she could have said with certainty that she would win.

Would Viridis now run again from such an opponent?

From a mere human who had trampled on her hope of recovery?


Her body was still heavy.

Her arms and legs were gone.

But her magical power remained.


The silver-haired woman gave the tip of her sword a light flick, then said, as though toying with a plaything:


"What a shame. I finally find one of the Four Heavenly Kings and she's practically falling apart. But once I've found you, I don't think I can stop myself."


As she said that, the silver-haired woman handed the more ominous of her two swords to the other woman, saying she didn't need it right now.

The way she spoke, and the fact that she was deliberately holding back a weapon, made Viridis grind her back teeth.

She was being mocked.


Viridis calculated.

Should she flee, or fight?

The calculation ended at once.

She would kill this woman quickly.

She could not allow her dignity to be stripped away any further.


A smile like madness spread over the silver-haired woman's face.


"Now then, let's fight!"


She raised her sword, blue flames leaping around it.

Viridis met her head-on.
