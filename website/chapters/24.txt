Episode 124: Telepathy Training








"Huh. This is a stylish inn."


Seeing the room at the inn where we'd be staying for the first time tonight, I muttered that under my breath.

Yes--

since I had the chance, I had decided to make a point of hopping between the inns in the towns around the Koshumar Great Mine.

Changing inns every day was fun. It felt like repeating a little adventure over and over.

In the morning I'd pack my things, look out at a new view, and by night I'd be wrapped in unfamiliar smells and sounds. Even in the same town, if the inn changed, then so did the atmosphere. Those little changes piled up and made the memory of travel richer.


And as for the inn we were staying at tonight...

it clearly aimed for luxury.

Thick curtains covered the windows and completely shut out the cold air outside. Candlelight softly illuminated the golden wall decorations, and pale shadows swayed over them.


The wooden floor had been polished to a glossy finish and felt solid beneath the feet.

The bed was large, the feather-stuffed bedding puffed up invitingly, and a faint herbal scent rose from the white sheets. On the desk sat a silver water pitcher and cup, and beside them a vase holding white flowers.


The vase alone made the room feel remarkably refined.


When I took off my mask and let out a deep breath, the logs in the fireplace gave a small pop.

Beyond the window, the lights of the town twinkled, and in the quiet the outside world felt just a little distant.

It was that kind of peaceful room.


"Hm-hm-hm... hm-hm..."


Lumiela had released her invisibility and was sitting on one of the two beds.

There was a book in her lap. She was studying. Maybe because the bed was so soft, she seemed oddly cheerful, humming while she read.


As she turned the pages and let out the occasional little breath, the profile of her face tracing over the words held a quiet intellectual stillness and the mystery proper to a spirit. It looked entirely different from her usual gluttonous self.


Watching that demure spirit, I finally felt that the day was over.

Running in the morning.

From noon until sunset, working on improving my swordsmanship.

Only now had a quiet, relaxed stretch of time finally returned.


The firewood gave another small crack, and outside the wind was blowing.

Through it all, Lumiela's humming continued, and I found myself thinking:

today really had been busy.


Tomorrow, and the day after that, I'd keep training without rest.

I had no intention of challenging things in a fair, honorable way, and I was sure I'd be using strategies too, but in the end, nothing would matter unless I raised my own basic combat ability.

Whether it was technique or stamina, without something backed by training, it would all break down somewhere eventually.

Eat, train, rest, and train again.

It was plain work, but important.


As I sat down on the edge of the bed thinking about that, my gaze drifted toward a wrapped parcel on the desk.

I'd already eaten dinner, but I was feeling a little hungry again.


Inside that bundle was cheese I'd stocked up on separately from the stuff I gave to Old Man Rowan.

Its scent drifted faintly through the room.

...It smelled good.


No, bad idea.

During sword training earlier today, while mindlessly swinging the blade over and over, I had suddenly remembered something from my previous life.


Athletes, in order to keep their physical condition and form in order, avoided unnecessary snacking.

Apparently, if you wanted to stay light and focused, it was better not to take in extra sugar or fat.


So I had decided that at the very least, I would stop eating snacks at night.

I steeled myself and looked away.

But my mouth had already remembered the taste.


My hand started to reach for the cheese.

After hesitating, I quietly pulled it back.

Not now, I told myself.

Even if I understood it logically, it felt like my brain was craving the saltiness of cheese.


"...You're not going to eat it?"


Lumiela was looking at me with a puzzled expression.

Still holding her book, she tilted her head, staring at me as if I were some strange creature she was observing. Every now and then, she flicked a glance toward the cheese.

Apparently she had her eye on it too.

Her hand had been reaching for it.


"Yeah. Starting today, I decided not to eat snacks at night. I figured I should start managing my condition properly for the fight against the Demon King."


When I answered that, Lumiela blinked a few times.

She looked surprised.

And just a little lonely.

But she seemed to accept it and gave a quick nod.

Then she stopped the fingers she'd been stretching toward the cheese and said,


"...Then I won't eat any either."


"You don't need to worry about condition management. That's just for me. You can eat it if you want, Lumiela."


I tried saying that. I was human, so maybe I needed to think about that sort of thing, but Lumiela was a spirit, so it didn't seem necessary for her.

But Lumiela gave a small shake of her head and turned her eyes back to the book at once.


"...I'm not in the mood for cheese."


The sound of a page turning echoed quietly.

But for that one instant--

just for the briefest moment, I saw those golden eyes flick once toward the cheese.


...That had not been the face of someone who wasn't in the mood.

If anything, it had been the face of someone very interested.

I told her she could just eat it without worrying, but Lumiela insisted she wouldn't.

We went back and forth for a while, but she showed no sign of changing her mind, and in the end--


"...Then let's each eat one tomorrow morning."


"Okay."


That was the compromise we settled on.




--------------------------------------------------




Tomorrow I was planning to run from the morning again.

Sleep early, rise early. Rest at night without thinking about unnecessary things.


But before that--

there was one last training session for today.


Tomorrow's training was important too, but there was one more thing I absolutely wanted to work on.

Telepathy.

Apparently, if I strengthened it enough, I'd be able to send not just words but images and memories directly into the other person's mind. I was gradually learning how to do it little by little each day, but tonight I intended to push a lot through at once.


"All right, let's try it again today."


"Sure, that's fine."


I took a deep breath and concentrated.

I searched the depths of my memory, scooped up a familiar scene--

and sent it across.


"Hm...?"


Lumiela's brow twitched slightly, and a confused sound escaped her.


"I can see some kind of strange building."


Good.

So it was coming through.

I focused harder and sent a little more detail.

The sign, the doors, the lights--

all of it.


"It's a building I've never seen before... Everything is square. The walls are kind of white, and there are green, white, and blue lines, and blue letters. Maybe a name? And the transparent part lets you see inside."


So she really was seeing what I was visualizing.


"...And there's something inside a transparent case."


Lumiela's voice grew just a little happier.

She was probably getting hungry.


"The coating is golden... and there's steam coming off it... wait, this is food, isn't it?"


What I was sending Lumiela this time was a convenience store.

Convenience stores had all kinds of things. Magazines, snacks, boxed lunches, and then--

the warm case by the register.

And speaking of fried food there...


"That's Famichiki."


I said it out loud.

Lumiela opened her mouth and stared at me blankly.


"Fami... chiki? That's something you said when we first met, Brave."


"Yeah. It's fried chicken. Crispy coating, that smell of oil..."


Lumiela let out a little laugh.


"Inside that building... it's amazingly bright even though it's night."


At the word night, I tilted my head.

I hadn't been consciously thinking of that, but maybe because of the current time, I had pictured a convenience store at night.


"Yeah. Convenience stores were always open."


Lumiela widened her eyes. "Always?"

Then she lowered her head slightly, as if thinking something over.

I was about to ask what was wrong, but before I could, Lumiela raised her face again.

She was pouting.


"...That's awful. Showing me delicious-looking food like that."


"Ah..."


So that was where it landed.

What was supposed to be telepathy training had turned into a late-night food attack.

Still, I had managed to show her part of a memory through telepathy, so that was enough practice for one night.


About my previous life--

and about the relationship between the original story and this world.

I still hadn't told Lumiela those things.

No, I hadn't been able to.


It would take some courage.

Someday I would have to tell her everything.

That had to be one of the conditions.

If I kept secrets from her, it wouldn't work. I understood that instinctively.

And yet even knowing that, I still couldn't bring myself to put it into words.


How was I supposed to explain to the girl walking beside me in this world that I myself was something unnatural, a being carrying memories from another world?

Every time I thought about it, a small hesitation formed deep in my chest.


But sooner or later, the time would come when I had to say it.

That time would be the trial of the Holy Sword Idea.


I knew that from my original-story knowledge.

In the manga, it had been depicted as though only the consciousness was sent away or something like that.

And the only ones who could take part in that trial were the original Brave, as a user of light magic, and Lumiela, the spirit of light.

To obtain the Holy Sword, one had to overcome the saddest event in the life of its previous owner.

In other words, we'd have to look back on the day the previous hero--

the great hero who had been contracted with Lumiela--

was brutally killed.


We would have to overcome that trial, which meant Lumiela would have to face her past.

In the original story, she had broken through it, but when I thought about the burden it would place on her, I couldn't help feeling a little worried.


If I were alone, I could deal with it however I wanted.

For me now, that trial was nothing more than something solvable through violence.

I could simply beat the hell out of any humans who attacked us.

There would be no need for understanding, or discussion, or any of those processes.

I'd just crush them.

That trial would become hell if you cared deeply about not hurting humans, or let yourself be shaken by the way humans repaid kindness with betrayal.

But if you didn't place much hope in humanity to begin with, then it wasn't such a major trial.


Even so, the reason I hadn't gone after the sword before defeating Kaeruleum and the others was that I had judged seeing those attacking humans would slash my motivation so badly that even my sense of obligation might disappear.


Now things were different.

There was a line I would never compromise on:

the happy ending for the two of us.

Everything else came after that.

Saving humanity was not the goal.

So my motivation wouldn't change.


When the time came for the trial of the Holy Sword Idea, I would send my memories of my previous life all at once, aligned with Lumiela's own past.

I too would face Lumiela without hiding anything from her.

If I did that, then in the truest sense, I would finally be able to stand shoulder to shoulder with her.
