Episode 115: The Underground Hideout




　

　

Our destination was the Koshumar Great Mine.

It was apparently the remains of one of the greatest underground kingdoms ever built by the dwarves, a place not only filled with passageways stretching in every direction and rooms scattered throughout, but also layered with countless hidden routes.

And only recently, one of those hidden routes had been confirmed to connect to the coal mine in this town, Rifat.


Why had it been discovered?

Because the Koshumar Great Mine had become the hidden base of a Kingdom faction.

Apparently they held meetings there day and night and came up with major strategies.


I had been invited to such a place, but not because I had come to meet nobles.

Right now in the Koshumar Great Mine, some of the residential districts had supposedly been opened to those whose achievements at the Adventurers' Guild branch offices had been judged high enough, or who had received a recommendation.

Like the branch offices, the place offered work accepting requests to exterminate demons, and the idea seemed to be that strong people gathered there, invited comrades, and worked hard together hunting demons.

Apparently there were various entrances besides the one in Rifat, and it made me realize just how absurdly huge the mine must be.

　

The place had not been fully opened--people could only enter their designated districts--so I probably would not have to deal with nobles.

The people I would actually meet would be, like me, those who had been recruited through the branch offices.

My goal this time was to interact with those other invitees, and also to see whether any useful magical tools might turn up, since they probably had access to equipment catalogs as well.

　

Apparently it also functioned as a black market where magical tools obtained through the branch offices were exchanged, along with information and goods too sensitive to be brought into the open.

When a branch-office official invited me, he told me this was a place where dealings skirting the edge of illegality were common and where the smell of the underworld hung thick in the air.

I thought inviting people to a place like that was insane, but the official just answered calmly that it was his job.

　

Well, if I happened to find even the slightest thing that might help me kill the Demon King, that would be worth it.

　

I had several plans in mind at the moment, but the more backup plans I could build, the better.

That was one reason I kept spending half my magic power almost every day.


When I explained that to Lumiela, she accepted it.

　　

And while I was at it, there was also someone I was looking for.

There might be clues here about where he was, so the trip wouldn't be wasted.

Maybe he'd even been invited here too.

That would be perfect.



When we reached the location marked with a circle on the map, armed soldiers stood there.

Behind them rose a huge mountain and a large iron door.

The passage beyond that door must be the one leading into the Koshumar Great Mine.

　

The soldiers stood before the iron door with spears in hand, watching so that outsiders could not come and go as they pleased.

When I took the permit I had received from the branch office from my coat and handed it over, the soldier accepted it without a word and quickly scanned the contents.

He had looked faintly stern, but soon gave a short nod of acceptance and pulled the heavy iron handle, opening the door.

With a creak, it swung inward onto a dark passage that looked exactly like a mine tunnel.

The soldier motioned with his chin as if to say, Go on through.


Once inside, another soldier handed me a map.

The whole tunnel system had been roughly sketched onto parchment with heavy lines, and each section was marked by number from Area 1 to Area 3.

It wasn't quite a maze, but there were so many branching routes that it looked easy to lose one's sense of direction if one entered without guidance.

There was a red stamp pressed into the edge of the page, presumably proof of official permission.

And our destination was marked with a double circle.


The paper stated that all three areas, 1 through 3, were currently accessible.

Since we had the chance, we could probably explore all of them, but there was no reason to skip around at random.

Better to start close by and advance steadily.

Lumiela, meanwhile, was silently on guard, fully prepared to intercept any noble who tried attacking us.

　

Without even needing discussion, our first destination was decided:

Area 1.

Checking the map, it wasn't very far from this entrance.

It looked like we could get there simply by following the gently descending tunnel straight ahead.


Wet rock walls lined the way, with wooden supports driven in here and there.

Droplets fell from overhead and made tiny pools on the stone floor.

The only light came from lamps fixed into the walls, and each time the flames flickered, the shadows stretched in eerie ways.

I wondered if Lumiela cast a shadow too, but apparently when she was invisible, even her shadow was hidden.

　

Holding the map, I walked on slowly.

Casually chatting with Lumiela.


'It's a mine, right? I wonder if we'd find gold or gems if we started digging around here.'


'Who knows? Probably?'


'...Think it'd be a bad idea?'


'Brave, do you want to dig?'

　

'Yeah, kind of. Doesn't excavation sound romantic?'


'Hmm, I don't really get it yet. But maybe I'd like it if I tried. Let's secretly do it sometime!'

　　

Whether it was fossils or treasure, I'd dug things like that up in games in my previous life, so the idea still attracted me.

Lumiela was on board too, so maybe when we had time I'd casually try digging into some wall or other.

　　

There were no footsteps from floating Lumiela, so only mine echoed through the mine.

At length, I saw a sign set into the rock reading Area 1.

Apparently we had reached the first district.

Now then, what kind of sight awaited us from here on?

It felt like I was stepping into some secret underground base, and a little tension and anticipation mixed inside my chest.





--------------------------------------------------

　


Farther in, the view eventually opened up dramatically.

The place was so wide it barely seemed underground at all, more like the inside of some enormous dome that could host live performances.

Near the ceiling, magical lights were hung at regular intervals, flooding the open area with light as bright as daytime.

Unlike the damp atmosphere of the tunnel, this place was thick with the scent of human life.


Wooden structures stood in rows around the area, and here and there I could even see skeletal frames still under construction.

The buildings were close to makeshift, but it was obvious the residential district was developing rapidly.

Along the walls were rough stalls and food stands, and the smell of food simmering in iron pots and spices tickled my nose.

Laughter and noise spilled from places serving liquor, and as I walked the street, hostile voices rose from all over.


What stood out most, of course, were the tough-looking adventurers and mercenaries.

Some sat around still wearing armor.

Others drank while carrying massive swords on their backs.

Quite a few openly sent me appraising looks.

There were plenty of bearded faces and scarred faces, and a generally grim, grubby mood hung over the place.


Even so, the place itself undeniably had energy.

Though still unfinished, this underground settlement had already begun to take shape as a kind of village.


Now then, there was no shortage of things to do.

There were problems.

Not just how to defeat the Demon King, but also how to obtain the Holy Sword.

As for me personally, I was probably fine now.

But for Lumiela, it was a problem.

That was why we needed preparation before taking action to obtain the Holy Sword.


Lumiela and I had been training together.

According to her, if we took our connection one step beyond our simple telepathy from before and let our thoughts touch directly, then it should even be possible to show one another memories.

So first, we needed to reach that point.


Until then, we'd just keep steadily doing what we could.
