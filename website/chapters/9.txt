Arc 5: The Koshumar Great Mine

Episode 114: Rifat, the Coal-Mining Town






After descending yet another valley, we found a town spread out below us, filling the deep ravine.

I let out a breath.

I exhaled the heavy air that had been building in my lungs, then drew in another breath.


"Ugh..."

　

The smell that struck my nose was coal.

A scorched, heavy, damp odor mixed into the air and sank deep into my chest.

Lumiela looked troubled too.


The coal-mining town of Rifat.

Lumiela and I had come to this town for a certain purpose.



--------------------------------------------------

　

　　

Two weeks had passed since then.

I had defeated Viridis of the Gale, persuaded Lumiela, and immediately left that place.

　

The reason was simple: I had used light magic against Viridis like I was pouring out water.

I had thought it was probably fine, since the tornadoes of Viridis's Wild Hunt had isolated the area and hidden us from view, but I left just in case.

Someone might still have seen my light magic from a distance.

And on top of that, once the tornadoes were gone and the area seemed safe again, soldiers or others from some nearby town might come to investigate.

If I ran into them then, the chances of my identity as the Hero being exposed would rise.

This world was terribly inconvenient, with no electronic devices and no developed information network, but at least that inconvenience happened to be on my side as someone who wanted to stay hidden.

　　

I chose not to return to Yuren.

...I didn't think it likely, but Lumiela might still decide to seal away the whole town.

And for the sake of my own resolve, I decided not to go back until I had defeated the Demon King.

　

For a while, I wandered from town to town.

Sometimes I listened quietly in taverns. Sometimes I stopped by the branch offices of the kingdom in local Adventurers' Guilds.


What I heard there was one surprise after another.

Apparently Imperial Princess Retzel was still alive.

Not only that, she had supposedly defeated Viridis.

No, no, no, Lumiela and I beat her, didn't we? was my first thought, but then a chill ran through me:

what if we had failed to finish her off?


From what I heard, she died a little while after fighting us.

But before that, she still might have spoken to her subordinates.

She might even have let something slip that would make the princess aware of my existence.


And yet even after a week, nothing about me surfaced.

All I heard were words praising the princess, or denouncing her.

Apparently that deranged princess had attacked the Kingdom army deployed in that region too, on top of killing Viridis.

Because of that, she was taking heavy criticism for being unworthy of a hero.

From my point of view, that princess was as far from a hero as anything could get; she seemed more like a monster wearing human skin...

Still, she had made the brilliant play of killing one of the Four Heavenly Kings for us, so my opinion of her had shot upward.

Not that I ever want to meet her.

Absolutely not.


As a powerful helper--or rather, an ally of humanity... sort of?--I was glad she existed.

Actually cooperating with her seemed impossible, though.

　

'Brave. You were spacing out. Is something wrong?'

　

In front of me, Lumiela tilted her head and peered at me. Her golden hair swayed in the light, and her clear eyes looked straight through me.


'Hm? Ah... I was reflecting on the mistake of letting the Wind Four Heavenly King escape.'


'That. Sigh... I didn't notice either. The way she talked made her sound so proud. I never imagined she'd run away...'


'Seriously, when did she escape? There was a slightly charred arm there, so our beam really did hit after we blew away the cocoon of wind, right...?'


'After the fight ended, you listened carefully to make sure, and if she'd been hiding nearby, there's no way we'd have missed her. If that's the case, then she must already have been far away by that point...'

　

Really, when and how had Viridis escaped?

I had no idea at all.

At the very least, it was burned into me now that if I teamed up with Lumiela, we could overwhelm an ordinary Four Heavenly King in a straight fight, but if they focused on escaping, it became a real hassle.

Viridis was dead, but when it came to Rubrum Nox and the Demon King, we'd need to be careful.

　　

'Well, even if we think about it, we won't figure it out. And the story is that Viridis died, so... whatever.'


'That's true. We should forget about dead demons and look forward.'


'--All right, let's go.'

　　

Of course, we had not come to Rifat for sightseeing.

That sort of thing was no longer an option for me, not after making my resolve.

I had to spend my time figuring out how to kill the Demon King.

Apparently the tunnel had only been discovered recently--the one in this coal-mining town of Rifat that led to a certain place.


I had decided to fight for Lumiela and for the happy ending we pictured together, which meant I had no choice but to push forward without caring too much about the means.

Pride, hesitation, disgust--I had to set it all aside for now and use anything available to me.

That was the sort of resolve I needed.




--------------------------------------------------

　

The rooftops of the houses were coated in soot, the plank walls were blackened, and dim lights spilled from the windows.

　

'So this is the coal-mining town of Rifat. It really does have... a unique atmosphere, I guess... a strange place. The smell is intense. Don't you think so, Lumiela?'

　

'It is. Everyone's faces and clothes are dirty with soot, but the townspeople are full of energy. They feel very alive.'

　

Mines opened like mouths along the mountainside, and the carts pushed out from within screeched as they slid down rails.

The shouts of miners and the sounds of pickaxes echoed ceaselessly through the valley, making the entire town throb like part of some giant machine.


The market was piled high with coal, and beside it people sold black bread and dried meat.

Women chatted by the well while children played nearby.


Though wrapped in rough soot-smoke and dust, the heat pulsing through the place had a strange force to it.

It was the scenery of daily life born where the depths of the earth and human industry had become one.


As we moved through the narrow lanes, children ran barefoot through the dust.

Their cheeks and arms were black with soot, but their laughter was clear as it echoed through the ravine.

A little girl carrying a small bucket drew water at the well, while beside her a mother beat laundry clean.

The cloth that had supposedly been washed, and the laundry already hung out to dry, had all turned gray again anyway.


Handcarts piled with coal and broken pickaxes lay carelessly by the roadside.

Right beside them, men sat on crude chairs holding wooden mugs of ale.

Fatigue was etched into their faces, but low laughter escaped them too, making it obvious they were enjoying a brief pause in their labor.

Wrapped in soot, the people still lived with their roots sunk into the earth, carving out their days.


And among those people were quite a few others who, like me, clearly stood out as total outsiders.

Broad-shouldered mercenary types.

People who looked like adventurers in sturdy armor.

Warriors whose severe faces alone made them intimidating even before they drew a weapon.

Every one of them looked like they were proclaiming with their entire body, We can fight.

They clearly stood apart from the ordinary townspeople.

In other words, they had probably come here, just like me, after either being invited by a branch office or hearing information passed along through one.


"Sigh..."

　

My mood grew a little heavy.

Well, that didn't necessarily mean trouble was guaranteed.

I looked at Lumiela.

Lumiela, who had once vanished from my side, was here beside me now.

Just thinking that made my motivation rise.
