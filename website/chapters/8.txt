　　　　Character Introductions: End of Arc 4


　　　　　　　[Character Introductions]


　<As of the end of Arc 4>

　


Brave


At long last, the protagonist has found both a reason to fight and the resolve to do it. No longer reluctant out of mere obligation, he is finally motivated.

From here on, he will fight not as a Hero, but simply as a swordsman of light magic.

Watching even a thieving woman--a terrible human being, objectively speaking--become one of Edelweiss's companions for the sake of protecting someone dear to her, despite the danger that would now dog her steps, made him understand something:

people can fight hard to protect precious companions.

By fighting as a tag team with Lumiela, his combat ability has improved dramatically.

Risking his life to challenge something far above him because he wants to save someone precious to him--a person? ...a spirit?--is about the closest thing Brave has to conventional protagonist energy.

That said, wanting to defeat the Demon King for Lumiela's sake rather than for the sake of the people of the world is not exactly the image of a typical Hero.

And at this late stage, he showed no sign of awakening to some properly heroic worldview like, Actually, I've grown to love this world.

As an aside, these days he somehow uses up about half his magic power even without fighting.




Lumiela


Brave's companion.

She nearly carried through with a miniature-garden plan that would have sealed away an entire town so that the person precious to her would not have to fight or be hurt.

In truth, aside from the one point that Lumiela herself would be sacrificed, Brave had agreed to that plan immediately, so in that sense it was correct.

She is a support specialist who uses thought-guidance to make the light bullets and beams Brave fires haphazardly track their targets, thanks to overwhelming parallel thought-processing beyond the bounds of humanity.

She can use healing magic too.

Because of this, she can maintain walls of defensive light while also using Radiant Funeral, and then heal any injuries on top of that, leaving almost no openings.




Cain


Today too, he remains a healthy, nausea-inducing evil.

He is living cheerfully amid a life of wine, women, and excess, and lately has done almost nothing except pass information on the Four Heavenly Kings to Geanote.



Liam


He set out to defeat Arhensilas of Mire Dawn, only to be ambushed by the Four Heavenly King Viridis. Even so, he survived.

Later, after Brave let Regingle's name slip, Regingle came to visit him. When Liam asked him to become an ally, he ended up having to settle a condition by trading blows with him.

Poor guy.

He is a man of self-sacrifice who stays close to the people and even gives away large sums of money to refugees in need.

That is precisely why he is a hero--because ordinary humans could never imitate him.



Princess Ceras


She handed command over to people more gifted in battle than herself, and is traveling through the Kingdom's towns to contribute where she can most:

giving speeches to the people and supporting them.

With the first confirmed defeat of one of the Four Heavenly Kings, she now holds onto hope that they are not invincible after all.

That said, no one yet knows that in truth three of the Four Heavenly Kings are already gone.

It is a tragedy that Brave has absolutely no intention of reporting important information properly, so no one can learn it.



Regingule


Long ago, he left Kagutsuchi village a few times, but he was disappointed by the way weak nobles strutted around acting important, and by how low the level of outside warriors was, so he returned home.

To verify what Brave had let slip about Liam, he went to meet him and declared that he would join him if his strength proved convincing, leading to a fistfight.

In simple power, Regingule was stronger, but in actual ability Liam barely came out ahead through the strength of his convictions.

By proving both conviction and strength, they acknowledged one another and successfully became allies.



Imperial Princess Retzel


She is hated like poison by every possible side.

Entirely understandable.

When she gets hungry, she attacks not only demons but any humans she happens across too, along with their horses and supplies.

She unsealed and released one of the Seven Great Demonic Beasts.

She massacred a human army to obtain sacrifices to bury in Viridis's grave.

So yes, she's awful.

Even so, she is also the one who properly dealt the finishing blow to Viridis of the Four Heavenly Kings.

Incidentally, because the Arhensilas Typhon had been eagerly looking forward to was killed by Viridis, one could say Retzel got a kind of revenge for her.



Typhon


She has repented? and become a sympathizer of Imperial Princess Retzel.

For some reason, she has made it her life's goal to leave behind Retzel's name as that of a legendary magical beast.



The Thieving Woman, Raia


A thief.

She was saved by Edelweiss, who protected her at personal risk and treated her kindly, and has become a companion from the bottom of her heart.



Viridis of the Four Heavenly Kings


If you don't get hit, then it's no big deal.

She put everything into evasion.

Both her speed and magical power surpass Caelruleum and Plulus.

Because she attacks from the air, she is especially well matched against Brave--or rather, against any creature that cannot fly.

When Brave and Lumiela fought her together, all they could manage was survive for a short while.

Even so, she did escape, so although she is arrogant and power is her only real virtue, she is undeniably strong.



Arhensilas of Mire Dawn


A disgrace to the Seven Great Demonic Beasts who ended up being remembered as, Well... maybe there were a few good points.

It has a reputation for efficient large-scale slaughter of humans, but in the end it existed mainly to make Viridis of the Four Heavenly Kings look stronger.

In terms of power, it is a useful benchmark sitting neatly between Four Heavenly King class, Seven Great Demonic Beast class, and Demon General class.



Rubrum Nox of the Four Heavenly Kings


At long last, the final remaining Four Heavenly King.

He has moved to the Empire and is killing synthetic beasts that strut around arrogantly as a way to pass the time.



Edelweiss


She had been living amid the rich natural beauty of an absurdly rural village, but even that remote countryside began receiving refugees who had been driven from their homes.

Hearing the vivid reports of the demons' brutal atrocities passed on through those refugees, Edelweiss made up her mind.

Though her parents and the villagers tried to stop her, she could not sit still and ran from the village.

From Eryudia, she had planned to head to the front lines as either a soldier or a mercenary, but after seeing Brave sprinting after the thieving woman, she got involved out of simple busybody concern.

As a result, she obtained the absurdly powerful ancient golem, Zeluas.



The World


Only one of the Four Heavenly Kings remains, along with the Demon King.

The Imperial Princess and Brave are both motivated, hero Liam and Regingule are around, and the Empire is full of synthetic beasts and dangerous organizations too.

However--


Brave -> wants a happy ending with Lumiela, just the two of them.

Imperial Princess Retzel -> wants to fight.

The nobles -> want to protect their own interests.

Hero Liam -> fights for the world and for other people.

Princess Ceras -> wants to protect the Kingdom and its people.


Even within the Kingdom alone, their ideals are all over the place...

About half of them look ready to crash into each other and drag each other down, but surely it'll work out somehow... right?
