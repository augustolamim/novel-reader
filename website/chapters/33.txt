Human-Faced Fly Parade (1)



Senior Demon General Valmar's Side






After being attacked by the human-faced flies and somehow managing to escape from the city of Aibelk, Valmar should have been savoring the fact that he had at least survived--

but he couldn't.


Heavy wingbeats kept slamming through the damp air.

They came after him endlessly.


The human-faced flies that had been in the city of Aibelk indeed had not pursued him directly.

But human-faced flies kept appearing wherever he went.


Valmar was made to understand their terror all over again.

Their overwhelming numbers, born from the way fairly powerful individuals appeared almost immediately.

And beyond that, the human-faced flies did not fear death.

They attacked like suicide charges and kept fighting to the very end even if their comrades were struck down.

Perhaps they did not even possess the concept of fleeing.

That alone made them terrifying.


Even if they were on the weaker side, it was still like demon-general-level beings coming in as disposable pawns, trampling everything through sheer number.

For Valmar--

no, for the invading Demon King's army as a whole--

the only ones with the quality needed to overturn that were the Four Heavenly Kings.


On the way, he encountered three separate groups of demons fleeing just as desperately as he was.

Shouts, quiet sobbing, ragged breathing.

Every face was one at its limit, yet the moment they met, they instinctively pressed close to one another, too shaken even to hide their trembling.

There was nothing any of them could do.


"North...!"


Whenever someone said that, every one of them raised their face just a little.

Their own continent lay to the north.

If they could just make it back, there might still be hope.

Clinging to that faint expectation, they forced their feet onward.


But as if mocking even that, the swarms of human-faced flies descended again and again.

They blocked the path ahead, circled overhead, and sometimes chased them at low altitude, skimming along the ground.

Each time the flies approached, the trees shook though there was no wind, and an unpleasant sensation passed over the skin as if a cold membrane had been stretched across it.

Day after day, they lived only by desperately praying not to be attacked by those human-faced flies that looked enough to drive one mad.


The number of companions gained through joining up only dwindled again as soon as it grew.

There was no time to learn names.

Voices vanished before he noticed.

Footsteps cut off.

When he looked back, some had already been taken by human-faced flies and carried off to somewhere unknown, leaving not even a shadow behind.

Screams of despair echoed from nowhere and were swallowed by the depths of the forest.

No one could even tell whose voice they were.

It sounded like the signal announcing the end, and that made his spine shake.


Water alone was plentiful.

Night dew and little brooks welling up from the earth barely moistened their parched throats.

But there was no food.

No humans anywhere.

No villages, no livestock, not even the faintest sign of a town.

The stillness was so complete that it felt as though the land itself was holding its breath, and his heart shrank in on itself.


There were times when hunger became unbearable and they tried to hunt monsters or animals.

But if they moved to hunt, they were found.

The human-faced flies appeared.

Right before their eyes, the prey they had been pursuing vanished like a shadow, and only the buzzing remained like an aftersound.

Someone muttered something small.

They fled and headed another way.

Human-faced flies appeared.

Toward the west.

Human-faced flies appeared.

Toward the south.

Human-faced flies appeared.

Toward the east.

Human-faced flies appeared.

Toward the north.

Human-faced flies appeared.


Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.

Their wings were always there.

They were everywhere.

They never stopped being there.


"...We can't... get away."


No escape.

No escape.

No escape.

There were only flies.

Flies, flies, flies.

Whether he fell to the earth, whether he looked to the sky, whether something brushed past his ear--

all that was there were human-faced flies.


Their wingbeats filled space itself, blurring the boundary between day and night.

When the moon came out, countless wings reflected its light and flickered like silver rain.


One of his companions spoke in a trembling voice.


"...We'll make it back alive, right...? I've got family waiting at home, hungry, waiting for me to bring back humans..."


Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.

Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.

Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.


That companion was devoured alive by the human-faced flies.

Then carried somewhere while still living.

Did those flies have families, in any recognizable sense?

Had he been delivered to them?

Or taken to become a place for egg-laying?


He found himself thinking even such mad things.

But no one brought them up.

Probably because every last one of them understood that answering such questions would break them.


The road north should not originally have been such a great distance.

And yet with every step, the air grew heavier, hope thinner, and the pressure of the wingbeats pursuing them from behind seemed only to grow stronger.


Even so, they kept walking.

There was nothing else to do.

Because if they stopped, it felt as though those wingbeats would at once come whispering over their shoulders.


The wind blew.

The grass and trees swayed, and in the distance came a sound like something bursting.

Someone gave a small scream, but even that was swallowed by the buzzing.

Then another night came.

Even now, darkness did not fully descend. The swarms of flies scattered the moonlight, and the road continued under a ghastly glow of pale drifting motes.

Even resting felt terrifying.

The moment they closed their eyes, it seemed those crawling presences would draw close to their ears again.


They just wanted to go home.

That alone was the one force still moving all of them.


In the northern direction, a small hill came into view.

If they reached there, perhaps something might change.

With that faint hope in their chests, they staggered onward.


But then--

from beyond the hill came the sound of wings once more.


"We only wanted to eat humans. How... how can it be that this is happening to us? It shouldn't happen. Demonkind should never be made to suffer like this... We are the predators. Humans are weak prey that cry out sweetly... how horrible... ugh, ugh... what did we ever do...?"


The words spilled out like delirium.

That was the moment Valmar realized the end was close.




--------------------------------------------------




"Heeeey!"


A hoarse voice came tumbling toward them.

From the dimness, a demon staggered into view.

His armor was scarred, his body caked in mud and blood.

And yet his eyes alone shone with an unnatural brightness, his face unable to hide his relief at still being alive.


Wings buzzed.

Muffled, as if enclosed.

Not from outside.

From inside.


"I... made it! Those flies didn't kill me!"


The demon laughed breathlessly.

There was joy in it.

Exaltation too.

Now and then, those who had seemed doomed did return like this.


"Those humans...! If not for those flies, those monsters...!"


His fists trembled as he went on.


"I... I survived. Ahh, the next time I see a human, I won't let them off! I'll tear them apart... torture them to death...! Hah. Hahaha!"


The dry laughter soaked into the forest.

But not one of his companions laughed.

Rather, every one of them edged backward.

Valmar too.

The returning demon noticed their reaction and frowned.


"...Why are you drawing your weapons?"


Unease had already begun to seep into his voice.


"Hey... what's wrong...?"


Perhaps he still didn't know.

Perhaps he had been dragged away before he could witness it himself.

Valmar forced the answer out in a crushed voice.


"...Look at your stomach."


"My stomach? What are you talking about...?"


He placed a hand on his own abdomen and laughed in confusion.


"Huh...? It's kind of... swollen? Hm? Why...?"


From there on, his own senses reacted before words did.

His breathing broke.

His knees shook.

His eyes lost focus.


"O-oh... o, ooh...?"


His voice cracked and gradually turned into something between a cry and a groan.


"Gii... giii...!"


Something was visibly writhing somewhere inside his abdomen.


Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.

Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.

Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz.


No salvation came.
