Omen of Great War (2)







For an instant, confusion flickered through Abelia's mind.

The battle with the demons already under way was large enough by any ordinary standard.

But then she grasped what Ceras meant, and reflexively drew a breath.


A great battle.

Something more decisive.

A battle that could change the shape of the world itself.

The meaning of those words sank slowly, but with undeniable weight.


"Don't tell me... we're going to clash directly with the Four Heavenly Kings?"


At that question, Ceras's eyes trembled.

Her lashes quivered, and her eyes closed.

She could neither affirm nor deny it.

That made her answer all the more eloquent.


Abelia did not quite want to call it hopeless.

Of the Four Heavenly Kings, only one had been defeated so far--the Wind Four Heavenly King, slain by the imperial princess.

Yet one had been defeated. That much, at least, was true.


Hero Liam's figure rose in Abelia's mind.

That hero's strength had already gone so far beyond her own scale that she could not truly measure it.

The heroes gathered around him were the same.

They were the sort who might actually hold their ground even against the Four Heavenly Kings.


And then there was the princess.

She was as unstable as a rampaging monster, but so long as it came to targeting the Four Heavenly Kings, she could indeed become strength on humanity's side.

Whether anyone could control her was another matter entirely, but if she happened to carve away at the Four Heavenly Kings on her own, then in purely practical terms, that was a blessing.

Such a realistic thought crossed Abelia's mind.


"A message arrived from the Empire, with which contact had long been lost."


"From the Empire...?"


The moment she heard the name, Abelia couldn't help tilting her head.

The imperial capital had fallen, and after that all information had been cut off.

Along the border, grotesque patched-together monsters had been writhing about, and some had even been judged to be at the level of demon generals, making any casual incursion impossible.

Abelia herself had taken part in the investigation and extermination of some of those unidentified creatures, and had nearly wanted to vomit through the whole process, though she had managed to kill a few.


Given how those things had been roaming unchecked, the Kingdom had concluded that the Empire itself might well already have perished.

And yet now, after all this time, a message.

And on top of that, one concerning battle with the Four Heavenly Kings.


"So the Empire managed to bring down those disgusting patchwork monsters."


At those words, Ceras lifted the corners of her eyes and anger rose plainly in her face.


"...Those were weapons developed with new Imperial technology. Their designation is Synthetic Beast 'Salvation System'■ 'Patchwork God-Machine.' And... one of its supreme masterpieces is currently restraining the movements of one of the Four Heavenly Kings for a limited time. Once preparations are complete, they intend to begin a joint assault, borrowing the strength of the Kingdom as well."


"...What is that even supposed to mean? Those patchwork monsters are our allies...? Honestly, I don't even know what to say."


"There was once a report, after the autopsy of a slain synthetic beast, that there had been human parts mixed inside it. In other words, the Empire is turning humans into monsters to reinforce its military strength...! This... this is unforgivable!!"


Abelia felt her heart squeeze sharply.

A chill ran down her back and seeped into her bones.

Imperial synthetic beasts.

To take living things--monsters, humans, any life at all--and fashion them into monstrosities.

Just imagining who had created such a mad technology, and for what purpose, was horrifying.


Whether it was the earthen dolls or these, madmen were everywhere.

It was revolting.


Their military strength ought to be increasing, and yet Abelia's chest only grew murkier, like stagnant water.

Not the slightest trace of reassurance came.


"And more than that... it seems the Empire has been producing soldiers through a special technique called 'beastmen.' Soldiers who manifest parts of monsters, or fragments of their abilities... This can no longer be dismissed as an accidental by-product."


To mix monsters and humans together and call the result strength--

Abelia could not think of that as progress, or even enhancement.

It was an act that trampled over the boundary of being human and twisted existence itself.


"...Even if we defeat the demons, if we cease to be human in the process... then we cannot call that victory...!!"


Tears spilled freely from Ceras's eyes.

Ceras did not believe that winning alone would be enough.

She wanted this war to end with humanity's pride still intact.

Perhaps she was agonizing over whether any future bought by discarding humanity itself could truly be called justice.


Reality was too cruel.

Abelia understood Ceras's feelings.

And she understood the Empire's, too.

Of course, she still felt the Empire had gone far too far.

The existence of the Empire's synthetic beasts and beastmen was abominable.

And yet at the same time, she also understood that if they did not win, everything would be destroyed.

Two irreconcilable feelings collided deep within her and ground against each other.


This really was war.

Not some shining tale of heroes, but a real war in which blood and flesh flew.

No--

perhaps this was a clash for the survival of entire species, humans and demons alike, neither side able to retreat.


And that war had already gone too far for pretty ideals.

All that mattered now was how much they could cut the enemy down, and how powerful a force they could gather.


Abelia hated battle.

But militarily, there was no other choice.

After that, all one could do was cling to miracles.


Yes.

That was exactly why they had no choice but to rely on heroes.

Not a war that ground human lives away, but a resolution brought by heroic strength.

That, at least, felt like the kinder outcome.




--------------------------------------------------




In front of Abelia, Ceras covered her face with both hands, her shoulders trembling.

Tears fell one after another to the floor.


"...So in the end, that means we'll be fighting the Four Heavenly Kings together with the Empire, right?"


Ceras lifted her reddened eyes toward Abelia only slightly.


"That is what it comes to. As for the Empire... for now... they are allies. There is a high chance that, just as in the former First Soleil, multiple Four Heavenly Kings may attack in concert once again. At that time, both the Kingdom and the imperial capital fell with shocking ease. If we are to avoid repeating that mistake, we will have to respond."


"...So it's finally happening... a direct confrontation with the Four Heavenly Kings. Just standing near them feels like it could kill me outright. It's terrifying."


Abelia clenched her fists.

Not as a symbol of resolve.

Not a warrior's tremor either.

It was simply a gesture to keep fear pressed down.

Even with enough strength in her hands to turn her fingertips white, the trembling would not stop.


Something cold spread slowly through the depths of her chest.

It was a bottomless fear, like the world itself sinking away beneath her feet.


One of the Four Heavenly Kings had already been slain.

That made things different from before.

The gap in strength was no longer such that running and hiding remained the only correct answer.


It was finally beginning.

The battle between humans and demons that would decide the fate of the continent.


And now that she saw Ceras like this, Abelia understood her feelings all too well.

The terror of anticipating a clash with the Four Heavenly Kings.

The uncertainty of not knowing what lay ahead.

And the anger toward the atrocities committed by the Empire.

All of that had been showing in her expression at once.


If Hero Liam and the others learned what the Empire had done, they too would surely recoil in disgust.

Those on the side of justice would never forgive the act of turning humans into materials for weapons.


At this rate, even if the demons and the Demon King were defeated, the technology and ideology left behind by the Empire might become the spark for some future disaster.


Ceras bit her lip and gave a small shake of her head.


"...What we can do does not change."


Her voice held weakness, and yet also unmistakable resolve.


"We support the heroes so that they can fight at their fullest. And we believe in them to the end. We believe that they will bring victory to humanity."


Those words sounded less like a statement than a prayer.

Yes--even though Felicite had died, the world would not stop for grief.

No one could stand still.

The likely greatest decisive battle between humans and demons was already almost upon them.
