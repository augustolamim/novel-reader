The Demons in the Empire Struggle (1)




Senior Demon General Valmar's Side





The nation known as the Empire was, in truth, an extremely troublesome opponent.

The Empire was such a warlike land of humans that Valmar found himself wishing he could be reassigned, even now, to the force attacking the Kingdom instead.


Even so, things should have been steadily wearing the enemy down.

And yet lately something had felt wrong. The number of demonfolk gathering at their rendezvous points was far lower than expected.

It was ominous enough to make Valmar fear that the demons might be getting cut down by humans in numbers even greater than he had imagined.


He had especially started feeling that way around the time human soldiers began appearing on the battlefield mixed with things that could hardly be called human.

Some had parts of their bodies transformed into monstrous abnormalities.

Others looked like grotesque beings made by forcibly mixing together great numbers of humans and monsters.


The reason they were being made to suffer through all this was simple.

It was all because Kaeruleum, one of the Four Heavenly Kings, had vanished somewhere.

There were rumors that he might have been defeated, but a monster like that could not possibly have been killed. More likely, he had simply decided to sit somewhere and watch from a safe distance.

But with Kaeruleum, one of the Four Heavenly Kings and the demon who held top-level command authority within the Empire, suddenly gone, the chain of command had fallen into chaos.

Rubrum Nox, another of the Four Heavenly Kings, acted freely and alone, so no one could count on him.


The abnormality of the Empire as a nation, and the disappearance of Kaeruleum.

Those two problems together had created this grotesque situation.


After discussion among the assembled demon generals and higher-ranking demons, they had decided for the time being to move from one human city to the next and destroy whichever one came into view.

Their immediate target was a mid-sized city called Aibelk.

They scraped together what forces they could and laid their plans to invade.


Break the stone walls, storm inside, and let the feast begin.


But when they entered the city, there were no civilians to be seen.

The markets and houses were silent, as though no one had ever lived there in the first place.


Instead, there were only around a hundred armed soldiers scattered throughout the city. It was as if they had been arranged in advance.

Not a single civilian was visible. Whether they had fled or been relocated, Valmar could not tell.


With a strange sense of foreboding rising in his chest, he stepped into the largest structure in the city of Aibelk.

At the end of a long corridor,

a human female soldier stood waiting there.

She held her sword quietly, as though she had been expecting him all along.


She gave off an unnatural presence.

She was a human soldier--

and yet scales had spread across half her face, while her right arm was entirely covered in inhuman scales, like those of a lizard.


For an instant, he almost hesitated over whether she really was human at all.

But lately, the Empire had been full of odd near-human creatures like this. Since he knew she was an enemy, Valmar did not lose his composure.

She felt somehow close to demonkind, but perhaps this was simply what remained of those who had sought power at any cost.


Without crying out, the female soldier merely stepped in and swung her blade within the stillness.

That one strike surpassed the limits of humanity.

Heavy, fast, sharp--

a slash worthy of something on the level of a demon general.

The moment he received it, a jolt ran through Valmar's arm.

But even so, Valmar, who ranked among the stronger demon generals, did not give ground. Growling like a beast, he pushed her back through sheer force.


Sparks flew, and metal shrieked against metal.

He knocked her blade aside and, with the return stroke, carved deep across her chest. Fresh red blood burst forth and stained the floor.

And yet a thin wound had been etched across Valmar's own shoulder as well. Blue blood dripped down from it.

It was shallow, but the fact that a human blade had undeniably reached him made Valmar frown.

Still, the outcome had already been decided.


His pride had been scratched.

He would not kill this woman easily.

What kind of scream could he make her give?

He could grind her to death while she still lived and enjoy the quiver of her flesh. Or perhaps feast on her while her blood was still warm, as though dancing.


Feeling the corners of his mouth curling upward, Valmar slowly approached the female soldier.


"Kukuku... Well then, how would you like to die? If you beg for your life, perhaps I might change my mind."


He said it with deliberate mockery, and the female soldier lowered her head for an instant--

but in the next moment, she raised her face and laughed.


"Ah... ah! To think my power has come this close to a demon general that once felt so impossibly far away. I'm happy...!"


"...What is this? Have you gone mad?"


But the female soldier kept laughing in a voice filled with delight.

No, not just her.

Before long, laughter could be heard from outside as well.

A wave of laughter was swallowing up the entire city.


It was starting to disgust him. She was on the verge of death, and yet she was happy. The absurdity of it made his skin crawl.


"Heh heh, does it seem strange, demon general? Don't worry. You'll understand soon enough. When that time comes, you will learn what humanity's counterattack truly is...!"


The female soldier's cheeks flushed as she continued laughing.

That was enough. Valmar drove his sword into her abdomen and twisted it viciously. Steel tore through flesh, and her laughter warped in her throat.


"Are you waiting for reinforcements or something?! Why don't you understand?!"


Spitting the words out, Valmar yanked the sword free again and again, each time choosing an uninjured spot to stab anew.


"It's pointless! All of it! How dare humans like you defy us, the noble demon race!"


When he shouted that, the female soldier still laughed.


"Ufufu... ufufufu! You're afraid! The demons are afraid!!"


It was unbearable.

That livestock would dare disgust the one meant to eat it was beyond unforgivable.

There was no point continuing this game. His enjoyable hunt had been ruined.

This one was a disappointment.

Valmar decided to look for different prey.

He spat lightly on the female soldier and glared at her, but the woman still kept snickering through her nose.

He would simply search for another human worthy of making the hunt enjoyable again. There were plenty of humans in the world.


"Die."


The word was short, and Valmar swung his sword for her neck.

The blade should have torn through flesh, adding a fresh wave of blood to the air.

But overlapping that sound came another.


Bzzzzzzzzz...


The sound of wings.

At first it was small, a noise like something snagging inside the ear. But it gradually layered over itself, becoming the buzzing clamor of a swarm loud enough to split the ears.

The corners of the female soldier's mouth twisted strangely.




--------------------------------------------------




"It's here."


The female soldier's brief words froze the air.

Valmar's gaze darted around by reflex.

In the next instant, it slid into view with the buzzing of wings.


It was a fly.

But impossibly large.

Its body was as big as a human torso. Every beat of its wings made the air cry out.

Its body gleamed black, its legs were gnarled--

and then there was its face.

A human face.

No, the face of someone who had once been human.

A fly with a human face. Human eyeballs were mixed into its countless compound eyes, and it buzzed while wearing a muddy smile.


The instant he recognized what it was, fear stole away Valmar's reason and locked his body in place.


Human-faced fly.

That felt like the only appropriate name for it.

Its flylike compound eyes reflected countless points of light as they rolled and writhed.


"Gshrrk... skreee..."


"That's disgusting!"


Spitting the words out, Valmar swung his sword.

At the same time, he activated fire magic and wrapped it around the blade, turning it into a flaming sword.

Flames drew a scorching trail through the air as he aimed for the human-faced fly's torso--

but it was fast.

It flew with a shudder through the air and evaded him. In the next instant it was already slickly close to his face.

Valmar struck precisely as it lunged, and his blade did indeed seize the creature's life.

Its body split in two with a dull tearing sound.


And yet--

the human-faced fly kept moving even though it had been cut.

Its torso remained split in two.

Its torn wings trembled, and scattering blood and black fluid, it hurled itself at Valmar.


"!"


Caught off guard, he failed to dodge in time, and it grazed his cheek.

He felt flesh being bitten away.

The dull pain came a moment later.

Blood ran down his face. But the pain was faint. It was only a shallow wound.

The human-faced fly scattered black bodily fluid as it fell to the ground.

It was nothing.

An absolute victory for Valmar.


"Kukuku! So this is the trump card you were waiting for?! It's nothing special!"


He had been startled a little, but only this much.

No different from the female soldier. If anything, perhaps slightly weaker.

Thinking he had been expected to fear such a disgusting piece of filth made Valmar laugh from the bottom of his heart.


"I hear them... so many sounds... ahh, long live the Empire..."


And yet the female soldier was not despairing at all.


"What the hell are you--?!"


He could hear wings again.

There had not been only one.

Black shapes were drawing closer, one after another.


Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz...

Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz...

Bzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz...


Then ten human-faced flies appeared.

They clung to the ceiling beams and walls.

Their compound eyes reflected the light as they all turned toward him at once.


At that moment, nausea welled up inside him.

His brain rejected the sight.


"Ufufu... aha. Ahaahahaha--!!"


The buzzing of the flies mixed with the female soldier's laughter.


"Ugh...!"


The sound escaped him before he could stop it.

Just looking at the human-faced flies felt as if it were stripping away his sanity.

Seeing Valmar's fear, the female soldier raised an even more ecstatic cry.


"Long live the Empire! Long live the Empire! Long live the Empire! Long live the Empire! Long live the Empire! Long live! Long live! Long live! Long live! Long liiiive!!"


Foam spilling from her mouth, the female soldier looked upward, drooled, and thrust her arms toward the heavens.

She bared her teeth and laughed, tears streaming down her face as she repeated the words again and again.

The focus in her eyes drifted through the air as if the last fragments of reason were peeling away.

Before long, she had become nothing more than a doll that praised the Empire.


"I said shut up!"


His blade split flesh, and the female soldier's head flew into the air.

Blood sprayed out, red flowers blooming across the floor.

Even after being reduced to only a head, she murmured, "Long live the Empire," before finally falling silent.


But silence did not come.

It would not grow quiet.

Above him, the human-faced flies were moving.

All at once, they started beating their wings.

The sound of writhing.

The stench of rot.

A smell so foul it was as if the very air itself were decaying.


"...This is the worst."


Flame flared in his left hand. It flowed into the sword in his grip, and the blade lit up crimson.


"I'll finish you off... insects!"


He leveled the blazing sword and leaped.

Flames roared, wings screamed.

Valmar was a demon general.

Whether human or human-faced fly, he would not fall behind such inferior life-forms.
