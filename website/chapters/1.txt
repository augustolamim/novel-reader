An Unfair Tag Team, Part 2

The tide of battle was turning.

Though she had tried to counterattack again and again, pain she could no longer ignore kept surging through her, and Viridis's movements were gradually growing dull.

The sound of her wind defenses being shattered rang in her ears, and the pain of the wounds being carved into her body gnawed at her consciousness.


For the briefest instant, her focus slipped. In that opening, the flow of wind she had spread like wings faltered, and her body began to drop, dragged down by gravity.

She clenched her teeth at the sensation of falling and tried to regain control, but the spirit kicked her body downward.

That merciless blow sent Viridis plunging once more.


But stubborn pride alone kept her going now. She manipulated the wind and somehow recovered. Even so, if she did not move again at once, their coordinated pursuit would strike her down.

Then, suddenly, it grew dark. The spirit floating above her cast its shadow over Viridis. A cold pressure.

　

The spirit hovered over her head, gathering a beam of light.

Yet for some reason, its stance looked leisurely, full of openings. The spirit ought to have been able to press her with a far swifter, sharper attack. Instead, it moved as if it were deliberately giving her time. Strange.


Falling, Viridis lowered her gaze slightly.

Riding the current of wind, she spread out a tornado and prepared to intercept, narrowing her eyes. No beam from the Hero was coming from below, either.


The ground was only a hazy blur far beneath her. And the Hero, who should have been there...

was gone.

She couldn't see him. He had disappeared.


"...Where did he...?"


In the next instant, the spirit fired its beam. There was no time to think.

Left with no choice, Viridis caught it with a tornado. The raging gale swallowed the light, and the sky trembled with the blast.

Right after that, another sound reached her ears. It was neither the slicing of wind nor the crack of an explosion, but something with an unnervingly raw presence.


Instinctively, she layered wind over itself again and again, forming protective strata around her body. She was already at her limit just contending with the tornadoes. She could not devote herself fully to defense.


"--Ugh!?"

　

The next moment, something grabbed one of her wings.

A shock tore through her, as if the wing were being ripped away from her back. Pain like fire ran through her spine.


"GAAAAAAAAAH!"


But when she turned around, no one was there. Empty sky. Nothing but transparent scenery stretching out before her. And yet, someone was there.

A low voice whispered into her ear.


"You don't need this, do you?"


At the same time as the voice, pain turned her mind white. Something that should have been there was gone. She knew from the sensation alone that her wing had been torn off.

　

Only then did the one holding up that wing, dripping blood and scraps of flesh, reveal himself.

The Hero.

The Hero must have turned invisible and approached her. Even at this height, without flying, he had reached her by jumping? What monstrous leaping power.

If the spirit could become invisible, then it only made sense that the Hero could as well. But realizing that now was too late.


Tossing the wing aside, the Hero continued to fall freely.

Viridis had lost a wing and was desperate just to preserve her balance in flight. She could still glide, but she could no longer fly as freely as before.


"...Then!"


She would finish the Hero before he hit the ground.

It was a clear opening. The Hero could not fly. He could only jump.

In midair, he was almost defenseless.

Wincing from the pain of losing her wing, Viridis dived.


"I won't let you have Brave."

　

As if mocking that resolve, the spirit swooped down to the Hero and pulled him close. With slender arms, it held him beneath both shoulders and flew with him in its grasp.


Even while being held by the spirit, the Hero fired beams from both hands at her. The light lances, loosed while moving, tore through the clouds as they rushed toward Viridis.


"Ghk...!"


In a hurry, she summoned a tornado in front of herself and used it like a shield.

The Hero and the spirit floated through the sky as though clinging to one another. Holding the Hero close, the spirit carried him with exquisite skill. Their speed ought to have dropped somewhat, yet she could not catch them.

No, she could not pursue. Having lost a wing, Viridis herself had slowed just as much.


"Don't mock me... the likes of you...!"


No matter how she shouted, reality did not change.

　

"I am... one of the Four Heavenly Kings...!"


She raised her voice as though to convince herself.

She crushed down the two words defeat, which stirred restlessly in the depths of her chest, and desperately forced herself onward.

That was right. The battle was not over yet. As long as she could still fly, it was not finished.


The Hero should already have been worn down before the spirit came. Viridis's slicing winds had wounded him as well.

He was by no means unscathed. It was not as if she alone was being one-sidedly trampled. Her wind had reached him. It had carved wounds into him.


And yet, at the same time, her vision wavered. Blood dripping from her torn wing scattered on the wind and clouded her sight. Pain and impatience dulled her thoughts.

　

Even so, she manipulated the wind, buying time so that she would not die.

She desperately endured the haze threatening to overtake her head. Yes. She could not be the only one in such a state. The Hero had to be nearing his limit as well. Surely he was enduring it too.


If it was now, perhaps she could exploit even the briefest opening.

Clinging to that hope, she looked toward the spirit and the Hero.


"Halo of Sacred Light."

　　

While carrying the Hero, the spirit murmured those words.

Dazzling light scattered through the air. It wrapped itself around both the wounded Hero and the spirit at once.

As if it were a blessing itself descending from the heavens.

And then the Hero's injuries vanished. He looked completely healed.


"Wh... a... don't screw with meeee!?"




　　


--------------------------------------------------


　


Viridis was left speechless. Rage blazed in the depths of her eyes, and indignation burst out of her at the thought, There's no way this is fair. Calm and fury clashed inside her, and the fist of her remaining left hand trembled.


His wounds gone, the Hero spoke in a composed voice.


"You don't look like you're hurting. I'll make it hurt more!"

　

At once, the Hero unleashed a massive volley of light bullets.

More than a hundred motes of light filled the sky. The way he fired them was far too crude.

Had the Hero already forgotten that powerful winds were raging inside this Wild Hunt?

Even if she did not bother dodging, they should have drifted off on their own. Viridis sneered--

　

"Hah?"

　

The light bullets changed course midway, as if resisting the wind.

Though they had scattered in all directions, as if imbued with will, they turned all at once and came cutting through the wind toward her.

Even when she dodged, they chased after her. Even when her tornado swallowed them, they burst apart and came at her again from different angles.


Impossible. Until now, the Hero had never controlled the projectiles after firing them. They had only flown straight, backed by brute force and speed.

And yet now, they clung to her like a swarm of serpents with minds of their own.


Then who is controlling them?


While somehow fleeing, Viridis looked toward the spirit of light holding the Hero. Its eyes were shining even brighter than before. It was calculating the future trajectories of hundreds of light bullets at once and guiding them with thought, as if manipulating them on threads of will.

It was the spirit that ruled the light bullets.


The moment she realized that, a chill ran down her spine. Until now, the Hero's attacks had been nothing more than overwhelming force.

But now, by gaining the spirit's processing ability, even what he fired could be controlled freely afterward. The Hero wielded the power, and the spirit guided it.


It was impossible to bat down every single light bullet. If she focused on defense, they would circle around and shoot her in the back. She would have to destroy them all.


"How irritating...!"


Her impatience escaped as a voice. Yet their relentless pursuit was only the beginning.

A single beam shot from the Hero's palm, sharp and swift. Before, it would have gone in a straight line, and she could have deflected it by thickening her wall of wind.

This time, the beam bent through space itself. It slipped through the blind spots of her vision and chased her like a living creature.


Even the beam was correcting its trajectory by will. Precision beyond belief. Countless light bullets, and a curving beam pursuing her.

The sky that had once been hers was no longer within her domain.


"Ghk... guuuuh!"


This was a higher form of coordination Viridis, who had always stood alone, had never known: each filling what the other lacked, each drawing out the other to the fullest.


It was impossible to break through.

Their difference in power was simply too great. By the moment it became two against one, she was already as good as defeated.

If only it had been one against one, she thought.

Then what meaning was there in fighting here? Viridis should have brought Rubrum Nox with her and forced one of them to deal with him, splitting the pair apart.

Her instincts told her the truth.

The choice before her was not to die fighting to the end.

It was to flee in defeat.

Even if she kept challenging the Hero and the spirit while isolated against the two of them, the ending was obvious.

The Hero's previously lacking precision, the spirit's close-quarters combat, and the way each covered the other's flaws... their completeness had entered a realm Viridis alone could no longer reach.

　

There was no path to victory. Realizing the question now was only how to escape alive, Viridis coldly accepted the truth.
