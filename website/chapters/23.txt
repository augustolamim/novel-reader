Episode 123: Let's Work Hard at Training








"Wrong! Like this!"


"Like this?"


"No, that's still wrong!"


I swung the wooden sword.

The sound of it cutting the air passed by my ear with a lonely whistling hiss.

The hand gripping the wooden blade was a little sweaty.

After buying liquor and snacks in town, it was time for training.

And Old Man Rowan was very much the intuitive type.


I kept swinging the sword, only to get criticized, or told to change the angle just a little--


"You're making progress little by little, but..."


When I glanced back, Old Man Rowan was standing there with his arms folded, watching me. He looked troubled.


"...What was wrong with that one?"


"What's wrong, hmm...? There's too much wrong with it. We'll just have to keep building up the basics."


"..."


That answer came instantly.

And it was a one-hit kill.


'Brave...'


'We'll just have to take it little by little. No helping that.'


Lumiela, who had been reading a book to improve her magical skill, had looked up from it with concern, so I answered her.


Old Man Rowan looked at the blade of my wooden sword and spoke.


"You've got a habit of swinging with brute force, but a sword isn't a stick. You don't just swing it around. You cut with it."


I know that.

I wanted to say so, but I couldn't.

Even if I understood it in theory, I couldn't do it.

When you swung a blade and it hit something, it cut it, right? Wasn't that what cutting meant? Apparently not.

I couldn't grasp the feeling at all.

Maybe manga swordsmen could, but I had no idea.


Old Man Rowan pulled a small pouch from his pocket and untied the cord around its mouth. From inside, he took out several leaves.

Before, he had demonstrated his technique with a wine bottle, but apparently he always carried leaves around as a substitute for when he didn't have one. They weren't as flashy as bottles, but supposedly girls still squealed over them.

What a terrible reason.


"Hmm. At the very least, I thought you'd be able to get to the point of cutting leaves with ease... Is teaching really this difficult?"


As he said that, he lightly tossed the leaves.

They rode the wind and fluttered upward.

In the blink of an eye, Old Man Rowan cut them all apart with his wooden sword.

With patterns worked into them too, effortlessly.

Watching him made it look simple, but I couldn't pull it off at all.


I steadied my breathing and tightened both hands around the wooden sword.

Watching my stance, Old Man Rowan waited until just the right moment to toss another leaf--and I swung at about the same speed he had. It would have been easy for me to swing faster than Rowan, but I figured that if I couldn't cut it under the same conditions, then it wouldn't really count as improving my technique.


Whff.

The sword cut through the air.

But the leaf fell to the ground as if nothing had happened.

Wooden swords can't cut...? Really?


I tried again and again.

He threw leaves, and I swung.

They fell. I swung. They fell.

Swing, fall, swing, fall.

A repetition so stupid it almost felt absurd.


Old Man Rowan came to stand beside me.


"Don't let the blade line waver. Don't swing from the shoulder. Guide it with your elbow and wrist. Let the tip trace a line, always sliding through the center. If you focus, you'll start to see it."


"Focus..."


I relaxed my strength and steadied my breathing.

Old Man Rowan tossed another leaf.


This time, instead of rushing and chasing the leaf with my eyes, I tried to feel the flow of the wind.

The wind moved left to right.

The leaf swayed along with it.


Now...!


The wooden sword flashed.

And missed again.

Yes...

that was no good.


The leaf fluttered mockingly before my eyes and dropped to the ground.

It had felt like I was on the verge of succeeding, but no, I wasn't going to conveniently awaken to some hidden power and suddenly cut a leaf.


"I think your talent as a swordsman is... normal, at least. Jane, how exactly have you fought up until now?"


"That would be... with brute force. Like this."


I picked up one of the fallen leaves and tossed it into the air.

Then I put force into my arms and swung the wooden sword. No technique, no graceful form, just raw power.

A sound rang out.

Not the sound of a sword--

but a shockwave.

The instant the wooden sword swung, the air burst as if screaming, and the tossed leaf disappeared in an instant.

Vanished without a trace.

Not even dust remained.


"You're kidding..."


Old Man Rowan's eyes went wide.

He even took half a step back.


"What kind of speed is that...? It's absurd. Well, of course you could fight like that up until now. ...But with this, do you even need technique?"


"I do. Demons are strong."


The answer came short and immediate.

No hesitation.

At those words, Old Man Rowan's expression changed. Half exasperation, half understanding.


"...That's true. In my day, most opponents were human, but this era's different. If your opponents are demons, then yes... maybe you really do need technique on top of that strength."


After that, we continued training.

We paused the leaf-cutting practice for the moment and instead focused relentlessly on correcting the way I swung the sword.


I just hoped the day would come when I could cut one.

I still had no intention of challenging the Demon King myself for quite a while yet, so I had time to train.


The more time I had, the more it worked in my favor.

Though of course, that only held as long as Lumiela didn't start weakening.

Even so, apparently she would be fine for another two or three years, so ideally I wanted to settle everything within one more year.




--------------------------------------------------




Exhaling, I finished another swing.

The sweat that had started to bead ran down my mask and dripped onto the hilt of the wooden sword.


"...Hmm. Shall we call it there for today?"


Hearing Old Man Rowan's words, I stopped my practice swings dead and turned back.


I had grown a little more on the technical side today too.

Well, in the beginning, when you didn't know anything at all, maybe it was natural for improvement to come faster.


I felt like I could get a little better again tomorrow.


"Thanks for the help today."


As I said that, I handed him the liquor and snacks I'd bought in town that morning.


"Well now... this is a pretty extravagant haul today, isn't it?"


Old Man Rowan gave a wry smile, but he didn't seem altogether displeased.


He sat down on a nearby rock and popped a slice of karasumi into his mouth.


"...Mm."


Then he made a slightly awkward expression and spent a while grunting over it.


"Well... if there's food, that's good enough. The taste can be whatever."


After saying that, he started eating away without hesitation.

He chewed on the strongly salted karasumi, taking a sip of wine every now and then.


When he ate the cheese, on the other hand, he nodded with clear satisfaction.

But the moment he picked up the pickles, he went silent.

Without saying a word, he simply chewed them expressionlessly, swallowed, and then reached for the cheese again.


Once the wine was opened, he drank straight from the bottle, gulping it down with loud swallows.


"Nggh... whew... ahh, that hits the spot."


After saying that in satisfaction, Old Man Rowan flopped down right there onto the ground.

Using one arm as a pillow, he stared up at the sky without moving.

Then, while closing his eyes, he gave a lazy little wave of his hand.


"See you tomorrow."


"Yeah. I'll be counting on you again tomorrow."


Lying there on the ground, Old Man Rowan was already giving off faint snores, still holding a piece of cheese in one hand.


'Let's head back to the inn.'


'Yeah... I still need to train tonight too.'


Realizing that my entire daily schedule was now packed full of training made me let out an involuntary wry smile.
