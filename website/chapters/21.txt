Episode 121: Let's Bring Some Booze








By the time we finished training for the three-legged race and started down the mountain, the sunlight had already grown soft.

The sweat on my skin cooled, and the wind felt good.

I decided to head to the town I had seen from the summit and ask its name. Once I knew that, figuring out my current location on the map would be easy.

After going down the mountain, reaching the road, and running a little farther, I spotted a small town ahead.

And while making that detour, a thought suddenly came to me.


'Since we're here, maybe I should buy some liquor in this town for Old Man Rowan today.'


Every day when I went to train under him, I made a point of bringing alcohol. He had said that only once in a while was fine, but there was no way I could take that at face value.


In my previous life, I once took lessons in an artistic field. The teacher I studied under then had been very strict about manners.

Remembering that, and thinking that Old Man Rowan also seemed to have at least some pride in his own skills, I figured that when learning important techniques like these, one was probably expected to show proper respect.


That was exactly why, if liquor was what he wanted, I wanted to make sure I gave him what ought to be given.

Those little courtesies might become a form of trust.


'But is that Rowan person really okay drinking that much? The more liquor you bring him, the more he's been drinking.'


Yes.

That was the problem.

I kept giving it to him because he wanted it, but lately I'd been wondering whether I should actually stop him for the sake of his health.


'...If he's happy, then okay?'


'If he's happy, maybe that really is enough. That matters too.'


Lumiela seemed satisfied with that answer.

...I'd already been rather rude toward Old Man Rowan, after all.

If I had been meeting him as my ordinary self, I probably would have been bowing and scraping while asking to be taught.

But right now, I was appearing in the world as the masked man "Jane."

If I went around bowing my head too much, people would look down on me. Word spread easily, and Old Man Rowan was a drunk to begin with.

If the people around us learned that I wasn't actually strong-willed, I might start attracting people who saw me as easy prey.


So I had to avoid being disrespectful, while still preserving the dignity of Jane as a fearsome man who cruelly killed demons. I kept that delicate line in mind whenever I chose my wording or tone.

Still, at the very least, it should be fine to give him things.

A gift of thanks.

Though in a way it felt like our relationship was only tied together by goods rather than money.


When we entered town, the savory smell of food shops and the smoke of grilled skewers drifted by.

Just as I was about to look for a liquor store as usual, I had another thought.


'...Up until now, I've only been giving him booze.'


'? ...Yes, just booze. But he was happy.'


'I was thinking maybe I should bring some snacks for him too.'


'Snacks...! In other words, food!'


Lumiela didn't seem interested in the booze part, but the moment I mentioned snacks--food--her eyes lit up.


'Let's look for snacks while we're shopping for the liquor.'


'And if anything looks tasty--'


'Yeah. We'll buy some for ourselves too.'


Watching Lumiela beam happily and nod, I started down the cobbled road, when a certain scent suddenly brushed past my nose.

The sweet smell of ripe grapes and fermentation.

Wine.

Lumiela went expressionless, and I ended up making a similarly indescribable face. Masks really were useful at times like this.


Following the smell around a corner, I found an old signboard--a tasteful liquor shop carved with grapevines and a goblet.

It was still early morning, but when I pushed the door open, the heavy hinges groaned slowly. The moment I stepped inside, the air changed.

Countless barrels lined the walls, and farther in, shelves reaching all the way to the ceiling were packed with bottles.

Sunlight coming through the colored-glass windows lit up bottles of amber, crimson, and grape-purple.

The place had atmosphere.


At the back of the shop, a broad-armed owner was stirring a barrel with a wooden ladle. A distinctive wine scent rose from the surface, where crushed grape residue had settled.


"...O-oh. That's a fine mask you've got there... yeah. Welcome. Traveler, are you?"


"Yeah. I'm looking for some liquor as a gift for an acquaintance who likes to drink."


He was startled by my mask at first, but maybe he decided there were all kinds of people in the world, because he quickly responded normally. Occasionally you did see someone around town wearing a mask and looking like they came from some tribe or other, so this was one of the nicer things about a fantasy world: dressed like this, I only barely escaped being treated as suspicious.


At my words, the owner stroked his mustache and narrowed his eyes.


"Ohh, for an acquaintance, is it? And one who likes to drink! Then one or two bottles won't be enough. Let's pick out the local recommendations for you."


He led me to a wooden counter in the center of the store.

Apparently, this was where they pulled the stoppers from the barrels and let customers sample the wine.

The owner tilted a glass skillfully and poured a small amount of red wine into it.


"This is a bottle favored even by nobles in the royal capital. Fragrant, with a long finish."


When he swirled the glass, the wine's alcoholic aroma spread softly through the air.

And then...

he handed it to me.

What was I supposed to do now, drink it?


I took the offered glass and drank.

But I couldn't really tell what made it good. Was this wine actually delicious?

Maybe I should let Lumiela try it too--


'Ughhh.'


'Ah, right. Yeah. I can handle it myself.'


Seeing Lumiela trembling like a little puppy, I decided against it.

As usual, I'd have to confirm it for myself.




--------------------------------------------------




Next he led me to the back shelves.

There, a number of simple bottles were lined up in wooden boxes.

All of them had labels written by hand by artisans.


"These are made in the farming villages around here. They're strong, rustic wines with a rich sweetness from the grapes themselves. The kind of wine that feels like part of everyday life."


I tasted those too and still had no idea.


"Two bottles of this one, and two of the one from earlier."


I made the decision immediately.

If I kept hesitating, I'd never figure out what was good or bad anyway. So I chose based on the owner's utterly confident expression.

If he was that confident, then it was probably fine.


Once I paid, he wrapped the bottles in cloth and carefully packed them into a hemp sack. I remembered how the last time I'd brought wine, Old Man Rowan hadn't cared at all about the wrapping and had just ripped it open roughly.

Thinking of that, it felt like wrapping might not even be necessary, but still, manners were manners...

Well, yes.

We'd only known each other for a few days. I couldn't claim to fully understand what kind of man he was.


Settling on that conclusion, I looked around the shop to pass the time.

Against the wall stood an old grape press as decoration. The scars etched into its wooden gears and iron handle told the story of a store that had operated across generations.


By the time the wrapping was done, we left the shop.

As I walked, I listened to the faint clinking of the bottles inside the bag.


All right, next up is snacks.


Maybe because I had a clear goal in mind, my steps felt strangely light.

I gave a wry smile, feeling like I couldn't exactly tease Lumiela.

Having food as the objective really did make it this much easier to walk than before, and I found myself realizing that only now.
