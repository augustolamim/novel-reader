Episode 120: Zoo







"Since we're here, should we take a quick look at Area 3 too?"


That was what Mr. Rowan proposed right after we left Area 2.

His face was red.

And in his hand was a liquor bottle I hadn't seen before... I was pretty sure he hadn't had that before we fled. He might have stolen it on the way out.

Come to think of it, Mr. Rowan was a drunk too.

Maybe being drunk had made him bold.


From any sensible point of view, it was far too dangerous a place to drop in on.

Still, I was curious enough to want to see it.


"If Area 3 is packed with things like that, then I am curious what the place actually looks like."


"Fuheheh. The biggest pain, that S-rank adventurer, is off invading Area 2, so it should still be peaceful enough! We'll be fine, we'll be fine. We'll manage!"


Wearing a crisp, confident smile that looked exactly like the expression of someone who had lost the ability to make sound judgments thanks to alcohol, Mr. Rowan grinned at me. He was missing a few teeth, so he didn't look cool at all, and it gave his words absolutely no credibility.


'Lumiela. What do you think? The more dangerous a place is, the more likely it feels like there might be magical tools there or something.'


'...That S-rank adventurer seems to be the strongest one around, so I think we'll be fine, but if we're going, then let's be careful.'


That one line gave me the final push.

Honestly, I had to defeat the Demon King. If I got cold feet over something like this, then maybe that itself was the real problem. To build up my nerve, I really should go.


We passed through a dim corridor, leaving the uproar of Area 2 behind us as we headed toward Area 3.

For a while longer, I could still smell the scorched odor drifting from behind us, but every time we turned another corner, it thinned further and eventually disappeared.


And then--

we reached Area 3.

The instant I stepped in, slowly and carefully, the air changed in a way that was impossible to miss.

This place was...

another world?

No, more accurately, it felt like common sense itself had vanished somewhere. Like I had been reincarnated into yet another different world.


"Aaaaaaaarghhh!!!"


A scream split my ears.

When I turned toward it, I saw a shirtless man punching a wall. He was covered in blood and still punching. Judging by the blood pouring from his head, he had probably headbutted it too.


Right beside him, I heard another voice.


"Punching bag! Punching bag? Punching bag!"


When I looked, I saw a middle-aged man with a heart painted on his cheek bowing, kicking, and punching at empty air.


Faced with people who were clearly out of their minds, I froze where I stood.

Mr. Rowan had been drinking beside me, but his hand stopped partway to his mouth, and with a pale face he muttered,


"...Yeah, they're completely insane."


Exactly.

That was all I could think.

Lumiela stared at the same scene, her expression frozen over.

She was supposed to be a spirit of light, yet at the moment there was not a trace of brightness in her eyes. She slowly turned toward me and said in a flat voice with dead eyes,


'Let's go home. This is impossible.'


Her voice was faint as wind, and she turned her back like someone fleeing.




--------------------------------------------------




"Should we go back, then-- huh?"


I had just been about to say that to Mr. Rowan when another strange person entered my field of view.

A sticky voice echoed out.


"Ribbit. Ribbit ribbit..."


When I raised my eyes, something clinging to the wall moved.

Its skin was green. Its hands and feet had suction cups. It looked exactly like some kind of frog monster.


'A giant frog monster? Brace yourself, Brave!'


Lumiela went on alert.


'No, that... is a human.'


What I had taken for a frog monster...

turned out to have a human face.

The face belonged to a middle-aged man. His body wasn't frog-like either. It looked more like a middle-aged man in a frog costume, wearing something like a skinned frog-shaped stuffed suit.


'Is that really human...?'


'Probably...?'


I couldn't be sure.

For all I knew, maybe it really was some monster with only a human face--


"Ribbit."


The moment our eyes met, the frog-man beamed and started springing around the wall with his suction cups.

It was as if he had spent so long imitating a frog that he had convinced himself he truly was one.


As that was happening, I next heard the sound of flesh being pounded: thud, thud, thud.


"Hmph! Ooooohhh!"


There stood a huge man wearing nothing but furs around his body. He beat his chest with his fists and stamped the ground, completely like some imitation gorilla.

He bared his teeth and growled, "Uhooo!" in what sounded like a threat display.

At this point he was practically drumming. Maybe he needed to be returned to the wild.


I had already lost the ability to comment on any of this.

And yet there was something even stranger.


There were zombie-like figures too, walking in a silent line.

Looking closely, I could tell their bodies and clothes belonged to humans.

But the way they moved was unnaturally stiff.

As if someone were controlling them, every one of them tilted their head at the same angle, turned at the same speed, and looked in the same direction.

Even if I listened carefully, I could hear no heartbeat. Their breathing and even their footsteps were unnervingly uniform, like zombies...

or rather, like some kind of mechanical swarm.

They had human shapes, but they were different.


Their faces were frozen into terrifying distortions.

Expressions as if they had solidified in the middle of despair. Their eyes were wide open, hollow like black pits, with only the dried tracks of tears running down their cheeks. Their mouths were stuck halfway through a scream, hanging open as if their throats had split.

Faces ruled by pain and terror were turned toward us in countless numbers. It was as though they wanted to cry for help but had even had their voices stolen from them.

Their lightless eyes only stared at us without a single flicker. Their bodies crawled forward slowly, yet without ever stopping.


Like empty shells with their souls torn out, they seemed to have been ordered to do nothing but walk, and to keep walking forever.

I had the feeling our eyes met, and I stopped breathing.


Necromancy or anything like that shouldn't exist in this world.

No, maybe this was being done through some learned magical technique, like barriers or something.


No, this was too much.

This had gone far beyond the bounds of horror.

Everything in front of us was made up of people I wasn't even sure counted as human anymore.


Mr. Rowan and I slowly stepped backward without taking our eyes off them, just like people backing away from a bear, and fled from Area 3.


It felt like I had seen the bottom of humanity.

A nest for those who had thrown away reason.

That was probably what this place called Area 3 really was: the end.

And we had turned back even though we were only at the entrance. That was just the tip of the iceberg. There were probably countless more deeper inside.


"It's a zoo..."


More than humans, it felt like some zoo that collected bizarre human-like creatures.

My curiosity had gotten the better of me, but this was definitely the kind of thing that would show up in nightmares.

And on top of that...

one of those things was following us.


That zombie-like thing was slowly chasing after us.

Silent, simply watching us.


'I'll keep watch behind us.'


'Yeah.'


What even was this...?

My spine felt clammy with cold.


"At first, Area 3 was under the control of the underworld too. But then the madmen gathered there, and they abandoned it. Now it's their den. It's completely mad."


"Do the crazies at least get along with each other?"


"Who knows. But there were corpses lying around, and plenty of those lifeless walking-dead things too. I get the feeling it's a worse hell than it looks, even if we can't see all of it."


From the standpoint of humanity, they were probably on our side, technically speaking.

But no, that was impossible.

They had gone beyond mere criminals.


Well, at least now I had seen all the areas.

Area 2 might still be worth visiting if needed, so long as the mohawks weren't invading at the time.

But I swore to myself that I would never go to Area 3 again.

...It didn't seem like the kind of place where negotiation would work.
