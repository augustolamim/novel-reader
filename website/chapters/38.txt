Episode 128: Let's Go Get the Holy Sword








"All right. Everything's ready."


The day of departure had come.

As I loosened up my body in the inn room and put my thoughts in order by saying them out loud, that was what I said.


The coal-mining town of Rifat was wrapped in morning mist.

A thin layer of frost had settled over the roofs of the lodgings, and the morning sun lit it up in gold. Wagons carrying loads from the mine creaked down the slope in dull rhythm.

The miners' breaths came out white and slowly dissolved into the sky.

The smell of iron, earth, and sweat.

It had been harsh when I first arrived, but somewhere along the way I had started getting used to it.


At last, then.

My training with Lumiela, sending her fragments of my memories from my previous life--Famichiki, convenience stores, and the like--had for now reached its stopping point.


"You won't even let me snack, and then you show me things like this... that's just mean. If you keep it up, I might secretly eat something delicious out of spite, you know."


Lumiela had puffed out her cheeks and said that, which was about how much practice we'd done.

Now that she understood the sense of how to visualize things, the training was probably complete enough.


My swordsmanship training with Old Man Rowan would be on pause until I returned.

As for what I'd gained from it...

well, it wasn't as if I hadn't improved.

But those delicate, godlike techniques of Rowan's were still far beyond me.

Then again, swordsmanship wasn't the sort of thing you mastered overnight. This was probably just how it went.


I'd keep up the repetitive practice even when Rowan wasn't around, of course--


"Lumiela... isn't that too much?"


Lumiela kept packing emergency rations into the large backpack she'd only recently bought. Dried meat, hard black bread, portable soup, dried fruit--she was shoving in as much as would fit, as if preparing for a move.


When I said that, half exasperated, Lumiela stopped dead and put her hands on her hips.


"Brave. You're strong, but if you don't eat, you can't use your strength. If we get stranded, we need to have a lot of food."


"R-right."


The certainty in her tone made it obvious just how confident she was in that statement.

Then Lumiela turned back to the bag and resumed trying to cram things in, pushing it down only to have it pop back up. ...That bag looked like it might break.


"By the way, Brave... are you really taking that many books? We're going to get the Holy Sword, right?"


She asked that while trying to force the flap of the backpack shut with both hands.


"Well... once we climb the sacred peak, you'll understand."


"The sacred peak Mont Celerune... How can the Holy Sword be on that mountain...? ...I know. You still can't tell me yet, right?"


Lumiela looked troubled.

Naturally.

If she were going only by what she knew, then it would make perfect sense for her to believe that the Holy Sword had been taken away long ago by nobles.


But I knew from the original story.

The sacred peak Mont Celerune--

the highest mountain on the continent.

Its slopes were sheer, and even though it was not winter, the snow there never vanished all year long.

At the southeastern edge of the Kingdom, its summit rose up through the sea of clouds, wrapped in mystery.

From ancient times it had been worshiped as holy ground, and the mountain itself was regarded as a sanctuary.


At the summit of that mountain lay the Holy Sword Idea.

And the one who guarded it there--

the one who had reclaimed the sword from the nobles and, alongside the former hero, fought the Demon King--

should still be there as well.

Since she had lived for hundreds of years, of course her race was elf.

And for Lumiela, she was also an old friend:

the elven sage.


Though there was still a chance she wouldn't be there.

For example, Cain might already have contacted her, since she was an elf woman, or maybe out of spite he had thrown the sword away somewhere. No, though, if he had tried that, the sage probably wouldn't have stayed quiet.


I had never heard any mention of the sage being part of Cain's "World Breaker."

Her name hadn't been among them.

At least, I was pretty sure it hadn't.

...That said, I did have some rough idea why Cain might not have invited the sage to join him.


What came back to me was some casual talk I'd overheard back in the village from men around Cain's age. They had said such ridiculous things that the memory stuck.

According to Cain:

"Elves live a long time, sure, but if you're taking one as a wife, she's gotta be around your age or it's a no-go, right? A sage? Even if she looks young, she's still a hundreds-of-years-old hag on the inside. No way, no way, way too rough. Young elves are where it's at!"


He'd even gone so far as to name names while saying it, full of confidence.

The other villagers had no idea who some sage even was and had never met an elf in their lives, so they just got carried along by Cain's mood and started trash-talking elves along with him without even understanding what they were talking about.


Even so, it was still possible Cain had tried to recruit her as simple military strength, so I couldn't be sure.

Still, I hoped she'd still be waiting at the summit.


The moment I went to retrieve the Holy Sword, it was guaranteed that Jane = Hero would come out, no matter what.

And if at all possible, maybe the sage would even become an ally.

She was intelligent, after all, and if she could help us think through strategies and options for killing the Demon King, then that would only increase our choices.

At the very least, we had to make her keep quiet about there being...

well, not a hero, because I wasn't one...

but a human who could use light magic.

There was always a chance someone would publicly announce it under some excuse like "to reassure the people of the world."


Still, maybe that was asking too much.


In the original story, the sage cooperated with the original Brave's party, but did not travel with them.

She had lived quietly, almost like a recluse.

I couldn't expect much.


"I guess before we leave, I should at least say goodbye..."




--------------------------------------------------




Before departing, I went to say goodbye to Old Man Rowan, since we wouldn't be seeing each other for a while.


He had a campfire going and a pot set over it.

The fact that he was even preparing to start drinking from the morning on fit him exactly as always. I set down the twenty bottles of liquor I'd brought, along with a big assortment of dried meat and cheese.


"What is this, so much? I'm happy about it, mind you."


"I won't be able to come by for a while, so this is me making up for that."


"I see. The business you mentioned before, then--the one taking you away somewhere for a while. So training resumes after that?"


"Yeah."


Old Man Rowan narrowed his eyes suspiciously as he looked at me.

His gaze was sharp.


"Things have smelled a bit dangerous lately... though maybe it's got nothing to do with you."


"Dangerous? ...The nobles trying to stir something up again?"


"Hmm. The folks above are in a real hurry. They haven't told me a thing, mind you. What do you say? Want me to ask around? If I request it, someone might tell me."


"No, it's fine. I'm not interested."


Seeing how firmly I answered that, Old Man Rowan blinked in mild surprise, but soon went back to his usual wry smile.


If I stuck my nose into this sort of thing, it was guaranteed to turn out badly.

Noble power struggles, military movements--the more I learned, the more annoying problems I'd end up carrying with me.

The whole "you've learned something you weren't supposed to know!" development usually rolled in exactly the wrong direction.


Ignoring it was the safest move.

No, more than that, I should get moving already.

Right now, there was only one thing to do:

go get the Holy Sword Idea.


Old Man Rowan let out a small sigh and turned his back to me.


"...Very well. Your path is yours to decide. It's not my place to prattle on. But--"


He looked back over his shoulder and fixed me with sharp eyes.


"Don't go dying. If you don't come back, I'll run out of liquor and snacks and starve to death."


"...That's a uniquely selfish way to worry about someone."


Maybe he really was already drunk.

Well, I supposed when your skill was good enough to justify being drunk all the time, that was just how it was.


"You've helped me a lot. I'll be back."


"Mm. ...Take care out there."


Turning my back to Old Man Rowan, I began heading south toward the sacred peak Mont Celerune.

The cold morning air bit into my lungs. Lumiela drifted down beside me, and soft light brushed against my cheek.


'Come on, let's go. If it's the path you chose, then I'll follow it with you. It'll be fine. You're not alone.'


'Yeah... I'm counting on you.'


Walking along the road deeply grooved by wagon wheels, I felt a strange exhilaration rising in my chest.

We probably wouldn't be back for a while.

It would be another two-person journey with Lumiela.


In the original story, climbing Mont Celerune had been troublesome, but if we took the fastest route...

I would be lying if I said I had no worries about the trial of the Holy Sword Idea.

But there was no choice.


I fixed my eyes ahead.


"Let's go, Lumiela. We're going to get the Holy Sword."


Lumiela nodded firmly.

And so our journey, the two of us together, began once more.
